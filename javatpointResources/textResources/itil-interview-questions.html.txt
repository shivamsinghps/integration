p => [ ITIL is a short form of Information Technology Infrastructure Library. It is a set of comprehensive practices and guidelines that helps software developers provide the best IT services according to the current market trends and requirements. Additionally, it helps businesses establish a competitive environment and achieve their goals in the best possible way, including better plan, management, and deliveries. ]

h3 => [ 24) What is defined by the PDSA cycle? Also, explain its phases. ]
p => [ The term PDSA here is the short form of plan-do-check-act. It is a 4-step approach that is mainly used continuous improvements of any product or process in an enterprise. It is also known as the Deming cycle/wheel/circle. ]
p => [ The four phases can be explained as: ]
p => [  Plan:  Analyzing and identifying the current problem. ]
p => [  Do:  Developing the necessary solution for the problem. ]
p => [  Check:  Testing a solution and checking how effectively it handles the problem. Additionally, finding other options that could be helpful in the improvements. ]
p => [  Act:  Implementing the final solution. ]

h3 => [ 29) How will you define an Incident and a Problem? ]
p => [  Incident:  It is usually defined as an event that is not part of a standard process. In other words, it is the event that causes unplanned interruption or disruption to an IT service. ]
p => [  Problem:  It is generally defined as the underlying cause of a conflict or chain of events. ]
h3 => [ 30) How will you differentiate proactive problem management from reactive problem management? ]
p => [ The main function of proactive problem management is to stop incidents before happening by finding potential problems and IT infrastructure errors. On the other side, reactive problem management's main function is to identify and eliminate the main reasons of recognized incidents. ]

h3 => [ 33) What is defined by the term PIR in the context of ITIL? ]
p => [  The term PIR is a short form of  'Post Implementation Review' . PIR in ITIL is usually defined as the identification and examination of the complete working process, along with the solution. The post-implementation review evaluates the implementation's effectiveness after the change request is implemented into the system. It is a free-form review report that is generally processed once the live running session of the project has been done. ]

p => [ The primary goal of capacity management is to ensure that IT services and resources are appropriately sized to meet service level objectives for current and upcoming business needs in a cost-effective and timely manner. ]
p => [ Capacity Management has three following sub-processes: ]
li => [ Service Capacity Management ]
li => [ Component Management ]
li => [ Business Capacity Management ]
h3 => [ 35) What is meant by the term OLA? ]
p => [  The term OLA is a short form of  'Operational Level Agreement' . OLA is usually defined as the contract that emphasizes the different IT groups within the organization. This also defines how the various IT groups will design and deliver their services to support SLA (Service Level Agreement). ]
h3 => [ 36) What are some commonly used Recovery options? ]
p => [ The following are the most commonly used Recovery options: ]
p => [  Fast Recovery:  As the name suggests, this recovery option is speedy and takes place within 24 hours. It is also called a hot standby. ]
p => [  Intermediate Recovery:  This type of recovery mode usually takes a few days. Here, recovery possibly takes three to four days. It is referred to as warm standby as it used mirroring load balancing. ]
p => [  Gradual Recovery:  This type of recovery may take a relatively long time. It can take a few weeks. But, the time will always exceed 24 hours in this recovery mode. It is also called a cold standby. ]
h3 => [ 37) What does the Freeze period mean in ITIL? ]
p => [ The freeze period is defined as the specific time within the development process, after which the methods required to make changes to the source code seems much more difficult. ]
h3 => [ 38) What is defined by the term CAB? ]
p => [  The term CAB is a short form of  'Change Advisory Board' . CAB involves a different authoritative and representative group of professionals who mainly help in Change Management. The CAB people assist with assessment, authorization, prioritization, and scheduling of the requested and the necessary changes whenever required. ]
h3 => [ 39) Define the following terms: Service pipeline, Service portfolio, and Service catalog. ]
p => [  Service Pipeline:  It is defined as a listing of those services that are not yet ready and reside in the development mode. ]
p => [  Service Portfolio:  It is defined as a listing of the comprehensive services offered by a service provider to the customers or shared throughout the entire market. ]
p => [  Service Catalog:  It is defined as a listing of those services that have already been developed to be provided to the customers. It is known as the subset of the Service portfolio. ]
h3 => [ 40) Define Expedite/Urgent Changes and Emergency Changes. ]
p => [  Expedite Change:  It is a kind of change that meets a specified business or legal needs but does not relate to any restoring service. ]
p => [  Emergency Change:  It is a kind of change that is taken care of on a priority basis. It is considered a top priority change in any organization that must be implemented as quickly as possible. ]
