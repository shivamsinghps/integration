h3 => [ 1) What is SAP MM? / What do you understand by SAP MM? ]
p => [ We know that SAP stands for Systems, Applications &amp; Products in Data Processing. SAP MM is a functional module in SAP that deals with procurement handling and material management. In SAP MM, MM stands for Material Management. The SAP MM module contains master data, material valuation, invoice verification, material requirement planning, system configuration, transactions, etc., to complete the procurement to pay process. ]

p => [  Goods Receipt:  In SAP MM, goods receipt is the physical inbound transfer for goods or material into the warehouse. h3 => [ 12) What are the different steps used in Procurement Lifecycle? Explain them in detail. ]
p => [ Following are the different steps used in Procurement Lifecycle: ]
strong => [ Requirement and Information Gathering ]
p => [ The procurement process begins with gathering information about a product and its quantity first. Then for required products and services, it is necessary to look for suppliers who can satisfy the requirements. ]
strong => [ Supplier Contact ]
p => [ This step comes after requirement and information gathering. After gathering requirements, one will look for the suppliers who can fulfill the requirements. Based on that, we can make a quotation request or information request to the suppliers or direct contact with them. ]
strong => [ Background Review ]
p => [ Once the supplier is known, product/service quality is checked, and any necessities for services such as Installation, warranty, and maintenance are investigated. Some samples of the products can be obtained for qualifying examination. ]
strong => [ Negotiation ]
p => [ Some negotiations with suppliers are made regarding the products/services' price, availability, and delivery schedule of the products/services. In this step, a contract is signed, a binding legal document between the supplier and the ordering party. A contract will include all necessary information like price and quantity of material, delivery date, etc. ]
strong => [ Order fulfillment ]
p => [ Ordered material will be shipped, delivered to the ordering party, and the supplier is paid accordingly. Training and Installation of products or services may also be included. ]
strong => [ Consumption, Maintenance, and Disposal ]
p => [ As the products/service is consumed, the performance of the products or services is evaluated, and any follow-up service support, if required, is analyzed. ]

h3 => [ 43) What do you understand by consumption-based planning (CBP)? ]
p => [ CBP stands for Consumption-Based Planning, which is used to forecast to find out the future requirements. It is dependent upon past consumption values. Based on CBP, the net requirement of goods is calculated. CBP is activated either when the stock level comes below a particular reorder point or when the forecast needs to calculate past consumption values. ]
h3 => [ 44) What do you understand by the term material requirement planning (MRP)? ]
p => [ In SAP MM, MRP stands for Material Requirement Planning. It is a module or a sort of planning tool in SAP ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning). It is used to assist procurement and production planners in establishing realistic and possible plans to help initiate procurement and production procedures. ]
h3 => [ 45) What is the difference between the terms MRP and CBP? ]
p => [ The primary difference between the term CBP and MRP is that when we plan material, we use MRP to predict the requirements of materials based on SOP (sales and operations planning). On the other hand, in CBP, we have to forecast the MM (material management) depending upon the historical demand for materials. ]
