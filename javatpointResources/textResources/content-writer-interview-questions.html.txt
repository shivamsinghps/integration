h3 => [ 13) What is proofreading in content writing? What are some proofreading techniques used by proofreaders? ]
p => [ Proofreading is a type of review work where the proofreader reviews for grammar, punctuations, correct use of language, and format. It is the final touch in content writing and ensures that the written content is correct, consistent, and professional. ]
strong => [ Following is a list of some proofreading techniques that can be used to get the perfect content: ]
p => [  Take a break and then check the article:  This is important if you are a content writer and you have to proofread your article. It is recommended to take a 12 to 24-hour break from the content and then proofread it for maximum effectiveness. ]
p => [  Cross-check the article:  It is recommended to take a printout and then do the proofreading rather than on a laptop. ]
p => [  Remove excess words:  Good content is concise and straightforward. Excess words, like adjectives or adverbs, can detract from the real message of the content. So, it is advised to remove excessive, repetitive, and erroneous words from the content when proofreading. ]
p => [  Remove Punctuation mistakes:  Punctuation mistakes are the most common ones in content writing. So, checking the punctuation is necessary for proofreading. ]
p => [  Check sentence structure:  The sentence structure of the content must be good. A proper sentence must have a subject, a verb, and have a complete sense. A proofreader should review each sentence for proper structure and punctuation. ]
p => [  Use software:  There are a lot of online tools and software that a proofreader can use. For example, you can use Microsoft's Spelling and Grammar tool or Grammarly's online editor to find spelling, grammatical errors, and syntax errors that may be escaped by your spell-check. ]
p => [  Review flow and sequencing:  A proofreader should check the formatting consistency, order, the numbering of images, tables, and appendices, and the general presentation within the content. ]
p => [  Fact check:  The facts and the information used in the content must be trustworthy and accurate. Every detail must be correct. A proofreader should cross-check all these things. ]
