Clampers (electronics)

The clampers are used to add the dc level to the ac input signal. The input swing of a waveform is equal to the output swing. But, sometimes, it becomes necessary to add a certain dc level to an ac signal. The circuits possessing such capability to increase the signal level are known as clamper circuits. The clamper circuits are also known as dc restorer circuits or dc inserter circuits.

The basic elements of a clamper circuit are diodes, resistors, and capacitors. The diode conducts the current in a particular direction and prevents the circuit from high voltages. The resistor limits the current for the diode, and the capacitors are the charge storage elements.

The input waveform of most circuits is AC sinusoidal waveform. The ac level is the level of the sinusoidal AC waveform. Thus, it is termed as the ac level.

The DC level is added in the input signal that shifts the waveform to the desired level (upward or downward) without changing the width of the signal. It is called as DC inserter because it adds a DC voltage level to the output waveform. We can also say that clamper adds value to the Y-axis of the signal waveform.

The steps to analyze the clamper circuits are as follows:

  1. The capacitor stores the charge when the diode is operating.
  2. When the diode is not conducting, the capacitor uses the stored charge to maintain the voltage level of the circuit.
  3. The output waveform is drawn.
  4. The dc shift introduced in the output waveform can be positive or negative.

Advantages of Clampers

The advantages of clampers are as follows:

  • It is used to remove the distortion in the circuit.
  • It can be used as a voltage multiplier because of the output voltage, which is 2Vm, i.e., twice the maximum input voltage.
  • Clampers in the amplifiers protect it from large signals.
  • It is used to add the dc level to the input signal.
  • The battery in the clampers circuits is used to control the dc level to the desired value.
  • It also helps to identify the polarity of the input and output voltage of the circuit.
  • The clampers are used to modify the shape and range of the input waveform.
  • It is used as a dc restorer due to the presence of a capacitor that stores charge. The stored charge can be further used when the diode is in non-conducting state.

Clampers vs. Clippers

Let's discuss the differences between the clampers and the clippers circuit.

Category Clampers Clippers
Definition It is used to add the dc level to the ac input waveform. It is used to remove a specific portion of the input waveform.
Capacitor The capacitor is a charge storage element, whose charge can be utilized when the diode is in a non-conducting state. No capacitor is used.
Types The clampers are classified as positive clampers and negative clampers. The clippers are classified as positive clippers and negative clippers.
Output voltage It is twice or more than the input voltage. It is less than the input voltage.
DC level It shifts upwards (positive axis) or downwards (negative axis). It remains the same.
Shape of the waveform The shape of the waveform signal is generally sinusoidal. The shape of the waveform signal can be sinusoidal, rectangular, etc.
Applications It is used as voltage multipliers, dc restorers, noise reducers, etc. It is used in power supplies, FM (Frequency Modulation) transmitters, etc.
