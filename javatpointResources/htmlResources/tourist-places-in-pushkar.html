Tourist Places in Pushkar

The town of festivals and fairs. One of India's oldest cities in Pushkar. Thousands of visitors and worshippers go to Rajasthan each year, and Pushkar, a beautiful city northwest of Ajmer, is a famous destination. Five hundred ten meters of hills encircle Pushkar on three sides. The "Nag Pahar," or Snake Mountain, naturally separates Ajmer and Pushkar. The famous Pushkar rose, sometimes referred to as "the rose garden of Rajasthan," is sold worldwide. Pushkar is a fascinating city with a rich mythological history and timeless architectural legacy.

According to tradition, a lake was instantly created when Lord Brahma, believed to be the universe's creator, dropped a lotus on the earth. In honour of the lotus, he then chose the name Pushkar for the place. The city of Pushkar is home to the only temple in the entire globe devoted to Lord Brahma. For Hindus, visiting Pushkar is the greatest achievement of pilgrimages that one must do to find salvation.

1. Pushkar Lake:

Pushkar Lake is said to be as old as creation itself because of the legend that it was created when a lotus fell from the hands of Lord Brahma, the creator deity. Hindu mythology describes the five lakes: Man Sarovar, Bindu Sarovar, Narayan Sarovar, Pampa Sarovar, and Pushkar Sarovar. The holiest of all of them is believed to be Pushkar Lake. Devotees believe that bathing in the lake on Kartik Poornima (during the Pushkar Fair) will atone for sins and cure all skin problems. This artificial lake was created by building a dam hidden away in the Aravalli range, surrounded by a desert and hills.

The lake contains 52 bathing ghats (steps that go into the water), and it is said that the water at each ghat has unique healing potential. Over 500 temples may be seen along the shores of Pushkar Lake, indicating that it is a well-liked pilgrimage site.

The Ways to Get here:

Buses depart from Ajmer station every hour. Also available are cabs and tuk-tuks.

Locations to see which are close to Pushkar Lake are

  • Ajmer 15 km,
  • Jodhpur 186 km and
  • Jaipur, 145 km.

Activities include:

  • Doing pooja for the ancestors.
  • Praying at the ghats.
  • Participating in aarti.
  • Taking photographs.

Open 24 hours a day.

Free entry.

2. Brahma Temple:

The Jagatpita Brahma Mandir, close to the well-known Pushkar Lake, honours Lord Brahma, the Hindu God of Creation. Although the existing structure comes from the 14th century, the original construction is roughly 2000 years old. Pushkar is home to more than 500 temples, many of which are quite old, but the Brahma temple is the most prominent one. After coming to earth to carry out a yajna, according to mythology, Brahma built his temple here. Hindu philosopher Adi Shankara renovated the temple in the eighth century. However, Maharaja Jawat Raj of Ratlam is credited with repairing and making minor modifications to the existing structure. The Lord Brahma and Gayatri, his second wife, are honoured in the temple's.

Marble and stone stabs are used to construct the temple. The temple has a striking hamsa (bird) theme, and its scarlet pinnacle (shikhara) may be spotted from a distance. A celebration honouring Brahma is held in the town on Kartik Poornima during Pushkar Festival. After taking a bath and purifying oneself in the famous Pushkar Lake, devout pilgrims go to the temple. One of the top 10 most spiritual locations in the world, the Brahma Temple is regarded as one of the five holiest pilgrimage sites for Hindus.

The Ways to Get here:

Buses depart from Ajmer station every hour. Cabs and tuk-tuks are also available.

Locations to see which are close to Brahma Temple are

  • Ajmer16 km,
  • Jodhpur 186 km, and
  • Jaipur 145 km.

Brahma Temple Rd. in Ganahera, Pushkar, Rajasthan 305022 is the address.

Opening times are from 5:00 am to 1:30 pm every day.

Free entry.

3. Gurudwara Singh Sabha:

Starting in the early 19th century, the eastern part of Pushkar's Gurudwara Singh Sabha was built to honour the visits of the first and tenth gurus, Guru Nanak Dev and Guru Govind Singhji.

Location: Pushkar, Rajasthan 305022, across from Indian Overseas Bank.

Open all the time.

4. Varaha temple:

The Varaha Temple is Pushkar's biggest and oldest temple. This temple was built by King Anaji Chauhan in the 12th century and honoured Lord Vishnu's third appearance as a wild boar. The mythology says that Varaha prevented a devil from dragging the earth into the primordial ocean (Hirnayaksh). One of Pushkar's most visited temples is this one.

The Varaha Temple was destroyed by Mughal emperor Aurangzeb because he found the temple's representation of a man's body with a boar's head offensive. In 1727, the temple was restored by Raja Sawai Jai Singh II.

Pushkar Main Market, Rajasthan 305022, is the address.

Opening times are from 5:00 am to 1:30 pm every day.

Free entry.

5. Savitri temple:

This temple is situated on a mountain just behind the Brahma temple and is dedicated to Lord Brahma's first wife, Goddess Savitri. One may get a panoramic view of the lake, nearby temples, and dunes while acclimating to the long flight of steps leading to the temple. Savitri cursed Brahma for marrying Gayatri while beginning his yagna at Pushkar; that's why there is only one Brahma Temple in Pushkar.

The goddesses Savitri and Gayatri's idols are kept at the temple. You can go through Ratnagiri Hill to reach the temple as the route goes through many hills. This trip is particularly crucial from a religious perspective since it is seen as a form of atonement for our sins.

Address: Kharekhari Rd, Pushkar, Rajasthan 305022

The doors are open every day from 5:00 am to 1:30 pm.

Entry is free.

6. Rangji Temple:

Another well-known temple that attracts many pilgrims and visitors each year is the graceful and striking Rangji Temple. Lord Rangji, who is said to be an avatar of Lord Vishnu, is the subject of the temple. The temple's architecture influences South Indian, Rajput, and Mughal styles. Another aspect of the temple that attracts tourists is the tall "Gopuram," which is mostly found in temples in South India.

Location: Rajasthan, Pushkar 305022

Opening times are from 5:00 am to 1:30 pm every day.

Free entry.

7. Rose Garden:

Pushkar is surprisingly well-known for its rose growing. The Rajasthan Rose Garden is the name given to Rose Garden. The farmers in this area raise a lot of different varieties of roses, which are exported all over the world. Various coloured, fragrant, and stunning red roses cover acres of farmland, enticing one to relax and spend some beautiful time. Travel to these popular Pushkar tourism destinations for an unforgettable trip with your loved ones.

Activities: Take a seat, unwind, and rest while taking in the fascinating fragrance of roses.

8. Naga Pahar:

The Naga Pahar hills separate Pushkar from Ajmer. According to mythology, the hills of Naga Pahar are still eroding and may soon totally disappear. The famous Sage Agastya is thought to have lived among these mountains. Nag Kund Lake is located in the mountains as well. From its height, Naga Pahar provides a magnificent view of Pushkar and is a popular destination for photographers to take pictures. It is among the top sites in Pushkar to come for a special vacation.

Activities: taking pictures, short hikes, and contemplative walks

Opening times: Every day

Free entry.

9. Kishangarh:

There are many attractions to see even in Pushkar. Kishangarh, a township outside Pushkar, is well-known for its majestic forts and marble mines. Roopangarh Fort and the Phool Mahal provide a glimpse into the area's historical past. To better understand the procedures involved in creating the marble slabs in your home, visiting the marble mines in Kishangarh is worthwhile.

Visit the forts renovated into hotels and shop for décor made of marble.

10. Man Mahal:

Raja Man Singh of Amber constructed this stunning palace, and it is near the Pushkar Sarovar. There is a temple inside the palace, which was constructed using conventional Rajasthani style. The Rajasthan Tourism Development Corporation cares for the palace, which provides a lodging facility for tourists. The stunning sunset views of the nearby lakes and temples are the main attractions for tourists. On request, tourists can arrange for camel rides and camping. Unquestionably, the Man Mahal is among the top destinations in Rajasthan to see in January.

Activities: Take a camel or cart ride, enjoy Pushkar's beautiful scenery, and attend the evening aarti.

Times: Every day of the week

Free entry.

11. Apteshwar Temple:

The Apteshwar Temple, which is devoted to Lord Mahadev or Shiva, is also included on the list of the top attractions in Pushkar. This underground temple is said to have been constructed in the 12th century before being covered. It is very important to the people, so the temple is usually crowded with believers yearly. To obtain the graces of God, they arrive bearing gifts such as milk, bel (leaf), and other goods.

Location: Rajasthan's Pushkar

6:30 am to 8:30 pm are the times.

12. The Laughing Buddha Café:

One of the finest things to do in Pushkar is to satisfy your soul with the delicious flavors of Rajasthani cuisine, which is easy to achieve given that Pushkar is also a well-known travelling destination. When you become hungry while exploring Pushkar, come to the Lord Buddha Vegan Cafe, where you may eat a variety of dishes. Your stomach will be filled, and your heart will be joyful after eating the vegan dish made from fresh fruit.

Location: Near the Gau Ghat Entrance on Main Market Road in Badi Basti, Pushkar, Rajasthan 305022

Time: 9:30 AM to 10:30 PM

13. Pap Mochini temple:

The Pap Mochini temple presided over by the goddess Ekadashi Mata is said to provide devotees relief from their sins. The temple in Pushkar's northern region resembles a jewel in the city's crown. One of the most well-known temples in Rajasthan is the Pap Mochini temple, which is highly respected spiritually and has beautiful architecture.

14. Merta:

Merta is nearly 400 years old and well-known for the Meera Temple, a devotee of Krishna. This city, originally known as Medantak, is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Pushkar since it is the birthplace of Meera Bai. Here are two other temples, Dadhimati and Charbhuja, which see many visitors annually. A famous destination in Pushkar, this city is also rich in historical value because of the numerous conflicts it has seen. If you are going to Pushkar, make sure it is one of them.

What to do:

Attend aarti at Charbhuja Temple and see the renowned Raj palace and temples.

From sunrise to sunset, Mangla aarti at Charbhuja Temple occurs between 5:30 and 6:30 am.

Free entry.

15. Mahadeva Temple:

One of the top tourist destinations in Rajasthan and one of the most well-known cities in India is Pushkar, famous for its stunning temples. The city has many attractive temples. These temples offer a spiritual retreat and lead visitors through this old city's historic streets. The Mahadeva Temple, one of the well-known tourist attractions in Pushkar, is one such temple. The 19th-century Mahadeva Temple is among Pushkar's most well-known attractions. The main God of the temple is Lord Shiva, who is shown inside as a statue made of white marble. This temple's highlights include beautiful murals and polished floors. The embossed pink pillars are another prominent feature of its construction.

16. Pushkar Bazaar:

A trip isn't complete without visiting the local market to buy some things. Therefore, if Pushkar is your first tourist spot, visit its namesake market. You will discover many people selling Rajasthani-inspired handicrafts, purses, souvenirs, and colourful clothing here. Rose pulp and gulkand are a couple of the specific items available here. Additionally, you may buy various leather products made from camel skin. As one of the best sites to visit in Pushkar, make sure you walk through the streets of Pushkar Bazaar.

Location: Rajasthan's Pushkar Bazaar

Timings: Anytime

17. In Pushkar, where can Visitors stay?

In Rajasthan, Pushkar has some of the most luxurious and diverse lodging options. The top resorts in Pushkar for every price range are listed below:

  • For luxurious facilities: Aaram Bagh and Pushkar Resort.
  • Cheap hotels include Hotel Lake View and Hotel Lake Palace.

18. Going To Pushkar:

The Pink City Express and Shatabdi Express are the top choices when travelling to Ajmer from Delhi and Jaipur. The nearest railway station is in Ajmer (11 km). Pushkar has nonstop bus service to Ajmer (30 minutes, 20 INR), Jaipur (3 hours, 220-410 INR), Udaipur (6 hours, 450 INR), and night buses to Delhi (10 hours, INR 400-850). From Jaipur, it is only 147 kilometres via road. It is one of the greatest cheap excursions in India because of how easily it can be reached from Delhi.

Pushkar is not a particularly big city, so getting around is simple by walking. Here, the tour companies arrange walks and spiritual trips. Another choice is to rent a bike and explore the neighborhood. Another excellent choice for exploring the holy city is a camel safari.
