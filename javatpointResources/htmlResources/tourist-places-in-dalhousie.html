Tourist Places in Dalhousie

Dalhousie, located in the Chamba district of Himachal Pradesh, is present amidst oak trees and green pine, and it is a gorgeous yet pristine hill town of the state where one can plan his holidays trip with family, friends or loved ones. Dalhousie, a tourist attraction which is characterized by colonial architecture, cool mountain breeze, glistening river & lush green mountains, is positioned on the Dhauladhar Range of Himalayas. One of the serene towns in India, Dalhousie is famous for its vintage charm and preserved beauty. The best time to visit this place is from April till June when the summer season starts. At the starting of summer, there is a little bit of cold, but towards June, the weather warms up here. The reason that this time and this season is considered the best season and time to visit the Dalhousie is one can go for exploring and sightseeing tourist attractions and natural beauty with the support of the weather. This weather supports the tourists for making some picture-perfect memories, and they can also take some scenic walks all around this tourist attraction. Not just one or two, there are many tourist attractions in this hill town of Himachal Pradesh that one can visit during their trip to Dalhousie and experience some of the most awesome things of their life. This article talks about all such tourist attractions of Dalhousie, which one should include in their list while planning a trip here.

1. Subhash Baoli

Named after the Subhash Chandra Bose, a freedom fighter, this tranquil location in Dalhousie is an ideal spot to witness the natural beauty and relax in the calm environs. This place is a spring known to possess medicinal properties, and many people say they have treated and cured the health of Subhash Chandra too. Visitors can pay tribute to the freedom fighter here and enjoy an evening walk and picnic. The snow-dusted hillock catches the eye along the spring that cascades into a frosty stream.

The place is open from Monday to Sunday

Entry charges - None

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Evening stroll near the spring
  • Picnic with family and friends
  • Loosen up in its calm environment

2. Garam Sadak

It is a route in the Dalhousie that link Gandhi Chowk with Subhash Chowk and is amongst the undiscovered locations to explore in the small town. Visitors can enjoy the valley's picturesque view from the Thai road fringed with tall trees and luxurious greenery. Wandering around in this place is considered the most touristy thing to do in the town. In addition, visitors will be able to see extraordinary Tibetan Rock Paintings sculpted on the walls on the wayside.

The place is open from Monday to Sunday

Entry charges - None

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Witness incredible scenes of the valley
  • A leisurely stroll
  • Watch beautiful Tibetan Rock Paintings carved on the roadside

3. Panchpula

Panchpula is a preferred picnic area in the town, placed 3.5 KM far away from the shopping market, Gandhi Chowk. Surrounded by a blanket of pine and deodar trees, it is famous for the Saptdhara waterfalls, which is the Ganji Pahari trek's starting point. A Samadhi or monument is also erected nearby this place to memorialize Sardar Ajit Singh (martyr Bhagat Singh's uncle), a rebellious and revolutionary.

The place is open from Monday to Sunday

Entry fee - No entry fee

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Ziplining
  • Trekking
  • Burma Bridge
  • Taste local snacks and tea

4. John's Church

St. John's Church was built back in 1863 and is the oldest church in the town. This church is a perfect mix of history and beauty and is considered one of the best attractions to explore in Dalhousie. It is quite popular amongst photographers due to its beautiful construction. On Sunday, various locals and visitors assemble at this church to witness the performance of the Sunday Service. As this place is also a reminder of the British rule in the region, it is admired by history lovers. In addition, the church showcases architecture embellished with glass paintings of St John the Baptist together with St Peter.

Opening hours - 7:00 am to 7:00 pm

Entry fee - No entry fee

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Photography
  • Acquire knowledge about historical events
  • Be present at Sunday Service
  • See the beautiful glass paintings and impressive architectonics

5. Gandhi Chowk (Mall Road)

GPO or Gandhi Chowk is a place in Dalhousie to enjoy a shopping tour and is a mall road in this small town. Visitors can buy traditional handicrafts from local markets and take gifts for themselves and their loved ones. The Himachal Handloom Industry Emporium and the Tibetan Market are among some of the famous shops at Gandhi Chowk from where one can buy memento, woolen clothes and much more.

The place is open from Monday to Sunday

Entry charges - None

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Explore the Emporium on Thandi Sarak
  • Buy traditional handiwork and keepsakes
  • Pay a visit to Bhittico to get Kullu shawls, pashmina and angora

6. Ganji Pahari

Ganji Pahari in Dalhousie offers its visitors an astounding view of snow-topped peaks. One has to trek to reach the peak that begins from Panchpula, and as this trek is easy to moderate, anyone can do it. Also named Bald Hill, Ganji Pahari is popular for its complete absence of vegetation on the hillock. Tourists can plan their winter trip to see a thick blanket of snow covering this hillock. Explore Ganji Pahari during dawn or sundown for the best experience.

The place is open from Monday to Sunday

Entry charges - None

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Trekking
  • Camping (advance booking is required)
  • Watch eye-catching views of mountains

7. Kalatop Wildlife Sanctuary

Kalatop Wildlife Sanctuary has a wide range of luxurious meadows, thick deodar foliage, freshwater streams and more, making it a must-visit tourist attraction in the town for wildlife admirers. Kalatop means "black cap", which refers to the black forest enwrap in this wildlife sanctuary's highest summit. Various trekking and hiking trails are available inside the sanctuary, which makes up for a refined nature walk.

Opening hours - 7:00 am to 6:00 pm

Entry charges - fluctuates from season to season. Contact the officials before visiting the sanctuary.

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Be amused by the scenic views of the Pir Panjal range
  • Have a taste of snacks and tea at a small tea stall
  • Hiking and trekking
  • Watching rich flora and fauna
  • Stay in the small hostelry inside the site.

8. Satdhara Falls

Surrounded by astounding views of the Chamba Valley, Satdhara Falls is considered one of the best attractions to explore in Dalhousie. The luxuriant green pine and deodar trees are worth witnessing, and the cool breeze caressing the face makes one feel refreshed. It is believed that this place contains mica, which is popular for its medicinal properties in treating and curing skin diseases. If one wants to go out for a peaceful nature stroll, then this is an ideal location for them. Additionally, the fragrance of flowers will vanish all the visitors' worries and hypnotize them with its charm.

The place is open from Monday to Sunday

Entry fee - None

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Picnic with loved ones
  • Trek to the falls
  • A beautiful nature walk
  • Witness the picturesque charm
  • Capture the breathtaking falls and their environs

9. Chamera Lake

Chamera Lake is one of the great destinations to explore in Dalhousie and to enjoy a fun day out. It is a popular picnic spot for families and couples who wants to spend some quality time together. One can even enjoy some water activities here for an unforgettable time. Placed at an elevation of 763 meters above sea level, the lake is enveloped by dedodar conifers and snow-topped mountains. For a passionate photographer, Chamera Lake is amongst the inescapable locations in the town.

The place is open from Monday to Sunday

Entry fee - None

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Enjoy picnicking with family and friends
  • Capture beautiful pictures of the lake
  • Have fun doing boating, kayaking, canoeing and river rafting
  • Have a taste of beverages and snacks at local shops and stalls

10. Khajjiar

Also referred to as "Mini Switzerland of India ", Khajjiar is a small town positioned at a distance of 20 km from Dalhousie. This place has a plentiful natural beauty that draws the attention of travelers from everywhere. Visit a small lake in the midst of grassland and click photos for memories. Visitors can get involved in fun activities like paragliding and create thousands of memories that will last evermore. The nine-hole golf course is the most popular part of Khajjiar, placed in the centre of luxurious greenery. For a great experience, travel here during winter to play in snow.

The place is open from Monday to Sunday

Entry charges - None

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Savor local food
  • Handicraft shopping
  • Horse riding, paragliding, zorbing

11. Chamba

Chamba is a Himalayan town in Himachal popular for its alluring prehistoric shrines, caves and monuments. It is a haven for a history enthusiast. Placed at an elevation of 996 meters, it sits on the Ravi River's banks. Few people know that Chamba is an unconventional place that provides natural beauty and tranquil environs. Also, not known by many, it is the campground for several camps in the great Himalayan ranges.

The place is open from Monday to Sunday

Entry charges - None

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Trekking
  • Witness eye-catching views of Pir Panjal ranges, Zanskar, and Dhauladhar
  • Shop for traditional handiwork and artwork
  • Attend the famous festivals of Minjar Mela (2nd Sunday of August), Suhi Mata Mela (March/April)

12. Dainkund Peak

With the scenic views of snow-topped peaks and pleasing valleys, Dainkund Peak is amongst the most popular tourist attractions to explore in Dalhousie. Located at an elevation of 2755 meters above sea level, it is the highest point of this attractive hill town. Trekking to this peak is quite easy whether one is with family or in a couple. Visitors can follow the pathway laid with tall deodar trees and colorful flowers and get to the peak to enjoy a panoramic view of valleys and hills.

The place is open from Monday to Sunday

Entry charges - None

Activities to enjoy at the place:

  • Camping
  • Trekking
  • Visit Pholani Devi Temple and Air Force Station
  • Witness striking views of snow-clad mountaintops.
