20 Best Tourist Places in Gujarat

Gujarat, located in India's west part, is the hustle and bustle state that draws people throughout the year. The state is a phenomenal mixture of beautiful scenery, exquisite architectural sites, religious sites, exciting monuments, festivals, conservation areas, breathtaking views, coastline, galleries, stunning arts and crafts, local culture, and tasty cuisine. All of these complements provide tourists with a truly memorable vacation experience. Gujarat is also home to the beautiful Asiatic lion, found nowhere else.

Gujarat's economy is heavily reliant on tourist activities, and the Indian Brand Equity Foundation claims that 44.8 million tourists visited Gujrat in 2017. Many hotels, including luxury resorts, budget hotels, and lodgings, can be found around the state, accommodating diverse visitors. This article will look at the places to stay here at Gujrat, but we will look at a few best and most viewed tourist destinations.

1. Dwarkadhish Temple

Dwarkadhish Jagat Temple in Dwarka, Gujarat, is a prominent holy place and is one of the top four holy destinations. Dwarka is made up of two words: "dwara," which means "road," and "ka," which means "enduring value." According to legend, Lord Krishna formed his empire at Dwarka and remained there for an extended period. This five-story temple is assisted by 72 pillars that, as per archeoastronomy, are immensely important. Dancers, musicians, elephants, and divine entities are displayed in frames on the temples' granite stone pillars and sidewalls. Sudama Setu, a bridge at the temple's base, connects the Gomti river, further connecting to the beach.

2. Gir National Park

This wildlife reserve, also called Sasan Gir, is Asiatic lions' only surviving territory. It's the only area where you might witness this animal, except in Africa. Gir National Park is decided to close from June to October each year, with April and May being the perfect periods for animal watching. Here you will discover fascinating geography composed of mountains, highlands, and portions of the region's seven major rivers, including the Hiran, Datardi, Rawal, and others. It features around 400 species and is one of Gujarat's top locations. The park is home to several rare wildlife (animal and bird) species, such as hyenas, Asiatic lions, nilgais, chinkaras, crocodiles, anacondas, birds, and brown eagles.

3. Somnath

Somnath is a spiritual place and is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas, which means "Living god of the Moonlight." It is a place that is encircled by a tremendous amount of tradition. Aside from temples, there are museums, beaches, and other tourist attractions in this area. Somnath is one of the top sites to visit in Gujarat; if you're traveling to Gujarat, you have to go to Somnath.

4. Laxmi Vilas Palace

The Laxmi Vilas Palace is among India's finest ancient buildings. Another of the most fantastic destinations to explore in Gujarat is this palace. Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad III's private house was Laxmi Vilas Palace. This palace, which covers approximately 700 acres, still belongs to the Gaekwads of Vadodara's royal family. The greenery gardens near the palace add to the attraction of this location. Tourists may occasionally observe monkeys and peacocks wandering the area. The palace was established in 1890 after a twelve-year construction period. At the time, the entire construction cost for this palace was roughly £180,000. If you wish to visit a beautiful destination in Gujarat, Laxmi Vilas Palace is an excellent choice.

5. Khijadiya Bird Sanctuary

One of the finest destinations to explore in Gujarat is the Khijadiya Bird Sanctuary, positioned 10 kilometers from Jamnagar. Let's all inform everyone that this reserve is home to over 300 various species of migrating birds. The Khijadiya Bird Sanctuary covers an area of 605 hectares, and the accessibility of freshwater resources and the ocean brings birds to this reserve. Freshwater lakes, saltwater beds, and wetlands are needed for Migrating birds, which are plentiful here. In 1982, the Khijadiya Bird Sanctuary was recognized as a secure place for all types of birds. This bird sanctuary is a must-see for any bird or wildlife lovers.

6. Porbandar Beach

Porbandar Beach, also famous as Chowpatty, is one of the Country's most popular beaches. It is based in Porbandar. You can go here with your friends and family and have a great time. A skater facility is a beautiful place for kids to have fun. The Huzur Palace, situated near the shore and a popular tourist destination, is a must-see for visitors. The Country's largest port is located here, and the coastline plays an essential role in the state's commercial growth. If you're seeking a relaxing area to visit in Gujarat, Porbandar Beach is the place to go.

7. Marine National Park

Gujarat's Marine National Park is one of the particular parks globally. This National Park in Gujarat is a great location to explore. The Marine National Park, which occupies 458 square kilometers, is a popular tourist destination in Gujarat. Visitors may see wild cats, jackals, royal eagles, green sea turtles, flamingos, and other animals in this national park. This park is a marine animals' heaven. There are around 30 different varieties of migrating birds that can be observed here. If you wish to visit a beautiful destination to get fresh air and relaxation sound in Gujarat, Marine National Park is an excellent place to explore.

8. Saputara Hill Station

Saputara is a delightful modest hill station in Gujarat's Dang district, located in the Western Ghats. With its beautiful natural rainforests, hills, and waterfall, it welcomes visitors from the whole Country. Saputara is the city's top tourist site. At an altitude of 875 meters above sea level, the location is a haven for environment lovers and animal enthusiasts. It is situated in the Dang region of Gujarat, 250 kilometers away from Mumbai. Let you explain that the dictionary definition of Saputara is "snake house." This region's native residents respect and worship the snakes. There are several walking trails and plenty of flora in this area. Visitors come here to relax and enjoy the peacefulness. If you are planning a trip to Gujarat, Saputara is a must-see.

9. Patan

Patan is a popular tourist attraction in Gujarat, and UNESCO World Heritage has added this tourist place to the UNESCO World Heritage Sites list. Patan is a popular tourism destination in India that draws visitors from all over the world. Patan served as the state capital for around 650 years during the medieval period. Patan and its people have a solid commitment to growth and learning. With its glory, this place captures everyone's heart. Patan has a rich history and has been the subject of archaeologists' investigations. There are several dargahs, temples, and Jain temples in Patan. Patan should be on your schedule if you wish to visit any holy sites in Gujarat.

10. Rajkot

Rajkot is a modest yet important Gujarati city. It is among the top destinations in Gujarat to visit because it is rich in cultural and architectural value. Aside from its monuments, the city is a vital hub for the vehicle, handcraft, and textile industries. Rajkot is the freshest city in India and the world's 22nd rapidly expanding metropolis. Rajkot is a must-see for everyone fascinated by architecture and history.

11. Champaner Pavagadh

This historical park is among Gujarat's most popular tourist destinations, recently acknowledged on the UNESCO World Heritage List. The Champaner-Pavagadh, Historical Park is traditionally and mythologically extremely important. This park is home to many stunning historical sites, and artwork in both Hindu and Islamic styles is shown. The most significant characteristic of this area is that the mountain of Pavagadh is said to be a portion of the Himalayas that Hanuman brought to Lanka in the epic Ramayana. This site is one of the top places to visit in Gujarat because of its fascinating history.

12. Gandhinagar

Gandhinagar, the state's capital of Gujarat, is a popular tourist attraction. Gandhinagar has a rich cultural heritage, gorgeous shrines, and a peaceful environment to offer visitors. The Akshardham Temple in Gandhinagar is one of the Country's most stunning temples. Brahmani Mandir and Hanuman Mandir are two other religious sites in Gandhinagar. Even though these temples are not well-known, they provide visitors with a memorable experience. Besides temples, there are several tourist attractions in Gandhinagar to see, ranging from stunning monuments to landscaped gardens surrounding itself the Sabarmati. You may visit the Sabarmati museum to learn about the life of Indian independence warriors. On the other side, you can enjoy wandering around and seeing several nature reserves and waterfalls. Other sights include the Family's Park and a specifically designed stepwell.

13. Girnar

Girnar is a lush greenery mountain in Gujarat, one of the state's most popular tourist sites. It is a religious destination and a hiker's heaven where hikers can enjoy stunning mountain views. Both Hindus and Muslims adore the Datar mountain. The Jain society's holy places, such as Meravasi Temple and Neminath Temple, are also located here. The Girnar Parikrama Event is yet another extremely prominent in this location. Every year during January and February, the Parikrama Festival takes place. This fair attracts a considerable number of Hindu and Jain worshippers. Mount Girnar, Gujarat's highest point, is 1,031 meters above sea level.

14. Ahmadabad

Ahmedabad, India's fifth-biggest metropolis, is Gujarat's trading area and one of the greatest destinations to visit in the state. The city is known for its rich culture, beautiful architecture, and dynamic folks. King Karnadev established Ahmedabad in the 11th century, and it was known as Karnawati until Sultan Ahmed Shah attacked the kingdom in 1411 and dubbed it after himself. Explore Ahmedabad during one of the city's big festivals for a unique experience. The most well-known events here seem to be Navratri and Makar Sakranti, commemorated with enthusiasm and intensity. The Kite Festival is held here with genuine excitement, and kite players worldwide come to show off their capabilities.

15. Rann of Kutch

The Rann of Kutch, situated between the vast Thar Desert and the Arabian Sea, is a stunning combination of salt and sand. This fully beautiful white piece reaches its peak upon the full moon night. Kutch is rendered bizarre because it is immersed in water during the monsoon. It's a massive expanse of white salt during the other eight months of the year than monsoon, giving it the illusion of a white desert. Cultural activities reflecting Gujarat's rich culture and customs are generally held at Dhordo on full moon evenings. One of the must-do activities in the Rann of Kutch is a night-time camel ride.

16. Sasan Gir National Park

In the Junagadh District, Sasan Gir National Park has been one of India's most recognized tourist attractions. It is the only area in Eurasia where you may see the jungle king wandering in the vast jungle. The Asiatic Lions are the major attraction to this protected region. Leopards, sambar, Hyenas, marsh crocodiles, antelopes, and a large variety of bird species can be found in this park, making it a perfect contender for one of India's best wildlife sanctuaries. Although many visitors see the lions, bird lovers travel to Gir for one of India's best bird viewing sites.

17. Lothal and Dholavira

You may travel back in time by seeing the remains of the historic Indus Civilization. Lothal was once a commerce center that was related to the Sindh area. Dholavira is an old city that has been explored and is expected to be around 100 hectares in size. It is the fifth biggest of India's eight Harappan period sites. It is one of India's most important archaeological finds. This site, located on the Khadir Bet island, is a sight to see and a must-see for all visitors.

18. Mandvi

This place, with its sandy beach and glittering water, the coastal town of Mandvi, has long been a popular vacation spot. It's a wonderful area to go swimming. It's a one-of-a-kind town that encapsulates the real colors of Gujarati culture?Kutchi culture. The Maharaja's Palace, which offers the greatest view in town, is well worth visiting.

19. Dwarka

Dwarka, one of India's historic towns, used to be the home of Lord Krishna. Dwarka is a Hindu pilgrimage location as a result of this event. The Dwarakadhish Temple is a famous pilgrimage destination, but don't forget to visit the Bet Dwarka Temple and Rukmini Devi Temple to understand the city fully. Near the Gomti ghat, there is also a lighthouse. It is a must-see since it is one of the oldest lighthouses in the world.

20. Statue of Unity

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, India's Iron Man, is remembered with the Statue of Unity. The statue was erected to convey Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel's dream of India and empower Indian citizens with his patriotic and freedom struggle. The bronze statue of India's founding father is 182 meters tall, attracting tourists worldwide.

Best Time To Visit

If you are organizing a trip to Gujarat, you must understand that the best time to visit is during the winter months, from October to February. Gujarat is a semi-arid area with high temperatures and humidity. Even during the monsoon, traveling here is a viable alternative. During the summer, the heat in this area climbs tremendously, making it impossible to visit tourist attractions.

Famous Food In Gujarat

During your stay in Gujarat, you may sample a wide range of cuisines. Gujarat's cuisine is as rich, unique, and colorful as its traditions. The cuisine is prepared unusually, and Gujarati food has a wide variety of tastes depending on where you go in the state. Kathiawar, Surat, Kutch, and North Gujarat are the most diverse. Here, you may eat mostly vegetarian meals, and you may get a sense of the flavor of several peppers here. The meal is served on metal plates, as is traditional.

Curries, dals, salads, chapatis, puris, pickles, and papads are certain unique dishes that are all part of Gujarati food culture. Dhokla, Debra, Fafda, Thepla, Kachori, Khandvi, Ganthia, Handvo, Aundhiya, and Surat Paun are just a few amazing Gujarati foods you should try. Shrikhand, Puran Poli, Ghevar, and Malpua are some of the classical desserts without which your Gujarat tour would be unsatisfactory.

How To Reach Gujarat

Gujarat is a prominent Indian state that draws visitors from all over the world and inside India due to its cultural vitality and religious splendor. Let us inform you that Gujarat is one of the busiest tourist destinations in the world nowadays. If visitors wish to go to Gujarat, they can do so via road, plane, rail, or sea.

  1. By Road: These are some of the best highways in western India to go to Gujarat by vehicle. Gujarat's highways, including state highways, national highways, and other types of roads, total expand in around 68,900 kilometers. There are many two- and four-lane highways in Gujarat. A lot of buses are operated by Gujarat's State Road Transport Corporation. Buses run across the state and can take you everywhere. During their visit to Gujarat, tourists might use the bus or a cab.
  2. By Rail: Gujarat is easily accessible by train. Gujarat is connected to the rest of the Country through rail. Gujarat is part of the Western Railway Division of Indian Railways. Rajkot, Surat, and Ahmedabad are only a few of Gujarat's major railway stations. Vadodara is the busiest station, and it can be reached by train from Mumbai and Delhi. Superfast trains frequently travel between these stations and other parts of the Country.
  3. By Air: Gujarat has great air availability. There are around 17 airports in Gujarat. The Gujarat Civil Aviation Board promotes aviation services across the state. Gujarat is connected to various parts of the world and several of the Country's major cities through aircraft. You must travel to Ahmedabad's Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport if you are visiting Gujarat from another country.
