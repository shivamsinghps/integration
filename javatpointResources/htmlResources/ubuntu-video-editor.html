Ubuntu Video Editor

A video editor is convoluted in post-production and video production of filmmaking. The responsibilities of a video editor affect decisions about the combining and selecting of shots into arrangements, the addition of attending the sound music and effects for ultimately creating a finished snipe, promo, commercial, television program, or movie.

Usually, video editors apply non-linear editing software for accomplishing the editing task. Technically, a video editor is an inclined individual that creates decisions of creative video editing.

Also, a video editor can refer to a system device controller that manages video machines for mechanically putting the film's pieces together with a 9-Pin Protocol. Also, it is referred to as linear or machine to machine.

In this article, we will discuss some best video editors in Ubuntu, their aspects, advantages, disadvantages, and how we can install them on our Linux distributions.

Kdenlive

It is an open-source and free video editing application from KDE that supports dual video monitors, basic transitions, basic effects, customizable layout support, clip list, and multi-track timeline.

It supports a huge range of file formats through a huge variety of cameras and camcorders, including DNxHD streams, DVCPR050 streams, DVCPRO, DVCAM, DVCAM (D10), IMX (D10) streams, XDCAM-HD streams, HDV, AVCHD, h.264, mpeg4, mpeg2, dv, avi, and raw.

Kdenlive will be our best bet if we are looking for an alternative to iMovie for Ubuntu.

Features of Kdenlive

  • Keyframeable effects
  • Huge hardware support
  • Automatic save
  • Proxy editing
  • Video and audio scopes ensure that the footage is balanced correctly
  • Plenty of transitions and effects
  • Easily make tiles using images or text
  • Configurable shortcuts and interface
  • A huge range of video audio formats
  • Multi-track video editing

Pros of Kdenlive

  • Not too difficult for those who are usual with video editing
  • Video editor (all-purpose)

Cons of Kdenlive

  • Applications of KDE are disgraceful for being bloated
  • Still, it might be complicated if we are looking for anything extremely simple.

Shotcut

It is a video editor for Ubuntu that could be put in a similar league OpenShot and Kdenlive. Shotcut is more advanced along with the 4K video support.

Supports a number of video and audio formats, effects, and transitions are a few of the numerous aspects of Shotcut. Also, an external monitor is supported here. It is available for macOS and Windows, so we can use what we learn on other OSes.

Features of Shotcut

  • Hardware support
  • External monitoring over an extra system monitor/display
  • Trimming on source timeline or clip player with many ripple options
  • Clip resizing, cutting, rotation, snapping, trimming, and scaling
  • Unlimited redo and undo for playlist editing such as a history view
  • Multi-track timeline with waveforms and thumbnails
  • Cross-platform and available on Windows, macOS, and Linux
  • Video filters and transitions
  • Audio filters, effects, and mixing
  • Mix and match frame rates and resolutions in a project
  • Native timeline editing
  • Supports a huge range of image, audio, and video formats

Pros of Shotcut

  • Available on macOS and Windows with Linux
  • Supports 4k videos
  • Video editor (all-purpose) for basic needs of video editing

Cons of Shotcut

  • Too many aspects diminish the simplicity of the software

How to install Shotcut?

It is present in Snap format. We can find it within the Ubuntu Software Center. We can also get the exe files through its download page for other distros.

OpenShot

OpenShot is a video editor (multi-purpose) for Ubuntu. It helps us to create several videos with effects and transitions and adjust the levels of audio. It supports most codecs and formats.

Also, we can burn a film to DVD, export it in several basic video formats, and upload it to Vimeo or YouTube. OpenShot is a bit simpler as compared to Kdenlive. So, if we need any video editor along with basic UI, then OpenShot could be a great choice.

Features of OpenShot

  • Digital video effects including chroma key, grayscale, hue, gamma, brightness, etc.
  • Audio editing and mixing
  • Speed changes and time mapping on clips
  • Fame accuracy
  • Scrolling the credits of motion picture
  • SVG friendly to create and include vector credits and titles
  • 3D animated effects and titles
  • Supports 2D animation by image sequences
  • Title templates, sub-titles, and title creation
  • Compositing, watermarks, and image overlays
  • Video transitions using real-time previews
  • Clip resizing, cutting, rotation, snapping, trimming, and scaling
  • Unlimited layers or tracks
  • Desktop development with the support of drag and drop
  • Powerful keyframe animations (curve-based)
  • Supports a huge variety of image, audio, and video format
  • Cross-platform and available on Windows, macOS, and Linux

Pros of OpenShot

  • Available on macOS and Windows with Linux
  • Video editor (all-purpose) for average needs of video editing

Cons of OpenShot

  • Still, we might not search the mark of a movie making professional-grade editing application
  • It might be normal but if we are extremely new to the process of video editing. There is a learning curve associated here.

Lightworks

If we are looking for software for video editing that contains more aspects, then it is the answer. This tool is a professional and cross-platform video editor, available for Windows, macOS, and Linux

It is a professional and award-winning non-linear video editing application that provides resolutions up to 4K and video in HD and SD formats as well.

Lightworks is present for Ubuntu, although it's not open-source software.

It has two different versions which are mentioned below:

  • Lightworks Pro
  • Lightworks Free

The Pro version contains more aspects like the support of Blue Ray and 4K, higher resolution, etc. Extensive documentation is present on its website.

Features of Lightworks

  • Wide range of video and audio filters and effects
  • Supports drag and drop feature
  • Export videos for Vimeo/YouTube, HD/SD, up to 4K
  • Proxy workflows (Lo-Res) for 4K
  • Access excellent royalty-free video and audio content
  • Ready to use and real-time video and audio FX
  • Easy timeline trimming and editing
  • Intuitive and simple user interface
  • Cross-platform

Pros of Lightworks

  • Feature-rich and professional video editor

Cons of Lightworks

  • Restricted free version

How to install Lightworks?

This tool offers DEB packages for Ubuntu and Debian-based distributions of Linux and RPM packages for Fedora-based distributions of Linux. We can search many Packages on the download page of Lightworks.

Blender

Blender is a cross-platform, open-source industry-grade, and professional video editor. It is famous for 3D works. It has been used in many Hollywood movies like the series of Spider-Man.

Also, it can be used for many video editing purposes and has many input capabilities along with a range of formats, however originally developed for 3D modeling.

Features of Blender

  • Speed control, filters, keyframes, transitions, adjustment layers, and more
  • Up to 32 slots to add effects, masks, scenes, audio, images, and video
  • Audio mixing, waveform visualization, scrubbing, and syncing
  • Live preview, histogram displays, chroma vectorscope, and luma waveform

Pros of Blender

  • Professional grade adding
  • Cross-platform

Cons of Blender

  • Primarily for 3D animation, not emphasized on general video editing
  • Complicated

How to install Blender?

The latest Blender version can be installed and downloaded from its download page.

DaVinci Resolve

If we wish Hollywood-like video editing, we can use the DaVinci Resolve tool. DaVinci Resolve through Blackmagic is what several professionals are applying for editing tv shows and movies.

This tool is not our normal video editor. It is a full-fledged tool for editing purposes that gives color correction, editing, and professional post-production of audio in one application.

It is an open-source tool. It offers a non-paid version for Ubuntu like Lightworks. Its Pro version is for 300 dollars.

Features of DaVinci Resolve

  • Non-linear VFX editing
  • Timeline curve editor
  • Speed effects
  • Filter and transition effects
  • Multi-cam editing permits footage editing from more than one camera
  • Audio Overlays
  • Advanced trimming
  • All types of editing such as append at the end, fit to fill, replace, ripple overwrite, insert, overwrite, etc.
  • High-quality playback engine

Pros of DaVinci Resolve

  • Professional video editor
  • Cross-platform

Cons of DaVinci Resolve

  • A few features are not present in the non-paid version
  • Not open-source
  • Not convenient for average editing

How to install DaVinci Resolve?

We can download this tool for Ubuntu from its official website. We will need to register ourselves, even for the non-paid version.

Cinelerra

Since 1998, Cinelerra has been present and it has been installed over more than 5 million times. Back in 2003, this tool was the initial video editor for providing non-linear editing over 64-bit systems. It was a very handy video editor for the users of Ubuntu at the time, but it lost its gloss after a few developers discarded this project.

The nice thing is that it is back on track and it is being actively integrated again.

Features of Cinelerra

  • Split pane editing
  • Unlimited layers
  • Several video effects
  • Frame rendered (built-in)
  • Supports HD videos
  • Non-linear editing

Pros of Cinelerra

  • Video editor (all-purpose)

Cons of Cinelerra

  • No packages are available
  • Not convenient for beginners

How to install Cinelerra?

We can download its source code through SourceForge. More details are available on its download page.

VidCutter

VidCutter is a simple video editing tool, unlike another video editor explained here. It does not do much except merge and split videos. Although, sometimes that is what we require and VidCutter provides us just that.

It is known as a robust video trimmer present in macOS, Linux, and Windows. It is effortless for cutting or joining a video in Ubuntu.

Features of VidCutter

  • Merges and trims the videos, nothing other than that
  • Simple interface
  • Supports almost every basic format of video such as FLV, 3GP, MOV, MP3, WMV, MPEG 1/2, MP4, AVI, etc.
  • Cross-platform application available for macOS, Windows, and Linux
  • Doesn't take too much time for cutting or joining video
  • Offers us to cut more than one clip from a similar video
  • Provides us to trim or cut larger video files into shorter ones
  • Built with the help of the Qt5 and Python script

Pros of VidCutter

  • Good for general merge and split
  • Cross-platform

Cons of VidCutter

  • Often crashes
  • Not convenient for normal video editing
