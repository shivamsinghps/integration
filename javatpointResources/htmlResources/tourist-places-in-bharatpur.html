14 Best Tourist Places in Bharatpur

Positioned in the Brij area of the desert state of Rajasthan, Bharatpur is one of the most popular districts of the state, which is famous for its tourist spots and other destinations where every year, visitors come from all over India. Bharatpur is famous for its natural beauty, religious spots, and calm environments, and tourists from all over India come every year to enjoy their vacation with family or friends. Even though one can also plan a solo trip to this city, Bharatpur offers a wide range of places, spots, and destinations that one would love to enjoy while having a companion with them. The city houses the famous Keoladeo Ghana National Park, which is counted among one of the most popular tourist destinations in Bharatpur, is naturally rich, and it is also a paradise for wildlife photographers because it offers a diverse range of fauna and flora along with many beautiful and colourful migratory birds in the winter. Not only has a paradise for wildlife photographers and nature lovers or enthusiasts, but this national park has also become a pride symbol of India since it has recently been included in UNESCO's list of World Heritage Sites.

Bharatpur tourist spots are not restricted to only forests and wildlife sanctuaries like Keoladeo Ghana National Park, but it also has many other historical, spiritual, and different types of tourist destinations where tourists can be seen in numbers at any time of the year. The list of most popular tourist destinations of Bharatpur includes Band Baretha, Dholpur Palace, Deeg, Ganga Mandir, Seetharam Temple, Laxman Temple, Chawad Devi Temple, Lohagarh Fort, Bankey Bihari Temple, Bharatpur Palace and Museum, Government Museum Bharatpur, and many others. The popular fort of Bharatpur, Lohagarh Fort, was built in the 18th century with Fateh Burj and Jawahar Burj, and this is clear evidence of the popularity of Bharatpur for its historical remains from past centuries. The Fateh Burj is also counted as one of the strongest structures of Rajasthan that stands in its full glory. Apart from this, there are many other tourist destinations in Bharatpur that one cannot afford to miss during their trip to the city, and this article describes all such tourist attractions of Bharatpur.

(1) Kaman

Popularly known for being a part of Brij Bhoomi, Kaman is an old town in Bharatpur, and it is also called Kamaban. Brij Bhoomi is the place where Lord Krishna spent most of his childhood days, and it is the most worshipped place by Lord Krishna devotees. Including the Chakras Khamba Temple, one of the most popular temples all over India, Kaman has a total of 84 temples which makes it more famous among visitors. This temple has 84 intricate carved pillars on its walls, and one of the unique things about this temple is that it has no idol for worship but is still known as a temple. The beautiful carving and architectural style of the temple attract tourists to visit Bharatpur from all corners of India.

There is one interesting fact about this temple which creates a mystery about this temple, and this mystery attracts more and more tourists from all over the world. According to this belief, if any visitor attempts to count all the 84 pillars of the temple, they never reach the number 84 because every time, the count exceeds or recedes the number 84.

(2) Jawahar Burj

Jawahar Burj, which is positioned at the Lohagarh Fort, Gopalgarh, Bharatpur, was constructed as a victory tower and a coronation site for the Jat rulers, and there are three mandapas in this place. Out of these three mandapas, there is one which has paintings of epic scenes, and there is also a hammam or royal bathhouse with frescoes and intricate carvings. A part of this place has full portraits of Maharajas who are posing with their animal trophies, and this place is beautifully decorated with frescoes. These frescoes are currently in the state of extinction, and in this place, there is an inscribed iron pillar having genealogical carvings of the rulers. This place tells the stories of incredible battles fought between great kings, surviving, it's winning, and there is also an Ashtadhatu Gate in this place which also allures visitors from all parts of India. All these interesting things and facts about this place make it a must-visit one for the tourists while having a trip to Bharatpur, and the timings of this place are from 10 in the morning to 8 in the evening.

(3) Chawad Devi Temple

Chawad Devi Temple, which stands as a token of the tradition, culture, and history of the region, is a lovely tourist attraction in Bharatpur, and this temple is seldom populated. This means that this temple allows tourists to be immense themselves in solitude so that they can use this temple to revive their drained energy, and a visit to this peaceful temple allows the visitors to rejuvenate and relax in the serenity and peace that pervades the air of this temple. To experience a magical moment in this temple, tourists should visit the temple in the early morning time. This temple is open for 24 hours, and there is no entry fee charged to visitors.

(4) Seetharam Temple

Seetharam Temple, which is positioned inside the Keoladeo Ghana National Park, Bharatpur, is one of the serene places to visit in the district and a beautiful place to catch up on some serenity and healing solitude. Seetharam Temple is a small but scenic place which is located deep inside the national temple, and it allures tourists to come and enjoy the serenity of this nature. This temple is a lovely place close to the natural bounty of nature, and the only thing which will disturb the meditations of the visitors is the play of monkeys and the call of birds. There is one more temple positioned close to this temple, and that temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. There is a popular legend about this temple which makes this temple a truly holy spot, and according to this legend, the lingum present in the temple originated from the earth around 300 years ago. There is no entry fee charged for the visitors to enter the temple, and its timings are from 8 in the morning to 5 in the evening.

(5) Band Baretha

Positioned approximately 45 KMs from the main city of Bharatpur, Band Baretha is the large town of the district and one of the major tourist attractions of the city. It is also one of the most popular tourist destinations of the city because one can enjoy various attractions of the town in its environs. The heritage and influence on the architecture of the town by the Mughals clearly depicts how long the history of this town had been associated with the Mughal's rule, and this can also be seen in its manifold monuments. Some of the most famous historical attractions in Band Baretha include the BarahKhambonki Chhatri and the old fort. Apart from these attractions of the town, the dam located on the Kakund River is also a great spot for the visitors where they can visit for some frolicking and picnicking.

(6) Dholpur Palace

The glamorous Dholpur Palace, which is positioned at the Nihal Ganj, Dholpur, Bharatpur, is one of the best palaces to visit in the whole of Rajasthan, and it is beautiful tourist sight to behold. Popularly known as Raj Niwas Palace, Dholpur Palace is very attractive in that it catches the eyes of everyone from afar, and it was built in the early 19th century. The intricate architecture of the temple makes it interesting to gaze upon and adds meaning and depth to the structure. Tourists can spend some hours here enjoying their time in Bharatpur, and walking through the red sandstone halls of this palace is a unique experience in itself. The mellowing sun adds a magical quality to this palace, and therefore, evening, in particular, is a fantastic time to spend in the Dholpur Palace. There is no entry fee charged from the visitors to enter the palace, and its timings are from 6 in the morning to 7 in the evening.

(7) Deeg

Deeg, which is positioned approximately 32 KMs to the north of the main city of Bharatpur, is a small but fascinating town in the district, and any visitor, who wants to explore the city around Bharatpur, cannot afford to miss visiting this town during their trip. The two most popular attractions of this town are the three-day Diwali festival, which is usually celebrated in the month of September here, and the Deeg Palace complex. The structure of Deeg Palace is wrought in the traditional Rajasthani style, and it is an imposing structure which allures many tourists to this town. This Deeg Palace is constructed in a unique manner in that countless palaces, and water gardens are interwoven with a series of Charbaghs. A magnificent way to spend some hours in the Deeg is roaming the grounds of this palace with friends or special ones. One more site that adds character to the region is the Deeg Fort, which attracts visitors with its pleasant and intricate architecture, and it is also known as Jal Mahal popularly.

(8) Government Museum Bharatpur

The government museum of Bharatpur, which is located inside the Lohagarh Fort, contains a lot of interesting artefacts, which are related to the tradition, history, and art of the city, and that's how this museum is a strong reflection of the culture of this area. The main attraction of this Government Museum consists of the local artthat, miniature paintings, weapons, coins, metallic objects, terracotta items, inscriptions, stone sculptures, and many more, and all of them have been housed in this museum for years. Inside the museum, the art gallery, which is also open for public viewing, contains miniature paintings on leaves of old litho papers, mica, and Peepal tree. For tourists, a good way to spend some time in the city is by admiring the grand views and the artefacts and spending some couple of hours inside the museum with family or friends. INR 20 as an entry fee is charged from the visitors to enter the museum, and its timings are from 9 in the morning to 5 in the evening.

(9) Laxman Mandir

Laxman Mandir, which is positioned at the Tara Mahendra Colony, Bharatpur, have imposing idols, grandiose arches, embellished walls, and Intricately carved doors, and all of these characteristics of the temple make it one of the best tourist destinations in Bharatpur. Inside the halls of this temple, a wealth of culture and history is inscribed, which makes it one of the most popular temples in whole Rajasthan, and many visitors come to Bharatpur from all over India just to visit this temple. The Laxman Mandir contains statues of Lord Laxman and his wife Urmila, and it was built in order to pay tributes to the brotherly virtues of Lord Laxman, brother of Lord Rama. Apart from the statues of Lord Rama and his wife Urmila, statues of Lord Ram, Bharat, Shatrughan, and Lord Hanuman are also present in this temple. The statues are made up of a unique combination of 8 metals, popularly known as "Ashtdhaatu", and these statues are particularly renowned for their uniqueness. To experience the beauty of Rajasthani culture in a different way, tourists should visit this beautiful temple at least once in their life. There is no entry fee charged from the visitors to enter the temple, and its timings are from 7 in the morning to 7 in the evening.

(10) Ganga Mandir

Ganga Mandir, which is positioned at the Pai Bagh, Bharatpur, is one of the most famous tourist destinations of the district because of its unique history; this temple was constructed in 1845. It took around 90 years to complete the construction of this temple, and the result of this long construction is an immaculate and beautiful structure present in the heart of the busy city. The hall of this temple is adorned by many striking idols of popular kings in the history of Bharatpur. Among these striking idols, the statue of Ganga Maharaj is the most awe-inspiring one, and other than this, the statue of King Bhagiratha, which is 4 feet tall, is another marvellous sight that the temple boast of. The statue of Ganga Maharaj stands imposingly in the temple, and it is entirely made up of Marble. Tourists, who are visiting Bharatpur city, should include this temple, which offers a deep combination of divinity and purity, in their list. There is no entry fee charged from the visitors to enter the temple, and its timings are from 5 in the morning to 9 in the evening.

(11) Bharatpur Palace And Museum

Positioned in the main city of Bharatpur, Bharatpur Palace And Museum is one of the best tourist attractions to visit in Bharatpur, and it is an imposing and elegant structure. This palace cum museum offers an interesting insight into Bharatpur's lifestyle, and it is a reflection of the heritage and culture of this old Rajasthan city. The style of this palace makes it a unique style of the monument because the structure of this palace fuses the Rajput and Mughal style structure, and the decorated walls and richly patterned flooring add glamour to the palatial apartments. Kamra Khas, which is a museum, is also present inside this palace, and this museum contains ancient scriptures & craft wares that depict the typical culture and art of Bharatpur, 861 local art, over 581 stone sculptures, and a vast number of antiques. The timings of Bharatpur Palace And Museum are from 9 in the morning to 5:30 in the evening.

(12) Bankey Bihari Temple

Bankey Bihari Temple, which is positioned inside the Lohagarh Fort, Gopalgarh, Bharatpur, is a world-famous and delightful temple in the city, and it was built during the Mughal rule on the town. This temple is a perfect spot to revive oneself and pleasant to look upon, and it is also one of the most popular temples in Rajasthan. This temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna, and tourists, especially the Lord Krishna devotees, come from all over India to visit this temple. The soothing gongs that reverberate the still air inside the temple, as well as the peace and silence that hang around the temple, are perfect for building a serene and auspicious atmosphere inside the temple. There are houses inside the temple, and these houses have several idols, which can be seen celebrating the power and virtue of Lord Krishna. INR 20 as an entry fee is charged from the visitors to enter the temple, and its timings are from 9 in the morning to 5 in the evening.

(13) Lohagarh Fort

Lohagarh Fort, which is positioned at the Gopalgarh, Bharatpur, is one of the strongest structures in the whole state, and it is also known as Iron Fort because of this. This fort was built in the early 18th century, and because of its unassailable defence quality, Jat ruler Maharaja Suraj Mal gave this fort its powerful name at that time. Because of its strength, and historical significance, this fort is among the best tourist attractions for visitors to visit during their trip to Bharatpur. The calm, strength air of the Lohagarh Fort and its austere and imposing qualities allure tourists from all over India to visit this fort at least once in their life. The government museum is also housed inside the fort, and the museum in itself is another tourist attraction where visitors can learn about the art & culture of the city. Another reason visiting this fort is known to be a visual treat is that the fort also commands grand views of the region. INR 20 as an entry fee is charged from the visitors to enter the temple, and its timings are from 10 in the morning to 6 in the evening.

(14) Keoladeo Ghana National Park

Positioned at the Agra-Jaipur Highway, Bharatpur, Keoladeo Ghana National Park is the main reason behind the popularity of Bharatpur all over India, and this national park is popularly known as Bharatpur Bird sanctuary. The intensity of the wildlife tourists can experience in this national park is startling, and this national park is one of the must-visit tourist destinations in the city. This sanctuary or national park has a wide variety of flora and fauna, and it is well-known for the hordes of birds that its hosts year-round; all of these qualities of this national park make this tourist destination a widely active place all year. Most of the migratory birds arrive in the sanctuary by the time of October-November, and that's why this time is considered the best time to visit this sanctuary. During this time, one can witness lots of rush in Keoladeo Ghana National Park because tourists from all over India come to witness the place's chirp and bustle, making it more magnificent. If one wants to explore inside the national park for multiple hours, then one can also choose to stay at the accommodations which are also available inside the sanctuary. The timings of this national park are from 8 in the morning to 5 in the evening, and INR 50 for Indians & INR 400 for foreigners are charged as the entry fee for this national park, and INR 100 from all those visitors who want to opt for a rickshaw ride.
