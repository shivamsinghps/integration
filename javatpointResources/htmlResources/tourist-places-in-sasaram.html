12 Best Tourist places in sasaram

Bihar's Sasaram is a stunning city with a rich cultural and historical legacy. Do not skip Sasaram if you are planning a trip to Bihar. It is a lovely city. The town is situated in the state's northwestern region. Sasaram is renowned for making stone chips and for the quarrying sector, in addition to being one of the most significant cities in Bihar. People from many languages, cultures, and ethnicities coexist peacefully and harmoniously in the metropolis. The region's primary languages are Hindi, Urdu, and Bhojpuri. The city has been a popular tourist destination in Bihar for many years. The town historically enjoyed a solid reputation as the state's economic hub during the British era.

History of Sasharam

Sasaram holds an important place in Indian history. The city is the birthplace of the renowned Sur dynasty, whose leader Sher Shah Suri conquered the Mughal Emperor Humayun in the Battle of Chausa (1539) and the Battle of Kanauj or Billgram to gain control over Delhi and the surrounding northern area of the country (1540). King Suri oversaw the city for five years after defeating Humayun, the Mughal emperor. In the centre of an artificial lake in Sasaram, the ancient red sandstone tomb of the Afghan king Sher Shah Suri is 122 feet (37 metres) tall. An Iranian influence may be seen in the blue and yellow glazed tiles used in the Indo-Afghan style construction of the tomb.Sasaram is also home to the mausoleum of Sher Shah Suri's father, Hasan Khan Suri. Both graves bear striking similarities to Buddhist stupas from the Mauryan era. The city also features a historic swimming hole known as the baulia that the monarch frequented for bathing. The town of Sasaram is also home to the well-known Fort at Rohtasgarh. In the seventh century AD, the Fort was constructed. Raja Harishchandra, a Hindu king, built the Fort as a tribute to his son, prince Rohitashwa. During the time of Mughal emperor Akbar, who appointed Raja Man Singh governor of Bengal and Bihar, Man Singh seized the Fort and converted it into an administrative centre.

It is a stunning example of Indo-Islamic architecture, constructed of red stone and has elaborate carvings on its façade.
