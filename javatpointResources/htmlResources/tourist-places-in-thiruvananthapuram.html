Tourist Places in TVM

To get the most out of your trip to Thiruvananthapuram, India, we've compiled a list of some of the best places to visit while you are there. From ancient temples and markets to beautiful nature reserves and bustling towns, there's plenty to see and do in this fascinating city. Some best places to visit in TVM are:-

1. Veli Tourist Village

The Veli Tourist Village is an amazing picnic spot close to the lake in ethereal surroundings, making it one of the best places to visit in Trivandrum. A stunning garden brimming with vibrant flowers and magnificently kept is one of the primary features of this location. A small number of playthings and an area designated specifically for children are provided, and there is also the opportunity to go for a boat ride on the lake.

In addition, visitors can enjoy the village by participating in various fun activities, such as horseback riding or playing games. The Veli Tourist Village is one of the most tranquil and peaceful places in which one can go to spend some time relaxing amidst the natural green and beautiful surroundings.

2. Karikkakom Devi Temple

It's hard to believe this old temple, which dates back more than 600 years, is still standing. The landscape that surrounds this temple is peaceful, quiet, and meditative. The temple is exceptionally tidy, and its maintenance is of the highest possible standard. It is believed that the goddess Karikkakom is a reincarnation of the goddess Chamunda Devi, so the temple is dedicated to her. The entire temple and its surroundings are quite enchanting, and it is located in a very convenient location close to a bypass for a national highway. Within the temple grounds is a sizable area designated for parking vehicles.

3. Vellayani Lake

The Vellayani lake is the largest in the city of Trivandrum and is widely considered one of the city's top tourist destinations. The lake provides some enticing perspectives of the village and the backwaters, making it an excellent location for socializing. Enthusiasts of fishing will be pleased to learn that this lake provides excellent fishing opportunities.

This beautiful Lake is ideal for a picnic with the whole family. The lake, which can be found in the more rural part of the city, is home to a variety of waterbirds, including some white ducks and lotus flowers. You will be amazed and inspired by the wonderful atmosphere surrounding the lake, and it will compel you to spend a significant amount of time here in this enchanting area. One of the many activities that can be enjoyed in Trivandram is boating on this lake. There are also a lot of other things to do in this city.

4. Kanakakunnu Palace

One of the most amazing and beautiful places in Trivandrum, this destination is renowned for its traditional Kerala architecture. It serves as one of the city's most popular tourist spots. This stunning palace is exceptionally well-kept, spotless, and close to the Napier Museum and zoo. The nature around the palace is very amazing, with lots of lush vegetation all around.

The location exudes a sense of peace and tranquillity throughout. Just wandering around is a great way to spend time here. You can get an excellent cup of tea or coffee at the eatery that is located within the palace complex, and there is also a place to purchase food there. During the weekdays, individuals bring their musical instruments to this location so they can play here. You can pick up some souvenirs of your trip or a unique piece of handiwork from one of the many stalls set up as shops inside the palace.

5. Attukal Temple

This mesmerizing temple is devoted to the goddess Bhadrakali, one of the many forms of the goddess Mahakali. It is considered one of the most popular tourist destinations in the Trivandrum area. The temple's architectural style is awe-inspiring, and the tranquil atmosphere makes it one of Trivandrum's most popular tourist attractions. The Attukal temple holds several Guinness World Records, one of which is the record for the most people assembled for any festival. The Pongal festival is celebrated on the temple grounds with energy and enthusiasm.

6. Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

This wonderful temple has a dazzling architectural design and an atmosphere that amazes you. It is also one of the top sites to visit in Trivandrum. This magnificent temple also has the distinction of being the wealthiest temple in the whole world. Shri Padmanabhswamy, the lord of Trivandrum, is the deity of this temple.

This is also a holy location that receives the most visitors from India and outside the globe. The massive temple is a work of beauty in and of itself, and each component has been meticulously created. This temple is home to an immensely extensive and illustrious history and legacy. There is a stringent dress code; the only kind of gowns permitted are those from the local culture.

7. Napier Museum

It is a magnificent museum constructed in the middle of the 19th century by a British commander called Napier. It is considered to be one of the most popular hangout spots in the city of Trivandrum. A significant hub for the city's artistic and cultural life may be found at the museum. It is the greatest site to learn about the immensely rich cultural and historical history of the state of Kerala, and this fascinating museum is the perfect venue to visit.

The museum is home to some beautiful sculptures as well as coins from a variety of various periods in Kerala's history. The enticing musical instruments from various periods and locations around Kerala are one of the fascinating aspects of the museum. The museum may be found inside the gates of the municipal zoo. The zoo's landscape is quite beautiful and has a lot of lush vegetation. A one-time charge of 20 Indian Rupees (INR) is required of each individual upon entry.

8. Sagarika Marine Research Aquarium

This Aquarium can be found near the seaside town of Kovalam in the Indian state of Kerala and has become a popular tourist destination. The Aquarium, which covers an area of two acres, is home to a wide diversity of marine creatures that may often be difficult or impossible to find in other parts of India. The Ministry of the Environment and Forests has designated it as one of seven national aquaria in the country.

Sagarika Marine Research Aquarium has been a valuable resource for academics and specialists for over four decades, thanks to its great biodiversity and unmatched competence in marine biology. This Aquarium is run by the state's College of Fisheries and may be visited by the public.

9. Shri Chitra Art Gallery

Over three decades, the Shri Chitra Art Gallery has promoted art appreciation and growth in India. It does this by presenting the work of prominent Indian artists and by offering facilities that are on par with the most cutting-edge technology. Every traveller who comes to Trivandrum needs to make time to check out this attraction.

In addition, the gallery showcases the work of various internationally renowned painters. In addition to hosting exhibits, the gallery also hosts workshops on topics like pottery making, painting, sketching, stone carving, miniature painting,and the use of fabrics from various parts of India. Film screenings that highlight regional cinema from all across India are also held here, along with theatrical shows that display a variety of traditional dance techniques from various parts of the nation.

10. Shangumugham Beach

An amazing beach called Shangumugham Beach may be found just in the neighbourhood of the city airport. This beach, which has crystal-clear seas and snow-white sand, is a stunning location to unwind and enjoy the ethereal atmosphere of its surroundings. As a result of the beach's impeccable cleanliness and the fact that it has not been altered in any way, it is widely considered to be one of the city's most picturesque locations. In addition, there is a little shrine dedicated to Devi located on the shore.

The beach provides amazing sunset views. There is a lengthy stretch of beach that is both peaceful and beautiful. The fact that this beach isn't quite as busy as some of the other prominent beaches in the city is one of its finest features, with the possible exception of the period around sunset when quite a few people come to take in the breathtaking views of the setting sun.

11. Agasthyakoodam

At an elevation of 1890 meters above mean sea level, Agasthyakoodam is the second highest peak in the Indian state of Kerala. It is also a part of the Sahyadri range of mountains. The woods of Agasthyakoodam are home to an abundance of exotic animals and a wide variety of medicinal herbs, plants, and brightly coloured orchids.

This renowned mountain, a haven for birdwatchers, may be reached on foot from either Kotor, near Neyyar Dam, or Bonacaud. The months of December through April are considered to be the best seasons for going on hikes. This mountain is located in the Thiruvananthapuram district of Kerala, India. It is also a part of the Agasthyamala Biosphere Reserve, located on the boundary between the states of Kerala and Tamil Nadu. In March of 2016, UNESCO decided to include the Agasthyamala Biosphere Reserve as a part of the World Network of Biosphere Reserves.

12. Padmanabhapuram Palace

The magnificent palace can be seen at Thuckalay, which can be found on the road to Kanyakumari, which was once the seat of the former kings of Travancore. This magnificent example of the traditional building of the Kerala School of Architecture is a timber palace that has been carefully maintained. The magnificent paintings, delicate flower carvings, and the black glossy granite floor have all been able to withstand time.

This palace is one of India's biggest wooden palaces, and most of its construction was completed using rosewood and teakwood.

This palace is located 20 kilometres from Nagercoil and 50 kilometres from Thiruvananthapuram. It is owned by the State Government of Kerala and is kept in good condition by that government. The Western Ghats' Veli Hills provide a panoramic view of the palace on the slopes of those hills.

13. Akkulam Lake

The peaceful Akkulam Lake can be found in the Aakkulam region of Trivandrum, and it is the ideal destination for anybody who enjoys spending time outdoors. This lake, encircled by lush vegetation, is perfect for anyone looking for a peaceful spot to visit where they can unwind among the splendour of nature.

Akkulam Lake's most memorable feature is its boat trip, which you may do with a loved one or on your own. Take a boat trip and take in the stunning sunset scenery. If you are searching for a place to get some much-needed relaxation after a long and taxing day, go no further than Akkulam Lake; it has everything you want.

14. Neyyar Dam

This dam is an abode of paradise in Kerala. It is an amazing tourist destination to spend a quiet day in the sun surrounded by nature's finest delights. It is also one of the favourite tourist attractions in Trivandrum. Take in positive energy and allow it to revitalize all of your senses as you are surrounded by stunning natural scenery.

The dam is home to several rare species of both flora and fauna. You may observe wild animals and medicinal plants at the dam, which generate a feeling of well-being in those who visit. Stop by this dam if you're looking for a relaxing day trip; you won't regret it.

15. Aazhimala Siva Temple

This temple was built in honour of Lord Shiva and had a vibrant range of architectural styles. The temple is well-known for having an architectural design that is both complex and breathtaking. The areas just next to the temple are quite scenic. In direct proximity to the temple is a stretch of coastline dominated by several rocky cliffs. The temple is home to a statue of Lord Shiva that is both stunning and impressive in its size.

This is undoubtedly one of the most renowned temples dedicated to Shiva in Trivandrum. The view of both the sunrise and the sunset that can be had from this vantage point is quite breathtaking. This location is one of the top attractions of all of Thiruvananthapuram.

16. Varkala

In the district of Thiruvananthapuram, one may find a wide variety of beach resorts and spas. In addition, it is a significant location for religious tourism among Hindus. The famous social reformer Sree Narayana Guru is said to have been laid to rest on a hill known as Sivagiri, located close to the city of Varkala. According to one version of mythology, the wise man Narada was once confronted by a band of wandering mendicants who all admitted to having sinned.

It is said that Narada tossed his valkkalam, a fabric made from the bark of a tree, into the air, and the location where it fell is now known as Varkala. Narada instructed the wandering monks to perform their religious rites in the new location established on the shoreline. The spot on the beach where they knelt to pray for deliverance was eventually given the name Papanasham Beach ( Papanasham means redemption from sins). The Sree Janardhana Swamy Temple, which dates back some 2,000 years, and the Nature Care Centre are the two primary places of interest in this area.

17. Priyadarshini Space Planetarium

The Priyadarshini Space Planetarium, which opened its doors to the public in 1994, is now considered one of India's most accessible and multipurpose planetariums. It is outfitted with cutting-edge technology that makes it possible to see all the components that make up the universe.

This planetarium educates visitors on various interesting topics, including the vastness of the universe, our place within it, and the world around us.

On the campus of Kerala State, Science and Technology Museum is where you will find the museum. Ocular apparatuses are used to make projections of astronomical occurrences. The evolution of astronomy, the make-up of planetary systems, and, in the end, the beginning of the universe are some of the topics covered in 'The Man and the Universe,' which is one of the most important shows taking place here.

18. Vizhinjam RockCut Cave

The Vinadhara Dakshinamurti god resides inside the Vizhinjam Rock Cut Cave, which has a rock-cut sculpture dating back to the 8th century and has a single chamber.

The exterior wall of the temple is covered with many depictions of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. In addition, a sculpture that hasn't been completed yet depicts Lord Shiva. Inside it, he is seen dancing while carrying a bow. The Bhagavathy Temple is yet another important temple that can be found close to the Vizhinjam Rock Cut Cave Temple.

19. Vizhinjam Lighthouse

The Vizhinjam LightHouse can be found on the peak of Kurumsal Hill, which is just ten kilometres distant from the city of Thiruvananthapuram.

In 1925, construction was started on the lighthouse, which has a masonry tower that stands 57 meters above mean sea level and has a height of 36 meters. One may take in the breathtaking panoramas of the Arabian Sea from the top.

A breathtaking scene is a sun setting over the city, with the lighthouse as background. The light turns on at 6:00 in the evening and stays on till 5:30 in the morning. In addition, there are a lot of small restaurants in the area that is near the shore.
