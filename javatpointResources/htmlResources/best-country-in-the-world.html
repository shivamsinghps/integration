Best Country in the World

Every country is beautiful and charming in the world. They are the best in their field. Some countries are best in Cleanliness; some are in Gross Domestic Ratio, Environment, Unemployment, Happiness, Sex Ratio, and Human Development Index, but we will find out that country stands in front of these indices:

1. Norway

Norway is a Nordic country in Northern Europe; the main territory comprises the northernmost and western portion of the Scandinavian Peninsula. Norwegian, Sami is the official language of the country. The ethnic composition of Norway is 81.5% (Norwegian) and Non-Norwegian Comprises (18.5%). The major religion is Christianity. The country has Unitary Parliamentary Constitutional Monarchy. Their total GDP is $350 billion, and their per capita income is $64,987. They also have a high development index and a high GDP ratio.

2. Australia

Australia is officially called the Commonwealth of Australia and is a sovereign nation consisting of Tasmania Island, the Australian mainland, and numerous other Islands. Australia is a mega-diverse country, the driest, oldest, flattest country inhabited continent. Its national capital is Canberra. The country's national language is English, and the major religions dominating the regions are Christianity, Islam, and Hinduism. They have Federal Parliamentary Constitutional Monarchy Government. Australia has a $1.605 trillion GDP per capita income of $61,194. Their Human Development Index also stands very high, i.e., 0.944

3. Switzerland

Its official name is Swiss Confederation, and the nation is landlocked and present at the confluence of Southern, Central, and Western Europe. The country shares its border with Liechtenstein to the east, France to the west, Italy to the south, and Austria and Germany to the north. Some of the largest economic centres and cities present in the nation are Basel, Geneva, and Zurich. These cities house offices of major and powerful international bodies like the World Trade Organisation, the World Health Organisation, the UN's second-largest office, and Fifa's headquarters. The official language of the nation is Italian, German, and French. Dominating religion of the country is Christianity, with 61.2%. They have a federal assembly independent directorial republic under a semi-direct democracy government. Their GDP size is $584 billion, and their per capita income is $67,557.

4. Canada

Canada is situated in North America and extends from the Atlantic Ocean to the Pacific Ocean and northward to the Arctic Ocean. Ottawa is the national capital. Their three largest metropolitan cities are Vancouver, Montreal, and Toronto. The official language of the country is French and English. They have a Federal parliamentary constitutional monarchy, their GDP is $2.237 trillion, and their per-capita income is $57,812. The country possesses a very high ranking on Human Development Index. Canada has the 8th largest advanced economy in the world and is filled with maximum natural resources and well-established trade routes. The nation is part of various intergovernmental and international powerful organizations like NATO, the United Nations, the Group of ten, the organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD), the World Trade Organization, the G7, and the G20.

5. Germany

The Formal name of Germany is the Federal Republic of Germany, which is present in Central Europe. Germany shares its border with France, Belgium, Luxembourg, the Netherlands to the West, Austria and Switzerland to the south, the Czech Republic to the east, Denmark to the north, and Poland. Germany's capital and largest city Ruhr and the financial centre in Frankfurt.

Germany's official language is German, and they possess Federal Parliamentary Republic Government. Germany's GDP is $4.319 trillion, and the per capita income is $51,860. The nation ranks top in the Human Development Index, and its economy is in the much-advanced stage. Their economy is the fourth-largest in the world by nominal GDP and is the leader in the fields of scientific and technological, and industrial sectors.

Interestingly, Germany is 3rd largest importer and exporter of goods. It provides high social security, environmental protection, a universal health care system, and education. The country stands 18th in the most peaceful country. Due to the huge economic size and advancements in the technological field, the country is part of several groupings: G7, NATO, United Nations, and OECD.

6. The Netherlands

The nation is present in North-western Europe. It shares a border with Belgium to the south, Germany to the east, and the North Sea Coastline to the west and north. Dutch is the official language of the country. The ethnic composition of the Netherland is 75.8% Dutch and 7.9% other European. 38.3% of Christianity dominates the region. It has a Unitary parliamentary constitutional monarchy Government.

Netherlands's GDP is $1.055 trillion, and the country's per capita income is $60,461. The nation was a founding member of many international organizations like OECD, NATO, G10, and WTO. Some eminent intergovernmental organizations and international courts are present in the beautiful Hague city. The Netherlands stands top in the indices like quality of life, economic freedom, press freedom, happiness index, and Human Development Index.

7. France

France is officially named the French Republic and shares boundaries with nations like Italy, Monaco, Switzerland, Germany and Luxembourg, Belgium, Spain, and Andorra. French is the nation's official language, and 93% of French-speaking people live there. Christianity as a religion dominates in the region. France has a Unitary Semi- Presidential republic form of Government. France's GDP is $3.667 trillion, and the country's per capita income is $44,747. France is a leading tourist destination site, and it is believed France receives a huge number of tourists every year.

France has outperformed in the indices like education, life expectancy, health care, and Human Development Index. France has considerable influence in Global affairs of the world. France has a permanent seat in the United Nations Security Council and has been declared a nuclear weapon state. France plays a key role in groupings like the Group of 7, the North Atlantic Treaty Organisation, and the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development.

8. Denmark

It is a Nordic nation present in Northern Europe and Scandinavian countries. Danish is the country's official language, and the country's capital is Copenhagen. The ethnic composition of Denmark is 86.11% and 13.89%. Christianity as a religion dominates the region. Denmark has a Unitary Parliamentary constitutional monarchy form of Government. Denmark's GDP is $ 406.011 billion, and the country's per capita income is $68,094. It is a highly modern and developed nation, and people have a very high standard of living. The country stands at the top in civil liberties, education, health care, democratic governance, LGBT equality, and Civil liberties. The nation is a founding member of various international organizations: the United Nations, NATO, the Nordic Council, and the OECD. They maintain linguistic, political, and cultural ties with all Scandinavian countries.

9. The United States of America

It is a transcontinental nation that is present in North America. The US comprises 50 states, and the country's national capital is Washington, DC. English is the national language of the nation. The ethnic composition of the nation is 61.6% white, 12.4% Black, 6% Asian, and others. Christianity as a religion dominates the nation. The US has a Federal Presidential Constitutional Republic form of Government. The US GDP is $25.35 trillion, and the country's per capita income is $76,027. The nation possesses highly advanced technology, and the country's economy is highly developed. It is said that the US GDP share in the nation is a quarter of the total share of the world. They spend a huge amount of sum of money on the military. The US military power dominates the world. The US is the founding member of international and national organizations such as NATO, the International Monetary Fund, the United Nations, and the World Bank. The US is a permanent member of the United Nations Security Council. The country is tops in the quality of life, economic freedom, income and wealth, education, Human rights, and the low level of corruption. But the nation is still behind in Universal Health Care and possesses a high level of inequality and incarceration.

10. New Zealand

It is a small island land nation present in the southwest Pacific Ocean. The land mass comprises South Island and North Island. The capital of the nation is Wellington. The official language of the island nation is English NZ sign Language and Maori. The ethnic composition of the nation is 71.8% European, 16.5 Maori, and 15.3% Asian. The dominant religion of the nation is Christianity. New Zealand has Unitary Parliamentary Constitutional Monarchy. New Zealand's GDP is $226.566 billion and per capita income is $47,499. New Zealand ranks very high on education, economic freedom, Human Development Index, Protection of rights and freedom of speech, and economic liberty. The country has a very developed ecosystem for facilitating tourism and dominates in the industrial sector and agriculture. The nation is part of several groupings and international organizations like the Pacific Community, Commonwealth of Nations, OECD, ASEAN Plus Six, Asia- Pacific Economic Cooperation, Pacific Islands Forum, and the United Nations.

11. United Kingdom

A Sovereign nation located in the Northern Europe, the nation is made up of Northern Ireland, Scotland, England, and Wales. The nation is enclosed by the English Channel, the Celtic Sea, the Irish Sea, the Atlantic Ocean, and the North Sea. The official language of the nation is English. The ethnic composition of the nation is 87.1% white, 7% Asian, and others. Christianity as a religion dominates the region. The United Kingdom has the Unitary Parliamentary Constitutional Monarchy form of Government. The nation's GDP is $ 3.376 trillion, and the country's per capita income is $49,761. It has the sixth-largest economy in the world. The purchasing power of the nation is the eighth largest. The country outperforms on several fronts like life expectancy, healthcare, education, and Human Development Index. The UK has a considerable influence on global affairs of the world. The nation is a nuclear stage and is the leading champion in the cultural, scientific, and technological. The country is part of intergovernmental, international groupings and institutions like:

  • Interpol
  • Organization for the Economic-Cooperation and Development
  • North Atlantic Treaty Organisation
  • the Group of Ten
  • the Group of 7
  • Commonwealth of Nations
  • The Council of Europe
  • AUKUS (Australia, United Kingdom, United States)
  • THE United Organisations
  • The World Trade Center
  • Permanent member in the United Nations Security Council

12. Japan

An Island nation located in East Asia. The nation's capital is Tokyo. The national language of the island nation is Japanese. Japan has a Unitary Parliamentary Constitutional monarchy form of Government. Japan's GDP size in the economy is $6.110 trillion, and the nation's per capita income is $48,814. Japan is a highly urbanized and densely populated nation. The nation's third-largest economy is tops on the Human Development Index, leading in the robotics, automotive, and electronic sectors. Their culture, arts, cuisine, and language are well recognized worldwide. The nation has contributed heavily to science and technology. Japan is an influential nation, part of the many international groupings and intergovernmental organizations like the Group of Sevens, the Group of 20, and OECD.

13. Finland

A Nordic nation shares boundaries with Norway to the north, Sweden to the North-West, the Gulf of Bothnia, and Russia to the east. The capital of the nation is Helsinki which is the largest metropolitan city. The official language of the nation is Swedish and Finnish. The ethnic composition of the nation is 91.98% Finns and 8.02% others. Christianity as a religion dominates the nation. Finland has a Unitary Parliamentary republic form of Government. The GDP size is $ 267.61 billion, and their per capita is $53,745. The nation outperforms on several indices like economic competitiveness, education, human development index, civil rights, and health care. It has stood 1st in World Happiness Index and second in the Global Gender Gap. Finland is a high welfare state and a prosperous nation. It is a rapidly developing economy and an advanced nation.
