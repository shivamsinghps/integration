Tourist Places in Nagercoil

One of the most demanded locations for a tranquil weekend trip is this unassuming town in the Kanyakumari region, which is gifted with outstanding natural beauty and photo scenery of Southern India.

The small village, which is 18 kilometres from Kanyakumari, is far enough from the city to allow for a quick trip for a few hours or days so you may get immersed in the unique environment. Beaches and lakes, where you may relax and take pictures of bizarre sights, interrupt the rocky environment. There are several local restaurants and bars along the whole southern coast where you may eat delicious meals and sample some of Tamil Nadu's unique flavours.

Whether you want to spend a few days in a treehouse in the south or immerse yourself in the culture, read on to learn about some of Nagercoil's most fantastic tourist attractions.

1. Vivekananda Rock Memorial:

This location is noteworthy for a rock that emerged from the sea. Before beginning his philosophical tour of the globe in 1892, the well-known Swami Vivekananda meditated here. Even one footprint may be seen on this rock. Many people think that Devi Kumari's print is her identity. The government erected the memorial in 1970 as a tribute to this rock.

  • Ferry journeys cost about INR 20 per person, and entry fees are roughly INR 10 per person every day of the week.
  • Vivekananda Rock Memorial is open daily from 8 am to 4 pm.
  • Address: 629702 Kanyakumari, Tamil Nadu.

2. Muttom Beach:

This rugged seashore is well-known for its impressive caverns. Because of the nearby fishing communities, this location is perfect for weekend excursions. One of the most pristine beaches in our nation is this one. There are no entrance fees required to enter this location.

  • Muttom Beach is open daily from 6:00 am to 6:00 pm.
  • Kanyakumari Muttom Beach Location: Vellimalai, Tamil Nadu, 629202, India.

3. Ullakarvi:

If you're an adventurer, you'll enjoy this site. This location is around 18 kilometres from the town and descends to a waterfall. The waterfall may be reached by following this path; however, it is frequently still hidden by tiny bushes, plants, and rocks. If you're fortunate, you can see the two waterfalls present here.

  • Ullakarvi Open from 9:00 am to 3:00 pm.
  • Time Required: 2 to 3 hours.

4. Keeriparai Reserve Forest

One of the top tourist attractions in Nagercoil is this. This area, around 30 kilometres from the town, is home to elephants, lions, bears, and pythons. A vast collection of several medicinal plant species are also present here. One plant species grown here is used to make the ayurvedic medication Siddha. When visiting this forest, don't forget to bring some drinking water with you. A small entrance charge of about INR 4 is required to see this location.

  • Keeriparai Reserve Forest is open daily from 11:00 am to 5:00 pm.
  • Entry into Keeriparai Reserve Forest costs Rs 3 per adult and Rs 2 per child.
  • Address for Keeriparai Reserve Forest Kanyakumari is Veerapuli R.F, Kanyakumari, Tamil Nadu, 627133, India.

5. Government Museum Kanyakumari:

A little entrance charge is required to enter this location, and it is open almost daily. This museum's collection of stone sculptures and statues from the fifteenth century is well-known, and the famous Nataraja bronze sculpture from the 7th century may be found here. In addition, this museum has a vast collection of historical artefacts and old coins. This museum's artefacts can provide a bird's-eye view of our ancient Indian past.

  • Entry to the Government Museum in Kanyakumari is Rs. 5 for Indians and Rs. 3 for Indian kids; for foreigners, Rs. 100 per person.
  • The Government Museum in Kanyakumari is open daily from 9:30 am to 5:00 pm.
  • Address of Government Museum Kanyakumari is Beach Road in Kanyakumari, Tamil Nadu, India, 629702.

6. Kanyakumari Wildlife Sanctuary:

The Sambar deer, Bison, Python, and endangered tigers have significant reserves in this sanctuary. A large variety of gorgeous Western Ghats plant species are also present here, along with such wildlife.

  • The Kanyakumari Wildlife Sanctuary is open every day from 6:00 am to 6:00 pm.
  • Veerapuli R.F., Kanyakumari, Tamil Nadu, 627133, India, is the address for the Kanyakumari Wildlife Sanctuary.

7. Thiruparrapu Waterfalls:

Thirparappu Falls is only 55 kilometres from the seaside town of Kanyakumari, making it a popular destination for locals looking to reconnect with nature. In the pool where people congregate to swim, the water from this waterfall pours down fifty feet below.

Pilgrims congregating at the waterfall to make offerings at the well-known Mahadevar Temple also stop at the waterfall. Famous for both its spectacular architecture and historical inscriptions. Additionally, a large number of locals go boating in the location. A pool that was created specifically for children is popular on the weekends.

  • Where to Find
    Kulasekaram is 5 kilometres, Nagarcoil is 42 kilometres, and Trivandrum is 55 kilometres away.
  • Tiruparapu, Kanyakumari, Tamil Nadu 629161, India, is the address for Thirparappu Falls.
  • The Thirparappu Falls Time: 7:00 am to 6:00 pm.
  • Thirparappu Falls Cost: INR 21.
  • The ideal time to see Thirparappu Falls is from 8:00 am to 4:00 pm.
  • Thirparappu Falls visitation time is 01:30 hours.

8. Tsunami Memorial:

The Tamilnadu state of Kanyakumari is home to Tsunami Memorial Park. The southernmost part of India, Kanyakumari, was among the various places damaged by the tsunami that shook the world in December 2004. It was believed that over 10700 people from Tamil Nadu and approximately 800 people from Kanyakumari perishedin the massive damage caused by the tsunami's enormous waves. In honour of everyone who died in the 2004 tsunami, the Kanyakumari Tsunami Memorial was eventually built on the town's shore.

Shri. B. Kanagaraj Cangan was used in designing and constructing the Kanyakumari Tsunami Memorial Monument. It is a steel statue that is sixteen feet tall and blue. The monument honours those who perished in the tsunami that struck on December 26, 2004; it depicts a giant tsunami wave being held in place by a hand. At this memorial, thousands of people visiting Kanyakumari pay respects to the Tsunami victims. The Tsunami Memorial is unquestionably very remarkable in memory of the people who lost their lives during the destruction caused by the enormous waves.

  • Kanyakumari's Tsunami Memorial Park is close to the south shore in Tamil Nadu, India, 629702.
  • Opening times are 6:00 am to 7:00 pm every day.

9. Thengapattinam Beach:

In the Vilancode taluk, Thengapattinam Beach is a well-liked tourist destination close to Paimkulam town. This lovely beach, 35 kilometres from Nagercoil, is surrounded by palm and coconut trees.

This beach is famous for having a lovely estuary where the river meets the sea. Many people come here to picnic and take advantage of fishers' catamaran (small boat) trips. They can arrange to go to the historic 1200-year-old mosque close to this seashore.

  • The ideal beach for children is one where they may play on the sand for hours at a time or create sand castles.
  • There is no entrance fee.
  • 12 am to 12 pm is the Daily opening Time.
  • Thengapattinam Beach address is Paimkulam Village, Kanyakumari, 629001, Tamil Nadu.

10. Nagaraja Temple:

Since devotees believe this location may correct "sarpa dosha" flaws, it is well-known among them. People worry that this illness may cause infertility and a short life span. People come to this location on Sundays to present milk to the local god to obtain his blessings for prosperity in their life.

In addition, the Kanyakumari district is home to several breathtaking Nagercoil tourist attractions. Chakravarthy Cinemas, Padmanabhapuram Palace, Sanguthurai Beach, Guganathaswamy Temple, Koilvilai Temple Garden, and others are a few examples of these unique destinations. This town is gorgeous all year round, but winter is the ideal time to visit. The city of Nagercoil will always hold a special place in the Tourist's hearts.

  • Address: N Car Street, Ozhuginasery, Tamil Nadu 629001, 5CQM+365
  • 5 am to 11:30 am and 5 pm to 8 pm daily is the opening time.

11. Udayagiri Fort:

This fort features a lovely antique chapel belonging to the Dutch battalion and is constructed on massive granite rocks. Because a monarch used it as part of a defensive strategy to produce weapons, this location is of great historical significance. Recently, the government converted this area into a park dedicated to biodiversity, which is now home to several plant and animal species.

  • Mon to Sunday, 9:30 am to 5:00 pm.
  • How to reach:

On the national highway between Thiruvananthapuram and Nagercoil, Udayagiri Fort is situated in Puliyoorkurichi. It won't take more than 10 minutes to get from Padmanabhapuram Palace to the fort. About 2 kilometres (km) from Padmanabhapuram Palace, 3 kilometres (km) from Thuckalay, 14 kilometres (km) from Colachel, 16 kilometres (km) from Marthandam, 18 kilometres (km) from Kulasekharam, 19 kilometres (km) from Kuzhithurai, 6 kilometres (km) from Eraniel, 14 kilometres (km) from Nagercoil, 20 kilometres (km) from Boothapandi, 33 kilometres (km) from Kanyaku The closest airport is in Thiruvananthapuram, while the nearest train station is at Eraniel.

12. Vattakottai Fort:

This location is surrounded by the Western Ghats mountain range and the waves of a blue sea. Visitors love this place because it makes for the ideal picnic location and offers stunning views of the natural environment. The VattaKottai Fort provides a strong reminder of the Travancore kingdom's dominance. This court with a circular frame design is an intriguing place to see close to Kanyakumari. This monument on the shore of Kanyakumari is made of granite and extends partially into the sea. It has withstood the test of time and continues to stand firm. In its day, this fort was built to protect the sea lanes. It is a well-liked tourist site nowadays. Being on this ancient fortification is both a peaceful and a powerful experience. The location's history is fascinating and full of activity, yet the sea and the lush grass have a calming influence that makes the background look surreal. Since the fort is so close to Kanyakumari, you really must go there while you're there. Additionally, by following the Kanyakumari schedule for one day, explore the city's top attractions.

  • Address for the Vattakottai Fort is VattaKottai Fort in Kanyakumari, India's Nagercoil, TN.
  • The Vattakottai Fort is open from 8:00 am to 7:00 pm.
  • Price of Vattakottai Fort is 120 INR.
  • Visit Vattakottai Fort between 8:00 am and 7:00 pm for the best experience.
  • The visitor must allow 2:00 hours to explore Vattakottai Fort.

13. Suchindram Thanumalayan Temple:

The importance of Suchindram Thanumalayan Temple is that the statue of Lord Hanuman, 22 feet tall and carved from a single block of granite, stands majestically in the temple. Furthermore, it is the only linga of its kind in India since it has representations of the three main gods of Hinduism. Devotees also hold that in 1740, when Tipu Sultan, a monarch of the Kingdom of Mysore, attacked the temple, Lord Hanuman hid underground to defend it. One of the architectural and design features of the temple grounds are four melodic pillars, each carved out of a single stone and standing at the height of 18 feet (5.5 m). The gopuram was engraved with scenes from the Ramayana and Mahabharata using herbal ink. The goddesses Aram Valartha Nayaki, Indra Vinayaka, Kala Bhairava, and Saakahi Ganapathy are also present. This temple has inscriptions from the 9th century, giving it a more ethnic feel overall.

There are 1035 more pillars in the temple's dance hall, all of which have exquisite sculptures.

  • The times of Suchindram Thanumalayan Temple are 4:30 am to 11:30 am for morning darshan and 5 pm to 8:30 pm for evening darshan.
  • How to go to Suchindram Thanumalayan Temple is as follows:
    Air: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, located 70 kilometres from the temple, is the closest airport.
    Rail: The closest station with frequent trains is Nagercoil junction, which is 3 km away from the temple.
    Road: Buses travel from locations including Trivandrum, Kanyakumari, and Thirunelveli.

14. Kumari Amman Temple:

The goddess Devi Amman is said to be present everywhere in Kanyakumari. For a faraway lighthouse, People are claiming that sailors often confuse her nose ring. This temple is where this god is worshipped, and many religious people come here in search of quiet and happiness.

15. Suchindram Theroor Birds Sanctuary:

The distance from the Kanyakumari district to this bird sanctuary is around 13 kilometres. For those who enjoy the outdoors, this site is perfect for them. Here, you may see various bird species, including terns, geese, and storks. One may see migratory birds in this sanctuary, including pelicans, darters, and egrets. Astonishing pink lotuses and purple lilies are also in bloom all over the area.

  • Mon to Sunday, 12 pm to 5:00 pm.

How to Get There in Nagercoil

The closest airport to Nagercoil is Trivandrum Domestic Airport, which is around 70 kilometres away. From there, one may take a bus or a cab to get to Nagercoil. All surrounding and significant cities are easily accessible from Nagercoil via bus services. The town is connected to favoured locations, including Kanyakumari, Trivandrum, Chennai, and Madurai via Nagercoil Railway Station.

Ideal Season to Visit Nagercoil

The year-round climate of Nagercoil is essentially pleasant and temperate. The winter season, when temperatures range from 17 to 32 degrees Celsius, makes October to March the most fantastic time to visit the city. People particularly appreciate this period since it is ideal for excursions, sightseeing, and other tourist activities. The temperature rises a bit in the summer, although intense rainfalls and thunderstorms are common throughout the rainy season.
