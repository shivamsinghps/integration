Zareen Khan

Zareen Khan is a gorgeous Bollywood actress and model. She works in films mostly known in the Bollywood films industry and has also starred in the Punjabi, Telugu, and Tamil films industry. Zareen Khan made her movie debut in 2010 in Anil Sharma's historical picture Veer, co-starring alongside Salman Khan, for which she garnered a Zee Cine Nomination for Best Female Debut. She later featured in the extremely famous item number in a song "Character Dheela Hai" in the 2011 blockbuster comic movie, Ready.

She made her appearance as a gorgeous model in the 2012 romantic comedy movie Housefull 2 gained commercial success in Bollywood and remains her highest-grossing movie to date. Khan made her Tamil film debut the following year with Naan Rajavaga Pogiren, in which she played an item dance performance in the song "Malgove."

She returned to Bollywood in 2015 with the sensual thriller Hate Story 3 after appearing in the Punjabi film Jatt James Bond in 2014. And in 2019, she made her Telugu film debut with the action thriller Chanakya.

Early Life

Khan was born in Mumbai on 14 May 1987 to a Muslim Pathan family. Her father's name is not known till now. She has spoken up in a public interview that her father abandoned them at a very young age. Her mother's name is Parveen khan. She is a homemaker, and she loves her mom. They both share a very close bond.

She completed her initial education at Duruelo Convent High School in Mumbai and did her education at Rizvi College of science; her mom said she was very good at her studies. Still, Due to her family's money troubles and financial problems, Khan was compelled to drop out of college, begin working, and move ahead. She tried her attempt at modelling because she had relevant talents. She completed a series of little tasks that paid her enough money to keep the house running. She started her career in acting while visiting the Yuvraaj set at Subhash Ghai's film school Whistling Woods. Salman Khan became acquainted with her and chose to cast her in his film Veer. She was cast in the role of Princess Yashodhara after an audition. She put on eight kg to be more credible as a 19th-century princess.
