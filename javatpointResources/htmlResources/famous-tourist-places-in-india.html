11 Famous Tourist Places in India

India is the ideal place for adventurous activities, desert safaris, and wild safaris. India has many honeymoon spots, nature-seeking places, spiritual places, and historical places. The key reason for India's tourism fame is that it offers a diverse range of tourist spots in one location. India not only attracts art and culture lovers, nature lovers, and history seekers but it also attracts food travelers as every place in India has its own special cuisine, which attracts food travelers. Some of the India's famous tourist places are described below.

1. Goa

Goa is a famous tourist destination in India. It is the smallest state in India. It is famous for its endless beaches, diverse seafood, fantastic nightlife, and stunning architecture. Goa is one of the few places in the world that opens 24X7. Goa is a combination of Indian and Portuguese cultures that is the most distinctive feature.

The Goans are very friendly to visitors and also organize numerous festivals across the year. Goa's most well-known festivals are Goa Carnival and New Year. The seafood in Goa is fantastic. In India, Goa provides the best nightlife scenes with fashionable pubs, discotheques, beach shacks, a variety of clubs, and beautiful cafes.

Popular beaches in Goa

1. Baga beach:
This is Goa's most well-known and popular beach. It is famous for its throbbing beach parties, vibrant nightlife, and tasty seafood. This beach is in North Goa.

2. Calangute beach:
Calangute is called the "Queen of Beaches" in Goa. Calangute is known for its mercurial rise and fall of the waves and gleaming golden sand. This beach is in North Goa. This beach is a traveler's paradise, with its sun lounges, stylish restaurants, clubs, and shacks.

3. Candolim beach:
Candolim is a wonderful place to rest and revitalize during beach vacations because of its picturesque beauty and calm atmosphere. The scrub-covered sand dunes are one of Candolim beach's main draws. This beach is in North Goa.

4. Sinquerim beach:
The picturesque Sinquerim beach, which has swaying palm trees, is the ideal spot for a few peaceful moments. This beach is in North Goa. This beach provides breathtaking views of Fort Aguada.

5. Vagator beach:
Vagator beach has a laid-back vibe while yet this beach is a part of the rave culture. The beach is divided into 2 beaches that are big Vagator and small Vagator. This beach is in North Goa.

Goa's other major attractions:

Mangeshi temple, tiracol fort, Shanta Durga temple, chapora fort, Maruti temple, fort Aguada, saptakoteshwar temple, corjuem fort, Palolem beach, mahalasa Narayani temple, Santa Cruz church, agonda beach, Brahma temple, and butterfly beach.

The famous dishes of Goa that tourists must try are dodol, chicken xacuti, pork vindaloo, croquettes, sorak, patolea, bebinca, shark Ambot tik, feijoada, nevri, Goan fish curry, sannas, Goan khatkhate, sorpotel and chicken cafreal.

2. Udaipur

It's one of Rajasthan's most popular tourist attractions. Udaipur is commonly called the City of Lakes. Udaipur is famous for azure lakes, majestic palaces, colorful culture, and wonderful cuisine. Along with a must-see site, this place is also the best place in India to enjoy luxury. One of the most amazing views and pleasures of the Udaipur tour is boating in the glistening lake Pichola. Udaipur is also called the "Venice of the East," it is undoubtedly one of India's most romantic destinations.

Udaipur's major attractions

1. Udaipur City Palace:
This is Udaipur's most well-known and popular tourist destination. This palace is situated on the shores of Lake Pichola. The palace's beautiful interiors surprise visitors with meticulous mirror work, paintings, antique furniture, murals, and marble work.

2. Lake Palace:
The famous Lake Palace attracts tourists with its magnificence and picturesque setting; this palace is on the 4-kilometer-long man-made Lake Pichola. This palace provides breathtaking views of the Aravalli hills. This is a romantic location in Udaipur.

3. Jagmandir:
This is another romantic location in Udaipur. This palace is also located on lake Pichola. The palace's sheer magnificence and serenity attract visitors. Bara Patharon ka Mahal, Garden Courtyard, Kunwar Pada ka Mahal Darikhana, Gul Mahal, Zenana Mahal are the sections of this palace.

4. Sajjangarh Palace:
This palace is situated atop a hill. Monsoon Palace is another name for this palace. This palace provides breathtaking views of the Fateh Sagar Lake and Sajjangarh Wildlife Sanctuary. This palace is a great example of the Rajput rulers' grandeur.

Udaipur's other tourist attractions:

Jagdish Temple, Vintage Car Museum, Saheliyon Ki Bari, Lake Pichola, Udaisagar Lake, Fateh Sagar Lake, Jaisamand Lake, Jiyan Sagar, Rajsamand Lake, Maharana Pratap Memorial, and Doodh Talai.

3. Jammu and Kashmir

This is the most famous tourist destination in India. It is known throughout the world for its natural beauty, abundant wildlife, snow-capped mountains, exquisite monuments, friendly people, and handicrafts. J&K has abundant spectacular mountains, forests, gigantic glaciers, splendid monasteries, rivers, turquoise lakes, verdant meadows, and tall pine trees.

The activities like skiing, camping, golf, trekking, river rafting, and paragliding make a J&K trip more exciting. The state's unrivaled beauty and charm, as well as the locals' sweetness, will be stamped on visitors' minds for the rest of life.

Jammu and Kashmir's major attractions

Khardung La Pass, Gulmarg, Vaishno Devi, Dal Lake, Kashmir Valley, Sonmarg, Shalimar Bagh, Amarnath, Srinagar, Yusmarg, Betaab Valley, Patnitop, Sanasar, Gurez Valley, Pahalgam, Pulwama, and Chandanwari.

The famous dishes of Kashmir that tourists must try are rogan josh, Kashmiri pulao, paneer Chaman, rajma, malai kofta, Kashmiri saag, aloo dum, gushtaba, yogurt lamb curry, and patisa. The Kahwa is the most famous drink in Kashmir.

4. Nainital

Nainital is a picturesque hill station in Uttarakhand. It is near to both Dehradun and Delhi. Nainital has a lovely climate all year that makes it a favorite hill station for families, couples, and even lone travelers. The Nainital's heritage structures, boating, and old temples, as well as stunning views at every turn, attract visitors.

Nainital's main attraction is the Naina Lake. Naina Lake has Thandi Sadak on one side, Mall Road on the other, and the bus stop directly across the street. Visitors boat on this picturesque Naini Lake, eat Garhwali and Kumaoni specialties and buy something as souvenirs from here.

Nainital's major attractions

Astronomical Observatory, Naina Peak, Hanuman Garhi, Sattal, Bhimtal Lake, Jama Masjid, Bhutia Market, Kamaltal, Eco Cave Gardens, Mukteshwar temple, Sariyatal, Gurney House, Kainchi Dham, Raj Bhawan, Khurpatal Lake, and Pangot.

The famous dishes of Nainital that tourists must try are Bhatt ki churkani, gulgula, bun tikki, bal mithai, baadi, momos and thupka, ras, aloo ke gutke.

5. Lonavala

Lonavala is Maharashtra's most popular hill station and the best destination to visit during the monsoon season. Lonavala has abundant lakes, waterfalls, and hills surrounding it. It is a great place for trekking, camping, and adventure sports. Lonavala is also known for its treks, such as the Andharban trek, which begins in Pimpri and finishes in Bhira.

Lonavala's major attractions

Tiger's leap, bhushi dam, bhaja caves, duke's nose, Narayani dham temple, lohagad fort, Karla caves, Tikona fort, pawna lake, rajmachi fort, ryewood park, visapur fort, valvan dam, canyon valley, imagica Adlabs, aamby valley, amrutanjan point, reverse waterfall, bhairavnath temple, Kune falls, Lonavala lake, bhimashankar trail, and Shirota lake.

Lonavala's chikki is very famous, and every tourist must try that chikki. The other famous snacks are fudge and jelly candy.

6. Amritsar

Ambarsar is a nickname for Amritsar. It is the place where the magnificent Golden Temple is located. This destination is famous for the Jallianwala Bagh massacre and for being close to the Wagah Border. Amritsar market has the best clothes, Amritsari juttis, and accessories that entice the tourist to buy.

Amritsar's major attractions

Khalsa college, mata lal devi temple, wagah border, jama masjid khairuddin, ram tirath ashram, golden temple , akal takht, gobindgarh fort, tarn taran, durgiana temple, gurudwara baba atal rai, and jallianwala bagh.

The famous dishes of Amritsar that tourists must try are kulcha, gulab jamun, paya, bheja fry, Amritsari lassi, mutton chaap, kulfa, pinni & besan laddoos.

7. Madurai

This place is in Tamil Nadu. It is one of India's earliest inhabited cities. Meenakshi Amman Temple is a popular tourist attraction in Madurai. This temple's main deity is the goddess Meenakshi. Madurai has abundant historic temples that attract devotees and tourists.

Madurai's major attractions

Azhagar kovil, pazhamudhir solai, Thirumalai nayakar mahal, athisayam, Gandhi memorial museum, mary's cathedral, vaigai dam, koodal azhagar temple, yanaimalai, kazimar big mosque, samanar hills, Iskcon Madurai, goripalayam dargah.

The famous dishes of Madurai that tourists must try are Fluffy Soft Idlis, Idiyappams, Kari Dosai, Elumbu Roast, Kola Urundai, Kalkandu Sadam, Meen Kuzhambu, Paruthi Paal, Jigarthanda, Sooji Appams, and Vazhiyal.

8. Rishikesh

Rishikesh, commonly known as Hrishikesh, is a Hindu pilgrimage center in Uttarakhand. These places are famous for trendy cafes, ancient temples, and adventure activities. There are many Ashrams in this place that teach spirituality, meditation, yoga, and Ayurveda. Non-vegetarian food and drink are strictly forbidden in this devout town.

Rishikesh's major attractions

Shri Bharat Mandir, Laxman jhula, the Beatles ashram, ram jhula, Triveni ghat, tera manzil mandir, neelkanth Mahadev temple, Shivpuri and jumpin heights.

Activities to do in Rishikesh

Giant swing, cliff jumping, kayaking, hot air ballooning, camping, bungee jumping, rafting, and flying fox.

9. Shimla

Shimla is a lovely and huge city in Himachal Pradesh. This place is a favorite destination among honeymooners and Indian families. Shimla preserves its old-world charm with exquisite colonial buildings, pedestrian-friendly Mall Road, and a ridge lined with stores, cafes, and restaurants.

It is well-known for its stunning scenery, Victorian architecture, and exotic plants and wildlife. Shimla impresses visitors with its gorgeous historical monuments and a variety of enjoyable activities such as riding the Shimla toy train, ice skating, trekking, etc. Shimla is also noted for its handicrafts and wooden toys; tourists can purchase them from Mall Road or Lakkar Bazaar.

Shimla's major attractions

The ridge, green valley, Chadwick falls, mall road, kali bari temple, Annandale, Kufri christ church, Kalka-Shimla railway, Tara Devi temple, Himachal state museum, jakhoo hill and temple, mashobra, and Johnnie's wax museum.

The famous dishes of Shimla that tourists must try are dhaam, babru, madra, sidu, thukpa, chha gosht, mash daal, and chicken anardana.

10. Dalhousie

Dalhousie is a well-known hill station. Tourist enjoys the views of the Dhauladhar ranges' snow-capped summits in Dalhousie. Dalhousie is known for its waterfalls, lush forests, and meadows. This place is a favorite destination among honeymooners and Indian families. Dalhousie's Tibetan handicrafts, woolen Himachali shawls, and Chamba handkerchiefs are very popular.

Dalhousie's major attractions

Panchpula, khajjiar, satdhara falls, chamera lake, Ganji Pahari trek, dainkund peak, Indo Tibetan market, Laxmi Narayan temple, paragliding at khajjiar, St. John's church Dalhousie, Subhash baoli, sach pass, rock garden, Chamba, bakrota hills, garam sadak, Bhuri Singh museum, and Pohlani Devi temple.

11. Munnar

It is a hill station in Kerala, and it is a popular tourist destination. This site is not only popular in India but is one of the most well-traveled tourist sites in the world. This place is particularly a favorite among honeymooners. This place is famous for its tea plantation. Munnar's tea plantations are like a piece of heaven plucked from the sky. Munnar's magnificent tea gardens are a must-see place for any traveler.

Munnar's major attractions

  1. Eravikulam National Park:
    This park is home to a wide range of flora and fauna. Elephant, Nilgiri marten, lion-tailed macaque, Atlas moth, Nilgiri langur, small-clawed otter, and a rare tiger or leopard are few of the prominent animal species that can be seen here. The eco-tourism of the park includes the KFDC flower garden, tea museum, rajamala, lakkom waterfalls, lakkom log house, marayoor sandal division, and chinnar wildlife sanctuary. For Indian citizens' the park entrance fee is 65 rupees, and for foreigners, the park entrance fee is 245 rupees.
  2. Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary:
    This is one of Munnar's most popular tourist attractions. This Sanctuary's other name is Thattekad Bird Sanctuary. Night Heron, Sunbird, White-bellied blue flycatcher, Bee-eater, Wynaad laughing thrush, Crimson-Throated Barbet, Black-winged Kite, Rufous Babbler, and Malabar grey hornbil are few of the bird species that can be seen here. At this place, visitors enjoy the bonfire and grilled fish in the open air. For Indian citizens' the sanctuary entrance fee is 10 rupees, and for foreigners, the sanctuary entrance fee is 100 rupees.

Munnar's other tourist attractions

Chokramudi Peak, nyayamakad waterfall, blossom park, pallivasal tea garden, kundala lake, tata tea museum, meesapulimala, lakkom waterfalls, sevenmallay tea estate, Lockhart gap, rose garden, kolukkumalai tea estate, mattupetty dam.

The famous dishes of Munnar that tourists must try are Kerala prawn curry, kairali thali, puttu and kadala curry, idiyappam with egg curry, and appam with stew.
