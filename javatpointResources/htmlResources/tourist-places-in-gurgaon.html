Tourist Places in Gurgaon

The City of a Million Dreams Gurgaon has some of the top and fun destinations to visit and has of entertainment hubs. The city is named after Dronacharya, who was guru of Kaurava and Pandava in Mahabharata. Gurgaon has something for everyone, from bird sanctuaries and museums to art and history fans, pubs and bars for partygoers, and sports arcades for adventure lovers.

Gurgaon has long been known as a centre for a wonderful destination of North Indian food. Gurgaon is the place to satisfy your taste buds and replenish your soul, with age-old eateries maintaining history and culture and trendy hotspots trying to bridge the gap between traditional and international food. The tourist attractions in Gurgaon include resorts and restaurants with ancient architecture that speak of India's bygone era.

1. Heritage Transport Museum

The Heritage Transport Museum, located in Tauru near Manesar, Gurgaon, is India's First Transport Museum. The museum covers the history of the evolution of human mobility. There is a vast display of all modes of transportation from the British era to the present day. The museum is open Tuesday through Sunday from 10 a.m. to 7 p.m.

This museum also hosts exhibitions from time to time. There are 12 collections on show here, including Pre-Mechanized Transport, Automobile Gallery, Aviation, Railway, Marine, etc. Each set is beautifully crafted to reflect the period it represents. It is the first of its type in India and was designed only to capture, display, and distribute transportation-related information.

2. Kingdom of Dreams

The Kingdom of Dreams was the country's first live entertainment and theatre facility. The Kingdom of Dreams, or KOD, is a popular tourist destination in Gurgaon. You can learn about India's rich cultural heritage, crafts, and cuisines by visiting this location. Among the numerous significant attractions here, the Nautanki Mahal and Shosha Theater are two places to see great shows.

Zangura is one of the best Kingdom of Dreams concerts you must see. It is situated in Gurgaon and is easily accessible by public transportation or taxi. It is always visited by people, mainly families, and is considered one of the most entertaining spots to visit in Gurugram.

3. Fun N Food

Fun and Food Village is an entertainment park that features the Lazy River, the world's longest water channel (400 feet). It is an excellent choice for a day excursion for children and adults. It is open all year, except during the significant Hindu festival holiday of Diwali etc., and may be visited between 9.30 a.m. and 6.30 p.m. It has activities for schoolchildren, corporate personnel, and college students.

India's largest indoor snow park is located on Old Delhi Gurgaon Road. It includes cutting-edge infrastructure and water parks, and a variety of bone entertainment attractions popular with tourists. Apart from water sports, there is also delicious cuisine here. People come here to spend quality time with family and friends.

4. DLF Cyber Hub

One of the most interesting sites to visit in Gurgaon is the Cyber Hub. It is a vast retail space featuring a variety of shopping locations and high-end cafes. Other attractions include Smash and numerous other pubs. There are several family-friendly attractions that you may take your children to.

Visit this restaurant for some delectable multi-cuisine dishes or shop for something unique. It features a rooftop terrace and an auditorium, and a world-class design. This location's ambience makes it one of the most interesting locations. It is ideal for young people in Gurgaon to socialise and hold parties.

5. Ambience Mall

Ambience Mall is one of Gurgaon's greatest shopping malls, drawing people of all ages. Ambience Mall in Gurgaon is a shopping sanctuary for anyone who enjoys the great outdoors. You could spend all day exploring here. Several alternatives are available to you, ranging from national to worldwide brands.

The mall covers a total space of 21 lakh square feet. There are also lots of options for eateries. There are various alternatives for kids, such as the Stellar Children's Museum and Wakao, where they may have endless fun while their parents keep a watch on them. It is one of the largest retail malls in Asia. The mall has no admission fee; however, you must pay a parking fee for your vehicle.

6. The Paintball Company

If you're looking for fun places in Gurgaon for youngsters, Paintball Company is one of them. Paintball is the fastest-growing sport in which players work together to destroy their opponents. This location is recommended for families looking to have some fun while providing their children with plenty of space for various explorations.

The Paintball Company is in Sector 31, Gurgaon. It is a large open space covering an area of 2 acres. One acre is dedicated to paintballing, while the rest is open space. It is a well-designed and imaginative play area that provides an enjoyable paintballing experience. This paintball battlefield provides an amazing paintball experience to new arrivals and experienced players.

7. Leisure Valley Park

Leisure Valley Park is a lovely urban park with fountains and melodic water performances. It has a lovely rose garden and is popular for picnics. It is situated in Gurugram's Sector 29. It is open seven days a week and closes at 9 p.m.

Most visitors come here for a pleasant family trip. The good part about this park is that there is no admission price, so everyone can visit. There is, however, a parking fee if you park your vehicle here.

8. Camp Tikkling

Camp Tikkaling is a village and adventure camp on the outskirts of Gurgaon. Rappelling, zip-lining, trekking, rappelling on natural rocks, jumping, paintball, paramotoring, and other activities are available here. Schoolchildren, corporate clients, and adventure seekers all visit the camp.

This location is in the heart of the Aravallis and is ideal for relaxing the body and mind. The adventure sports available here provide an incredible adrenaline rush. There are various camp ticket packages available.

9. Smaaash

Smaaash in Sector 29, Gurgaon, is a great place to visit if you enjoy gaming. It is an excellent entertainment location where you may engage in various sports such as sky karting, go-karting, cricket, etc. Birthday parties, team-building activities, and even corporate parties are conducted here.

Smash is a fun place for both kids and adults to visit. It is located at Cyber Hub Mall, one of India's most well-known gaming and entertainment venues. It is one of the most popular places in Gurgaon for families to engage in irresponsible fun activities. There are various packages for various purposes.

10. Vintage Camera Museum

If you want to learn more about the history of museums, go to the Vintage Camera Museum in DLF Phase 3. This location is also known as Museo Camera. It features a significant collection of cameras accumulated over 100 years. You will appreciate this site if you enjoy photography. The museum houses personal camera collections of well-known photographer Aditya Arya. People usually come here to learn more about photography.

11. Appu Ghar

Appu Ghar is one of India's first and most popular amusement parks. It has about 15 rides and a banquet seating capacity of around 2000 people. It is home to the country's fastest, highest, and fastest water rides, which attract people of all ages and children, with a 10-acre property in a fantastic position near HUDA City Metro Station. The cost of a ticket varies based on the day and age. That's all it takes to be pleased by the world of water splashes.

12. Lohagarh Farms

Lohagarh Farm is a popular outdoor destination for corporate and family outings. You can do things like take a mud bath and bathe under a tube well at this location. This farm brings back memories of people's childhoods and village life. This location is popular with friends and couples and with families and children. You and your family may participate in various indoor and outdoor activities. Evenings here are filled with folk music and dancing performances. A full-day tour costs Rs 1150 for adults and Rs 650 for youngsters.

13. Damdama Lake

This lake is a beautiful lake located about 25 kilometres from Gurgaon. This lake was built to store water and is located in a lush green environment. This Damdama Lake currently serves as a habitat for over 200 bird species. This Damdama Lake, located away from the hustle and bustle of the city, is a great place to spend quality time with your family or friends.

14. Pataudi Palace

The Pataudi Palace in Gurgaon is also a popular tourist destination. Let us tell you that this palace is also known as Ibrahim Kothi and is associated with the royal family. This palace, which covers an area of more than 25 acres, features stunning gardens, lawns, and fountains; it is a kind of romantic destination for couples in Gurgaon. In such a case, if you want to enjoy roaming in royal style, you should come here. You will require permission to enter this palace.

15. Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary

It is a national park which is located in Farukh Nagar, 15 km from Gurgaon. It is a beautiful lush green place full of trees, shrubs etc., and you can watch the wildlife from a safe distance. You can watch different varieties of birds from their four watch towers located at different points. There are all the important facilities for the visitors, and there is also a tourist complex built in the park.

Farrukh Nagar Fort

Farukh Nagar Fort was built in 1732 by Faujdar Khan, the governor of Farukhsiyar. The town was famous for its salt trade until the British banned it in the early 1900s. This rich cultural heritage of the city, along with Jama Masjid, Sheesh Mahal, Sethani ki Chhatri, many Havelis, and Sultanpur National Park, can be included on the list of tourist places to visit in Gurgaon.

16. Aravalli Biodiversity Park

Biodiversity Park of Aravalli contains over 170 bird and animal species and 160 plant species. The Aravalli Biodiversity Park covers 1.54 square kilometres and is located 21 kilometres from the city centre of Gurgaon. It is one of the most interesting places to visit in Gurgaon. The Aravalli Biodiversity Park was created to restore and preserve the desert's vegetation ecologically. The park opened on World Environment Day in June 2010, after the Supreme Court banned stone mining in the area.

17. Digambar Temple

The Digambar Temple in Gurgaon is a Jain temple constructed during the Mughal era. This temple was constructed in the 17th century. This temple is also well-known for its tranquil atmosphere. This temple is dedicated to Lord Mahavir, the 24th Tirthankara of the Jains. There is a bookstore within the temple grounds to see many artefacts and study materials related to Jainism.

18. Sheetla Mata Temple in Gurgaon

In Hindu mythology, Mata Sheetla is a widely worshipped goddess in North India, West Bengal, Nepal, and Bangladesh. Sheetla Mata Temple is well-known for its festival in the Hindu month of Chaitra.

How to Reach Gurgaon

The closest airport to Gurgaon is in Delhi. It is also easily accessible from nearby major cities to Gurgaon. Gurgaon has its railway station, which is well connected to India's major cities. Gurgaon is also served by a metro system connecting it to other Delhi parts, including Noida and Faridabad.

  • By Flight: If you plan to take the flight to Gurgaon, you should be fully conscious that there is no airport. The nearest airport to Gurgaon is Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi, 28 kilometres away. This airport has direct flights to major cities throughout India and the world. As a result, you can fly to Delhi airport from any major city.
  • By Train: Gurgaon has a small railway station that connects it to a few major cities from a few railways. Trains operated by Indian Railways can easily transport you from major cities in India. Nizamuddin Railway Station and New Delhi Railway Station are the nearest railway stations. Taxis and cabs are available outside the railway station to take you to the tourist attractions of Gurgaon.
  • By Road: Even though Gurgaon is well connected by road to major cities and towns throughout India, tourists can visit this city using various bus services. There are also luxuries, large AC sleepers available in Gurgaon for tourists, depending on their needs.

Local Food in Gurgaon

As a centre of industry, commerce, and tourism, Gurgaon has an amazing array of food options. Delicious food from India and worldwide and North Indian food culture are readily available in the dhabas, restaurants, and streets. Gurgaon has some delicious North Indian dishes that you should try. You can sample a variety of parathas, chicken, mince, delicious pulses, and egg dishes here.

Best Time to Visit Gurgaon

Summers in Gurgaon are extremely hot, making it difficult to venture out into the sun. After September, the temperature in this city drops significantly, and the weather remains pleasant until December. After September to December, February and March are ideal months to visit Gurgaon. You should avoid visiting this city in January because the weather is extremely cold and severe.
