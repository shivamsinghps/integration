10 Best Tourist Places in Palampur

Palampur is a hill station, 35 kilometres from Kangra, situated in Himachal Pradesh. It is a quiet town on a plateau surrounded by pine forests, deodar, and Green Tea states. Palampur is closely linked with other parts of Himachal Pradesh by road and rail. It is also known as the Green Valley of Himachal Pradesh. The place is trendy for plantations. This place also has an ancient temple and colonial architecture.

Temperature

  • Winter (November- March): 0 degree Celsius- 10 degree Celsius
  • Summer (April to October): 8 degree Celsius- 30 degree Celsius
  • Suitable time to visit: March to December

Stays

Since Palampur is known for its tourism, the place has numerous hotels present here. Some of the hotels where tourist stays are:

  • Hotel Highland Regency (NH20)
  • Hotel Surbhi (State Highway 17)
  • Hotel Thamsar (VPO, Sungal)
  • WelcomHeritage Taragarh Palace (Taragarh PO)
  • Sai Garden (PO Budla Tea Estate)
  • POPS Hotel and Restaurant (VPO Chachian)
  • Silver Oaks Mountain Resort (Chandpur Tea Estate)

These hotels and restaurants provide excellent service and amenities like clean bathrooms, furnished rooms, and food on pay. They are known for their hospitality.

Eating Area

Tourists relish local foods in Palampur and should try Lungru Pakoras, Patroda, Lasiada, Chinese delicacies, and Braha Flower chutney. Chinese foods that are given in these restaurants are extremely delicious. Some of the prominent restaurants are Pizzicato Café (Palampur Shopping Centre), Neugal Café, Skyroom Bar and Restro, Taj Restaurant (Shubhash Chowk), The Longue and Golden Dragon, Sai Gardens (Holiday Resorts and Country Club), and Hotel Surbhi Restaurant (Near Main Market). Here Tourists can enjoy a sumptuous meal at affordable rates.

Tourist Hotspots in Palampur

1. Tashi Jong Monastery

The monastery is located 15 kilometres away from the main district. It is situated in Tashi Jong village. It is one of the centre-point of tourist attractions in Palampur. The Tibetan monastery is surrounded by hills, lush green plants, and the beautiful Himalayas. The building is decorated with Buddha statues, carvings, and paintings. The 8th Khamtrul Rinpoche founded the monastery, and the monastery believes in the Drugpa Kagyu sect (one of the sects of Buddhism). Society and community practice Drukpa Kagyu tradition, and they follow acetic yoga. Indian Tantric Yogi deeply influenced the Kagyu School- Tilopa emerged through Mahamudra meditation. Later, Tibetan came out with a technique, namely Kadampa; both resulted in the establishment of Drukpa Kagyu School. The main temple is constructed in the inner complex of the monastery, and the stupa of the Yamantaka retreat Centre and Khamtrul Rinpoche is restricted to the ordinary public. Khamtrul Rinpoche crafted this monastery. The monastery has an excellent collection of craft and art displays and many Tibetan artifacts. The ambiance present around the monastery is serene, blissful, and immersing. The place is paradise and an excellent place to detox your inner soul.

Entry Fees: No entry fees

2. Saurabh Van Vihar, Palampur

Saurabh Van Vihar is situated 4 kilometres away from Palampur. The place is spread across 13 kilometres. The park is devoted to brave soldier Saurabh Kalia, the martyr in Kargil War. The park is on the bank of snow-fed Neugel Khad in Kwat Village. It is an excellent place for recreation. The park also provides natural habitat to a sizeable chunk of bird species, and it makes the park more glorious. Saurabh Van Vihar was founded to create public awareness regarding environmental degradation and depletion. The park has now turned into a picnic spot for the people. Now, people enjoy bouldering, birdwatching, and boating in the park.

Historical Background

Saurabh Kalia who killed in action in Kargil War in 1999. This park is made in remembrance of the Indian Army Captain. Captain was brutally tortured in the captivity of Pakistan. He was tortured for more than 20 days during the war. It is an exemplary tribute to our soldier who has given their lives in defending the nation's territorial integrity. This nature park will inspire the coming future generations.

3. Palampur Tea Garden

Palampur is also known as the tea capital of Northwest India. Tea is just one factor that makes the place memorable. The place has abundant water, and its closeness to the mountains makes the city's climate more pleasant. The town has derived its name from the indigenous word, palum, which means a lot of water. Dr. Jameson established Palampur and introduced a tea plantation in the hill station. Private people own many tea gardens, and some plots are maintained by the Council of Scientific and Industrial Research. Large plantation estates can be observed on the way to Dharamshala, and Palampur Co-operative Tea Factory owns it. The Green tea plant will transport you to another heavenly world. Tourist love to click pictures of the tea garden.

  • Entry Fees: No Entry Fees
  • Timings: 10 am- 5 pm (All days of the week)
  • Distance From the Bus Stand: 5.7 kilo-meters

4. Baijnath Temple

It is one of the widely popular temples present in Kangra, Himachal Pradesh. Baijnath, or Vaidyanath, is an incarnation of Lord Shiva. Lord Shiva is also worshipped as the God of Healing. Two native merchants constructed the Baijnath Temple in 1204. The temple is 16 km far away from Palampur. It is where a person can attain untainted tranquillity and sterling peace. Here, Demon King Ravan and Lord Shiva are both worshipped. Ancient scriptures of Hindu tradition say that Lord Shiva has placed 12 jyotirlingas, pillars that emanated pure light and unencumbered power, across the country as a challenge to lord Brahma and lord Vishnu. The stunning temple has been constructed in the backdrop of the great Dhauladhar Himalayan Range.

The building architecture is based on a famous early medieval North Indian style called Nagara Style. This style was blended with other styles that were mainly popular in Orissa. Therefore, it is a unique mixture of an artistic blend to the State of Himachal Pradesh. Two entrances are there, North and South. The historical background of the temple is inscribed on the temple's wall, which suggests that before the building of the temple, a shrine of Lord Shiva was present.

Baijnath Temple is India's heritage, and elaborate security arrangements are established to protect the temple and devotees. Mahashivratri is celebrated in the temple with great enthusiasm, and all walks of people come to offer prayer on that day. So, the Shivratri is the best suited to visit the temple.

Ways to reach.

As no direct trains link to Baijnath, Amb Andaura is the nearest railway station present near Baijnath and is 65 kilometres from the temple, but all major cities are well connected with bus service and the nearest bus station is within walking distance of 4 minutes from the temple. Gaggal Airport is the nearest airport, 37 kilometres away from the Baijnath temple. Intra- city traveling is done through motor rikshaws or foot.

5. Dhauladhar National Park

This national parks also have Zoo that is called Gopalpur, which is located 13 kilometres away from Palampur city. The park is vibrant with flora and fauna, and it is a habitat for different types of red foxes, angora, leopards, angora rabbits, deer, sambhar, Asiatic lions, and black bears. The area has spread over 30 acres. The best suited time to visit the park is March and June.

6. Neugal Khad

Negual Khad serves as a famous picnic hotspot 3-4 kilometres from Palampur. From Neugal Khad, the panoramic view of the Dhauladhar Range is magnificent. It is a large stream of water that passes close to Palampur. One of the major tourist centres points apart from the river and river is an iron bridge. Those who are photogenic provide them with an excellent opportunity to capture the mighty bridge and surrounding nature.

Neugal park is situated at a short distance from Neugal Khad, and eateries and cafes are located there. Tourists can request to place a table outside, and with coffee, they can enjoy the magnificent view of the mountain. It is an excellent place for an outing with your family.

7. Birni Devi Temple, Palampur

Birni Devi Temple is a famous Shrine present in Palampur. Devotees could reach the temple via Gallu Langha, Lohna, and Upper Bundla. People have to do moderate trekking to reach the temple. The temple offers a majestic view of the Dhauladhar range and overlooks a gorgeous view of Kangra Valley. Tourists can see the whole Palampur by using binoculars. Baijnath town can also be seen from here.

8. Dorzong Monastic Institute Palampur

Dorzong Monastic Institute is the Institute for practicing and studying Buddha Dharma. The Institute is located on the foot of the Dhauladhar Mountains range. It is located 20 kilometres from the Dharamshala and close to Gopalpur Village. Eight Kyabje Dorzong Rinpoche established this Institute in a beautiful place engulfed with Cedar forests to protect the Drukpa Kagyu Community and Buddhism. It presents the opportunity to the students from Himalayan border hill areas like Kinnaur, Zangskar, Tibetans, and Lahoul-Spiti living in India to practice and study Buddha Dharma and to learn about their traditional culture. Several Buddhist prayers, ceremonies, and festivals are held, and medical assistance is offered to the needy local population. The Institute is identified as a yellow-roof monastery.

9. Sobha Singh Art Gallery Palampur

Sobha Singh Art Gallery is located in Andretta village, 14 kilometres south of Palampur. This art gallery is dedicated to the work of eminent artist Sobha Singh. Sobha Singh is a prominent painter who rose to fame with his masterpiece of Guru Nanak, and she is from the Sikh community. The art gallery presents some of the impeccable work of Sobha Singh that comprises of Hir-Ranjha (Popular Punjabi couples) and Sohni- Mahinval. It is a significant art centre with considerable value in Punjab's heritage. His paintings have dominated the Kangra School of art. The art gallery stores some remarkable busts and sculptures of prominent Punjabi icons like Prithiviraj Kapoor, M.S. Randhawa, and Nirmal Chandra and portraits of National freedom fighters like Lal Bhadur Shastri, Shaheed Bhagat Singh, Mahatma Gandhi, and Kartar Singh.

Best time to visit the Gallery

Best Suited Time to visit the art gallery: 9:30 am - 5:00 pm (All through the week)

10. Andretta Artist Village Palampur

This is an interesting place for art lovers. The Modern Andretta Artist place is popular for its Sir Sobha Singh Art Gallery, Andretta Pottery and Craft Society, Norah's Mud House, and Norah's Centre for Arts. Norah Richards was an Irish dramatist and writer. The Andretta Artist Village Palampur's heritage is no match to others. Andretta Artist Village has positioned itself as a connoisseur of arts in India. Tourism Advice Centre owns the Andretta Pottery and Craft Society.

Historical Background

Norah Richard, in mid-1930, shifted from Lahore to Andretta after her husband's demise. She started teaching drama to Punjabi students and received a fellowship from Punjabi University Patiala. Professor Jaidayal (pupil of Norah's husband) and B.C. Sanyal (doyen of Indian Modern) was invited by Norah. Other stalwarts like Prithviraj Kapoor, Sir Sobha Singh, and Sardar Gurucharan Singh brought studio art pottery to India and founded small pottery for summer purposes in Andretta. At the time of Norah Richards, various deliberations, discussions, and debates were held about art, philosophy of living, and drama in the composed village ambiance of Andretta. Several veterans from different fields of arts were actively involved in the discussion.
