Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU)

It is a government-funded major research university situated in New Delhi. The Institution is named after the country's first Prime Minister, Shri Jawaharlal Nehru, and was founded in 1969. It is one of the nation's top applied and social sciences research faculties. It is ranked number 2 in India by the NIRF (National Institutional Ranking Framework). It is one of the top demanding universities for postgraduates and research studies. University has received the status of "University of Excellence" by the University Grants Commission (UGC), and NAAC has given an "A" grade to the University. JNU. Provides 100 courses for Undergraduate, Post Graduate, and Doctoral degrees. JNU delivered courses in several disciplines like Mass Communication and Management, Computer Applications, Engineering, Arts, Law, Medical, and Science. JNU also gives Diplomas, Advanced diplomas, and certificates in language courses. Students must clear CUCET (Common University Entrance Test) for Undergraduate and Postgraduate courses. The body responsible for conducting CUCET is NTA (National Testing Agency).

Quick Information

  • The President of India has given the best university award to JNU.
  • According to QS World University Rankings, JNU comes in the top 10 Indian universities at the international level.
  • It is the first University to provide foreign language courses under the integrated MA Program.
  • The teacher-student ratio at Jawaharlal Nehru University is 1:10.
  • JNU is equipped with highly qualified, educated, and sensitive faculty members.

Background

The University came into existence by the act of parliament. The first chancellor of the University was G. Parthasarathy. Rector and Founder Chairman was Prof. Moonis Raza. M.C. Chagla, former minister of Education, placed the bill in the Rajya Sabha for the formation of Jawaharlal Nehru University on 1 September 1965. Lok Sabha cleared this bill on 16 November 1966, which became the JNU Act and came into effect on 22 April 1969.

The Indian School of International Studies merger with the Jawaharlal Nehru University occurred in June 1970. After the merger, the "Indian" word was replaced with School and became known as the School of International Studies of the JNU.

Results

It has odd and even semester criteria and is known as the winter and monsoon semester. Monsoon semester result is announced in December- January. The result of the Winter monsoon is made available in May- June. Results are uploaded on the JNU website. Students must fill in the required information, such as the program, registration number, and field of study.

Procedure to Check JNU Result

Steps are listed below to check the result;

  • Select submit button

Scholarships

University is known for granting financial aid to worthy and deserving students, and selection is conducted through various criteria. The followings are information related to scholarships:

  • Okita Memorial Fellowships: It is provided to those meritorious Indian students who have admitted themselves to the MA 2ND year program, and M.Phil. 2 Fellowships valuing INR 5000/month with annual support of INR 10,000 is awarded to chosen students.
  • Merit- Cum means Scholarships: Those students whose parent/guardian earns below INR 2.5 Lakh/annum.
  • Tendulkar Scholarship: This scholarship is given to two students from several Centres of the School of Social Science by rotation. The monetary value the student received is INR 400/month.
  • Ford Foundation Scholarships: A limited number of scholarships are present for students who are enrolled in the MA. The monetary benefits are INR 700/month and are provided purely on merit.
  • G Parthasarathi Endowment Fellowship: Students coming from ST/ SC/ Backward Tribe (Scheduled Tribes and Scheduled caste) are given an amount of INR 1000/ for a year.

General Questions

Q1- Where does JNU stand in NIRF Ranking?

Ans: NIRF has ranked JNU at number 2 in the University category newly published NIRF 2022.

Q2- I scored 60% in my BA. How do I get admission into the JNU?

Ans: For Candidates who want to take the MA program at the JNU. The candidate should appear for the JNE Entrance Exam, and they have received a minimum of 50% marks in graduation.

Q3- What method does JNU uses to offer a scholarship facility?

Ans: JNU gives students scholarships based on the applicant's financial status and merit.

Q4-What is the process to see semester result at JNU?

Ans: The University declares its result on its website. As soon as the results get declared, candidates can look for their results.

Q5-How do Foreign Students get admission at the JNU?

Ans: International students are also eligible for PG and UG courses at Jawahar Lal University. University separately conducts an admission process for International Students. International Students can avail application form from JNU official website.

Q6- How much are the fees for M.Sc at JNU?

Ans: Students must pay for M.Sc is Rs 2,622 and should visit the JNU portal for further information.

Q7- Does JNU holds direct admission or conduct entrance exams?

Ans: JNU holds Entrance Exams for the shortlisting candidates. Candidates are selected through marks scored in the Jawaharlal Nehru University Entrance Exam, and the viva voice National Testing Agency is responsible for conducting the examination.

Q8- How can we address our problems of payment failure queries to JNU?

Ans: Applicants are given the mail id [email protected] for redressal of any query. Students can also approach the University directly.

Q9- Can candidates change their exam centre after submitting the admission form?

Ans: Students are prohibited from changing their exam centre after submitting the final UG and PG forms. The exam centre will be allocated based on the preference given by the student while filling out the application form.
