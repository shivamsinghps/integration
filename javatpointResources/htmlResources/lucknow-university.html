Lucknow University

The University of Lucknow, popularly known as Lucknow University, is a state university. It is a public university that was established on 25 November 1920 in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh. It is one of the oldest government-owned higher education institutions in India. The motto of the University is Light and Learning.

Lucknow University has two campuses, one of its campuses is situated in Badshah Bagh, University Road area, which is its main campus; the second campus is located at Jankipuram. It has an academic affiliation with University Grant Commission. The Chancellor of Lucknow University is the Governor of Uttar Pradesh, and its Vice-Chancellor is Alok Kumar Rai.

The University of Lucknow is a residential, teaching, and affiliating university. More than 500 colleges and 17 institutions in and around the city are affiliated with Lucknow University. Colleges in four districts, Hardoi, Raebareli, Lakhimpur Kheri, and Sitapur, are also affiliated with Lucknow University.

History

Lucknow University was established by Raja Sir Mohammad Ali Mohammad Khan, Khan Bahadur, K.C.I.E of Mahmudabad. Sir Harcourt Butler, who was the Governor of United Provinces (now Uttar Pradesh), is quite famous for making Mohammad Khan's interest in every matter, particularly in education. Mohammad Khan through his article in a then-popular newspaper, The Pioneer, urged for the founding of a university in Lucknow. For this purpose, initially, a General Committee of educationists was formed, and a meeting was held at the governor's house. Harcourt Butler being the chairman of the committee, outlined the scheme for a new university.

After rounds of discussion, it was decided that the University of Lucknow would be a teaching and residential university on the lines of recommendations of Calcutta University Mission 1919. It was also decided that the university should consist of Science, Law, Medicine, and Faculty of Arts, including oriental studies. Whether to incorporate the Medical College or not was left open for further discussion for the time being. The Lieutenant Governor and Governor-General gave their consent to the Lucknow University Act, No. V 1920. There were three colleges that provided the nucleus for establishing the University of Lucknow:

  • King George's Medical College ( now popular as King George's Medical University)
  • Isabella Thoburn College
  • Canning College

These colleges bought their rich inheritance to the newborn university both intellectually and materially. The generous Talukdar of Oudh also added rupees thirty lakh endowment.

Campus

Sir Swinton Jacob designed the beautiful campus of Lucknow University in the Indo Saracenic style. For many experts, the design of the building was very impressive, elegant, and distinctive that it was sent for demonstration at the Exhibition in London. Walter Burley Griffin designed the university's Central Library, known as Tagore Library, and it is among the richest libraries in the country. It has more than five lakh books and fifty thousand journals. It also has ten thousand PhD dissertations. The library also has its own website and is active online. Its campus is 219.01 acres. It has 10 faculties and 51 departments of different subjects. Lucknow University offers 15 different undergraduate programs and 67 postgraduate programs.

There is a total of 16,365 students who are studying at this university. There are a total of 426 and 1926 teaching and non-teaching staff, respectively. There is a total of 17 centers/ institutes and 545 colleges that are associated with the university. It also provides residential facilities to its students, teachers, and non-teaching staff. The university has also created 3 independent bodies to maintain a healthy relationship between students, teachers, and non-teaching staff:

  • Lucknow University Student's Union
  • Lucknow University Teacher's Association
  • Lucknow University Worker's Union (Karmachari Parishad).

To create awareness among students about social service, the university regularly organizes National Service Scheme programs. Through its NCC wing, it also imparts military training to its students.

Further, the university campus offers various facilities to its students like a health and medical center, sports facilities, canteen, Yoga Center, counseling and guidance cell, central placement cells, happy thinking laboratory, coaching schemes, student hostel, and guest houses, etc.

Courses and Admission

Lucknow University offers various courses at UG and PG levels like B.Sc, BBA, BA, B.Com, BFA, B.PEd, B.VA, B.Tech, LLB, BA- LLB, MBA, M.SC, LLM, M.Com, MSW, MPEd, MA, MPH, etc. Along with these courses university also offers diploma courses and Ph.D. in different subjects. The procedure of admission includes both merit-based and entrance tests or aptitude tests followed by group discussion and personal interviews. University has released its admission form for the session 2022-2023. University also provides two scholarships to its students Chhatra Kalyan Scholarship and Karmyogi Scheme to students who need financial support.

Accreditation and Ranking

  • NAAC, BCI, and NCTE accredit the University of Lucknow.
  • The university is also recognized by UGC, ACU, AIU, AICTE, and DEC.
  • It was ranked 13 for Law in 2021 by India Today. It is the only state university of Uttar Pradesh that has been kept in the 150-200 rank band by NIRF. However, King George's Medical College has got 9th
  • Education World 2020 State ranking has ranked Lucknow University on 7. It is ranked 52 by Education World 2020 Indian ranking. Outlook 2021 for science has ranked university 105.
