TCL Company

Tata Communications Ltd. (previously known as Videsh Sanchar Nigam Ltd.) is a prominent supplier of a modern paradigm of communications services. Tata Communications, which has a strong position in developing countries, utilizes its enhanced services skills and domain experience throughout its worldwide network to provide customized services to multinational corporations and communications service companies. Telecommunications Company Tata Communications provides about 30% of the world's broadband routes and links companies to 80 percent of the world's cloud giants as well as four out of every five mobile users. With a worldwide network that includes the world's biggest fully owned subsea fiber backbone and an enterprise-class Internet protocol network that provides access to more than 190 nations and territories throughout the globe, the Company's capabilities are bolstered. In addition to the Bombay Stock Exchange and the National Stock Exchange of India, Tata Communications Limited is a publicly-traded company that trades on the Bombay Stock Exchange. It was established in 1997 and had its headquarters in Mumbai, India. It was started by a group of entrepreneurs.

History

Originally known as Videsh Sanchar Nigam Limited, the firm was established in the year 1986. Besides commencing its very own Land Earth Station (LES) near Pune in 1992, the corporation set the standard for a committed satellite-based business service that offers consumers superior speed, accurate data circuit design on a point-to-point basic principle through ground station smartly situated close the consumers' site. The corporation has also facilitated the emergence of mobile communications by commencing its own Land Earth Station (LES) near Pune in 2012. While it was still in the early stages of development, it launched the Inmarsat-C service, which allows for the exchange of signals via small portable terminals, as well as video conferencing services, which were hosted by studios located at the firm's core gateways in Mumbai, New Delhi, Calcutta, and Chennai.

In 1994, the business collaborated with British Telecom UK to establish the Concert Packet Service (CPS) for Indian consumers, which was then expanded to other countries. Founded in August 1995, the firm began offering Internet access services in Mumbai, where it continues to operate today. Chennai, New Delhi, Calcutta, Bangalore, and Pune are the Company's primary locations, and it is also the country's major commercial supplier of Internet access. In addition to Halisahar (Calcutta), the business establishes two Standard A' Intelsat earth stations, one in Korattur and the other in Korattur (Chennai).

Tata Tele Services (TTSL) was founded in 1996 to serve as the group's primary entry point into the telecommunications industry. Tata Communications and TMI (Tele Media International), a fully owned subsidiary of Telecommunications Italia, have collaborated to launch the Universal Connect services in the United States. The MOU was signed on December 12, 2010. Following in the footsteps of many other Asian telecom firms, Huawei has taken legal action against the Federal Communications Commission (FCC), the US telecom regulator, over its unilateral determination of tariffs for access to the United States telecommunications networks. Signed on March 31 was the official accord between the Vijaya Seva Nigam Limited and ICO Global Communications for the building of an ICO Spacecraft Access Node (SAN) in Chattarpur, New Delhi. When the corporation entered into an agreement of loyalty for access to aptitudes on Project Oxygen, an expanded fiber optic cable network, and then another bilateral agreement for participation in the South Africa and Far-East (SAFE) underwater optical fiber scheme in 1998, it was considered a watershed moment in its history. Together with Microsoft, the business has established a Microsoft download server in India (http://www.vsnl.net.in/msdownload), which may be found here. The server is the first of its kind in India and serves as a mirror site. On January 25, 1999, the business and the Department of Transportation (DoT) entered into a user agreement, according to which the firm was granted a non-exclusive license to provide Internet access service in six cities around the United States. VSNL was bought by the corporation in the year 2000, giving it a controlling share. Tata Indicom, the parent company of Tata Telecom Service, officially begins operations. VSNL, which was formerly a government-owned corporation, was privatized in February 2002 and became a subsidiary of the Tata Group.

Vulnerability Management Service (VMS) was introduced by Tata Communications in partnership with Qualys and is based on QualysGuard vulnerability management and compliance solutions, which are the industry's best. For enterprises, VMS offers a complete way for monitoring, mitigating, and managing risks that affect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets inside the corporate information architecture.

Southeast Asian destinations, including the Philippines, Malaysia, Taiwan, and Thailand, have been added to Tata Communications' worldwide MPLS (Multi-Protocol Label Switching) services.

It was reported in May 2014 that Neotel and Vodacom SA shareholders had reached a favorable agreement on the commercial structure and conditions necessary to go forward with Vodacom's acquisition of 100 percent of Neotel's shares which were valued at ZAR 7.0 billion at the time. Neotel links South Africa's largest cities to each other and to the rest of the globe through its Tier 1 network, which is part of Tata Communications.

On the subsurface, Tata has more than 500,000 kilometers of fiber, while on the ground, it has more than 210,000 kilometers (130,000 miles). The worldwide undersea fiber network has received a $1.19 billion investment from the corporation. Ethernet, SD-WAN systems, content delivery networks (CDNs), the internet, and Multiprotocol Label Switching (MPLS) are among the network services and software-defined network platforms it offers. They have a server farm, a private cloud, and public cloud management as well as co-location in 44 locations, totaling over 400 points of presence (PoPs).

It is a continuation of a long-standing partnership between Tata Communications and Microsoft that enables businesses throughout the globe to make use of cloud-based technologies. With the help of Tata Communications' IZO Private Connect solution, companies may have secure, borderless, and dependable access to Office 365 apps and data. Tata Communications presented a state-of-the-art cyber security response center in Dubai on September 9. 365 days a year, the Middle East region's clients will have access to 24/7 cyber security services provided by the cyber security response center.

Services

  • Ethernet Tata Communications provides Ethernet services that allow its clients to link their offices throughout the world at rates of up to 10 Gbit/s in either point-to-point or multiple access arrangements. Its Global Network offers a variety of bandwidth choices ranging from 2 MB to 10,000 MB utilizing Fast-E, Gig-E, and 10 Gig-E technologies. In addition to Global Dedicated Ethernet, the Company also offers External Ethernet Network to Network Interface (NENWI), Low Latency Dedicated Ethernet, and MPLS-over-Ethernet services.
  • International Private Line Customers that use Tata Communications Overseas Private Line for their international connections have the option of selecting from a variety of routing, uptime, and service configurations. It provides a connection for phone, video, and data services. It is based on its subsea cable network, which includes a fiber-optic ring as well as more than 20 consortium cables that span 300,000 kilometers (190,000 miles) and cross 200 nations and jurisdictions.
  • Content Delivery Network When Tata Communications purchased BitGravity, a CDN provider located in the United States, it was part of an effort to improve the Company's worldwide content delivery network (CDN). Because of this purchase, it can now provide its clients with a cloud-based content delivery solution that supports the distribution of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, videos, and high-definition live-streaming material. Once this service was implemented, it was integrated with additional support services such as security, satellite teleports, fiber-based video networks, and transcoding.
  • Data Centres Tata Communications has a data center network that is spread over 44 sites throughout the globe. Tata Communications' data centers are built to fulfill international requirements for redundancy and are immediately connected to the Company's worldwide IP network through fiber optics. Server, storage, and networking equipment are housed in its collocation facilities, which offer controlled power, cooling, and physical security to the equipment in question. A 74 percent stake in its data center business was sold to Singapore Technologies Telemedia in May 2016 to free up capital to invest in other areas of the firm. Tata Communications' data center, which spans 93,000 square meters and has more than 10,000 racks, is the largest in the world (1,000,000 square feet).
  • Unified Communications and Collaboration Tata Communications delivers a complete range of Unified Communication and Collaboration (UCC) solutions to clients all over the world via its subsidiary Tata Communications. In addition to providing global SIP Connect services, the firm also provides unified conference calling, unified communications as a service (UCS), and hosted contact center solutions.
  • Global SIP Connect Tata Communications unveiled Global SIP Connect, a white-label Unified Communications solution, on January 18, 2016, as part of its ongoing commitment to innovation. This solution was created to meet the voice needs of the Company's business clients while also increasing the Company's Unified Communications portfolio. In addition to allowing its service provider clients to consolidate and manage voice services, the SIP Trunking solution also allows them to supply all worldwide on-net and off-net phone services in order to connect their enterprises across borders.
  • Cloud Services It was in October 2014 that Tata Communications introduced its IZO platform, which the Company described as "a global platform for improved hybrid multi-cloud capabilities." The IZO Cloud Platform, developed by Tata Communications, brings together more than 20 cloud service providers, three large hyper-scale cloud platforms, and 43 data centers with which its customers may interact and share information.
  • Media Services Tata Communications offers a wide variety of services and solutions to businesses in all areas of the media and entertainment industry, including gaming, hotel, sports, leisure, education, news, music, cable, and broadcasting. Tata Communications is headquartered in Mumbai, India.
    Tata Communications announced the launch of their "Media Ecosystem" in April 2015. The ecosystem integrates conventional video contribution services with IP connections to provide a seamless user experience.
  • Mobility Services 3G/4G roaming is now available to Tata Communications customers thanks to the combination of the Company's Diameter Signalling Exchange (DSX) infrastructure and the IPX Connect service. A solution for LTE roaming migration is provided, with support for 2G, 3G, and 4G roaming, all supported in the same framework.
  • Internet of Things Tata Communications MOVE is an Internet of Things Connection Management Platform that provides worldwide mobile connectivity for a wide variety of linked devices, ranging from automobiles to consumer electronics, according to the Company. Using Tata Communications' worldwide mobile network operator contacts, the platform provides a technology-neutral approach that facilitates cellular-linked device connections all over the globe. A LoRa-based low-power wide-area network (LP-WAN) is managed by Tata Communications in India, and it is used to serve a variety of applications such as smart cities, smart manufacturing, Smart Grid, Smart Meter, and transportation.
  • Wholesale Voice Tata Communications provides worldwide wholesale voice transit and termination services, backed by a global network of data centers and call centers. It supports high-definition voice in its whole, meets with the GSMA's IPX requirements for direct transit, and is future-proof for operators considering the transition to Voice over LTE. Tata Communications offers its clients a comprehensive range of Voice Access Services that are available in 105 countries worldwide. In more than 90 countries, such as the United States, ITFS users may make toll-free calls from both fixed and mobile phones using a single number.
    The use of the UIFN allows them to give a single number that is reachable from more than 45 countries across the globe. Customers may also acquire local number ranges via the Company's Bulk Local Number Service, which is available to them.
  • Wifi+ Tata Communications announced Wi-Fi+, a cloud communications solution for mobile network operators, in June 2016, as part of its ongoing commitment to innovation. With high-quality, secure Wi-Fi, operators can give their consumers limitless calling, messaging, and data access. For seamless connection, it takes advantage of Tata Communications' Cloud RTC expertise as well as iPass's network of more than 21 million Wi-Fi hotspots spread across more than 100 countries.
    Announcing its cooperation with Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL) in January 2017, Tata Communications stated that as part of the deal, it would deliver 44 million Wi-Fi hotspots in much more than 100 nations encompassing railway systems and foreign aircraft. Tata Communications announced the availability of WiFi6 support in October of the following year as a consequence of its cooperation with its ally Cisco, which was formed in 2012.

Subsidiaries of Tata Communications Ltd

Business transformation, managed network operations, network outsourcing, and consultancy services are provided to telecommunications enterprises throughout the globe by Tata Communications Transformation Services (TCTS), which is a fully owned subsidiary of Tata Communications Ltd. Tata Communications Transformation Services (TCTS) is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Tata Communications Ltd. It was earlier known as Tata Communications Banking InfraSolutions before changing its name to Tata Communications. This firm, Tata Communications Payment Solutions Limited (TCPSL), is wholly owned by Tata Communications and operates as a subsidiary of the corporation. TCPSL is among India's extremely fast Payment Solutions providers, with an emphasis on providing end-to-end B2B and B2C payment solutions through a single point of contact. TCPSL is a banking and financial services company that focuses only on banking and financial services. It provides one-stop payment solutions to all types of banks, including public sector, private, multinational, and cooperative banks. TCPSL's White Label ATM solution, marketed under the brand name Indicash, is the Company's first venture into the business-to-consumer (B2C) market. TCPSL provides ATM services across the nation via the Indicash ATM network, with a particular emphasis on semi-urban and rural locations, as part of this service offering.

Conclusion

Tata Communication Ltd (previously known as Videsh Sanchar Nigam Ltd) is a well-known provider in the contemporary realm of communication technology. Tata Communications takes use of its worldwide network and leading position in developing regions to offer managed solutions to multinational corporations and communications service providers. Tata Communications' portfolio of services includes communications solutions and business transformation services that are available to both global and Indian organizations, as well as to individual Indian customers. Its worldwide network is connected to more than 240 countries and territories via more than 400 points of presence (PoPs).
