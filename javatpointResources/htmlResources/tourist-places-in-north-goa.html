The Gaud Saraswat Brahmins' favourite deity, Goddess Durga, is honoured by the dedication, and Vishnu and Shiva are connected via the help of the goddess Shantadurga. The temple's centrepiece is a gilded palki (palanquin) on which the god is wheeled during the Mahapanchami festival.

42. Corjuem Fort

In Goa's Aldona village, next to the River Mapusa, lies the inland fort known as Corjuem Fort; it was erected in 1705 by the Portuguese. One of Goa's most compact forts is known locally as Khojuem Fort.

Despite being a relatively undiscovered location, it has recently become more significant due to the expansive views it provides. It is also one of only two forts that, despite being encircled by Portuguese and Mughal emperors, is still standing and mostly undamaged.

According to history, the fort has seen many conflicts and has protected the town of Corjuem from several attacks. Despite being in ruins, it is nevertheless lovely in its current form and narrates the tales of a bygone period.

43. Houses of Goa Museum

In the Houses of Goa Museum, maybe find A beautiful collection of houses from Goa's Colonial era. An outstanding representation of a ship-like structure is the museum itself.

The museum, which Gerard de Cunha planned and built, has furnishings, things for the home, window and door frames, railings, ceramic tiles, and other remnants of the Goan way of life during the period when Portuguese architecture was famous.
