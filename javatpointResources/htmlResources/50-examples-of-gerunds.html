50 Examples Of Gerunds

A gerund is often made by adding '-ing'. It can be used in a variety of sentences. It can be utilized to refer to actions as well as to explain why something happened. It can be used in a variety of contexts.

Definition of Gerund

As defined by the Cambridge Dictionary, a gerund is "a word usually ends in 'ing' that is formed from a verb and employed like a noun."

Merriam-Webster Dictionary has a considerably more detailed definition. A gerund, as per them, is "the English verbal noun concluding in -ing that serves as a substantive while also displaying verbal qualities like as tense, voice, and the ability to receive adverbial qualifiers and regulate objects."

In a statement, a gerund is a verb which acts like a noun. In the case of the object, the subject where the names are employed in the sentence is used. A gerund is formed by appending the -ing tag to a verb root.

It is critical to distinguish between the gerund verb and the present participle. The "ing" tag is not always applied to gerunds, but it can also be applied to participles.

Verbs in the present participle indicate continuance.

Examples ;

Skating is my favorite sport.

Eating fish is a healthy food.

A gerund is defined as "a noun in the state of the present participle of a verb (that is, concluding in -ing)" by the Oxford Learner's Dictionary. A gerund is a noun created from a verb that refers to an activity, process, or state, as defined by the Collins Dictionary. Gerunds conclude with 'ing' in English.

Points to Consider When Forming a Gerund

A gerund can be formed from any verb. A gerund is often formed by the addition of '-ing' to the ending of the base verb.

  • If the verb concludes in an 'e,' omit the 'e' but then add '-ing' to the remainder of the verb.
  • However, there are certain verbs that keep the 'e'. When the verb finishes in 'ee,' this happens. In this scenario, the '-ing' suffix is simply attached to the last of the verb, as it is with other verbs.
  • For verbs concluding in 'ie', you will need to substitute the 'ie' with 'y' and then incorporate'-ing' to the remaining portion of the verb.
  • For monosyllabic verbs along the consonant-vowel-consonant structure, double the final consonant and add '-ing' to it.
  • Longer verbs with the same construction as a consonant-vowel-consonant must have the final consonant doubled simply if the last syllable is emphasized.

Gerund Phrases

Gerunds can also be combined with an object or a modifier to make gerund phrases.

Examine the following statements to determine which are gerunds and which are not.

  1. Rema is combing Helen's curls.
  2. Abhishek has not been working for two weeks.
  3. I will be interviewing my relatives today or tomorrow.
  4. We were viewing a film when they returned home.

The phrases' combing,' 'working, "interviewing,' and 'viewing' in the preceding sentences are also in the format of verb + ing, but they are present participles and signify different tenses.

  1. Jogging daily will enable you to stay in good shape.
  2. Following an eating plan will assist you in losing weight and maintaining your BMI.
  3. Wow! She is brilliant at solving sums.

The terms' jogging,' 'following,' and 'solving' in the preceding lines are gerunds. Furthermore, 'jogging' is a subject, 'following an eating plan' is a gerund phrase that functions as a noun, and 'solving sums' is an object.

How Should Gerunds Be Used in Sentences?

In a sentence, a gerund can be employed as a subject, a direct object, or an indirect object. It serves as a noun in this instance. It can also be used as a prepositional object. It can also be used as a present participle to denote various continuous tenses. However, in order for a verb to function as a gerund, it must first function as a noun.

What are the 5 Different Kinds of Gerunds?

Gerunds are classified into five groups based on their function in sentences.

Gerunds as Subjects

Gerunds can be subjects in statements using the S-V-O pattern. The word subject is a noun function that refers to the item that executes the action in the phrase, such as:

Walking is beneficial to the heart.

In this sentence, the grammatical subject is "walking. "

Direct Object Gerunds

Gerunds can also be guided by the verb in the objective case. Here's an example:

Example :

Jessica enjoys performing.

In this sentence, "performing" is the subject, "enjoys" is the verb, and "performing" is the direct object. Performing is what Jessica enjoys doing.

Gerunds as Indirect Object

A gerund might be an indirect object as it is a noun. Gerunds can also be action receivers.

Example of a Gerund :

Jessica made performing a preference.

Here, "performing" is a noun accompanying the verb "made." "Preference" is the sentence's direct object. The noun "performing" serves as an indirect object in the sentence.

Gerund as Subject Complement

A gerund can be a subject complement since it is a noun.

Example of a Gerund :

It was performing that made Jessica joyful.

Since it comes after the connecting word "was," "Jessica" is the sentence's subject complement. The subject is "it," and the gerund "performing" is the complement.

The Object of Preposition Gerunds

A gerund can also serve as a prepositional phrase's object. Prepositional objects are usually used with prepositions in sentences, for instance:

She was dismissed for bluffing.

Besides performing, Jessica likes partying.

The subject of the phrase is "Jessica," the verb is "likes," and the noun is "partying" (direct object). However, as a prepositional phrase starter, "besides performing" has been inserted into the sentence. The noun "performing" is the object of the preposition "besides."

The importance of infinitives and nouns is revealed here. Infinitives are constructions that you add to, and -ing is Gerund form. Gerund, as a word meaning, is a noun-verb combination. Noun-verb phrases are also called as verbs.
