Tourist Places in Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi, one of Madhya Pradesh's most picturesque and charming tourist locations, is great for seasonal family vacations and relaxing weekend trips. Pachmarhi is home to several magnificent and enchanting attractions, such as mysterious caves, ancient temples, and silvery waterfalls.

We've put up a list of attractions in Pachmarhi, Madhya Pradesh, so that you may depart with a suitcase full of fascinating memories and stunning photos.

1. Pandava Caves:

One of the most prominent sights in Pachmarhi is a series of five stone temples surrounded by naturally occurring gardens and crudely carved columns and pillars. These beautiful caverns are veiled in various legends from many historical periods. They were last hewn by Buddhist monks who utilized them for meditation since the natural way greatly helped them.

The most widely spread legend is that the Pandavas stayed in these caverns during their exile, becoming known as the Pandav Caves.

  • Time: Every day of the week, 8 AM to 6 PM.
  • Entry is not charged.
  • Destination: 461881 in Madhya Pradesh, Pachmarhi, India
  • 1 to 2 hours are ideally needed.
  • About 2 KM away from Pachmarhi Bus Stand.
  • Resorts in Bhopal provide everything you need to make a boring day into an exciting one if you choose to spend your days and evenings in a relaxed mood.

2. Bee Falls:

Imagine huge amounts of water falling gracefully from a height in the middle of a beautiful forest. One of the most well-known attractions in Pachmarhi is Bee Falls, and after you've descended the many stairs and arrived at the waterfall, it's impossible not to be in awe of it. Around the fall, you can discover little sellers sending snacks and tea. You may not only relax and enjoy the scenery, but you can also dive into the natural freshwater pool and enjoy a swim.

  • Height: 35 meters.
  • Location: Bee Fall Road, Pachmarhi, Madhya Pradesh, 461881, India.
  • Entry fee: INR 15 for each adult Indian.
  • Best Time to Go: any time of the year, go during the monsoon to experience a lot of rain and relax in the pool during the summer.

3. Satpura National Park:

This national park is located in Madhya Pradesh's Hoshangabad district and is spread out over the Satpura Hills. The Sanskrit word "Satpura," which means "seven hills," was given to the chain of hills in the central Indian highlands. It is a 524 square kilometre region that is a component of the Pachmarhi Biosphere Reserve.

The deep gorges and undiscovered jungles of Satpura National Park are home to many species, including Water Buffaloes, Leopards, Elephants, Barasingha, Honey Buzzards, and Malabar Pied Hornbills, Paradise Flycatchers, and the rare Rhesus monkeys.

  • Times: 7 AM to 6:30 PM, seven days a week.
  • Entry Fee: The cost for a permit is INR 1250, while the cost for a gypsy is INR 2100.
  • Destination: Hoshangabad, Madhya Pradesh, 461001, India; Satpura National Park.
  • It will take around 6 hours, or half a day, if going on a safari.
  • Two hundred ten kilometres from Pachmarhi Bus Stop.

4. Mahadeo Hills:

Mahadeo Hills is located on the northern border of the Narmada basin, separating it from the Wardha and Wainganga rivers. The hills generally run east to northeast, with the northern slope mild and the southern side steeply dropping down suddenly.

The plateaus and slopes of the hills were formed millions of years ago, during the Carboniferous period. Tourists may visit the holy temple of Lord Shiva at the cave temple Gupt Mahadev on Mahadeo Mountain, one of Pachmarhi's three highest locations. In addition to Gupt Mahadev, this temple also has Bade Mahadev, a shrine devoted to Lord Shiva. These temples are highly regarded and extensively worshipped. Your senses will be calmed by the easily visible view of nature and the surrounding greenery.

  • Time: 24 hours.
  • Free entry.
  • Destination: India's Satpura Range, Pachmarhi
  • Required time estimate: 2 to 3 hours.
  • About 10 KM away from Pachmarhi Bus Stand.
  • Treks close to Bhopal help everyone, whether they are nature lovers, adventurers, or just those searching for a break.

5. Handi Khoh:

The 300-foot-high sheer rock creates the best ravine in the nation. Handi Koh is surrounded by thick forests and flowing waters, and mythical tales are related. It is said to have previously been a large lake, but the wicked snake protecting it was killed by Lord Shiva's fury, and the lake was reduced to a little pot (Handi). It is one of the most beautiful sights in Pachmarhi to visit because of the beautiful nature and enjoyable journey.

  • Time: Every day of the week from 7 AM to 5 PM.
  • No entry fee is required.
  • Address: 461881 in Pachmarhi Cantt, Madhya Pradesh.
  • The recommended time needed: 1 to 2 hours.
  • Distance from Pachmarhi Bus Stand: 5 km.
  • The greatest way to travel is by road; find out where to go on a road trip close to Bhopal.

6. Dhupgarh:

Tourists will be impressed by the view from the top of this mountain, which is the tallest in the Satpura mountainous region. The summit provides views of the smaller Satpura mountains and Vindhya mountain and the top view of the water flowing.

This plateau has a heavenly appearance thanks to the rocky scarps, ravines, and clefts, yet this mountain is still less popular than other Pachmarhi tourist attractions. Some key attractions here are the Dhupgarh Fall and the tranquil surroundings.

  • Time: a full day.
  • Entry is not charged.
  • Location: Satpura Mountain Range, Pachmarhi, Madhya Pradesh, India.
  • Required time estimate: 2 to 3 hours.
  • About 10 KM away from Pachmarhi Bus Stand.
  • Amazingly, whether you are a couple that believes in the delicacy of romance or a pair looking for adventure and pleasure, Dhupgarh may create the ideal date for you.

7. Priyadarshini Point (Forsyth Point):

The name Forsyth Point derives from the year 1857 when Captain Forsyth made the discovery. It's amazing to see hills layered on top of one other and covered with clouds and mist. One of the few tourist attractions in Pachmarhi that can offer such breathtaking beauty is Priyadarshini Point.

Rajat Prapat seems to be a silver strip that plunges into the forests as it descends an about 350-foot-high cliff. To view the fall from one end of the mountain, you must first trek uphill for a short while. Once you reach the top, you will see the stunning Satpura hill range in the distance, with the fall being surrounded by greenery. Rajat Prapat is one of the wonderful places to visit in Pachmarhi.

9. Chauragarh Temple:

Chauragarh Temple is located at the top of Chauragarh, nestled between a lush valley and undulating trees. The respected shrine is known as Chota Mahadeo, devoted to Lord Shiva, and is another name for this temple. It is not simple to get there since one must climb a route of around 1250 steps, but if you do, you will undoubtedly be rewarded with the greatest surrounding views. Visit one of Pachmarhi's holy sites to capture beautiful scenery photos with your camera.

10. Jata Shankar Cave:

The cave has stone structures that resemble Sheshnag, a mythical Hindu serpent with one hundred heads, and it is believed that Lord Shiva protected himself there from Mahisasur, another mythical figure.

You will see a natural Shiva Lingam as you enter the cave, worshipped by tourists from all over the nation as it stands in the shadow of a large rock. One of the popular tourist destinations in Pachmarhi, Jata Shankar Cave, offers a singular experience for each tourist.

  • Monday to Saturday, 7 AM to 7 PM.
  • Entry is not charged.
  • Address: 461881 Jatashankar Rd, Pachmarhi, Madhya Pradesh
  • There is a time requirement of about 1.5 hours.
  • The distance from Pachmarhi Bus Stand is 5 kilometres.

11. Apsara Vihar Waterfall:

A picnic with friends and family would be the perfect activity at this beautiful waterfall and pool. It is a well-liked attraction in Pachmarhi that will have you fascinated with the site for hours. After a 10-minute downhill hike to get there, you may satisfy your hunger at the stalls selling snacks and tea. People may be seen diving and taking dips in the tiny pool, which gathers the chilled water from the fall. The people gave the pool the name Apsara Vihar because they frequently observed English ladies swimming there while the British ruled the country, who looked to them like fairies.

  • The height of Apsara Vihar Waterfall is 9 meters.
  • Location: Near Jaistambh, Hoshangabad District, Pachmarhi, Madhya Pradesh 461881, India.

12. Pachmarhi Catholic Church:

On Sunday mornings, you may go there for prayer and church. It's a tranquil haven. This church only opens on Sundays since there aren't many Catholic families living in or around Pachmarhi. The church's attractiveness is defined by its old-world aesthetics, tinted windows, neo-gothic altar, and far-off ceiling.

The structure was constructed in the 19th century, more than 150 years ago. While the exterior may appear a little run-down, the interior is just as artistically pleasing as it would have been hundreds of years before.

  • Time: It is only open for a few hours on Sundays during the day.

  • A suggested amount of time is one hour.

It feels amazing to have completed the ridge that leads to the mountain's opposite side. It is one of the Pachmarhi attractions that are most frequently suggested. Visitors will need sturdy footwear to climb a few stones and rocks. The cold air that flows through the tunnel mesmerizes people the most.

Visitors may enjoy some boating for a few hours on this beautiful little lake. The major attractions of this location, where visitors may enjoy snacks with a view of the lake, are the nearby restaurants and cafés. Vanashree vihar is the perfect getaway location for a breezy evening.

Near the lake, visitors may relax and spend quality time participating in a few sports like swinging, quad riding, horse and camel, etc.

Duchess fall is one of Pachmarhi's most stunning waterfalls and attracts huge crowds of visitors each year. After a ten-minute difficult downhill journey, an impressive view of a waterfall and the calming sound of the water roaring as it falls from a height may be yours.

Please take part in devouring a dish of Maggi by the falls; it will taste much more delicious. One of the many places to visit in Pachmarhi is this one, and you'll want to go back there again and again.

16. Gupt Mahadev:

One of the oldest Gupta temples and the biggest attraction of Pachmarhi is the cave temple known as Gupt Mahadev. It is a Shiva-honoring cave that is 40 feet long. Gupt Mahadev, also known as Chhota Mahadev, is situated 13 kilometres from the Pachmarhi Bus Station in the Mahadev hills. One of the oldest Gupta temples in Pachmarhi and a prominent attraction there is Gupt Mahadev, a cave temple. The 40-foot-long Gupt Mahadev cave is devoted to Lord Shiva. In this temple, people worship an avatar of Lord Ganesha and the Shivling.

17. Ramya Kund:

Ramya Kund is a natural lake in Pachmarhi, Madhya Pradesh. It is among the popular tourist attractions in Pachmarhi and is situated about a kilometre ahead of Reechgarh. Ramya Kund, also called Irene Pool, is a gorgeous natural pool and one of Pachmarhi's less-visited destinations. Despite all the movement, it is easy to see the pool's bottom, and the water doesn't reach higher than the waist in most areas. It's the ideal location for some leisurely time spent in the cold sea.

Visitors must first walk for about 10 minutes on flat land to get here, and then you must climb a steep path for 300 meters. The greatest time to view this gorgeous mountain pool is during or right after the rainy season. Honeycomb remnants, vibrant butterflies, and the verdant Satpura Hills surroundings may all be seen all around the route.

  • 6 kilometres from the Pachmarhi Bus Stand.
  • Times: 8 AM to 6 PM.
