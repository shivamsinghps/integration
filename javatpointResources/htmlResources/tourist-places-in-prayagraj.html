28 Best Tourist Places in Prayagraj

One of India's most revered Hindu towns, Prayagraj, also known as Illahabad, Allahabad, is a well-known tourist destination close to Kanpur. With Pra meaning "first" and Yag meaning "devotion," Prayag or Prayagraj is where Brahma made his first sacrifice after establishing the universe.

In addition, Prayag symbolizes Triveni Sangam, the confluence of the three holy rivers Yamuna, Ganga, and Saraswati. Millions of pilgrims from all over the world go to this location as part of Allahabad tour packages to witness the Maha Kumbh Mela, the most extraordinary Hindu celebration on the banks of the Sangam, which takes place here every twelve years.

The Vedic era is when Allahabad's beginnings may be found, which makes it the second-oldest city in India. According to legend, the town was formerly a part of the Kuru dynasty, which made it its capital when floods destroyed Hastinapur, and later gave it the name Kaushambi. When Muhammad of Ghor captured the city in 1193, it joined the Delhi Sultanate.

Following the Mughal assault in 1526, Emperor Akbar gave the city the name Illahabad, which means "Abode of God", and was inspired by the surroundings. The town was called Allahabad by the emperor Shah Jahan. The Maratha Empire took control of the city, which gave to the British East India Company in 1801.

Here we discuss Prayagraj's tourist places.

1. Triveni Sangam

Three of Hinduism's most important rivers come together at Triveni Sangam, a revered location in Allahabad. It is among the most well-known places in Allahabad and mostly holds some of the most significant religious fairs and festivals.

The rivers Yamuna, Ganga and Saraswati confluence is known as the Sangam. Due to the area's significance, Allahabad is sometimes called Sangam. Here, there are two super celebrations: the Kumbh Mela, which occurs once every 12 years, and the Ardh Kumbh, which occurs once every six years.

The Sangam has a massive crowd of tourists and residents during both events. Hindus bathe in the sacred water at the location where the rivers combine because they believe doing so washes them of their sins. Because of the Ganges and Yamuna rivers' distinctive colours and somewhat greener water, the Sangam is simple to identify.

  • Primary Focuses: Narrations of Hindu myths, Hindu pujas, and rituals; devotional music; holy baths; millions of people.

2. Khusro Bagh

Khusro Bagh is a nearby tomb building and a walled garden with Mughal architectural styles. Khusrau Mirza, Shah Begum, and Nithar Begum are just a few examples of the Mughal dynasty who are remembered by three skillfully carved ancient sandstone structures in Khusro Bagh. They were all related to Akbar's son Jahangir.

The three-tiered structure of Shah Begum's mausoleum is constructed in a manner comparable to Fatehpur Sikri. One of Prayagraj's most lovely tourist destinations is it. All three grave sites are decorated with chhatris, arabesque carvings, and conventional Mughal themes.

The greatest artists appointed by the emperor also created beautiful artworks on the domed roofs. Visitors should go to Khusro Bagh to learn more about Mughal history in India, mainly related to the famous place of Prayagraj. The tombs of Prince Khusro and Shah Begum, Jahangir's wife, are the main features.

  • Location: In Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh, Lukarganj
  • Times: 6 am until 9:30 pm

3. Anand Bhavan

The Indian National Congress' main headquarters was converted from the original Swaraj Bhavan in the 1930s, and it is now a well-liked tourist destination in Allahabad. As a result, Motilal Nehru was required to purchase another palace for himself and his family, which he named Anand Bhavan.

Motilal Nehru obtained European furnishings to decorate this house and convert it into a copy of the old Swaraj Bhavan. According to reports, the enormous home took more than ten years for renovation.

Many authentic artefacts are kept at this place, just like Swaraj Bhavan. The Indian government was given a house by Jawaharlal Nehru's daughter Indira in 1970.

  • Major Attractions:
    • Jawaharlal Nehru's valuables
    • Photographs of the Indian National Congress
    • Nehru's publications
    • The Nehru Planetarium
  • Location: Colonelganj, situated in front of Anand Bhawan, in the state of Uttar Pradesh
  • Entry Fee: 50 for foreign people and Rs. 10 for Indians
  • Time: From 9:30 am till 5:00 pm

4. Jawahar Planetarium

The Jawahar Planetarium, constructed in 1979 and located next to the Anand Bhavan, is the ideal combination of science and history. The planetarium also holds a large annual celebration called the Jawaharlal Nehru Memorial lecture and several programmes created to inform and fascinate visitors about the solar system and the beauties of space.

If you want to understand more about how planets move, visit one of its performances or attend the arranged seminars and specialist fairs. The stunning design of this planetarium will impress visitors as well.

  • Location:The location is Tagore Town in Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh.
  • Entry fee: for kids Rs. 20 and Rs. 35 for adults.
  • Time: 9:30 am till 5:00 pm

5. Allahabad Museum

The Allahabad Museum is one of India's most significant and impressive museums; it is a location of national significance and contains noteworthy items from modern history. This museum is used for study by academics, historians, archaeologists, and other experts.

The Allahabad Museum, which is served by a solar power system, was one of the first institutions in the world to use environmentally friendly, sustainable methods for producing electricity. Additionally, it features a collection of ancient rocks that serve as essential research materials in a museum dedicated to rock art.

Sir William Muir created it, and Jawaharlal Nehru and Madan Mohan Malviya later reopened it. It still includes certain documents and monuments that are significant to historians.

  • Major Attractions:
    • The Rock Gallery
    • Digital Eco-life, modern artworks
    • Artefacts from various historical eras in India.
  • Location:Chandrashekhar Azad Park is located on Kamla Nehru Road in Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh.
  • Entry fees: The fee of Rs. 20 per person for entry
  • Time: 10:30 a.m. until 4:45 p.m.

6. Allahabad Fort

This majestic structure, built in 1583, is located in Allahabad's Triveni Sangam and serves as a memorial to the city's renowned past. Allahabad's surroundings are believed to have significantly attracted Akbar, a well-known Mughal emperor. Due to this, he decided to build a giant fort. The Akshayavat, a holy tree to the local Hindus, was protected by this fort.

The Allahabad fort is maintained for visitors by the Archaeological Survey of India, which has identified it as a monument of great importance. Several key inscriptions can be seen in the fort's surroundings, highlighting the Mughal era's architectural excellence.

  • The area's main attractions are:
    • The Yamuna's picturesque appearance.
    • A boat ride.
    • The Patalpuri Temple.
    • The Akshayavat Tree.
    • Ashoka Pillar.
    • The Hindu-Muslim architectural style.
  • Location: Allahabad Fort, Prayagraj in Uttar Pradesh
  • Time:From 9:44 am until 6 pm.

7. Chandrashekhar Azad Park

Chandrashekhar Azad Park, an outstanding public park in Allahabad, was constructed throughout a massive area of 133 acres. During the East India Company's control, this enormous park was also referred to as the Alfred Park or the Company Bagh. It was created in 1870 to remember a visit of Prince Alfred to Prayagraj city.

The park was named Chandrashekhar Azad Park in 1931 to honour the national freedom fighter. Now, locals and guests enjoy spending time on the vast green grass.

  • The main features are:
    • The thornhill mayne memorial
    • The victoria memorial
    • The prayag sangit samiti (musical school)
    • The george v and victoria statue.
  • Location:Chandrashekhar Azad Park is located in Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh.
  • Fee for entry: 5
  • Time: 4:00 am until 8:00 pm

8. All Saints Cathedral

India's Prayagraj has a famous cathedral called the All Saints Cathedral, and it was constructed as a part of the Gothic-style structure in India under British rule. Sir William Emerson was tasked with designing and architecture for this church, and he started construction in 1887.

It can seat more than 400 people and is situated where the MG Marg and the SN Marg cross. It is a holy site to visit because of the church's lush surrounds, which are suggestive of the divine power that the architecture contains.

  • The main draws are:
    • Gothic-style buildings
    • Stone sculptures
    • Memorials honouring British citizens who passed away while the British ruled the country.
  • Location: Civil Lines and Sarojini Naidu Marg, Prayagraj in Uttar Pradesh

9. Swaraj Bhavan

The Nehru family lived in Swaraj Bhavan, a huge home that formerly belonged to Motilal Nehru, till 1930. Images of the Indian freedom struggle, including those of leaders like Mahatma Gandhi, Azad, and the Nehrus, are presented in the museum.

Visitors may take in the displays in the 42 rooms or unwind in the vast green spaces. There was also an underground room used by the leaders of the Indian freedom fighters to fight for meetings when they could not hold public demonstrations and seminars due to British repression.

  • Location: In Uttar Pradesh, Swaraj Bhawan Road, Tagore Town, Prayagraj
  • Entry Fee: 5 rupees for Entrance
  • Time: The hours are 9:30 am until 5:30 pm.

10. Akshaayavat

One of Allahabad's most critical religious destinations is the Akshaayavat. Legend states that Sage Markandeya once told Lord Vishnu or Narayana to show the world his powers.

Hindus all around the world adore it because they believe it is durable. Even today, visitors visiting the tree must gain special authorization from the fort officials; before years, the British took command of the Allahabad Fort and restricted access to the fort's sensitive areas. The tree is, nevertheless, accessible to the public and is worshipped by everybody on the day of the Kumbh Mela each year.

  • Location: Uttar Pradesh's Allahabad Fort at Prayagraj.
  • Time:Open at all times.

11. New Yamuna Bridge

The New Yamuna bridge was constructed in 2004 to decrease traffic on Allahabad's old Naini bridge. Over the Yamuna river built a bridge, and it continues to be busy right now, most hours throughout the day. Private manufacturers constructed the bridge; however, maintaining is the responsibility of the city's government.

As you cross the bridge and look down at the Yamuna river, it provides breathtaking views. The fantastic bridge construction is also an excellent subject for photos.

12. Sumitranandan Pant Park

One of the major attractions in the city of children is the Sumitranandan Pant Park, also known as the Hathi Park. Children aged five and under can enjoy a massive number of little rides. This lovely park has swings, rides, and stalls that provide food and drinks.

The park was declared after a famous Indian poet, Sumitranandan Pant, and entered by a white and green gateway. All visitors to this location enjoy the expansive greenery and the continuous singing of birds over the green trees. This Sumitranandan park is also ideal for art enthusiasts because of its mural paintings and wall art, including images of animals and jungles.

  • Location: In Uttar Pradesh, Old Katra, Kamla Nehru Road, Prayagraj
  • Entry fee: 5 for kids and Rs. 10 for adults
  • The time is 8 am to 8 pm.

13. Public Library

The Allahabad public library, previously known as the Thornhill Mayne Memorial, is the ideal location for anybody who enjoys reading or is a big reader. It is the most extensive library in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh and is located in the well-known Alfred Park.

The library has 125000 publications, 40 magazines, and many newspapers available to the public upon request. Visit the library to take in the stunning Scottish Baronial-style structure.

Richard Roskell Bayne designed it, and it contains solid pillars and turrets throughout. The beautiful structures make you think of a fantasy world, and the vast greenery will comfort your spirit.

  • Significant attractions:
    • Books
    • Journals
    • Manuscripts
    • Newspapers in several languages
  • Location:Chandra Shekhar Azad Park is located in Uttar Pradesh.
  • Time:It opens at eight in the morning.

14. Bargad Ghat

In Allahabad, the area known as Bargad Ghat is well-known for its old Hanuman temple. The red structure of the temple is decorated with a vast entrance coated in carvings, and it also gives views of the neighbouring pond.

On the banks of the Yamuna, there is a developed region. Visitors may visit this lovely Indian city to shop, eat, or take a nice walk.

  • Location: Prayagraj in Uttar Pradesh, Bargad Ghat Rd
  • Timings: Always open.

15. Minto Park

In Allahabad, there is a public park called Minto Park, also called the Madan Mohan Malviya Park. One of Allahabad's most historical importance attractions is this park, where Earl Canning read out Queen Victoria's declaration in 1858. This historic decision appointed the Crown of Britain as the new ruler of India, replacing the British East India Company.

During your visit to this park, remember the past while taking pictures of the enormous green areas. Earl Minto, who later gave the park its name, erected a four-headed lion memorial statue made of stone in this park in 1910.

  • Location: The Yamuna River's banks in southern Allahabad
  • Time: Open at all times.

16. Sri Mankameshwar Mandir

The Mankameshwar Temple, a historic temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and Parvati, is one of the world's oldest temples in Allahabad. Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati may be depicted in stunning artworks.

It is one of Allahabad's most visited attractions because, on Mondays, the temple attracts huge crowds of worshippers who bring milk, flowers, and a variety of other foods to the Lord and the Goddess.

The carvings on the walls and various pillars of the temple's construction are remarkable, and significant colouring is used on a few sculptures. The temple's four walls are covered with paintings that increase its visual appeal and represent stories from Hindu mythology.

  • Location: Kydgang, Fort Road, Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh
  • Time: 5:00 am to 10:00 pm

17. Bharadwaj Ashram

In Prayagraj, there is a temple and Ashram called the Bharadwaj Ashram. This area is dedicated to the mythology of Bhardwaj and the process through which he presented the Ganges to Earth. A mythological legend indicates that once Ganga came to Earth, Bharadwaj appealed to Lord Shiva to collect Ganga's water before she reached the earth and stop massive floods.

In the past, the river had covered the Ashram's steps; however, the Sangam Area is now the only place where the river is present. Visit this location to learn more about Indian mythology and see this peaceful but beautiful ashrama. It is among Allahabad's top attractions.

  • Address: Shiv Mandir Bharadwaj Ashram, Colonelganj, Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh
  • Time: 9 am opening

18. Shankar Viman Mandapam

A sacred temple honouring the Hindu deity Shiva, also known as Shakara, is the Shankar Viman mandapam, as its names indicate. It is located north of the Triveni Sangam. Thousands of worshippers visited the shrine, especially during the Kumbh Mela.

Adi Sankaracharya, a well-known saint and devotee of Lord Shiva, is supposed to have inspired the creation of the temple's four stories. The Vimana is the temple's primary topic, and the temple's structure is characteristic of South India. It has become a popular tourist spot in Allahabad and is promoted by individuals looking for spiritual tranquillity.

  • Location:Triveni Road, Beni Bandh, Himmat Ganj, Daraganj, and Prayagraj in Uttar Pradesh are the locations.
  • Entry Fees:Ten rupees are required for entry.
  • Time: 6 am till 1 pm and 4 pm until 8 pm.

19. Clock Tower

The Allahabad Clock Tower, a well-known tourist destination in Allahabad built in 1913, is also called by locals as the Chowk Ghantaghar. It is one of the oldest architectural styles in the nation and represents the beautiful Mughal architectural style, which is controlled over India. Tourists visit this tower because it is situated at Chowk, one of the city's oldest, most populous, and most active marketplaces.

  • Location:Ghantaghar Chowk is in Malviya Nagar, Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh.
  • Timings: Always open.

20. Nag Vasuki Mandir

The Bhagavata Purana and other early Hindu writings claim that Hindus have long worshipped snakes in various forms. The Hindu God Lord Krishna connected with the Vasuki Nag during his lifetime. Vasuki Nag allegedly picked up Krishna's ball as Krishna and his buddies played, according to mythology.

Krishna jumped into the river where the Vasuki Nag dwelt and eventually discovered the ball. On top of the crown of the five-headed serpent, Krishna triumphed after a terrible struggle. The Vasuki Nag is worshipped in the Nagvasuki temple, and devotees from around the nation adore the idol.

The temple occasionally becomes a site of charm and celebration due to the several elaborate events it performs throughout the year. Many worshippers come here to worship snakes during Nag Panchami every year. Incomparable glimpses of the Ganges River are another highlight of the temple.

  • Location: Daraganj Ghat in Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh, next to Rameshwar Mandir
  • Time: 10:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.

21. Iskcon Temple

One of the most excellent locations in Allahabad is the Iskcon Temple, and it attracts a massive number of tourists each year. This well-known temple is situated next to the water tank in Baluaghat, and This stunning temple is surrounded by farmland, numerous guest rooms, and a shopping store.

In addition, it includes a lovely garden. There is also a Mahaprasad counter to collect God's offerings.

22. Alopi Devi Temple

The temple of Alopi Devi is distinctive. Alopi Bagh is home to a well-known temple, and no deity or goddess sculptures can be seen at this temple. Instead, it features a wooden chariot.

The devotion of this chariot is attended by thousands of Lord Shiva worshippers from all across India. A fascinating legendary tale surrounds this chariot.

  • The main attractions are:
    • Sangam

    • The "Doli" wooden chariot.

23. Lalita Devi Temple

Allahabad's most famous temple is the Lalita Devi Temple in Sitapur. The well-known shrine is located on the Yamuna River's Lalita Ghat. Lalita Devi is an incarnation of Goddess Sati, also known as Shakti Peeth.

24. Fun Gaon Water Park

The Fun Gaon Water Park is a favourite destination for partygoers and advantage hunters. The renowned park is located in Allahabad's Quadirpur. The park offers several pools, slides, and rides to entertain children and young people. Inside the park, there is also a locker area and changing facilities.

25. Bade Hunauman Temple

One other holy site in Allahabad is the Bade Hanuman Temple, and the Fort of Allahabad is nearby. A statue of Lord Hanuman is located within the temple. You must see God underground, which is the temple's unique characteristic. Hindu pilgrims love to see this fantastic temple, and the Lord Hanuman statue and underground temple are the main draws.

26. Ashoka Pillar

King Ashoka of the Mauryas built the magnificent Ashoka Pillar. At a location named Kaushambi made it. But the Mughal emperors later transferred it to Allahabad, which is currently situated in front of the historic Allahabad Fort.

Numerous inscriptions of great Indian kings may be found here, and these kings are examples of Ashoka, Samudra Gupta, Jahangir, and others.

The primary draws are the inscriptions of India's historical kings.

27. Allahabad University

Allahabad University is one of India's most prestigious educational institutes. The institution is located in Allahabad's Old Katra and is connected to several state colleges.

As an "Institution of National Importance," this university also earned recognition. The phrase "Oxford of the East" is also used to represent this university.

28. Nandan Kanan Water Retreat

Among the famous water parks in Allahabad is Nandan Kanan Water Retreat. This lovely park is a little away from the city's bustle and is situated in a region known as Sahson. It offers lots of enjoyment for you, your family, and your friends.

This park offers more than just rides and water slides; it also includes lovely cabins. These cottages have room for 6 to 8 people, and these cabins provide meals you can purchase from the on-site snack shop and eat there.

Ideal Time to Go to Prayagraj

The Best Time to Visit Prayagraj is from October to March, and Prayagraj's Top Period runs from November to February.

How to reach

1. By air

There is an airport in Allahabad, and the distance to the central city is only 12 km. The other close-by airports are located at Kanpur, Varanasi, and Lucknow.

2. By rail

Several trains link Allahabad Junction with destinations like Delhi, Mumbai, Lucknow, Bhopal, Kolkata, and Jaipur, making it one of the essential connectors in Northern India. Allahabad's four principal railway stations are City Station at Rambagh, Allahabad Station, Daraganj Station, and Prayag Station.

3. By road

Good connectivity to other cities, including Gorakhpur, Varanasi, Jhansi, and others, is provided by the Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation. From Allahabad, buses are available to Lucknow, Varanasi, Kanpur, Gorakhpur, and other destinations.
