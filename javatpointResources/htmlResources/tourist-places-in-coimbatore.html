Tourist Places in Coimbatore

Coimbatore, commonly known as 'Kovai,' is a significant metropolitan city in Tamil Nadu. It is located on the Noyyal River's banks, surrounded by the Western Ghats. Coimbatore is Tamil Nadu's second-largest city, behind Chennai. It is often regarded as the "Manchester of South India" and is nestled in the foothills of the Nilgiris. It is a popular tourist destination in India. Several temples and pilgrimage sites represent the Jain and Buddhist religions. The renowned hill station in South India is surrounded by nature and offers amazing views from all sides. Coimbatore has a lot to offer regarding jungles, valleys, waterfalls, and running rivers.

The Adiyogi Shiva Statue is 112 feet tall and situated in Coimbatore. This architectural wonder has a place in the Guinness Book of World Records, is a famous tourist site in Coimbatore, and symbolizes self-transformation and inner calm. Camping and hiking are popular activities in Coimbatore, so don't miss out on some of the city's most interesting trekking routes, such as Dhoni Hills, Villari Mala, Perumal Peak, and Thalaiyar Falls Trek.

1. Perur Pateeswarar Temple

Perur Pateshwar Temple is a Shiva-dedicated Hindu temple in the western area of Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. For Lord Pateshwar's followers and admirers who go from around the world to pray, the large temple possesses religious, cultural, and historical importance. Karikala Chola established the temple in the second century AD. The temple also has ancient artifacts, paintings, sculptures, and carvings, which draw art fans. Perur Pateshwar Temple is also one of Coimbatore's biggest temples.

The statue of Lord Pateshwar may be seen with a mark on his head provided by Kamadhenu, and his footprints are also kept within the shrine. Many devotees feel that visiting the temple gives them great energy and helps restore their lives. The temple annually holds a classical dance week, usually including Bharatanatyam. It is one of Coimbatore's most popular tourist attractions. Tourists must travel from Coimbatore through the Chokkampudur route to access the temple.

2. Adiyogi Shiva Statue

The Adiyogi Shiva Monument is a 112-foot-tall steel statue of Shiva in Thirunamam in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. Guinness World Records says it is the world's "Largest Bust Sculpture." It is next to the Isha Yoga Complex in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. On February 24, 2017, Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated the statue modeled by Sadhguru Jaggi Vasudev, founder of the Isha Foundation. The massive statue took two years to design, but it was finished in approximately eight months.

The idol was made to inspire and promote yoga, and the name "Adiyogi" means "First Yogi" because Lord Shiva is regarded as the founder of yoga. People of all castes and religions are invited and encouraged to participate in the numerous cultural events and games hosted under the gigantic statue's shadow. You may take a state bus from the Gandhipuram bus terminal to the Dhyanalinga temple. You may stroll to the idol from Dhyanalinga Temple, which is only a 7-minute walk away.

3. Marudamalai Temple

Marudamalai Temple is a well-known 12th-century hill temple in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. The temple is located on a hill in the Western Ghats, about 12 kilometers from Coimbatore. The temple is dedicated to Lord Murugan and is said to be his seventh home. It was constructed during the Sangam era by Tamil emperors, as seen in Purananuru. Unlike the other Murugan temples on the hills, this Murugan temple is placed on the east side of the hill.

The temple is situated on a 600-foot granite hill close to Coimbatore. Many water sources around the temple, including Maruda Theertham and Snake Charmer's spring, are thought to have medical characteristics. The primary aspect of this hill is that it grows significant medicinal plants widely employed in the production of Ayurvedic medicine. It is one of Coimbatore's most popular tourist attractions.

4. Kodiveri Dam

A lovely spot for a picnic and a well-liked tourist site in the region is the Kodiveri Dam. The Dam not only acts as a tourist attraction but also functions as an irrigation system for around 25000 hectares of land. At the base of the Dam, a park has been created with various diversions, rides, and games for visitors' enjoyment. Coral Rovers also live in the region and catch and sell fresh fish. You may sample freshly cooked fish here if you desire.

5. Eachanari Vinayagar Temple

Harahnari Vinayagar Temple is a Hindu temple in the village of Itchanari near Coimbatore dedicated to the Hindu deity Vinayagar. The temple is roughly 12 kilometers from Coimbatore. The temple was constructed in the Dravidian architectural style, which may be seen across South India. According to tradition, many Hindu deities, holy animals, and other themes are etched on them. The idol stands 6 feet tall and has a diameter of 3 feet. It is one of the greatest tourist attractions in Coimbatore. On Ganesh Chaturthi, the Echanari Vinayagar Temple commemorates the deity's birth anniversary with a great ceremony.

The temple's first holy consecration, or Kumbhabhishek, occurred in 1977, and the second and final in 1990. The god was gold-plated in 2004 to give it a more majestic aspect. In the south, the winter months of November to February are colder and great for travel. Coimbatore is barely 12 kilometers away and takes less than half an hour to get there. You may hire a cab or a car to transport you to the temple on Coimbatore Pollachi Road.

6. VOC Park and Zoo

VOC Park is a garden and amusement park in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. The park has a children's play area, an aquarium, and a Jurassic Park where both children and adults may have fun. The park and zoo are popular picnic spots for families who wish to give their children a good time while also introducing them to the animal world. Coimbatore Municipal Corporation manages the park, named after freedom fighter V O Chidambaram Pillai.

Several fairs and yearly events are organized during Independence Day and Republic Day ceremonies. The amusement park includes an aquarium and a zoological park with 106 mammals, over 300 birds, and around 55 reptiles, attracting not only animal or nature fans but also students and researchers who want to observe the species in their natural environment. VOC Park and Zoo are located near ATT Colony in Gopalapuram, about 4 kilometers from the city center. The most practical modes of transit are buses and taxis.

7. Monkey Falls

Monkey Falls is a natural waterfall located in the upper valley of the Pollachi-Valparai route in the Annamalai Hills range in Coimbatore and is 65-kilometer away. Monkey Falls is around 30 kilometers from Pollachi. Monkey Falls, surrounded by lush evergreen forest and the Anamalai Hills, is one of the most appealing spots in Coimbatore for anyone looking to wander in the surreal environment. It is one of the greatest tourist attractions in Coimbatore.

Trekking at Monkey Falls will exceed all of your expectations for an adventure. Permission from the Tamil Nadu Forest Department is required one night before the trip date. These springs are suitable for bathing. However, remember that every tourist should be mindful of the monkeys in the area near Monkey Falls. Gandhipuram Town Bus Stand is the nearest to the falls, at a distance of 71 kilometers and around one and a half hours by car.

8. Sree Ayyappan Temple

Sri Ayyappan Temple is a significant temple devoted to Lord Sri Ayyappa, located in the center of Tamil Nadu's Coimbatore city. The temple is famed for its cleanliness and sanctity, and it also has temples dedicated to various deities, including Maha Vishnu, Lord Shiva, Lord Vinayagar, Sri Murugan, and Durga. The Ayyappan temple is created in ancient architectural styles and is a must-see for architecture enthusiasts, history lovers, and religious believers.

This temple is among the most important sights to see in Coimbatore. This temple is known as the "Second Sabarimala Temple" because of its style, construction, and worship. From Gandhipuram, the heart of Coimbatore is just one kilometer. The temple is so easily reachable from any location in Coimbatore by bus or private automobile. The fact that it is in the center of the city and attracts tourists and locals to visit the temple at least once is another factor in its appeal.

9. Kovai Kondattam

An eco-friendly theme park that is perfect for having hours of fun with friends and family is Kodai Kondattam. Kovai Kondattam is a water theme park located on Siruvani Main Road, 8.5 kilometers from Kondattam town and 2 kilometers from Perur Temple. The park, a venture by famous Tamil star Vijay, attracts many people because of its various water and other rides.

Rain dance, wave pool, dashing automobiles, and rock climbing are some of the most well-known inhabitants here. There are several ways to enjoy the fun at this water park. The entry fee of the theme park does not include access to the park's dressing rooms. Separate lockers for men and women are offered for a price. Because the park does not rent swimsuits, it is advised that you bring your costumes, etc. You may get to this location by taking public transportation. This park is roughly 9 kilometers from the city.

10. Vaidehi Falls

Visitors visiting Coimbatore should also pay a visit to the Vaidehi Waterfalls. Vaidehi Falls is well-known for its beauty. The waterfall has attracted many tourists due to its natural beauty and appeal. This water park offers a variety of ways to have fun. By visiting this location, you may sit quietly and shoot several photographs of the waterfall.

11. Siruvani Falls and Dam

One of the most stunning locations in Coimbatore within 200 kilometers is Siruvani Waterfalls, often referred to as Kovai Kutralam Falls. It is roughly 37 kilometers from Coimbatore. Throughout the year, travelers flock to this location to observe the wildlife and nature. While you're here, you may go bird watching, on an exciting climb through the Nilgiri highlands, or on a safari in the forest with your friends. Apart from that, Siruvani Dam is another spot to gratify your spirit.

How to Reach Coimbatore

Coimbatore has its airport and railway station. Visitors can directly land in the city.

  • By Flight: Coimbatore International Airport is located 11 kilometers from the city center. It has excellent connections to major Indian cities. Major airlines such as Air Costa, Air India, Indigo, SpiceJet, and Jet Airways offer regular flights from major Indian cities such as Bangalore, Tirupati, Hyderabad, Jaipur, and Vijayawada. In addition, Air Arabia and Silk Airways fly from Sharjah and Singapore. You can take a cab or a local bus from the airport to your location.
  • By Train: The primary railway station in Coimbatore is Coimbatore Junction, which is linked by train to major Indian cities and towns. This station is served by trains like the Trivandrum-Nizamuddin Express, Kongu Express, Kanyakumari Bangalore Express, Cheran SF Express, and Kovai Express. After arriving at the railway station, you may take a cab or bus to the main city.
  • By Bus: Coimbatore is easily accessible from places like Trivandrum, Cochin, and Chennai. The NH 67 and NH 209 routes take roughly three hours to reach Coimbatore. State buses and private buses travel to Coimbatore from Tamil Nadu and Kerala. Apart from that, Volvo and air-conditioned buses may take you to Coimbatore from these states.

Best Time to Visit Coimbatore

Coimbatore is best visited between September and March. During this season, the temperature is not extremely high, and travelers can enjoy their time in the city. It rains here in October and November, causing fewer visitors to visit, and the temperature here remains 15 ° C between December and March. These months are ideal for sightseeing in and around Coimbatore. Bring an umbrella and woolen bags if you travel to Coimbatore within this time frame.
