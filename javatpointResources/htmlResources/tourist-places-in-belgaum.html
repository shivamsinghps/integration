11 Best Tourist Places in Belgaum

Belagavi is another name for Belgaum. Belgaum is tucked away in Karnataka's northern foothills of the Western Ghats. The city, which serves as Karnataka's second capital, is home to numerous religious landmarks that can help you to reconnect with your spiritual side. Belgaum's breathtaking waterfalls and foggy hills will attract you to come again because of its natural beauty. This emerald-green city is home to a number of well-known attractions, including the Jamboti and Amboli Hills, Vajrapoha Falls, Gokak Falls, Kapileshwar Temple, Saundatti, and others. These places, along with other most visited places, are mentioned below.

1. Kapileshwar

kapileshwar temple is found in Belgaum City. It is known as the southern Kashi. This shrine is devoted to Lord Shiva. The Shahpur Flyover is next to the temple. This Flyover links Belgaum city to Shahpur. According to popular belief, visiting and seeing the 12 Jyotirlinga distributed across India cannot be completed unless it is started from the Kapileshwar temple.

An inscription found in the Kamal Basti indicates that this temple is one of the oldest in the state, going back to the year 1000 AD. Devotees are given access to the go near Shivalinga just like in Kashi. The temple's compound contains a group of Peepal, Ficus, and Banyan trees. The temple is open for worshippers from 5:00 AM to 9:00 PM daily. During the Mahashivaratri, a huge influx of worshippers travels to the Kapileshwar Temple.

2. Gokak Falls

Gokak Falls is renowned for its gorgeously tumultuous waters. The Gokak Falls are around 6 kilometres from the Gokak town. There is a 170-meter-long suspension bridge with ropes over the massive waterfall. This bridge allows tourists to stroll directly above the fall and experience the wind being driven by the river. The falls are approximately 60 kilometres from Belgaum city. The name "Gokak Falls" comes from the nearby Goki river. The waterfall, which rises 52 metres from the Ghataprabha river & goes a great distance before entering the Gokak stony belt, originates from this river.

This waterfall fall forms the shape of a horseshoe on the summit when it fall and colloids in the Gokak valley. Aside from the water's colour and 177-meter breadth, the Gokak Falls is similar in many ways to Niagara Falls in the United States due to its general features. Visitors come here to spend a few hours enjoying the beauty of the area near the waterfall.

3. Ramakrishna Mission Ashram

It is a well-known tourist destination in Belgaum. Ramakrishna Mission Ashram is a beautiful and tranquil location that offers tourists a break from the hustle and bustle of Belgaum. It is situated on the ground of Belgaum Fort. Swami Vivekananda spent nine days at this residence during his 1892 visit to Belgaum, which was given to the Ramakrishna Mission for spiritual and humanitarian reasons.

The ashram was constructed in 2001. Currently, the ashram features a Sharada Mantapa, which houses the kitchen, dining area, and monks' quarters. To educate, motivate, and provide calmness to guests, Ramakrishna Mission Ashram organizes a range of welfare events, talks, daily bhajans, and meditation classes.

4. Kamal Basadi

A well-known tourist destination in Belgaum is Kamal Basti. Kamal Basti is also called "Kamal Basadi". It is a Jain temple that was constructed under the rule of the Ralta dynasty. An older version of the temple was constructed in 1204 AD by Bichirja, a minister of Kartavirya IV. Two hundred years ago, The Lord Neminath idol was discovered in the forest.

The temple was constructed inside the Belgaum fort beside the Chikki Basti. As a result of the temple's 72 petals resembling a lotus, it was given the name Kamal Basti. The 24 Tirthankaras' names are written on each of its petals. The monument is now a monumental building that exemplifies the chalukya architectural style.

5. Fort Lake

One of Belgaum city's picnic areas is Fort Lake. Fort lake's other name is Kote Kere. Fort Lake is located just in front of the Durga Temple. Fort Lake is surrounded by beautiful plants, it has pathways around its edge, and regular passersby enjoy strolls. The lake's surroundings come to life in the evening when the sun sets over the still lake. Pedal boats and float eateries have been arranged on the lake's water by the lake administration. Every Saturday and Sunday from 6 PM to 8 PM, fountain shows are conducted that attract viewers from all over the city. There are food stands where tourists may purchase street cuisine. On the inside of Fort lake, there is a little theme park for children, and the lake's gates are open for guests every day from 6 AM to 8 PM.

6. Khade Bazaar

Every city has a fantastic market where virtually everything is sold. The Khade Bazaar and Ganpat Galli streets in Belgaum city are where you can find the global market. A huge influx of people comes into the market every day.

A 1-kilometre stretch of road has been transformed into a Khade Bazaar that sells a wide range of goods, including electronics, fashionable items, daily necessities, party supplies, restaurants, and street food vendors. On Saturdays, when residents from around the area come to Khade Bazaar and Ganpat Galli marketplaces to do their weekly shopping, these locations become the busiest.

The Bazaar opens at 9:30 AM and closes at about 10 PM on ordinary days, and it opens until 11:30 PM on festival days. During Ramadan, the Khade Bazaar is at its most joyous, and the spike on the final three days of Ramadan and Eid-al-Fitr is particularly high. Similar to how the nearby Ganpat Galli is flooded with lights and music during Ganesh Chaturthi, the celebrations reach the skies on Ganesh Visarjan day.

7. Rakaskop Dam

Rakaskop Dam is situated on the outskirts of Belgaum city across the Markandeya River. Rakaskop Dam is located approximately 16 km from the city's centre. The Dam is famed for its lush green surroundings that supply water to the whole city of Belgaum.

Families and couples who want to spend some time together tranquilly frequently visit the Dam. Not just among residents of Belgaum and its environs but also among tourists who occasionally visit Belgaum, Rakaskop has established itself as a popular picnic location. There is a mythology that a demon (Rakkasa) who lived in a cave close to the settlement gave rise to the village's name.

8. Yellur Fort

One of the most spectacular historical sites to visit in Belgaum is Yellur Fort. Another name for Yellur Fort is Rajhansgad Yellur Fort. The Marathas, Peshwas, Hoysalas, and Bahamanis, kings, were

the rulers of this old fort. From the fort, you may enjoy the breathtaking views of the below valley, shiny carpeted meadows and lush paddy fields. The fortress has a Shiva temple and a little well encased within the walls. Fort has a decorative entrance that was created in the Gomukhi pattern.

Entry Fee: INR 25 per person

9. Military Mahadev Temple

A well-known tourist destination in Belgaum is the Military Mahadev Temple. It is situated on Camp Road in Belgaum. This temple was maintained and built by the Indian Army. The temple is located in the middle of a beautiful park, and there is also a children's play area. This temple has undergone numerous changes and improvements over the years.

In the temple, every Monday, the Samuhik Panchamrit ceremony is performed. It is referred to as the Panchamrit's collective offering. Honey, yoghurt, ghee, and sugar are the four essential ingredients that are used in making this Panchamrit. The temple is open From 6 AM to 8 PM.

10. Godchinamalaki Falls

Godchinamalaki Falls is situated 15 kilometres from Gokak. Beautiful Godchinamalaki Falls is located along the Markandeya river. Falling downward into a lush valley, a falling waterfall's tinkling gurgle be heard throughout the entire area. Godchinamalaki falls' another name is Markandeya fall. Actually, the waterfall falls in two stages, and the spectacle is just beautiful to watch. Although swimming is not permissible here, you can briefly stand beneath the chilly waves. But be careful with the slippery rocks.

11. Vajrapoha Falls

Vajrapoha Falls is a stunning waterfall with glistening water that cascades over a rocky cliff and creates a wonderful scene. These Waterfalls are also the source of a number of streams, whose water is used for irrigation and drinking in nearby villages and fields. The visitors have to take permission from the Jamboti Forest Department in order to visit Vajrapoha Falls.
