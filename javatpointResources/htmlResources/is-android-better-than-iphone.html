Is Android Better than iPhone

Reasons Why an Android Phone is Better than an iPhone

Following are the reasons why an android phone is better than an iPhone:

1. Price and Affordability

In the event that you are looking to purchase a new Android or iPhone, do you need the best value for your buck?

Obviously, you do. Who needs to pay fundamentally more for an iPhone with fewer highlights than Android phones less powerful?

Effectively the core reasons why an Android phone is better than an iPhone has consistently been the affordability and cost.

2. Innovation

The Android phone is being invented by various companies that have consistently been on top of advancement.

There are new imaginative highlights pushed out pretty much every time a significant company delivers another Android phone. From in-screen fingerprint sensors to modular smartphones, cell phones that run Android consistently appear to have a decisive advantage over the competition.

Ever since Tim Cook took over Apple after the passing of Steve Jobs, many believed that Apple had not made any development with each new iPhone.

Every event which Apple holds where they declare an update to iOS or new iPhones, you generally get energized seeking after something new and creative.

Although, over the years, Apple WWDC and Special Events of Apple have increased a lot.

Apple's strategy has ordinarily been to take new fascinating highlights that were delivered, "awesome" them, and afterward delivered them two years after the fact.

3. Physical Back Button

There is a physical back button in almost every Android phone or an in-screen dedicated back button, and this is one of the great advantages of Android phone over iPhones. When you become acclimated to a dedicated back button, it's difficult to return.

At the point when you have a dedicated back button then you realize how it will behave every time.

Ordinarily, you would prefer not to go right back to the home screen and only want to return to one page.

The users of iPhone may argue that they can just hit the soft "ack button" or "swipe back" in the upper-left corner if they need to return; however, that is not the simplest alternative at all.

With the phone screens enlarged once again, it is neither easy to hit the back button every time to reach the top-left.

With the Android phones, the back button sits at the screen's bottom, where your fingers are already resting.

Additionally, Androids are entirely customizable, remember? So, you can customize the back button on the basis of your comfort.

4. Universal Charger

Every hail USB charger! For uncountable devices, including Android smartphones, USB chargers are the standard charger. Essentially all Android telephones utilize either USB-C chargers or USB micro chargers.

What does Apple have? All things considered; they selected to make their own chargers known as lightning cables. Not exclusively are these different and just usable for Apple devices, however, they tend to be of very poor quality.

For what reason would Apple do this, you ask? Since it gets them more money. When Apple altered the entirety of its iPhones in order to use USB-C chargers, they would lose money.

The reason behind that is you are able to purchase USB-C chargers at a less price on Amazon than what a lightning cable costs on Apple's site.

Also, when your Apple charger turns bad, you need to surrender a larger portion of your hard-earned money to buying another one.

5. Customization

One of the main Android's objective has constantly been the level of customization it permits. While Apple needs to take control of default applications to keep a homogenous software and hardware experience, Android allows you to select your own degree of customization. It spans all the way from modest things such as live wallpapers to custom ROM installs to alternative keyboards.

The detectors will always say that Hardcore Geeks care about this degree of customization, yet at the shallow end of the pool, this isn't accurate. Many iOS users adored it when Apple began permitting outsider keyboards and basic widgets. All that is confirmed is that this adaptability is something which people want.

There are some manufacturers who permit complex hardware customization. In LG mobile phone there is a replaceable leather backplates, Motorola has Moto Maker, as does Xiaomi. Those are only an instance of somewhat Apple will never accomplish for you. See yourself as fortunate to have rose gold and champagne gold available, which is pretty revolutionary for Apple.

6. Custom ROMs

Another purpose behind Android is better than iPhone is that you are able to change the software which came with your device along with the custom ROM in the event that you need to. Basically, it is installing a new OS, and numerous users of Android do it because their manufacturer or carrier is not fast in upgrading the new version of the Android platform, but in order to improve performance or gain access to various add-ons or tools, you can also do it. Undoubtedly it is the extreme, end of Android customization, and you need to follow little cautious to guarantee that you don't run into discomfort. All things are considered, as long as you may follow the instructions and your device is upheld, the advantage can be vast.

For hell's sake, there are even approaches to install totally different operating systems on various Android devices like Ubuntu, Sailfish, Firefox, etc.

7. More Free Apps and Games

It very well might be a double-edged sword; however, you can't get away from the fact that as compared to iOS, Android has more free games and apps. Sometimes there are various apps that carry a price label on iOS that are free of cost on Android. There also appears to be a more noteworthy eagerness to pursue the freemium or promotion upheld model.

Tragically, this might be, in any event somewhat, down to piracy concerns and, although more hits are being ported across, the capacity to acquire more from iOS development actually implies all the more excellent, premium applications and games release on iOS first. Android proceeds to dominate, and we have seen a alteration here; however, it is the major challenge for Android.

8. Launcher

Get an iPhone and sit close to another user of iOS and analyze your home screens. You will find that both of the home screens are identical, but in the case of Android, it will not happen.

In the event that you need to control over how your Android cell phone or tablet looks, at that point, you've possibly tried a custom launcher. You are able to select over a wide assortment of custom launcher applications in Google Play and modify everything from your home screen layout to your page transitions, gestures, etc. No risk is associated with launcher applications, and you can truly open up a universe of potential outcomes.

A launcher likewise is useful for those instances where you like a handset; however, you possibly aren't so enthused about the custom interface of the manufactures. A launcher goes far in these sorts of circumstances. Also, if you are the type of person who enjoys the freedom of Android however desires the looks afforded by Windows or iOS, there are even launchers which assist you with accomplishing a comparable look and feel.
