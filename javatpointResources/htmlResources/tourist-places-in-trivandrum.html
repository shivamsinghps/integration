21 Best Tourist Places in Trivandrum

Due to its beautiful, captivating nature and magnificent architectural wonders, Trivandrum, or Thiruvananthapuram, is a well-known location throughout the nation. The city is renowned for having a strong religious and cultural legacy.

In Thiruvananthapuram or Trivandrum, there are some very great sights to see, which will not only make your vacation memorable but also give you a good impression of the interesting cultural landscape of the city. The most populous city in the state of Kerala is Trivandrum. This city is also well known for its lovely beaches and classy British architectural styles that can be seen all over the place. In this article, we will see a few favourite tourist destinations available here in Trivandrum.

1. Agastya Mala Peak in Trivandrum

Agastyarkoodam is another name for Agastya Mala Peak. With a height of 1868 meters, it is Thiruvananthapuram's tallest mountain. The Tamirabarani, Karmana, and Nayyar rivers all start from here. It is a Hindu pilgrimage destination. A full-sized Agastya statue stands against the magnificent background of the peak's wonderful setting. Trekking is required to reach Agastya Mala Peak. However, it would be best if you first informed the Kerala Forest Department to walk to the top. Trekking is also only permitted in January and February. Pilgrims must also obtain permission from the Kerala government before visiting the shrine. On the route to the summit are several herbs and medicinal plants. There are also numerous unique kinds of flora and wildlife in the area.

2. Sree Chitra Art Gallery in Trivandrum

The Sree Chitra Art Gallery is close to the Napier Museum. This art gallery is well-known for its extensive collection of paintings by the well-known early painter Raja Ravi Varma. This art collection features paintings in the Rajput, Rajasthani, and Mughal styles. The paintings in the Chitra Art Gallery are outstanding in Balinese and Tanjore styles. These are composed of fine stonework and dark hues. It also exhibits Tibetan, Chinese, and Japanese art traditions. There is also a section dedicated to small artworks. These images will wow you. This art gallery is well-known across the world. Every day, hundreds of visitors visit. The Sree Chitra Art Gallery and its displays require at least two hours of your time.

3. Happyland Water Theme Park in Trivandrum

Happyland Amusement Park is located in Vembayam, on the outskirts of Thiruvananthapuram. Flying Fox, Uphill Drive, Challenger, Crazy Cruise, Columbus, and many other pulse-racing rides are available at the amusement park to add to the fun and heartbeat. The park is designed to meet the highest safety requirements. Water World is largely intended for family enjoyment. Children's Village contains rides designed just for them. It is close to the lovely beach view. Happyland Water Theme Park is a tremendous experience for you if you seek endless fun and excitement.

4. Science and Technology Museum in Trivandrum

The Napier Museum is relatively near to the Science and Technology Museum. The major goal of constructing this museum is to raise scientific knowledge among students and the general public. The Science and Technology Museum provides both a learning and entertainment facility. It is a big draw for the kids. It depicts all aspects of science and technology. It has several animated samples, effects, and electrical equipment. Bio-medical videos are also shown. This science and technology museum also features India's most advanced and technologically advanced planetarium. The entry charge is quite low here.

5. Napier Museum in Trivandrum

It is an attractive museum and the ideal venue to learn about Kerala's extremely rich culture and historical history. Trivandrum's museum is a major art and cultural centre. This wonderful museum, founded in the mid-19th century by a British commander called Napier, is one of the top tourist attractions in Trivandrum.

The museum displays some beautiful sculptures and coins from Kerala's past. The intriguing musical instruments from various locations and ages of Kerala are one of the museum's greatest attractions. The entrance cost is INR 20 per person. The surrounding area is rich and green.

6. Shanmugam Beach in Trivandrum

Shanmugam Beach is a beautiful beach located near the city airport. This beach with pure blue sea and white sand is a great location to calm and enjoy the ethereal surroundings. The beach is very clean and has maintained its natural beauty, making it one of the greatest spots to visit in Trivandrum.

On the shore, there is also a little Devi shrine. The sunset views from the beach are truly exceptional and enchanting. The peaceful beach is pretty long and quite attractive. The nicest thing about this beach is that it is not as crowded as some of the city's other famous beaches, except around sunset, when many people come here to take in the breathtaking sunset views.

7. Vellayani Lake in Trivandrum

Vellayani Lake is the biggest lake in Trivandrum and one of the greatest Trivandrum tourist attractions. The lake provides stunning views of the hamlet and the backwaters, making it a terrific site to visit. Fishing lovers can also test their skills at this lake.

It's a great spot for a family picnic. This lake, located in the city's rural sector, is home to white ducks and lotus blossoms. The magnificent surroundings of the lake will inspire you and make you want to spend time enjoying this wonderful lake. There are numerous things to do in Trivandrum, including boating on this lake.

8. Veli Tourist Village in Trivandrum

Veli Tourist Village, one of the greatest places to visit in Trivandrum, is an exquisite picnic location nestled beside the lake amid ethereal surroundings. The property includes a stunning garden loaded with vibrant flowers and carefully kept.

Veli Tourist Village is undoubtedly a peaceful area to spend quality time surrounded by gorgeous natural lush green. There is some equipment and play places for children, as well as boat trips on the lake. In the Village, visitors may also go horseback riding and participate in sports.

9. Magic Planet in Trivandrum

It is a museum with a very unusual concept, focusing on magic, and is a whole for children in Trivandrum. It claims to be the world's first magical themed park and is quickly becoming a popular tourist attraction in Kerala. The museum has a wonderful collection of street magic, performances, and tricks from many books, including The Indian Mango Mystery and The Indian Basket Trick. The magical planet will undoubtedly cast a spell on you, leaving behind the beginnings of wizards and the desire for magic.

10. Planetarium in Trivandrum

Priyadarshini Planetarium is, without a doubt, one of the top locations to visit in Trivandrum for people who are fascinated by the universe and wish to learn more about it. Priyadarshini Planetarium first opened to the public in 1994 and has been extremely popular. The planetarium is equipped with a GM-11 starfield projector, which allows you to observe the fascinating universe of planets and stars through its projection.

11. Akkulam Lake in Trivandrum

Akkulam Lake, a calm lake in Akkulam, Trivandrum, is ideal for nature enthusiasts. This lake, surrounded by vegetation, is for people who want to visit a tranquil area where they may rest amidst nature and make their Trivandrum sightseeing a must-see. The boat ride you may do with your loved one or alone will highlight your vacation to Akkulam Lake. Take a boat trip and enjoy the picture-perfect sunset. If you're searching for a rejuvenating getaway following a long day, Akkulam Lake has all you need!

12. Anchuthengu Fort in Trivandrum

The English East India Company erected this fort in the 16th century, roughly 32 kilometres from Trivandrum Central Railway Station. It was also the East Indian Company's initial colony in Kerala and one of the Company's most significant strongholds. This fort is located on the outskirts of the Anchuthengu beach and offers panoramic views of the city.

13. Padmanabhaswamy Temple in Trivandrum

It is a magnificent temple with a shining architectural design and an awe-inspiring ambience, and it is one of the top locations to see in Trivandrum. This memorial temple is also the world's costliest temple. The temple is dedicated to Lord Sri Padmanabhaswamy, who is regarded as Trivandrum's lord.

The temple has a long and illustrious history. It is one of India's and the world's most-visited holy sites. The vast temple is an architectural masterpiece, with every detail built with great care and consideration. A strict clothing code is in place, and only traditional attire is permitted.

14. Attukal Temple in Trivandrum

This interesting temple devoted to Goddess Bhadrakali - one of the forms of Goddess Mahakali is one of the greatest Trivandrum locations to see. This temple boasts awe-inspiring architectural architecture and a tranquil environment, making it one of the greatest tourist attractions in Trivandrum. Attukal Temple holds multiple Guinness World Records, including the most people for any celebration. Pongal festival is celebrated with tremendous zeal on the temple grounds. After visiting the temple, you may stop by a well-known Indian Coffee House for a great cup of coffee and breakfast.

15. Sree Chitra Art Gallery in Trivandrum

The art gallery, founded in 1935 by the Maharaja of Travancore Sri Chitra Thirunal, is one of the top sites to visit in Trivandrum. It is a lovely destination with some wonderful items from the Kingdom of Travancore and Kerala. There are some wonderful collections of paintings, many of which were produced by royal Travancore family members. The museum also features contemporary art exhibits from art hubs in Japan, Tibet, China, and Indonesia, among other places.

16. Kanakakunnu Palace in Trivandrum

It is an amazing and brilliant area built in the typical Kerala artificial design, making it one of the top Trivandrum tourist spots. This majestic mansion is close to the Napier Museum and Zoo and well preserved and tidy. The lush green beauty of the palace's environs is truly lovely. This region is serene and quiet.

A fantastic spot to relax. There is also a restaurant in the royal complex where you may have a delicious cup of tea or coffee. On weekends, people bring their instruments and jam here. There are retail kiosks in the palace where you may buy local goods and handicrafts.

17. Sagarika Marine Research Aquarium in Trivandrum

This little aquarium is quite famous among visitors since it houses some amazing unusual fish. Some of India's rarest fish are maintained here, and the vistas of these underwater residents will inspire you.

If you are interested in underwater life and fish, you must visit this location. Fish species include sharks, lobsters, starfish, beautiful corals, and more. The museum is well-kept, well-lit, and clean. The museum charges a fee of $20 per person to visit.

18. Aazhimala Siva Temple in Trivandrum

This temple is highly colourful and devoted to Lord Shiva. The temple is well-known for its unique and breathtaking architectural features. The temple's surroundings are pretty lovely. A seashore with many rocks is located near the temple. The temple has a magnificent and massive statue of Lord Shiva. It is one of Trivandrum's famous Shiva temples. The view from this cliff is incredible. The sunrise and sunset views from here are breathtaking. It is one of the top attractions in Thiruvananthapuram.

19. Neyyar Dam in Trivandrum

This dam, really the home of paradise in Kerala, is a fantastic Trivandrum sightseeing area to spend a pleasant day in the sun and is one of the best natural marvels and famous tourist destinations in Trivandrum. The dam is home to much rare flora, wildlife, and medicinal herbs that promote well-being. Immerse yourself in the positive vibes and refresh all of your senses in the magnificent surroundings. Visit this dam for a great day and have a good time.

20. Poovar Island in Trivandrum

Poovar Island is a well-known island resort in Kerala surrounded by tranquil backwaters. It is a tropical paradise in the south. Imagine amazing sunset views among coconut palm trees, immaculate beaches with golden sand, dreamlike blue waters, and the sound of waves slamming on the breathtaking shoreline. If you're searching for a lovely hideaway with floating cottages constructed on stilts, this is one of the greatest cottages in Trivandrum.

21. Palace Museum Or Kuthira Malika in Trivandrum

The Palace Museum is located in the Kuthiramalika Palace, established by Travancore's ruler, Maharaja Swati Thirunal Balarama Varma. The temple is on the list of top attractions in Trivandrum and is located in the fort region. It has excellent Travancore temple architecture. The museum holds pictures of the royal family and other valuable treasures. The 122 wooden horse fridges placed on the museum's outside walls are the main draw for most visitors.

How To Reach Trivandrum

1. By Flight

Trivandrum has an airport that serves domestic and international flights. This airport has excellent connections to all major domestic and international airports. You can take a cab from the airport to your location in Trivandrum.

2. By Train

Trivandrum Central Railway Station is the city's largest and busiest railway station. Several trains link to other key stations at various hours of the day. Trivandrum Railway Station is South India's third-largest railway station.

3. By Road

Regular bus routes link Trivandrum to all major towns and cities. The bus terminal is directly across the street from the Trivandrum Railway Station. Kerala State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC) runs six service classes that connect Trivandrum to southern Tamil Nadu and the rest of Kerala. Roads link it to all major cities, including Madurai, Bangalore, Kozhikode, Chennai, Kochi, and Coimbatore.

Local Food Of Trivandrum

Thoran, a dry mix of vegetables formed from coconut shreds, a stew in which potatoes and onions are cooked in coconut milk, and Kalan, which is composed of buttermilk, watermelon, and ripe mangoes, are all popular dishes in Trivandrum. Banana chips, which go well with tea, are a favourite snack in Trivandrum. Aside from that, non-vegetarian food is also well-known. A popular restaurant menu will include everything from seafood to chicken to steak and pig.

Best Time To Visit Trivandrum

The perfect time to visit Trivandrum is from October to February, when the temperature is mild. Summers are hot and humid, which may be uncomfortable. Monsoons, on the other hand, are quite chilly. As a result, winter is the greatest season to visit Trivandrum.
