Tourist Places in Ranchi

The state of Jharkhand's capital, Ranchi, is graced with stunning scenery. In the past, Ranchi was acknowledged as the Bihar state's summer capital, and Ranchi was also recognized as a hill resort city under British administration. Ranchi city is recognized as a state rich in minerals, and around 40% of the nation's total mineral reserves come from Ranchi alone. Aside from this, Ranchi is a lovely town with lush woods, peacefulness, and breathtaking views of the natural world. The elevation of Ranchi City is around 1140 feet above sea level, and Ranchi is gradually advancing.

There are attractions in Ranchi's every single corner. There are many things to explore here, including temples, waterfalls, stunning vistas, and more. You should visit Ranchi if you enjoy travelling. For those who are organizing a trip, here is a list we are going to compile. This will let you understand Ranchi in a better way, and you will end up appreciating its aesthetic surroundings.

1. Panch Gagh Falls in Ranchi

Locals and visitors love to visit Panch Gagh Waterfall, a bit outside the city and a well-liked picnic destination. Five waterfalls may be seen pouring from here, and that's how the waterfall received its name. Many visitors may take advantage of the waterfalls by exploring their beauty. At the base of the waterfall, tourists frequently take baths. The mountain produces cold, pure water all the time. To get here, you'll need to go up several steps, so if you're bringing kids, take care and spend your time having fun. So people who love to explore the mountain and the waterfall will surely love this destination.

2. Sun Temple in Ranchi

The Sun Temple is stunning in Ranchi. This temple has become one of the most well-liked tourist destinations because of its impressive architecture. The temple, situated on a hill, is built in the usual Sun temple style and has a gigantic chariot with 18 tires and seven horses pulling it. On the grounds of the temple, there is a sacred tank that Hindus widely love. Tourists must visit the Sun Temple, surrounded by tranquil scenery and abundant trees and bushes. This place will be liked by nature lovers and people who worship god, so do not forget to explore this place while visiting Ranchi.

3. Birsa Zoological Park in Ranchi

Birsa Zoological Park is a fantastic destination if you are travelling with children. Lions, Tigers, and deer are just a few of the many species of animals that may be found in the park. Here is the place to go if you want to learn more about the natural fauna and participate in the pet assistance program. It is located close to Ormanjhi on the Ranchi-Patna National Highway. The zoo's main gate has a small cafeteria where you can purchase beverages. The park opens daily from 9 am to 4:30 pm, except on Mondays. So, if you are a nature and a pet lover, include this spot so you will not miss out on exploring.

4. Tagore Hill in Ranchi

The famous writer Rabindranath Tagore, who is thought to have spent a lot of time here, is honoured by having Tagore Hill named after him. According to legend, Tagore frequently visited these hills to appreciate the peace while writing his novels. It is well-known for adventurous activities like rock climbing and hiking and is located at an elevation of roughly 300 feet. You may ascend 200-250 stairs to the top and take in the breathtaking city views once you get there. The peak provides a stunning view of the hills. The hill's encircling lush vegetation additionally enhances its charm. This spot is one more in the category of Nature lover, so you should explore this place too while visiting Ranchi.

5. Patratu Valley in Ranchi

The breathtaking Patratu Valley, with beautiful greenery, enchanting panoramas, and foggy mountains, is located 40 kilometres from Ranchi. Visitors may gaze onto the Patratu Dam on one side and the lushly forested Patratu Valley on the other. Roads featuring S-curves and curvy corners in this valley use every opportunity to make the ride thrilling. Between 9 am and 5 pm, you may also enjoy boating at Patratu Dam. Do not use these routes after midnight due to the region's strong kidnapper and attacker presence. So, bike and nature lovers will love exploring this beautiful spot.

6. Jagannath Temple in Ranchi

This hill temple, one of the city's most well-known historical attractions, is unquestionably a site that should be explored. The most visited historical attraction in Ranchi, Jagannath Temple, which has been in operation since 1691, is located at the hill's summit. The temple is a distinct combination of beautiful carvings and colourful stones, serving as a designed copy of the Jagannath Temple in Puri(Bhuwneswar). The Rath Yatra, held annually in June and July, is, without a doubt, the ideal time to visit the temple. It would be best to mount several stairs to get to the temple because it is on a hill. After visiting, you must enjoy the stunning view of the city from the mountain.

7. Rock Garden in Ranchi

The Rock Garden of Ranchi, constructed entirely from the rocks of Gonda Hill, is home to several fountains, statues, works of art, and breathtaking views. Weekends are the ideal times for the locals to visit this location. Another location Kanke Dam near the Rock Garden, situated on a tiny hill, further adds to its beauty. Don't forget to stop here and enjoy the sunset's splendour. You may also engage a guide to get the correct knowledge about the garden if you're interested in knowing more about its heritage.

8. State Museum in Ranchi

Its official name is Ranchi Museum. Historical artefacts are kept here in the State Museum in Hotwar. The weapons and ammunition, antiques, and jewellery are displayed here to reveal information about the state's past. There is an art gallery at the museum, and it has an impressive collection of artwork and fascinating paintings depicting architectural locations throughout Jharkhand. It serves as a hub of information on the state's history for travellers. The hours for visiting are 10:30 am to 4:30 pm (closed on Mondays). So, keeping in mind all these specialities, you should consider visiting this place to gain more information about the state's past.

9. Nakshatra Van in Ranchi

Nakshatra Van is situated in Ranchi, across from Raj Bhavan, the governor's residence. Its foundation is the original idea of constellations. The park is split up into many areas, and these each stand for a constellation and a sign of the zodiac. According to Hindu astrology, each Nakshatra is associated with a tree with healing, aesthetic, sociological, and economic significance. The park's attractiveness is enhanced by the musical fountain and the lovely walks surrounding it. The hours of operation are 9:30 am to 6:30 pm (closed on Mondays). So if you believe in constellations and the zodiac, you should visit this site to explore more.

10. Jonha Falls in Ranchi

Ranchi's Jonha Falls has come to symbolize the city. This hanging valley's stunning beauty is entirely obvious and memorable. You will have these moments with you for a very long time after visiting. Every day, a large number of visitors come here. You may have fun whenever you want because it is never a busy location; thus, you can relax here. Ranchi is 43 kilometres away from this waterfall. So if you are a nature lover and a crowd hater, you should visit this place.

11. Pahari Mandir in Ranchi

The city's most breathtaking location is the Pahari temple. It is located at the height of 2140 feet height at Ranchi Hill. Shiva worshipers assemble in great numbers here all year round. Particularly during the month of Shravan, there is a vast gathering. Followers highly regard the temple. There is supposed to be a force(God Power) in this place that can grant their requests. To get to the temple perched on a hill, one must ascend around 400 stairs. You can visit this place from morning until night. So, if you are a lord Shiva worshipper, make sure you go here and explore this place.

12. Dasam Falls in Ranchi

Dasam Falls, among the most visited tourist destinations in Jharkhand, is situated around 40 kilometres from Ranchi. Nature enthusiasts would like this location. One of the pleasures of these waterfalls is the ability to view ten streams of water flowing into the bridge when it is at its most spectacular. Due to the slow increase in tourists, numerous eateries and clean restrooms have been constructed here. It's now easier than ever to get around this place; in this lovely rainforest setting, the pure water of the Kanchi river cascades from 144 feet can be seen, and it looks stunning. This vista is equally attractive.

13. Panchghagh Falls in Ranchi

Locals love to visit Panchghagh Falls, which is 50 kilometres from Ranchi. The area is well-liked for picnics. Five waterfalls may be seen pouring from this location, which is how the waterfall got its name. If you visit on the weekend, you will witness plenty of residents taking the scenery tranquillity. You must climb hills to get here if you are travelling with children. This is a low-crowded area, so you can feel calm while visiting here, so make sure you include this site in your list and visit here when you get tired of seeing other significant sites.

14. Varahmihir Planetarium in Ranchi

The Varahmihir Planetarium is a well-known attraction in Ranchi. The Ranchi Science Center's grounds contain this planetarium. You can learn more about our universe right here. The entrance to this planetarium is paid. Here, an adult is paid 50 rupees, while children are charged 30 rupees. There are additional shows here with varying times. You're welcome to come and visit with your family and friend.

15. Dhurva Dam in Ranchi

A popular tourist spot in Ranchi is Dhurva Dam. This lake is lovely. There is a lot of space taken up by this lake. You are welcome to visit this place while it is raining. Here, you may enjoy some breathtaking sights. Around here, there is vegetation everywhere. You can come here in the evening to have a nice walk. Here, you'll get to witness a gorgeous sunset. Ranchi's Dhurva Dam is a must-see attraction. This is also a low-crowded area, so you can feel calm while visiting here, so make sure you include this site in your list and visit here when you get tired of seeing other significant sites.

16. Birsa Mrig Vihar in Ranchi

One of the most popular tourist destinations in Ranchi is Birsa Mrig Vihar. There are many deer to be seen here. Here, many species of deer have been preserved. Around here, there is vegetation everywhere. There are trees to be discovered. Sambar spotted deer, and black antelope may all be seen here. Ranchi's Kalamati neighbourhood is home to Birsa Mrig Vihar. Come here and feel free to wander everywhere. It is near the major road. You can also find the canteen, where you can purchase food and beverages. Coming here may be a lot of fun. So, make sure you go here and explore this place.

17. Getalsud Dam in Ranchi

One of Ranchi's top tourist attractions is the Getalsud Dam. The Swarnrekha River forms the foundation for this dam. The Rukka Dam is another name for this dam. It's a well-known picnic location in Ranchi. The Rukka block is where this dam is located. Rukka Dam is the resultant name. It is a pretty dam that spans a large region. This dam is situated in Ranchi's Ormanjhi location. From a tourism perspective, this dam is stunning. This dam has seven gates, and many people come to see it during the rainy season. There is a breathtaking sight to behold when the dam's water overflows. Aside from this, coming here in the evening is highly recommended since a view of the sunset is accessible. This spot is one more in the category of Nature lover, so you should explore this place too while visiting Ranchi. Here, you may have a wonderful time. A lovely time may be had here with your friends and relatives.

18. Palani Falls in Ranchi

One of Ranchi's top natural tourist attractions is Palani Falls. A stunning waterfall in the centre of a forest may be seen here. Here, towering rocks drop water in an adorable pattern. Here, you may take a bath. Only during the rainy season can you view this waterfall. Patratu Dam is about 4 kilometres from this waterfall. You may also like visiting here. You may ride a bike or a vehicle to get here. A short portion of the route must also be completed on foot. But once you get to the waterfall, you'll feel fantastic.

19. Shiv Lok Dham Marshili Mountain in Ranchi

Ranchi's Shivlok Dham is a stunning location. A Shiva temple and a pond are also accessible at this location. You can also go there to witness a giant statue of Hanuman Ji. You can see the lovely surroundings from here. Both a vehicle and a bicycle can get you here. This location is in Ranchi's Unidih district, and you can visit this place from morning until night. So, if you are a lord Shiva and Hanuman Ji worshipper, make sure you go here and explore this place.

20. Panchghagh Falls in Ranchi

A popular natural tourist spot near Ranchi is Panchghagh Falls. This waterfall is quite lovely, and it is encircled on all sides by thick trees. This waterfall has been created in a very stunning style by Jharkhand Tourism. To get to the waterfall, use the concrete stairs here. Additionally, a watch tower is present, from which you may enjoy the stunning panorama of the waterfall and its surroundings. In Ranchi's Khunti forest region, this waterfall is located. You may travel here by vehicle. Additionally, the parking options are decent here. Come to this waterfall to take a dip.

21. Ghaghari Dham Falls in Ranchi

A popular natural tourist spot in Ranchi is Ghaghari Dham Falls. You may view a stunning waterfall here. This waterfall is situated in Ranchi's Devgaon neighbourhood. You're welcome to come and visit. The rainy season is the most fantastic time to go because the waterfall is still at its most beautiful then. A temple can also be seen here. In the temple's central premise, Shivling is positioned. During the Sawan, a lot of people explore this location. The location of this waterfall is in the centre of a forest. Because of this, this area is green throughout the rainy season. This spot is one more in the category of Nature lover, so you should explore this place too while visiting Ranchi.

22. Sitakund Falls in Ranchi

Ranchi's Sitakund Falls is a popular natural tourism spot. Jonha Falls is located adjacent to Sitakund Falls. Here, amid the forest, is a stunning waterfall. Going down the stairs will allow you to have a closer look at this waterfall, which is extremely lovely. There are around 250 steps in this area. You may have a stunning view of the waterfall by climbing. This area is covered with forest, which is lush and green during the rainy season. During the rainy season, the waterfall also offers a stunning view. The entrance charge is applicable here while the parking is free of cost.

23. Diwri Mata Mandir in Ranchi

One of Ranchi's well-known temples is called Divdi Mata Mandir. This building serves as Mata Durga Ji's temple. This temple contains a magnificent figure of Maa Durga, who has 16 hands. All the followers rush to visit the temple because of the availability of beautiful idols. This temple is charming. You may view statues of several gods and goddesses within the temple along its outside walls. Coming to this temple will make you feel good because of its gorgeous look and surrounding peace. In Ranchi, this shrine has located some distance from the Ranchi-Jamshedpur Highway. This temple is easily accessible by vehicle.

24. Rani Falls in Ranchi

One of Ranchi's top natural tourist attractions is Rani Falls. Here, you may observe a stunning waterfall. This waterfall is visible during the rainy season. The Khunti forest region is where this waterfall is situated. The Khunti Tamar road lies a little bit within where this waterfall is located. You can get here quite quickly. One may enjoy the stunning view of the surrounding forest from here. It is an excellent location for a picnic and is about 40 kilometres from Ranchi city.

25. Lodh Falls in Ranchi

Lodh Falls is one of the attractions in Ranchi and is situated near Latehar, which is almost 195 kilometres from Ranchi city. With a height of 143 metres, Lodh Falls is the tallest waterfall in the state of Jharkhand. Additionally, this waterfall is the 21st tallest waterfall in India.

Best Time to Visit Ranchi

The ideal season to visit Ranchi is in the winter, from November to February, when the temperature is comfortable with a few chilly evenings here and there. Ranchi experiences extremely hot and muggy summers and average monsoon rains. As a result, the months of March through October should generally be avoided, although occasionally they can have advantages. Overall, there is never a wrong time to visit Ranchi, but the winter months are the busiest.

Local Food of Ranchi

From tranquil waterfalls and hills to exquisite and distinctive cuisine flavours, Ranchi is complete with fragrant, delectable foods. Ranchi's traditional cuisine is mainly inspired by its tribal population, although it also incorporates elements of Bengali, Mughlai, and Bihari cuisines. This area's cuisine has a unique flavour. The regional cuisine is still the same and has not been heavily marketed like other states' cuisine. In the city's restaurants, you may enjoy snacks like Samosa. One may sample the distinctive native cuisine of this section of the region by visiting any tribal village or a tribal wedding. The locals like the spicy, non-vegetarian meals quite a bit.

Typical Ranchi regional foods include

  • Dhuska
  • Peetha
  • Arsa & Chhilka
  • Litti &Chokha

How To Reach Ranchi

The capital of Jharkhand, Ranchi, is at the height of 2100 feet above sea level. Bihar's former capital was Ranchi. Any Indian city may be used to access Ranchi. It has train connections to the East, West, North, and South and road connections to every important nation region. It has air service to Delhi, Mumbai, and Patna.

  1. By Road

    Several highways connect Ranchi to other cities because of its location on a national road. Regular bus services are always available in Ranchi, which links the city's central locations to the outside world.
  2. By Rail

    There is a well-organized rail system in Ranchi. Thanks to the well-developed rail network, getting to Ranchi from places like Patna, Delhi, Kolkata, and Rourkela is simple. The South Eastern Railway system includes Ranchi's rail infrastructure. People can begin their journeys to Ranchi from several other cities as well. Nagpur, New Delhi, Patna, Mumbai, Jaipur, Ajmer, Cochin, Kanpur, Chennai, Bangalore, Allahabad, and Bhubaneswar are just a few of them.
  3. By Air

    Birsa Munda Airport, a major airport, is located in Ranchi. Regular flights from this airport connect cities like Delhi, Lucknow, Mumbai, Kolkata, and Patna.
