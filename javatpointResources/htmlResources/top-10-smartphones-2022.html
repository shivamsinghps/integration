Top 10 Smartphones 2022

A smartphone is a pocket device that integrates a mobile phone and a computer. Smartphones belong to the multipurpose mobile computing device and mobile phone classes. A smartphone has numerous computer-like features, such as a touchscreen interface, an internet connection, and an operating system that can run downloadable programs. People's lives have undergone a significant transformation thanks to smartphones.

Earlier, sending messages and taking pictures required extra work and a camera purchase. These characteristics are now integrated into one cutting-edge, rectangular technology that continues to become smarter and more intelligent daily. Since then, smartphones have never been discovered out of reach of users.

According to statistics, about half of the population owns a smartphone. In a 2016 Bank of America poll, 96% of millennials between the ages of 18 and 24 even stated that cell phones are essential to them. Additionally, 93% of them said cell phones were more important than toothbrushes and deodorant. The study also found that an individual checks their smartphone every 6.5 minutes.

Users may browse the internet more easily, thanks to smartphones. Mobile browsers built into these gadgets make it possible to surf and search the internet from anywhere at any time. According to a poll, opening browsers to browse the internet takes up 10% of all smartphone users' time. People may easily obtain information because of this. The finest smartphone should be chosen because cell phones are a critical part of our life. Here are the top 10 smartphones in India for 2022:

1. iPhone 14 Pro / Pro Max

With outstanding performance, great battery life, great cameras, and a variety of handy features you don't get with the normal versions, the Apple iPhone 14 Pro Max is the finest. Superior hardware and Widgets are part of the always-on display. It has superior cameras and outstanding wireless performance. Apple's largest and most feature-rich smartphone is the iPhone 14 Pro Max (beginning at $1,099), which is the best option in the iPhone lineup for anybody looking for a smartphone that can do it all.

The smaller iPhone 14 Pro costs $100 less, but we believe the Max is worth the extra money because of its bigger screen and longer-lasting battery. Additionally, the Pro Max outperforms the regular iPhone 14 due to its superior processing and always-on display. The iPhone 14 Pro Max boasts a primary camera that has been updated and is driven by a new Photonic Engine, as well as safety features like Crash Detection and Emergency SOS via satellite.

Similar to the iPhone 13 Pro Max and the iPhone 12 Pro Max, the iPhone 14 Pro Max has a simple design. In other words, you are familiar with it. Apart from the color options (which this year include Deep Purple, Graphite, Gold, and Silver), the Dynamic Island at the top of the display, and the size of the back camera module, nothing little sets the new smartphone apart from its predecessors.

The phone's size and weight are almost identical to last year's model, measuring 6.33 by 3.05 by 0.31 inches (HWD) and 8.47 ounces, respectively. It is, therefore, a big and hefty gadget, but Pro Max buyers should be accustomed to its size and weight by this point. Its dimensions are comparable to those of the Samsung Galaxy S22 Ultra (6.43 by 3.07 by 0.35 inches) and Google Pixel 6 Pro (6.45 by 2.99 by 0.35 inches). There is a somewhat smaller iPhone 14 Pro to take into account if the Pro Max is too big for you (5.81 by 2.81 by 0.31 inches).

Despite the lackluster design, Apple continues to set the standard for materials and construction quality. Unlike the normal iPhone 14, which has an aluminum frame, the iPhone 14 Pro Max has a stainless steel frame and is shielded by the manufacturer's proprietary Ceramic Shield. Apple claims that its Shield is stronger than competitor glass. However, we are unable to test this claim in a useful way.

The top-of-the-line Gorilla Glass Victus or Victus+ from Corning is used in phones from Google and Samsung. The iPhone 14 Pro Max features the same IP68 classification as most premium smartphones, making it resistant to dust and shallow water immersion for up to 30 minutes.

2. Google Pixel 7 Pro

The Google Pixel 7 Pro is not just the greatest Pixel; it is also one of the top smartphones available today. Google has already made several improvements, and there are still more to come. With the Pixel 7 Pro, Android now seems like a well-rounded, considerate operating system that is simple to use and performs most of the work for customers in every job. With a vast functionality list and a lower price than most top-tier rivals, the Pixel 7 Pro ($899) enters the flagship battle.

It competes with the $999 Samsung Galaxy S22+ and the $999 Apple iPhone 14 Pro by providing top-tier hardware, reliable cameras, polished performance, and long battery life. Stock Google software, unique app experiences, and cutting-edge machine learning and artificial intelligence features are some of the main advantages of the Pixel 7 Pro. The Galaxy S22+ is better because of its superior wireless performance, despite the Pixel 7 Pro receiving excellent scores for its exceptional photographic abilities and best-in-class software.

Since today's phone consumers want a large screen, the Pixel 7 Pro's display will likely be one of its key selling factors. It is larger than the S22+'s (6.6 inches, 2,340 by 2,080 pixels, 390 PPI) and the Pixel 7's screens. The 7 Pro's dynamic refresh rate display, similar to the 120Hz screen on the S22+ but faster than the 90Hz rate on the Pixel 7, oscillates between 60Hz and 120Hz to balance performance and smooth performance.

The 6.6-inch display on the Google Pixel 7 Pro is excellent. It utilizes LTPO OLED technology, like the iPhone 14 Pro, to run continuously without significantly depleting the battery. It's 1440 x 3120 resolution and 512 PPI make it even crisper than the Galaxy S22 Ultra or the iPhone. Peak brightness may be 1500 nits. The Google Pixel 7 Pro has three cameras on the back: a 50MP primary camera with f/1.9 aperture, a 48MP telephoto lens with 5X magnification, and a 12MP ultra-wide lens with bigger sensor pixels that can take macro photos.

The Pixel 7 Pro can utilize Google Ones to unblur your old photographs, even images the Pixel did not capture, which is where the true photo magic happens. The Pixel 7 Pro is Google's flagship smartphone, its primary mobile device, and the entry point to a vast ecosystem of platform-agnostic Google-connected goods and services. It is the thread that connects everything.

3. Samsung Galaxy A53

Today, we need a phone that offers us the greatest capabilities for the least amount of money, and the Galaxy A53 is the greatest deal smartphone you can get. Samsung's Galaxy S phones are the most innovative and finest phones currently available. The Galaxy A53 is an effortless phone to suggest because of the sophisticated cameras, the fantastic screen, and the high-end design. Samsung appears committed to providing a phone with sufficient juice at every price point.

Given this, it is no surprise that the Galaxy A53 5G ($449.99) fills the midrange void in the company's offering. This phone, however, falls short of striking a convincing balance between cost and performance. The phone has a bright screen and a big battery, which we appreciate, but it isn't as quick as similarly priced competitors, and the camera software should be improved. The A53 5G most likely falls between the upcoming A33 5G and the Galaxy S21 FE ($549.99), but that doesn't mean it should be purchased.

The S21 family of smartphones served as a major design inspiration for the Galaxy A53. Even I had trouble distinguishing the Galaxy S21 Ultra, my primary phone, from the A53 after turning them over. Although the A53's matte black rear and less sculpted camera bump make it more prone to attracting grease, the design is still sophisticated enough for folks to confuse your $450 smartphone for a $700 one. Similar in resolution and refresh rate to the S22, the A53's screen is beautiful.

Most users won't likely notice a significant difference between the display of this low-cost phone and that of the S22, save from size. If specifications are important to you, you will receive a 6.5-inch display with an FHD+ resolution, a 120Hz refresh rate, and a maximum brightness of 800 nits. Given that the Samsung Galaxy A53 offers a 5G connection and a brilliant screen, its 5,000mAh battery is useful.

The phone lasts about a day between charges, perhaps a little less with heavier usage. The main drawback is that a full charge takes more than two hours. The A53's photographic abilities are rather outstanding. It contains a 64MP f/1.8 primary camera and additional cameras of 12MP f/2.2 ultrawide, 5MP f/2.4 macro, and 5MP f/2.4 depth. There are phones that cost much more and have fewer photography choices.

The Galaxy A52's audio jack is gone, but it sports a single USB-C connector on the bottom. That's unfortunate because many people still like using wired headphones. The Galaxy A53 has its priorities all in order. With its enormous camera array and four unique lenses, you have a ton of alternatives. It is water resistant, has a fantastic screen, and has a long battery life. What you receive is pretty outstanding, considering the price.

4. Samsung Galaxy S22 Ultra

The S22 Ultra is the real heir to the Galaxy Note series since it offers all the great features of a Samsung phone without compromising on size, weight, or cost.

Even though it's not called the Galaxy Note, it's back in a major way. Samsung's Galaxy S22 Ultra (with prices beginning at $1,199.99) has all the distinguishing characteristics of the Note line, including a large body, potent technology, a long battery life, and, of course, an integrated S Pen pen.

The latest model outperforms both the Galaxy Note 20 Ultra and the Samsung Galaxy S21 Ultra from last year, if somewhat, and is a great option if you want the most phone you can get. The S22+, which offers many of the same capabilities as the Ultra at a lower price and in a more palatable form, is our top pick out of the three Galaxy S22 variants, though. The 6.8-inch, 1440 x 3088 AMOLED screen on the Samsung Galaxy S22 Ultra boasts a refresh rate of 120 Hz and a maximum brightness of 1750 nits. This is one of the nicest smartphone screens you'll discover in every way.

The Samsung Galaxy S22 Ultra features a big 5,000mAh battery, and regardless of how you used the phone, we discovered that it often lasted for a whole day. Additionally, it allows quick 45W charging; however, the packaging does not include a charger. On this list, the Samsung Galaxy S22 Ultra features the most complete camera system. It contains a 108MP primary camera, a 12MP ultra-wide, 10MP 2X zoom, and 10MP 10X zoom with genuine optical zoom.

Even 100X zoom shots are worth sharing, thanks to some great digital trickery that stabilizes lengthy zoom images and improves their clarity. The Samsung Galaxy S22 Ultra is capable of anything. Despite being waterproof, it comes with an integrated pen. It contains a functional 100X Space Zoom camera and a 108MP primary camera. It may function entirely as a desktop computer. The Galaxy S22 Ultra has everything you could possibly need if you're ready to pay for it.

5. One Plus 10 Pro

For OnePlus aficionados, the OnePlus 10 Pro is the best option. This relatively recent version offers a great display, lots of horsepowers, and quick charging capabilities. The best feature is that it is more reasonably priced than competing Pro phones. The OnePlus 10 Pro flails with a terrible software experience despite offering attractive photography features, rapid charging, and a great screen.

For those with high standards for smartphone design, OnePlus has long produced flagship models. This attitude was realized in the OnePlus 9 Pro, which combined a sophisticated design with a top-notch camera and lightweight software. The OnePlus 10 Pro ($899.99) regrettably seems a step backward. Although it is a gorgeous phone with great photos and quick charging, its present software is less appealing and useful than other Android phones. This is significant if, like me, you have chosen to use OnePlus phones in part due to their user-friendly software.

It's a delight to watch TV shows, play games, or consume social media information on the phone's 6.7-inch 2K Fluid AMOLED display with a 120Hz refresh rate. This is made possible in particular by the OnePlus' LTPO 2.0 technology for an automated refresh rate change and its high maximum brightness of 1,300 nits. The phone also supports HDR10+ and a billion different colors. The 10 Pro is identical to the 9 Pro in size and weight, measuring 6.42 by 2.91 by 0.34 inches (HWD) (6.42 by 2.89 by 0.34 inches, 6.9 ounces).

The phone comes in two finishes: cool greenish-blue or matte black. Before now, OnePlus has kept its pledge to make using a smartphone "burdenless" that founder Pete Lau made years ago. The shift from that ethos to OxygenOS 12 is significant, and the contrasts are clear.

To name a few, swiping down from the top of the screen frequently displays a widget shelf, the search bar in the app drawer is smaller, and the UI defaults to bigger app icons with rounded-square backgrounds and the OnePlus Sans typeface. It's merely a string of poor aesthetic decisions. However, the focus of the OnePlus flagships has always been on speed and style.

6. Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 4

The Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 4 has a premium feel and maintains your focus on your surroundings thanks to its crucial yet small front screen. For Samsung's large-screen Galaxy Z Fold, the third time was the charm (and the Galaxy Z Fold 4 is even better), while the smaller, more well-liked Flip required one more generation of development.

The Z Flip 4 ($999.99) fixes the battery problems of its predecessor while maintaining the same level of intrigue. I've seen this phone in airports, on the subway, and even at a public bus stop beyond a housing project in Queens. Samsung's mobile exec TM Roh frequently states that he wants foldable to be popular, and the Z Flip 4 is the closest model to that goal yet. Inside its little folding comes a massive 6.7-inch display.

The screen's max brightness of 1200 nits and 1080 x 2640 resolution is adequate. A 1.9-inch, 260 x 512-bit OLED panel on the cover screen, displays alerts and serves as a viewfinder for the primary camera while taking selfies. Because of its folding form and small construction, the Galaxy Z Flip 4's battery life was a little disappointing. A 3700 mAh cell is included.

However, it can only be charged at 25W. We utilize it all day long but end things the night before. The Galaxy Z Flip 4's cameras are disappointingly basic, with only two 12MP shooters in the back, but it has a hidden feature. Since the primary lens uses very big sensor pixels, images are clearer than you may anticipate.

You can also use the covering screen as a finder and point the primary camera straight at your face. Four standard colors are offered for Flip 4: Graphite, Pink Gold, Bora Purple, and Blue. A custom model with unique front and back panel color options is also available. For 75 possible permutations, you may include yellow, navy, red, khaki, or white elements. The custom model is $40 more expensive.

7. iPhone 14

The Apple A15 CPU from the previous year was housed within the iPhone 14 this year, so this chip was already more than capable of powering the greatest smartphones available. The iPhone 14 is one of our top selections despite lacking the superior cameras and screen of the iPhone 14 Pro due to Apple's best-in-class service and support and a high trade-in value in the future.

The 6.1-inch OLED screen of the iPhone 14 has a resolution of 1170 x 2532. It has a max brightness of 1200 nits. Because it's easier to grasp and Apple panels are consistently clear and vibrant, we prefer the smaller screen. The iPhone 14 has a rather long battery life despite having a tiny battery because of its tiny screen and well-optimized iOS 16. A day of usage between charges is reasonable. However, more could be stretching it.

The two back cameras on this device don't provide you much flexibility, but the AI optimization and dynamic range make up for it to deliver rather nice images.

The competent iPhone 14 is a smartphone with a gorgeous screen, a very speedy CPU, and decent cameras.

Although it is inferior to the iPhone 14 Pro, you still save $200, $250, or AU$ 350. This reliable, albeit unspectacular, iPhone may be for you if you're not seeking a large screen on a cheap (for that, consider the new iPhone 14 Plus). It has a few pretty exciting next-generation capabilities that you may never use. While the iPhone 14 shares many similarities with the iPhone 13, it has several internal changes that claim to give a better customer experience overall.

8. Google Pixel 7

The Google Tensor G2 chipset used in the Pixel 7 Pro is also used in the Google Pixel 7. That doesn't imply that it runs as quickly as Google's top products. Additionally, it means that you will receive future Google AI capabilities that are only available on Tensor phones, like as a better helper and photo unblurring. The Google Pixel 7 is a capable, well-made flagship phone that is also reasonably priced. With fewer compromises for the base model, Google's Pixel 7 costs cheaper than the Apple iPhone 14 and the Samsung Galaxy S22.

All of the unique software applications featured on the Pixel 7 Pro are available on the Pixel 7. Although the Pixel 7's screen isn't nearly as brilliant as the Pixel 7 Pro's excellent display, it's still incredibly pleasant to use. It is a 6.3-inch OLED with a maximum brightness of 1400 nits. In contrast to the iPhone 14, it features an always-on capability and a 1080 x 2400 pixel resolution.

We had no problems using the Pixel 7 for a day on a single charge, but not much longer. Its internal battery capacity is 4355 mAh and it has a 30W charging capacity. According to Google, charging may reach 50% in 30 minutes. Moreover, there is 20W wireless charging, which can wirelessly reverse-charge other devices. The Google Pixel 7 receives the same ultra-wide macro lens and primary camera as the Pixel 7 Pro.

9. iPhone SE

The iPhone SE (2022) offers Apple enthusiasts a more affordable way to possess one of the greatest phones money can buy even though iPhones are often pricey. This phone recently received a good upgrade from Apple and considering its inexpensive cost and use of the same internal components as the iPhone 14, it performs well.

Although it's not Apple's most capable iPhone, this model offers the greatest value for those looking to upgrade to iOS 16 on a budget. Compared to the finest iPhones, it has a significantly smaller screen, which some people could find appealing. In contrast to the recent iPhones, it still includes the traditional home button with a fingerprint sensor.

This updated iPhone SE is larger than the original iPhone SE but yet manages to fit in one hand thanks to its 4.7-inch screen, which was taken from the iPhone 7 and iPhone 8. That has become crucial for those who have been calling for a thinner bezel, especially because the iPhone 14 mini has not been updated.

If you're not a heavy user (who would probably choose the iPhone 11 series anyhow), you'll be satisfied with Apple's battery life.

It won't last you more than a day, but even with continuous use with 5G running all day, you can get up to 12 hours' worth of battery life. The iPhone SE (2022) is quicker and more linked than its predecessor, and it is now the most cost-effective new 5G iPhone available. But if you want an iPhone with larger displays, sharper cameras, and more cutting-edge features, search elsewhere.

Basically, the A15 Bionic makes every aspect of this phone great, including the 4K video recording and editing, console-quality gameplay, and AI-enhanced photos. It pushes the limits of what's feasible within the restrictions set by the 2017 design. This 5G smartphone offers excellent performance and connection. This is a time-tested, classic design. Using Touch ID is just like receiving a long-lost buddy home. Even better, none of it functions or behaves like technology from the past. Even today, the Touch ID is a remarkable technological achievement.

10. iPhone 13/ iPhone 13 Mini

Apple continues to offer older models at a discount while the iPhone 14 is ready, and the iPhone 13 remains one of the company's top smartphones. The iPhone 13 mini is the only option to acquire the smallest possible iPhone with the most functionality because Apple didn't release an iPhone 14 tiny. The iPhone 13 and iPhone 13 mini, although being last year's models, have many of the same fantastic features as the iPhone 14 and employ the same CPU.

The cameras have a strong track record for battery life and produce beautiful images. For a lower cost, you get everything an iPhone would offer. The larger iPhone 13 utilizes a 6.1-inch screen, while the smaller iPhone 13 mini uses a 5.4-inch screen. Both panels are incredibly crisp and have a maximum brightness of 1200 nits. Although the iPhone 13 mini has a smaller screen and a lesser resolution, it boasts a greater PPI density. The iPhone 13 mini's battery is slightly less than its larger sister's. Thus, it might need to be recharged more frequently.

The cameras of the iPhone 13 and iPhone 13 mini are identical. While their primary and ultra-wide cameras perform admirably, they lack a telephoto zoom. Apple's sensor-side image stabilization, which is often seen on pro-DSLR cameras, is included in the iPhone 13. There are several shooting modes and functions available, so there is a lot to experiment with and enjoy.

The iPhone 13 is a significant version that delivers greater battery capacity, a better CPU, and an updated camera configuration over iPhones that have gone before it. It is hardly a key differentiator for Apple's series of smartphones. This is a great option if you want a quick and powerful smartphone but don't require the added features of the more expensive Pro model.

Conclusion

67% of the world's population-5.2 billion people-would have mobile service subscriptions by the end of 2019. By 2025, this is expected to increase to 5.8 billion users, or 70% of the world's population, mostly in India, China, Pakistan, and Nigeria. In the meantime time, the connectivity gap is narrowing: over the last five years, mobile broadband has expanded to almost 1 billion more people, and by 2025, 1.2 billion more people will start using mobile internet, increasing the total number of subscribers to 5 billion (more than 60% of the population).

Mobile continues to contribute significantly to the global economy because of this development in connectivity. A critical year for 5G, 2019 saw the launch of the technology in 24 markets and a spike in customer interest in upgrading to the technology. By 2025, 5G connections will make up 20% of all connections worldwide, with adoption rates highest in developed Asia, North America, and Europe. In 2019, 52% of all connections used 4G, making it the most popular mobile technology worldwide.

Over the next several years, this percentage will rise, reaching a peak of slightly under 60% in 2023. The mobile sector contributed 4.5 trillion US dollars to the global GDP in 2021, or 5% of the global GDP. By 2025, according to the source, this will rise to 4.9 trillion dollars.

The Indian mobile market is anticipated to expand at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 23.8% from 2020 to 2023, reaching 495 million units by the end of the year, according to research by IDC. This would help India's total economy develop, which is anticipated to rise by 7.6% by 2022.
