Best Bike in India

No matter where you reside in India, congested streets and heavy traffic are a daily occurrence. A bike is an excellent alternative for commuting regularly. Indians prefer 2-wheelers over 4-wheelers because the bike is less expensive than a car, requires less upkeep, and is well-suited to India's roads. The following is a list of the top best bikes for everyday usage.

1. Bajaj Pulsar 200 NS

This is one of the top 200cc motorcycles on the market. The NS stands for "Naked Sports" in this bike's name. The bike comes in a variety of eye-catching color schemes and decal designs.

The SOHC, 4-valve-Triple Spark engine on the Bajaj Pulsar 200NS delivers 23.17 bhp at 9500 rpm while riding. At 9500 pm, the torque output is 23.17 bhp. The bike has a ground clearance of 167 millimeters, and the bike has a 199.5cc engine. According to the manufacturer's claims, the Bajaj Pulsar 200NS gets 58 kmpl on the highway and 35 kmpl in the city.

2. The Honda shine CB

On the sales front, this two-wheeled Honda shines. With sales of 1,16,222 units, it has risen to fourth place, beating the Bajaj Pulsar by 18,642 units.

The CB Shine was one of the first motorcycles in India to get the BS6 modification. This single-cylinder air-cooled 124.73cc engine now produces 10.88hp, up from the previous 10.31hp rating. The BS6 CB Shine's slight increase in power is offset by the inclusion of electronic fuel injection, which results in a more precise throttle response.

3. The 350cc Royal Enfield Classic

Old is gold, and The Royal Enfield Classic 350 motorcycle is a great example. Demand for the iconic thumper is picking up, with January sales of 40,872 units. This machine continues to be a top seller for heavy metal fans, having sold 2,93,421 units in the first 10 months of 2021.

To produce 19.8 horsepower and 28 nm of torque, the BS6 version of the Classic 350 has an injection system that utilizes fuel injectors. The new Classic is roughly Rs 11,000 more costly than the BS4 standard, with pricing beginning at Rs 1,78,000.

4. The Revolt RV400

While the Revolt RV400 seems to be a regular sports motorbike, it does not produce any noise when the ignition is turned on because it's an electric bike. This electric vehicle has a top speed of 85 km/h and a range of up to 150 kilometers per charge.

With a detachable 3.24 kW battery, the bike can be recharged indoors in under 5 hours. Disc brakes are used on both the front and rear wheels for further security. The monthly fee for a Revolt RV 400 is more than 3,000, making it seem to be less expensive. It costs more than Rs 1.40,000 on the market.

5. The KTM Duke 390

A new Bajaj motorcycle, the KTM 390 Duke, has arrived in India. KTM 390 Duke is a naked type bike with stunning style and delivers exceptional performance. With a standard ABS, the price is awesome. The claimed fuel economy for the KTM 390 Duke is 22-25 km/ltr. 44 BHP and 35 Nm of torque are available from a 375cc, four-stroke, single-cylinder, liquid-cooled-fuel-injected engine.

The weight of the engine is 36 kg. The manufacturer says that it can accelerate from 0 to 100 kilometers per hour in under seven seconds. The 6-speed gearbox provides excellent transmission. Improved ride quality on Indian roads may be achieved with a ground clearance of 172mm.

6. Kawasaki Ninja 300

Kawasaki Ninja 300 is one of the most popular sports motorcycles among young men. On the road, the Kawasaki Ninja 300 has a four-stroke, parallel-twin engine that produces 39 horsepower and 27 nm of torque at 11000 rpm. It cost a bit more than Rs 3 lakh, but it was well worth it.

This bike's highway mileage is 28.00 km/L, and its city mileage is roughly 22 km/L. The engine provides excellent performance with a 6-speed manual transmission and a wet multi-disc. Go for the ordinary version of this bike without ABS if your budget is less than 3 lakh. This motorcycle has a top speed of 160kmph. Rs. 3.7 lakhs is the price of the ABS version of the Kawasaki Ninja 300. ABS is a new addition, but else it is identical to the original Ninja 300. The Ninja 250R is being phased out in favour of this model.

7. Hyosung GT 250R

While riding, the Hyosung GT 250r can generate a maximum of 27.61 Bhp at 10,000 rpm and an average torque of 22.07 Nm from its 249cc, V-twin, four-stroke engine. The engine's stated displacement is 249 cubic centimeters. On the highway, you'll receive 30.00 kmpl, while in the city, you'll get 25 kmpl.

Manual gearbox with 5 Speed engine. Front and rear disc brakes with a combined diameter of 300 mm and 230 mm provide the finest in a class of stopping power. Size-wise, it is 2060.00 mm long, 655.00 mm wide, 1125.00 mm tall, and weighs 177.00 kg. Alloys, tubeless tires, and a 130.00-mm ground clearance are just some other characteristics. All three color variants of the Hyosung GT 250r are available.

8. The KTM Duke 200

The KTM 200 has 199.50cc of power and comes in white and black. Thanks to the trellis frame, this bike has a sleek and refined appearance. There are LED indicators and a brake light for Duke 200. The Duke's tank design is the most striking element of the motorcycle, and it lends the machine an air of sophistication. Duke's tanks are sculpted to the knees, making them suitable for riding. The engine's torque is enough, providing an exhilarating riding experience.

The bike's mileage ranges from 35 to 45 kilometers per gallon. If they are broken, you'll have to shell out a lot of money to fix things like disc pads, mirrors, and footpegs. Overall, the Duke 200 is a fantastic motorcycle that provides the rider with a wonderful experience.

9. Honda CBR 150R

The Honda CBR150R, sometimes known as the "baby CBR," is a fashionably styled motorcycle by Honda. The bike comes in two eye-catching color schemes: regular and deluxe. While riding, the Honda CBR150R will generate 17.58 Bhp and 12.66 Nm of torque at 10500 rpm and 8500 rpm, respectively.

The fuel-efficient engine of the Honda CBR150R delivers a mileage of 45 kmpl on the highway and 32 kmpl in the city. The engine has a 149.4cc capacity. Powered by a six-speed gearbox, the vehicle can deliver strong performance. This motorcycle can reach a maximum speed of 132 kilometers per hour.

10. Yamaha YZF-R15 version 2.0

This new Yamaha R15 is equipped with an all-new, 149.8cc, 4-stroke engine that produces 16.8 Bhp at 8500 rpm and 15.8 Nm at 7500 rpm of torque. This bike has a 149.8-cubic-inch engine. Power and performance are maximized with a six-speed manual transmission.

Thirteen seconds is all it takes for the bike to reach 100 kilometers per hour. To be competitive, it must go up against rivals like the Bajaj Pulsar 220 and Honda CBR 250R, which have almost identical power outputs. A 42kmpl highway rating and a 32kmpl city rating are possible with Yamaha R15 New Version 2.0.

Only one version of the Yamaha R15, New Version 2.0, is available. The bike's long wheelbase and wide, thick tires provide excellent traction and a secure ride. Disc brakes are installed on the bike's front and back wheels. There is a front-mounted telescopic suspension and a rear-mounted linked Monocross suspension.

11. Hero Karizma ZMR

Hero moto corporation saw a growing market for full-fairing motorcycles, particularly those with an aggressive appearance and an appealing sports bike design; it introduced the Karizma line of bikes in India. There are presently two models of Hero Karizma motorcycles available in India.

Hero Karizma R and Hero Karizma ZMR are their names. It has a 223cc, 4-stroke engine that produces 17.6 Bhp at 7000 rpm and 18.35 Nm at 6000 rpm while you're on the road. Multiple Wet Clutch and 5-speed manual gearbox. Just 3.8 seconds later, the bike reaches a top speed of 62kmph. The system's acceleration and performance are excellent. A peak speed of 125 mph is within reach. There is a ground clearance of 150mm. The Karizma ZMR bike's official mileage is 35-45kmpl. For Karizma ZMR, you have a choice of four different colors.

12. Bajaj Pulsar 220

A powerful sporty bike from Bajaj, the Pulsar 220. A black color scheme dominates the "fastest Indian" bike's design and styling. A variety of color options are available for the Pulsar 220 from the Bajaj Pulsar brand. 2035.00 mm x 750.00 x 1165.00 mm (length x width x height) is the dimensions of this two-seater, and weighing 150 kg.

The vehicle has a 165mm clearance from the ground. When riding, the DTS-I engine produces 20.8 bhp at 8500 rpm and 19.12 Nm at 7000 rpm, giving it plenty of power and torque. It had a five-speed transmission. The Bajaj Pulsar 220F has an average fuel economy of 44 kilometers per liter. The bike is capable of a peak speed of 144 kilometers per hour.
