27 Best Tourist Places in Guwahati

Guwahati is a world-famous tourist destination in Assam for those looking to enjoy the spirit of Northeast India. With its various hotels, activities, and attractions, the city provides guests with various needs. Because of its accessibility, Guwahati is a wonderful city. So, if you're planning a trip to Guwahati, you'll find lots of good things to do. This list only includes the finest of the best, ensuring that your experience here is nothing short of enjoyable. Important visitor information, such as entrance times and fees, is provided for each place to visit featured.

1. Kamakhya Temple: Famous Shakti Peeth.

Kamakhya Temple is a prominent tourist destination not just in Guwahati but across Assam, and Kamakhya Temple is a prominent Hindu pilgrimage place and one of the 51 Shakti Peethas. Kamakhya Temple, one of the most popular locations in Guwahati, is a unique temple with a special festival. The temple devoted to Devi Kamakhya is closed for five days since the goddess lady is supposed to ovulate during her menstruation. The temple is opened to worshippers on the sixth day after the god has been cleansed and completed rituals. Locals assemble for the major fair on this day, filled with festivities. Kamakhya Temple, one of Guwahati's most prominent tourist attractions, is yet another temple with a unique festival.

Time to entry: 8:00 am - 1:00 pm. and 2:30 am - 5:30 pm.

There is no entrance cost.

2. Assam State Museum: Learn about Assam culture.

Everyone should see the museum since Assamese culture is celebrated throughout the country. As the title suggests, the Assam State Museum focuses on promoting history and culture via its distinctive exhibits. This is one of the best sites to tour in Guwahati if you want to learn more about the cultural way of life. The Assam State Museum in Guwahati is one of the city's highly visited attractions.

It was erected in 1940 and contained several galleries, including iconography, archaeology, and epigraphy, to mention a few. The museum has sculptures, indigenous artworks, and textiles on display. From the 5th to 12th centuries, Surya's sculptures are a highlight.

Time to entry: 10:00 am - 5:00 pm.

The entry cost is INR 10.

3. Umananda Temple: Shiva Temple is a famous Hindu temple.

This is a must-see attraction in Guwahati. Umananda Temple is a joy and one of Guwahati's greatest tourist attractions. The Hindu god Shiva is honored at Umananda Temple, located on Peacock Island, which is the Brahmaputra River encircles. As a holy place destination, there will always be believers in the area, but the festival of Shivratri has a special attraction.

King Gadadhar Singh commissioned the temple in 1694 AD by Anno Domini, and the carvings and sculptures continue to entice visitors today. An earthquake damaged certain parts of the temple in 1897, but a local merchant later repaired it.

Time to entry: 5:30 am - 6:00 pm.

There is no entrance cost.

4. Nehru Park: Ambience of Lush Green.

Nehru Park, one of Guwahati's tourist attractions, is perfect for families. The park's lush greenery is relaxing and will ensure a wonderful time throughout your stay. The youngsters will enjoy this location since they can ride several swings. Concrete statues represent dances such as Deodhani, Bihu, Ojapali, and Bor Taal around the park. You also have an accessible theater where you may see performances by local performers if you have the time. One of Guwahati's most romantic locales is Nehru Park.

Time to entry: 10:00 am - 8:00 pm.

The entry cost is INR 10.

5. Guwahati Planetarium: Delight for Kids.

You'll have the most fun at the Guwahati Planetarium, which is another must-see in Guwahati for families with children. It provides both children and parents with an engaging learning experience. Officials have arranged various astronomy events to offer people a picture of life in space. Visit the planetarium and be ready to be astounded by the field of science.

Time to entry: 12:00 pm - 4:00 pm.

The entry cost is INR 30 and for students INR 15.

6. Guwahati Zoo: Spectacular Flora and Fauna.

The Hengrabari Reserved Forest is home to Guwahati Zoo, commonly known as the Assam State Zoo and Botanical Garden. Guwahati Zoo occupies 432 acres and is home to 895 animals, birds, and reptiles worldwide. Guwahati Zoo is one of the most prominent tourist destinations in Guwahati. The major attractions of Guwahati Zoo are the one-horned Indian rhinoceros, leopard cat, tiger, golden langur, clouded leopard, elephant, and Himalayan black bear. If you enjoy nature and animals, this is one of the greatest locations to visit in Guwahati.

Time to entry: 8:00 am - 5:30 pm.

The entry cost is INR 10.

7. Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary:

Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary is one of the most famous tourist destinations in Guwahati. For everyone who appreciates Guwahati Zoo, Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary is a must-see destination. It's 30 kilometers from Guwahati and perfect for those looking for a true wildlife experience. Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary, which covers an area of 38 km and is a place of residence for the Indian One-horned Rhinoceros, has diverse wildlife. Approximately 2000 migrating birds and reptiles make the sanctuary their temporary residence.

Time to entry: 10:00 am - 5:00 pm.

The entry cost is INR 450.

8. Navagraha Temple:

Between 1923 and 1945, Ahom King Rajeswar Singha built the temple. This is one of the most prominent destinations to visit in Guwahati for those with a spiritual mindset. Nine Shiva Lingas devoted to the nine different celestial bodies may be found at Guwahati's Navagraha Temple (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, North Lunar Node, and South Lunar Node).

Time to entry: 6:00 am - 12:30 pm and 4:00 pm - 8:00 pm.

There is no entrance cost.

9. Madan Kamdev: Kamarupa's Pala Dynasty.

Madan Kamdev is a 9th and a 10th-century archaeological site located near Kamrup (25 km) from Guwahati. Madan Kamdev is one of the top Guwahati tourist attractions for those interested in history and mythology. The ruins here date back to Kamarupa's Pala dynasty and are dispersed across a large region. The monuments in this area largely consist of temples in Guwahati, which vary in size. The location is one of Guwahati's most popular tourist destinations.

Time to entry: 6:00 am - 9:00 pm.

10. Fancy Bazaar: A Shopaholic's Paradise.

Visit Fancy Bazaar, sometimes known as the 'Chandni Chowk of Guwahati,' if you want to snoop about Guwahati's marketplaces. Everything is available here, from trendy clothing to traditional jewelry, stationery, and unique wooden furniture. It is one of the most vibrant spots to visit in Guwahati with friends, with several vendors, businesses, and roadside arrangements.

Time to entry: 10:00 am - 9:00 pm.

11. ISKCON Guwahati: Moments of Spirituality

ISKCON Guwahati, also known as Shri Shri Rukmini Krishna, is a popular tourist destination in Guwahati. ISKCON Guwahati, situated on the summit of Ulubari Chariali, offers spectacular views of the whole region. You may also spend enlightening hours here in the beautiful gardens, where you can relax and listen to religious speeches.

Time to entry: 8:00 am - 8:00 pm.

12. Haajo: Soulful Visits.

Haajo or Hajo is a popular tourist destination in Guwahati since it is the traditional gathering place for three major Indian religions: Hinduism, Buddhism, and Islam. The location lies on the shore of the Brahmaputra River and is about 24 kilometers from Guwahati's large city. The most prominent attraction here is Hayagriva Madhava Temple. You may also go shopping in Guwahati by visiting the surrounding marketplaces.

Time to entry: 24 hours

There is no entrance cost.

13. Tila Hills:

The neighboring town of Pandu is another excellent destination to visit in Guwahati. The location was called after King Pandu, the Pandavas' father. The Pandunath Temple, dedicated to Pandu, is located in Tila Hills. During their exile, the Pandavas are reported to have resided here. Plan a trip to this peaceful spot to see Guwahati's gorgeous beauty.

There is no entrance cost.

14. Bhuvaneshwari Temple: Panoramic Views

It is one of Guwahati's most famous tourist spots. The Goddess Bhuvaneshwari Temple is devoted to Goddess Bhuvaneshwari and is situated on a mountaintop with panoramic views of Guwahati. You may come here in the evenings to see the beautiful sunsets with the River Brahmaputra, increasing the location's beauty.

Time to entry: 5:30 am - 8:00 pm.

There is no entrance cost.

15. Regional Science Centre: It's Time to Study.

The Regional Science Centre in Guwahati's Jawaharnagar Khanapara is run by the Indian government's National Council of Science Museums. If you visit Guwahati with your children, you must visit this fascinating scientific center with unique treasures.

Time to entry: 10:00 AM - 5:30 PM

The entry cost is INR 25.

16. Kaziranga National Park: Rhinos with a single horn.

The Kaziranga is within 430 square kilometers of Guwahati, Assam, Nameri National Park, and Manas National Park. The world's second-largest population of one-horned rhinos, as well as a variety of other creatures, may be found in Kaziranga National Park. Kaziranga has meadows, grasslands, marshy lagoons, and deep forests that are home to a diverse range of wildlife and floral species on the shores of the Brahmaputra River. A safari trip, a bird-watching activity, or a stay at a lodge are all options among the dense jungles. The park was established in 1908 in the Golaghat and Nagaon districts of the Eastern Himalayan ecological regions. Some important animal species in the park region include the Great Indian One-Horned Rhino, Royal Bengal Tiger, Swamp Deer, Pygmy Hog, Hispid Hare, Gangetic Dolphin, elephant, and Wild Buffalo.

The fee for entrance is INR 100.

This facility is open 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

17. Dispur: City of the Capital.

Dispur, Assam's capital, is famous for its beautiful scenery, strong tradition, and rich culture. Because of its closeness to Guwahati, Guwahati visitors regularly frequented the location. The area is well-known for its tea garden marketplaces and nice weather that can be enjoyed anytime. The event is free to attend.

The facility is open 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

18. Manas National Park: Get to Know the Wild Cats.

Manas National Park is another large national park near Guwahati. It is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and it is well-known for its participation in Project Tiger Reserve. It is the area's only tiger reserve and a haven for golden langur and red pandas. It is, without a doubt, one of Guwahati's most well-known attractions.

Time to enter: 6:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m.

The entry fee is INR 20.

19. Dipor Bil: Freshwater Lake.

Dipor Bil is the easiest way from Guwahati and is located in the Kamrup district. It is a freshwater lake with a 40-square-kilometer surface area. Due to its significant wildlife, the area was officially designated as a bird sanctuary in 1989. Because of its gorgeous environment, it is one of Guwahati's most popular tourist destinations.

Time to enter: 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

There is no entrance cost.

20. Umananda Island: The little River Island.

Umananda Island is the world's smallest river island in the middle of the Brahmaputra River. During the British Raj, it was known as 'Peacock Island,' reflecting the island's beauty and peacefulness. This island is perhaps one of the best spots to visit in Guwahati for couples looking for a peaceful environment in a distant area.

Time to enter: Throughout the day

There is no entrance cost.

21. Basistha Ashram: Excursions of Peace.

Guru Vashishta, a legendary sage from Hindu mythology, is honored at the Basistha Ashram in Guwahati. According to locals, this Ashram was created by the guru, who lived here until his death. Because of its distinctive architecture and fascinating atmosphere, this venue is well worth seeing.

Time to enter: 6:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

There is no entrance cost.

22. Indira Gandhi Athletic Stadium: Football Fans Will Love This Place.

Indira Gandhi Athletic Stadium, popularly called Sarusajai Stadium, is a favorite destination for football fans in Guwahati. In 2017, this stadium hosted the FIFA U-17 World Cup, boosting its popularity. Due to its vastness, the 65th Filmfare Awards were hosted at the Indira Gandhi Athletic Stadium. This stadium was built in 2007 and is well-known among tourists.

23. Guwahati War Memorial: A Salute to the Martyrs.

Guwahati War Memorial is a must-see for everyone interested in learning more about Guwahati's historical past. Guwahati War Memorial is not just one of Guwahati's most popular tourist spots but also among history lovers. The Rajya Sainik Board established this memorial in honor of all martyrs. This war memorial opened to the public in December 2016 and has drawn many tourists due to its stunning architecture.

From 9 am until 9 pm, visitors can enter.

24. Srimanta Sankardev Kalakshetra: The Artistic Side.

In Guwahati, there is a cultural institution known as Srimanta Sankardev Kalakshetra. This institution was established to promote Assamese culture and art in its different forms. There is a fantastic exhibition of history in the middle that one may experience. In the year 1990, the Kalakshetra was created. Srimanta Sankardev Kalakshetra provides an opportunity to consider ethnic harmony, historic preservation, and promotion. The Srimanta Sankardev Kalakshetra includes a museum, a children's park, an art gallery, and a large library.

Time to enter: 10 am to 7 pm.

The entry fee for adults pay INR 30, youngsters under 14 years pay INR 10, while foreigners pay INR 100.

25. Sukreswar Temple: Gorgeous place.

The temple, devoted to the Hindu God Shiva, was erected in 1744 by then-Ahom King Pramatta Singhathe. The Itakhuli hill is the location of the Sukreswar Temple. This prominent location may see the amazing views of the surrounding Brahmaputra river and the valley below. One may descend the stairs from the temple and reach the river's beautiful ghats. The river provides wonderful scenery with gentle waves and distant little boats. Even though many people come to this temple to perform post-death rites, it is filled with quiet and serenity.

Time to enter: 6 am to 7 pm.

26. Chandubi Lake: All-Around There Is Silence.

Chandubi Lake is located in the Garo Hills' foothills. It is one of the top destinations to visit in Guwahati since it is bordered on one side by Assam and the other by Meghalaya. Chandubi is surrounded by beautiful greenery, tea gardens, and lovely towns. Chandubi Lake is a great place to visit if you want to relax and have fun. For couples and families who wish to spend quality time together, Chandubi Lake is wonderful. The lake, which covers a massive 2000 hectares of land, has its unique attraction.

Time to enter: am to 6 pm.

27. Saraighat Bridge: A Photographer's Paradise.

Saraighat Bridge is the first of its kind on the Brahmaputra River. Saraighat Bridge is a railway-to-road bridge that was completed in 1962. This bridge is one of the most important features in the country's Northeast region. The bridge connects the region and the rest of the country. In Guwahati, this bridge is one of the country's oldest and most well-known tourist attractions. It is approximately 1.4 kilometers long and connects the shorelines in Saraighat. It is a breath-taking sight to see.
