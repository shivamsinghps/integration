CL: Casual Leave

CL Stands for Casual Leave. The leave policies and guidelines vary from state to state. Earned, casual, and ill leaves are the many kinds of leaves regularly granted in India. These leaves are available to employees without causing them to lose any income.

According to India's leave policy, each employee is entitled to a specific number of annual leave days regardless of their industry. According to the earned and Casual Leave guidelines, the employer must provide the required number of leaves to their staff members.

Depending on the state, the employer, the sector, whether the business is covered by The Shops and Establishment Act or The Factories Act, and the number and types of leaves provided to each employee.

Each state has its leave policies and regulations. Earned leaves, casual leaves, and ill leaves are the many sorts of leaves that are frequently awarded in India. These leaves are available to employees without causing any loss of pay. Let's examine the guidelines for earned and casual Leave now.

What does Casual Leave (CL) mean?

An eligible employee is given casual Leave (CL) if an unanticipated circumstance prevents them from reporting to work. If an eligible employee needs a few days off for personal reasons but not a vacation, they can also use casual Leave.

Regarding casual leave policies, businesses might be strict and only permit three days in a given month. Additionally, the employee should use an attendance and time management system to request approval before taking this kind of Leave. The employer compensates for casual leaves.

Various Guidelines for Casual Leave:

  • Can generally be taken Casual leaves for a minimum of half a day and a maximum of three days. Should take Leaves as either earned or privileged leaves. The employee must get approval from the employer if they want to use all three leaves at once.
  • Employees who work for companies registered under the Shops and Establishments Act are eligible for six days of Leave without pay.
  • Maybe carry over No casual leaves to the next year. Casual leave regulations provide that new casual leaves expire immediately once the year is over.
  • It is impossible to cash in unused casual leaves the same way earned or privileged leaves may. They just become invalid at the end of the year.
  • Cannot combine Casual leaves with earned leaves or ill leaves.
  • The casual leaves are distributed proportionally to new hires and recently terminated employees. For example, if an employee starts working for the organization on July 1, which falls in the middle of the year, the number of casual leaves available will be half. It will be valid until December 31, which falls on the final day of that year.

How can One take Earned Leave (EL)?

As the names indicate, earned leaves (EL) are the vacation days employees earn after working for their employer for more than 240 days in a given year. You can utilize these leaves for vacation or travel. Employers may keep track of an employee's spent and unused vacation time by using a good attendance and leave management system.

The following are a few prominent guidelines for earned Leave:

  • For the calendar year, earned Leave is calculated every month. However, the number of leaves an employee is entitled to also depends on how many months they worked. Ideally, earned leaves are credited to the employee's leave account at the beginning of the calendar year. For instance, under the employer's leave policy, an employee's leave account is increased by 1.25 days after one month of service.
  • The eligible earned leaves shall be calculated on a pro-rata basis from the employee's start date to December 31 of the same year if they are hired in the middle of the calendar year.
  • The allotted leaves are computed pro rata until the last working day if an employee resigns. The ultimate settlement sum will be adjusted if the employee has taken any more leaves.
  • Unused earned leaves will be rolled over to the following year if employees can only use some of their eligible earned leaves in a given calendar year. However, the amount of leaves that may carry over to the next year is limited. Each employer has various carry-forward regulations, depending on the state and region. They may accumulate A total of 30 days' worth of leaves. The Shops and Establishment Act allows it to increase by 45 to 60 days, depending on the state's policy.
  • When an employee quits their job, or their accrued or unused leaves reach a certain limit, they are immediately cashed. The firm leave policy determines the encashment process in India.
  • Amount encashed = monthly gross wage * total encashable leaves divided by 22 is the method for calculating encashable earned leaves.
  • According to the regulations, cashing out leaves while working is taxed. State and federal government employees are completely tax-free on any leave encashment payments they obtain. The exemption amount has a limit in all other circumstances.

A comparison between earned leaves and privileged leaves:

The differences between privilege and earned management and employees should understand leaves. Both leaves perform the same role and are of a similar type.

However, the following features set them apart:

  • Employee leave benefits are earned leaves (EL) under the Factories Act and privilege leaves (PL) under the Shops and Establishments Act.
  • According to the Factories Act, a single earned leave is awarded every 20 working days or 18 leaves annually. According to the Shops and Establishment Act, five privilege leaves are given after four months of employment for 15 leaves per year.
