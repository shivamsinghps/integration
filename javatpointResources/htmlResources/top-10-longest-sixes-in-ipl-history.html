It might be exciting to watch the IPL. Any cricket fan might get enthused by the fast-paced nature of the game and the incredible and enormous sixes that are being struck by batters. We'll take a look at the ten longest and biggest sixes in the IPL era in this post, which will be discussed for many years to come. But before moving forward, firstly, we start with the concept of IPL.

What is IPL?

IPL, a T20 cricket competition first formed in 2008. The tournament will be extended to consist of a total of 10 teams. It is typically contested from April through June each year. Every franchise will face the other twice-at home and away-during the round-robin event that precedes the Indian Premier League. Teams get one point apiece if the game is abandoned, whereas sides receive two factors for victories.

The top 4 teams in the standings will advance to the playoffs at the conclusion of this phase, while the other teams will be eliminated. The first qualifier, which pits the top two teams against one another and determines who advances directly to the championship, kicks off the playoffs.

The eliminator, which pits the third and fourth-placed teams against one another, comes up next. The victor advances to the next qualifying round, while the loser gets disqualified. The champion of the elimination squares off against the losers of the initial playoff match in the subsequent qualifier. The game's victor moves on to the championship round.

When does the IPL Begin?

The IPL's specific start dates change from year to year although they usually fall during the first week of April. IPL 2022 takes off on March 26. Beginning with the group stages, the tournament continues through the rest of April and into May.

The elimination rounds start, and the final is typically planned for late May or early June. The competition has operated under this format for many years; however, these dates are susceptible to change.

Player Auction

Every tournament's preparation phase includes a player auction. The franchises will compete for the skills of the players who wish to participate in the IPL by bidding on them during the auction.

An agreed-upon base price, which serves as a kind of reserve amount, will be determined by the participants. That introductory price must be the minimum offer from any club interested in signing the player.

Squads are put together before the next season when the auction is over. Of course, teams may choose to substitute players for those forced to leave due to injury or other circumstances. The franchisees turn in a retained list prior to the start of that auction. These are the squad members that have already committed to the club for the next season. Once freed, other players can enter the auction if their services are no longer required.
