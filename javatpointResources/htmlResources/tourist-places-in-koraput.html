Tourist Places in Koraput

One of the most popular areas and tourist attractions is Koraput. This location is stunning by nature; the town and District of Koraput are surrounded by mountains, hills, dense forests, and waterfalls.

As a result, it is a well-known tourist destination in Odisha, attracting visitors each year from all over the world and from other Indian states to enjoy Koraput's exceptional natural beauty. However, Koraput's forests are losing various wild creatures and tribal groups, the primary draw of Koraput is its tribal communities, sometimes called "Adivasis," who are the area's original occupants and have their own unique tribal culture, festivals, arts and crafts, music, and dancing.

Presently, certain tribal people still live in Koraput in a very uncomplicated and traditional manner, which draws tourists to the area. Tourists desire to engage with these isolated from nature individuals.

1. Gupteshwar Caves

A limestone cave was found called the Gupteshwar Caves a few years ago. The Shivalinga, which is believed to be growing in size, is the location's big attraction. Visitors to this cave come from all across the nation.

Every year, the renowned Bolbom yatra is held here. The Ramgiri trees encircle the caverns. By going up a set of stairs, one can reach the caves. The Gupteshwar Caves, also known as Gupta Kedar in some parts of the country, are associated with Lord Rama from the Ramayana.

The lingam inside the limestone caves, which are devoted to Lord Shiva, is known as a lingam. According to mythology, the lingam was initially found by Lord Rama while travelling with Sita and his brother Laxman in the former Dandakaranya jungle.

2. Tribal Museum

In Koraput, the tribal museum is located behind the Jagannath temple. The museum has a variety of images and items from tribal customs and cultures. Visitors may find a variety of sculptures, artefacts, jewellery, pictures, and anthropological objects here.

Additionally, the museum has a library. Another museum in Koraput is the Government Museum, housed in the town hall, which houses artefacts from the Jain era, antiquities, and handmade items.

3. Duduma Waterfall

The waterfall is 175 meters high. The Machhkund river creates the waterfall. Mountains and green valleys around the region. The location is often referred to as "Matsya Tirtha."

Numerous tourists are captivated by this location by its lovely environment. Another tourist destination close to the Duduma waterfall is Ankadeli, three kilometres from Duduma, where members of the tribal community sell forest items to tourists.

4. Deomali: Mountain Peak

The tallest mountain peak is Deomali, which rises to 1672 meters above sea level. In addition to being a popular tourist destination, the area is also used by campers and sports enthusiasts for activities including trekking, mountaineering, and hang gliding. Visitors need a two-hour journey from the city of Koraput.

This mountain range is home to several tribes, including the Kansas, Barajas, Bhumia, Malis, and Bhotias. The region is rich in resources, including bauxite, limestone, and gemstones. The hills are also filled with brooks and deep valleys. The most representative example of Odisha's fantastical magnificence is the view over the valley from the top of Deomali.

5. Hatipathar Waterfall

The waterfall is fantastic, and thick trees encircle the area. It is a lovely site with two waterfalls on the river Nagavali. The two enormous rocks on the hilltop are like massive elephants. And a great spot to hang out with the family. Hatipathar, a deep-cut valley, is a lovely location for a picnic and a well-known tourist destination.

Tourists come to Hatipathar, famous for its surroundings and the two waterfalls the Nagavali River produces. There are several well-liked picnic areas nearby.

6. Jagannath Sagar

In the middle of the town of Jeypore is Jagannath Sagar's lake. There is also a park and play area around the lake and a boating club. In 1716, 10,000 labourers were engaged by the then-King Ramchandra Dev-II to construct a pond on 300 acres. The British East India Company declared the pond a tourist spot three years later.

The pond was constructed to maintain ecological balance and provide water to locals in the Koraput and Nabarangpur districts. Its beautiful attractiveness attracted a significant number of tourists until now.

7. Kolab Botanical Garden

On the banks of the Kolab dam, the Kolab Botanical Garden is a well-liked destination for weekend picnics. There are over 200 different flowering plant kinds in the well-kept garden. The Kolab Reservoir looks beautiful at night. Here, boating is possible, and everyone enjoys going there to go out with their family.

8. Maligura Tunnel

Maligura is a native word that translates to "Village of Malis." The Malis are a native group with a distinct culture and way of life from the other tribes in Orissa. The tallest wide gauge railway tunnel in India, a highly famous tourist destination, has been constructed close to this tribal village.

The monsoon season is the ideal time to visit Maliguda Tunnel. The tunnel is even more magnificent because lush green mountains surround it.

9. Minna Jhola

One of Jeypore's most famous holy sites is the Minna Jhola. The Minna Jhola is situated where three rivers confluence, and it is a well-liked location for relaxation and refreshment. Thousands of followers come to this Shiva shrine during Shiv Ratri.

With flowing rivers and lush, deep trees along the banks, the area surrounding Minna Jhola Jeypore is magnificent. In India, a combination is always admired. Nearly every river confluence in India is associated with a sacred festival, a temple, or other sites of spiritual significance.

10. Nandapur

Many visitors visit the historical centre to see the Batrisa Sinhasan, a renowned attraction. The Statue of Ganapati drew attention to the area's rich history, and the monuments and shrines at Sarbeswar are evidence of the area's ancient history.

Nandapur, the former home of the Jeypore Kingdom, is well known for housing Batrisa Sinhasana. It is a 32-step artefact connected to the legendary Vikramaditya throne. The six-foot-tall Ganesha statue, the Bhairavnath temple, and other ancient structures are all identifiable by their unique red colour. It is 45 kilometres from Koraput.

11. Papadahandi

There is a temple in the Papadahandi for Lord Shiva, the most revered deity in these places. In addition to being an important centre for pilgrimage, Papadahandi is a picturesque location. The temple is located deep in a tranquil setting, and champak trees line its area.

The summertime champak flower blossoms and fills the air with its pleasant smell, adding a peaceful atmosphere to the area. During the Shivaratri festival, many local pilgrims go to the temple. Papadahandi is a small village with crucial archaeological importance and a pilgrimage centre.

12. Patroput

Tourists are drawn to the village of Patroput by its historic bridge and the surroundings. This bridge can carry a 10-ton roller, and the British constructed it while they were in charge of India.

13. Raisal

Located 60 kilometres from Koraput and 3 kilometres from Laxmipur, this small town has a perennial stream running through it and is popular with weekend visitors. This area is perfect for hiking because it is surrounded by beautiful scenery. Huge, colourful celebrations are held yearly to honour "Anla Navami."

14. Sabar Srikhetra

Koraput is around 2900 feet above sea level in the middle of lush hills. It is a beneficial health destination due to the town's magnificent outlook and healthy environment, and the breathtaking beauty transports visitors to another world.

15. Kechela

A village on the southern bank of the Kolab is situated 10 kilometres from Koraput. There is a 30-foot-tall Jain temple with five images of the five Tirthankaras, such as Resavanath, Mahavir Jain, Ambika Devi, Jakhya, and Jakhyani. The pictures were carefully carved to high artistic achievement, proving that the Kechela was a centre of Jainism throughout the Middle Ages.

16. Gulmi

At Gulmi, the Kolab River creates a whirlpool that draws big crowds from the surrounding District and the state of Chhattisgarh. It is 25 kilometres from Chandeli and 100 kilometres from Koraput.

17. Sunabeda

The distance from Koraput to it is 18 kilometres. It is a modern township well-known for its MIG fighter aircraft, Sukhoi plant, and museum that showcases impressive aircraft engines.

18. Daman Jodi

The region of Daman Jodi is hilly and serenely beautiful. The largest aluminium plant in Asia, NALCO, is situated nearby, and this location is the most excellent option for those who enjoy trekking.

19. Kolab dam

Kolab dam is another nearby noteworthy location. Kolab Dam is a body of water home to a hydroelectric facility. This location offers boating services and is recognized as one of the main picnic areas in the Koraput district.

How to reach Koraput

1. By Air

By way of routine flights, Koraput is not well connected to other significant cities in the nation. Visakhapatnam has the closest airport, and the nearest airport is 25 kilometres from Koraput at Jeypore (Odisha).

2. By Train

From Vijayanagaram, Visakhapatnam, Bhubaneswar, and Calcutta, it is well linked.

3. By Bus

Throughout the District, there is NH 26. There are buses departing from Brahmapur, Rayagada, Vijayanagaram, and Visakhapatnam.
