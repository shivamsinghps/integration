25 Low Budget Tourist Places in India

Are tourists longing to hike to a stunning valley in the Himalayas and escape the dull routine at home? Tourists may give themself and their families a brief vacation to rejuvenate with little planning or financial outlay.

India has several inexpensive, accessible tourist destinations that may make a journey just as enjoyable as one to an exotic one. Here is the top list of 25 low-budget Tourist places in India tourists may travel to without worrying about their money account.

1. Puducherry:

  • Puducherry, a piece of France in the centre of India, is known for its picturesque beaches, cobblestone streets dotted with colonial architecture, and tranquil cafés serving some of the country's best French cuisine.
  • For those looking for peace and tranquillity, Puducherry has a lot to offer. Tourists A quick excursion to the Village, yoga and meditation sessions at Sri Aurobindo Ashram, or strolls along the Bay of Bengal are all options.
  • The best times to travel are from October to March.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • By Air: Chennai International Airport is the closest (144 km).
  • By Train: Major South Indian towns like Chennai, Trichy, Madurai, and others may easily be accessed from Puducherry Railway Station.
  • By Road: Puducherry has excellent road connections to all significant cities. From Chennai to Puducherry, tourists may travel along the East Coast Road (ECR) in three beautiful hours.
  • The daily cost of living is around Rs. 1000.

2. Pushkar:

  • One of Rajasthan's most significant religious destinations, Pushkar, is also a paradise for Hindus. Around the sacred Pushkar Lake, a Hindu pilgrimage destination with more than 52 ghats for bathing and performing ceremonial sacrifices, the town is situated.
  • The town's most notable temple, while several more are devoted to Lord Brahma, is Jagatpita Brahma Mandir. Pushkar, also known as Pushkar Camel Fair or Pushkar ka Mela, is well-known for its yearly animal fair that takes place in November.
  • If tourists are not religious travellers, they must go to the relaxed town to take a few days apart from their fast and busy life.
  • Pushkar's narrow lanes are crowded with low-cost restaurants and cafés, many of which have spiritual themes and provide a lively and tranquil atmosphere to relax. One of India's most outstanding and cheapest tourist destinations, Pushkar, is the ideal destination for rejuvenation.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days
  • October to March is the most outstanding season to travel.

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Sanganer Airport in Jaipur (151 km) is the closest airport.
  • Via Train: The closest stop is the 14 km away Ajmer Railway Station. By way of buses and taxis, Ajmer and Pushkar are easily accessible.
  • Via Road: Pushkar has good access to the most significant cities via road.
  • The daily cost for lodging and meals ranges between Rs 1000 and Rs 1500 approx.

3. Kodaikanal:

  • One of India's most popular hill towns, Kodaikanal, is regarded as the "Princess of Hill Towns." This charming tiny hill station is situated in Tamil Nadu and is characterized by its lovely lakes, picturesque hikes, rolling hills, spectacular pine trees, and fantastic temperature. Compared to Ooty, even during the peak travel season, there are few visitors everywhere visitors turn.
  • Whether visitors wish to go boating on Kodaikanal Lake, bike around it, or trek to the next hilltop at night for a stunning view of the Milky Way galaxy, Kodaikanal offers the finest of life experiences with budget-friendly options.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days
  • October to July is the best season to visit.

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Travelers can go to Trichy (150 km), Coimbatore (175 km), or Madurai (120 km).
  • Via train: The neighbouring train station, Kodaikanal Railway Station, provides easy access to the cities along the Dindigul and Madurai routes (79 km). There is a taxi available from here.
  • Via Road: From Chennai, Pondicherry, Coimbatore, Madurai, and other significant Tamil Nadu cities, there are frequent bus connections to Kodaikanal.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals ranges between Rs. 1500 and Rs. 2000.

4. Darjeeling:

  • The simple name of Darjeeling is enough to bring to mind the magnificent tea plantations and fascinating surroundings hidden in the East Indian foothills.
  • One of India's most cheap tourist attractions, Darjeeling, allows visitors to return home with beautiful memories of a trip to the heart of nature. Nothing compares to a hot cup of Darjeeling tea and the scent of the mountains as visitors watch the dawn over Tiger Hills in the morning.
  • Remember to join the famous toy train, which runs between Darjeeling and West Bengal's New Jalpaiguri Railway Station through Siliguri, Kurseong, and Ghum. The Darjeeling Himalayan Railway has been in operation since 1881. The fantastic toy train trip includes more than 500 bridges, rolling plains, and lovely green valleys if tourists complete the 80 km route.
  • The perfect holiday seasons are from April to June and October to January.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Bagdogra Airport, 12 kilometres from Siliguri, is the nearest airport to Darjeeling (70 km). Due to its small size, Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata may be visited by travellers before going on to Bagdogra. Tourists may take a taxi to Darjeeling from Bagdogra.
  • Via Train: The New Jalpaiguri railway station, which is the nearest one, connects the city to all major Indian cities. Various trains travel to New Jalpaiguri from cities like Delhi, Kolkata, Guwahati, Chennai, Mumbai, Bengaluru, and others.
  • Via Road: Bus service between Siliguri and Darjeeling is regular. Regular bus service on the route runs through Siliguri and departs from the Tenzing Norgay Central Bus Stand. A shared jeep is an alternative. Even though it is faster, the cost can occasionally be double that of a bus. The daily average price for lodging and meals is between Rs. 1000 and Rs. 1500.

5. Gokarna:

  • Gokarna is the place to be if tourists consider a beach a joy. Gokarna is a tiny beach town in the Indian state of Karnataka that gives a break from the stressful life of Goa while allowing tourists to rest and relax by the stunning Arabian Sea's aquamarine waves.
  • The gorgeous beaches of Gokarna, such as Om Beach, Half Moon Beach, Nirvana Beach, and Paradise Beach, provide visitors with the chance to enjoy mystical and spiritual encounters. Scuba diving, snorkelling, banana boat excursions, and many more exciting water sports activities are available in Gokarna. Gokarna is a popular tourist destination in India because of its many temples, and it is also one of the more affordable locations for spiritual and religious travellers.
  • One of the nation's oldest Shiva temples, Sri Mahabaleshwara Swamy Temple, should be visited. The granite temple, which dates to the 4th century and is a prime example of Dravidian architecture, is worth visiting even if tourists don't follow any particular religion.
  • Visiting is most enjoyable from October to March.
  • Ideal time: 3 to 4 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Fly to Goa's Dabolim Airport (140 km distant), then take a cab to Gokarna.
  • Via train: The closest train station, Ankola Railway Station, is around 20 kilometres from Gokarna and has good connections to the most significant cities, including Mumbai and Thiruvananthapuram.
  • Via Road: Gokarna has good road access to practically all of Karnataka's main cities. Additionally, it is conveniently accessible via National Highway 17, which links Kochi and Mumbai.
  • The daily average cost for lodging and meals is around Rs. 800-Rs.

6. Udaipur:

  • Udaipur, located in the lush Aravalli range, is famous for its expansive lakes and fantasy-like palaces. The pride and joy of Udaipur are the magnificent Lake Pichola, situated in the centre of the city.
  • The four-kilometre-long Lake Pichola was created artificially in the 14th century to provide some relief to the dry area. But for tourists, the lake's crystal-clear azure blue water and the mysterious Aravali Hills serving as the backdrop make for an outstanding picture.
  • A visit to Udaipur for architectural and history enthusiasts would only be complete with seeing several of the city's royal palaces.
  • The City Palace, a stunning structure made of marble and granite, is the most popular. The palace overlooks Lake Pichola and combines Chinese and European architectural traditions seamlessly.
  • Every component of the palace's design has a history, from the magnificent Moti Mahal inside the castle to the impressive Hathi Pol (also known as the Elephant Gate). And, surprise, tourists don't need to spend a fortune to live like a king or queen in this royal city.
  • Travelling is most enjoyable from September to March.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Maharana Pratap Airport is the closest and is about 20 kilometres from the city centre.
  • Via train: Udaipur City Railway Station has excellent rail connections to most of North India's main cities.
  • Via Road: Udaipur is well connected to other destinations in Rajasthan due to its status as a critical city in the state. Several buses operated by the Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation (RSRTC) run via Udaipur daily.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals varies between Rs 1500 and Rs 2000.

7. Itanagar:

  • Itanagar, situated in the Himalayan foothills, is well-known for its breathtaking scenery, Buddhist culture, unique history, and delectable Arunachalese cuisine.
  • The unexplored regions of North-east India provide the ideal vacation from the everyday. Don't forget to experience the local culture and food, which will no doubt leave visitors wanting more as they take in the beauty of the mountains lighted by the sunrise.
  • More than 500 kinds of orchids and the stunning great Indian Hornbill, the state bird of Arunachal Pradesh, may be found in the forest area. The famous annual cultural festival, the Ziro Festival of Music, maybe something tourists want to put on their schedule if they wish to visit Itanagar between September and December.
  • Travelling is most enjoyable from October to March.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: The Lilabari Airport in North Lakhimpur is the closest, 57 kilometres away.
  • Via Train: Harrmoty Railway Station is the closest train station (23 km).
  • Via Road: The majority of the central North East Indian cities, including Lakhimpur (62 km), Dibrugarh (197 km), and Guwahati, are well-connected by road (324km).
  • The daily average cost for lodging and meals is between Rs. 2000 and Rs. 2500.

8. Varanasi:

  • There are various names for the ultimate Hindu destination, including Varanasi, Benaras, and Kashi. Many pilgrims flock to Varanasi, one of the holiest cities in Hinduism, to apologize for their sins in the Ganges River, pay their respects to God at one of the city's many ghats, bury their loved ones and place their ashes in the Ganges, or to die here in the hopes of achieving Heaven, the release from the cycle of rebirth. Deeply steeped in spiritualism and mysticism, Varanasi celebrates life and death's authentic, personal rituals.
  • Varanasi, one of the world's oldest towns still in continuous occupancy, has long been the centre of religious, educational, and cultural activity. In addition to being one of the most affordable tourist destinations in India, Varanasi is a must-see location for an unforgettable experience.
  • Visiting is most enjoyable from November to February.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days.

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: The Lal Bahadur Shastri Airport, popularly known as the Varanasi Airport, is located 24 kilometres from the city centre.
  • Via Train: The two main railheads in Varanasi are Varanasi Railway Station and Kashi Railway Station. These two railway stations have excellent connections to other Indian cities.
  • Via Road: Varanasi has good road connections to important North Indian towns, including Ranchi, Lucknow, Patna, Gorakhpur, and Allahabad.
  • The daily average cost for lodging and meals is between Rs. 500 and Rs. 1000.

9. McLeod Ganj:

  • The popularity of McLeod Ganj, often known as Little Lhasa, among tourists rises yearly. McLeod Ganj, which is around five kilometres from the well-known hill resort of Dharamshala, is a reflection of the way of life and culture of the Tibetans who migrated here following the Chinese conquest of Tibet.
  • This lovely small town nestled in the Himalayas is home to the exiled Tibetan government and its spiritual leader, the 14th Dalai Lama. The experience of visiting McLeod Ganj is unlike any other hill station in India.
  • Beautiful hills and valleys may be seen in McLeod Ganj, a Buddhist pilgrimage centre and spiritual retreat. It is the starting point for the Triund Trek, one of India's most breathtaking and thrilling travels through stunning deodar, oak, and lush greenery. McLeod Ganj provides compelling experiences to art, culture, and cuisine enthusiasts and is Heaven for trekkers.
  • Nothing in McLeod Ganj, including the accommodations and cuisine, is out of reach, especially when tourists consider that Tibet is the only place where they can experience Tibetan culture better than this.
  • Travelling is most enjoyable from September to June.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: The closest airport is Kangra (18 km), and there are few flights available to this airport, though. Indra Gandhi International Airport in New Delhi is the second nearest airport (495 km).
  • Via Train: Pathankot Railway Station is the closest station (90 km). Rail connections between Pathankot and important cities like Delhi and Jammu are excellent.
  • Via Road: McLeod Ganj is well-connected to many cities, including Delhi, Chandigarh, and Dharamshala, with frequent and regular bus services.
  • Around Rs. 800-Rs. is spent on meals and lodging on a normal day.

10. Kasol:

  • Kasol is a small town in Himachal Pradesh's picturesque Parvati Valley, and it's the perfect place to explore nature.
  • The city is famous for its delicious and healthy cuisine and exhilarating excursions. Kasol provides various trek opportunities to Kheer Ganga, Parvati Pass, Malana, Tosh, and other destinations.
  • Tourists can see the stunning Parvati Valley, bordered on both sides by dense forests, by taking a stroll along the roaring Parvati River. Instead, go to Tosh town, which is at the bottom of Parvati Valley, to see cannabis plantations there.
  • Due to the significant number of Israeli tourists that go to the city every year, the little cafés there provide a variety of meals, including several items from Israeli cuisine.
  • All seasons are ideal for travel.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Kullu Manali Airport, also known as Bhuntar Airport, is the closest airport (31 km).
  • Via Train: There isn't a railway station in Kasol, and Joginder Nagar Railway Station is the closest station (144km). For better rail connectivity, visitors can also travel to Chandigarh Railway Station (296 km), from which they can take a bus to Manali or a taxi to Kasol.
  • Via Road: Kasol does not have any direct bus services. The best choices are to travel to Chandigarh or Bhuntar and then hire a taxi.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals varies between Rs. 1000 and Rs. 1500.

11. Amritsar:

  • The Golden Temple, their langar and Karah Prashad, Jallianwala Bagh, the Wagah Border, kulchas, tall glasses of lassi, and the many dhabas that provide delectable Amritsari food are things that spring to mind when someone discusses Amritsar. In Amritsar, one of India's less expensive tourist destinations, visitors may observe daily life as residents live it.
  • Amritsar, established in 1577 by Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru, is the centre of Sikhism in India. It would be an understatement to say that visiting the Harmandir Sahib, also known as the Golden Temple, is essential to any trip to Amritsar.
  • The iconic temple is the main reason to see the city. Try their langar, the most extensive communal kitchen in the world, and don't forget to order the ghee-laden Karah Prashad. At the same time, tourists let the tranquillity of the temple relax tourists senses.
  • At the Wagah Border, let patriotism grow among tourists. Every evening, just before the sun sets, Indian and Pakistani forces conduct a precisely timed and carefully organized ritual to officially seal the border for the night. There is no entry charge to the ceremony, which is open to everyone.
  • Visit Jallianwala Bagh, the scene of one of the most horrific incidents in Indian history, to retrace the memory alleys of the Indian freedom movement while tourists are in Amritsar.
  • All seasons are ideal for travel.
  • Ideal time: 3 to 4 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: The distance between the city centre and Sri Guru Ram Das Jee International Airport is roughly 11 kilometres.
  • Via train: Nearly all significant Indian cities have good rail connections to Amritsar.
  • Via Road: Amritsar has excellent road connectivity. Various cities and towns in Punjab, as well as important North Indian cities like Delhi, Chandigarh, and Jammu, all have regular bus services, both public and private.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals varies between Rs 1500 and Rs 2000.

12. Rishikesh:

  • The most peaceful destination in northern Uttarakhand is Rishikesh; the ideal trip there would include Ganga Aarti, rafting, camping, and yoga. In the 1960s, Rishikesh gained popularity as the destination where The Beatles and their Guru, the Maharishi Mahesh Yogi, resided. It is today referred to as the "Yoga Capital of the World," a spiritual hub, and a well-liked holiday spot for fans of water sports.
  • A witness to the Ganges' majesty as it rushes from the mountains down the valley and meets the Gangetic Plain at the pilgrimage town of Haridwar is Rishikesh, which is encircled by gorgeous hills (20 km downstream).
  • Tourists can find the ideal combination of action and spirituality in Rishikesh. Here, Tourists may spend the day pushing themselves to the maximum on the Ganges and feel spiritually uplifted in the evenings as Tourists witness the famed Ganga Aarti performed on the ghats at Parmarth Niketan Ashram.
  • Travelling is most enjoyable from September to April.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun is the closest (20 km).
  • Via Train: There is a train station in Rishikesh. However, only a few trains depart from there. Haridwar is home to the closest railway station in the entire operation (19 km). Significant towns in North India are well connected to the holy city of Haridwar.
  • Via Road: All the major roads and interstates are easily accessible from Rishikesh.
  • The daily average cost for lodging and meals is roughly Rs. 800-Rs.

13. Nainital:

  • One of North India's most well-known hill resorts, Nainital, is a fantastic destination for a leisure vacation. The charming hill station, known as Sapta-Sharing, is encircled by seven hills on three sides and is built around the lovely lake Naini Tal.
  • This little hill station provides a pleasant vacation with days spent enjoying walks through the pine, oak, and deodar forests, visiting different colonial structures, and shopping on Mall Road. Additionally, Nanital has excellent road connections to several other lesser-known hill towns.
  • The greatest seasons to travel are from March to June and October to February.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • By Air: Pantnagar has the closest domestic airport (65 km). However, there is little commercial connectivity between Pantnagar Airport and other cities. The next closest airport is Indira Gandhi International Airport in New Delhi (290 km).
  • Via Train: Kathgodham Railway Station is the closest (34 km). Kathgodham is connected to New Delhi and other important cities by several railways.
  • Via Road: Nainital offers excellent bus service to the most significant little cities in North India. Daily bus service is available from Kathgodam and New Delhi to Nainital.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals ranges between Rs 1000 and Rs 1500.

14. Kanyakumari:

  • Kanyakumari offers a rich experience unlike anywhere else in India, yet is one of the less expensive destinations to visit. The Bay of Bengal, the Arabian Sea, and the Indian Ocean meet in Kanyakumari, the southernmost point of the Indian Region. Kanyakumari, also known as Cape Comorin, gives the rare opportunity to view the sunrise and sunset from the water.
  • Tourists may observe the evening and the moon rising simultaneously on days with a full moon. The scene is made even more stunning by the Kanyakumari beach's multicoloured sand. Once the excitement of standing on the edge of a continent wears off, tourists may visit additional sights like the Vivekananda Rock Memorial, which provides a vantage point from which to see the Land's End.
  • One of the rocks that stick out into the water is where the Vivekananda Rock Memorial is located, and Visitors can take a ferry to get there. The 133-foot-tall Thiruvalluvar Statue is supported by another rock, which acts as its foundation. The 95-foot-tall monument is placed on top of a 38-foot pedestal.
  • Visit the well-known temple honouring the land-protecting virgin goddess Kanyakumari during tourist visits. The deity's diamond nose pin, which is as prominent as the deity herself, is believed to be seen from the sea.
  • Travelling is most enjoyable from October to March.
  • Ideal time: 1-2 days.

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Trivandrum International Airport is the closest airport (80 km).
  • Via train: Thiruvananthapuram, Delhi, Mumbai, and other significant cities are all easily accessible from Kanyakumari Railway Station.
  • Via Road: Rameshwaram, Thiruvananthapuram, and Madurai are nearby cities to Kanyakumari. Bus services are usually available between Kanyakumari and other major South Indian cities.
  • Lodging and meals cost between Rs 1500 and Rs 2000 per day.

15. Ooty:

  • Ooty, called the "Queen of Hills," truly lives up to its nickname. Ooty, hidden away in the Nilgiri Hills, has rolling hills, green valleys, and breathtaking lakes that provide its tourists with unmatched panoramic beauty. Since British control, Ooty has gained popularity as a hill resort, and the stunning tea plantations and colonial homes continue to attract tourists from all over the nation.
  • Visitors should ride the famous Nilgiri Mountain Railway toy train, visit St. Stephen's Church and the ancient Murugan Temple, go boating on the stunning lakes like Avalanche Lake, Upper Bhavani Lake, or Emerald Lake, and take in the natural beauty. The blue Nilgiri Mountain Railway (NMR) toy train, which UNESCO designated as a World Heritage Site in 2005, is a sight to see and a once-in-a-lifetime experience.
  • April to June and September to November are the best season to visit.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Coimbatore International Airport (87 km) is the closest airport.
  • Via Train: Mettupalyam is the closest railway station (40 km). Mettupalyam has regular rail service to Chennai, Coimbatore, Mysore, Bengaluru, and other important South Indian cities.
  • Via Road: Regular buses operated by the Tamil Nadu State Road Transport Corporation and various commercial service providers connect Ooty to significant South Indian cities.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals ranges between Rs 1000 and Rs 1500.

16. Jaipur:

  • Jaipur, sometimes called the Pink City, provides a glimpse of the Rajputana tradition and its illustrious heritage. Jaipur, the state's capital, continues to be among the most popular tourist destinations. Jaipur, well-known around the globe for its coloured jewels, forts, and palaces, draws tourists from all over the world all year. Jaipur was created by Raja Jai Singh II when he relocated his capital from Amber to the new city in 1727. Top attractions in Jaipur include the City Palace, Hawa Mahal, and Amer Fort, all located outside the town.
  • Visit the city's famous markets right away to get some lovely Rajasthani handicrafts after visiting the forts and palaces. The Johari Bazaar in Jaipur is home to shops that sell precious stones, diamonds, semi-precious stones, and handcrafted jewellery. If visitors are interested in purchasing some gorgeous Jaipuri jootis, visit Nehru Bazaar. A trip to Bapu Bazaar will capture all of Jaipuri for visitors who want to spend time learning about the local cuisine and culture.
  • Visiting is most enjoyable from October to March.
  • Ideal time: 3 to 4 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Jaipur's Sanganer International Airport connects Pink City with critical Indian cities like Delhi and Mumbai.
  • Via Train: Several direct trains frequently run between Jaipur Railway Station and other Indian cities.
  • Via Road: Major Indian cities may be reached by Jaipur via decent roads. With other regions of India, Jaipur is connected to National Highways no. 8, 11, and 12.
  • The expense of lodging and meals each day is between Rs. 1500 to Rs. 2000.

17. Hampi:

  • As the former capital of the mighty Vijayanagara Dynasty, the historical town of Hampi is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The ruins of Hampi, which are situated on the banks of the Tungabhadra River, are a remarkable example of lost greatness placed amid a boulder-strewn landscape. Since 1500 AD, there have been remnants of the old temple town.
  • Notable Hampi attractions include the monolithic statue of Sasivekalu Ganesha, the Hampi Bazaar, the Virupaksha Temple, the Vithala Temple, the Nandi Statue, the Lotus Mahal, and the Nandi Statue. Tourists may either bike through the magnificent monuments or stroll through the temple village to examine the ruins.
  • Visiting is most enjoyable from October to February.
  • Ideal time: 1-2 days.

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Jindal Vijaynagar Airport in Bellary is the nearest (37 km). Bengaluru's Kempegowda International Airport is the closest international airport (360 km).
  • Via Train: The closest rail station connecting Hampi with other key Karnataka cities is Hosapete Railway Station (13 km).
  • Via Road: Karnataka's main cities and towns have excellent bus connections to Hampi. In Hampi, two-wheelers may also be hired.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals is around Rs. 800-Rs.

18. Jaisalmer:

  • The golden city of Jaisalmer, which sits on the border of the Thar Desert, provides the ideal background for an Indian adaptation of the Arabian Nights story. Large areas of golden sand and the stunning Jaisalmer Fort, also known as the Sonar Qila (Golden Fort), provide an experience unlike any other.
  • The Sonar Qila, a fort constructed of unusual yellow sandstone, glistens like a giant flame in the vast desert when the sun of the Thar Desert shines upon it at sunrise and sunset. It is breathtaking to see. Visit the Akal Wood Fossil Park in Jaisalmer to learn about the geologic tragedies in the Thar Desert millions of years ago and trace their origins.
  • Include a desert safari, camel rides, and desert camping, and in addition to taking in traditional music and dance performances, tourists must taste the Rajasthani cuisine at the desert camp.
  • Visiting is most enjoyable from November to March.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Jodhpur Airport is the closest airport (285 km). Major cities, including Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, and Udaipur, are connected to Jodhpur by regular flights.
  • Via Train: Direct trains run from Delhi, Jaipur, and Jodhpur to the Jaisalmer Railway Station.
  • Via Road: Jaisalmer has a continuous bus service that connects it to essential towns in Rajasthan, like Jodhpur and Jaipur. Even though no buses go directly from New Delhi to Jaisalmer, travellers can always take one to Jaipur and then take another bus to finish their journey.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals ranges between Rs 1000 and Rs 1500.

19. Lonavala:

  • Lonavala, a well-liked weekend vacation from Mumbai and Pune, provides one of the most stunning escapes from the routine of city life. Popularly referred to as the "Jewel of Sahyadri," the hill town offers breathtaking ghat vistas, verdant valleys, gorgeous lakes, beautiful waterfalls, and rock-cut caverns with prehistoric symbols and writing. Driving to Lonavala via tunnels made of rock is an adventure in and of itself, and it is located on the beautiful Mumbai-Pune Expressway.
  • The Aamby Valley, Pawna Lake, the Kune waterfall, Tikona Fort, Lohagad, and Rajmachi forts can all be visited as day trips from Lonavala. Still, a more extended trip is necessary to see the surrounding attractions.
  • Visit Lonavala during the monsoon season to see the lush green flora growing on the dark ghat rocks.
  • The most incredible time to travel is from October to May and June to August.
  • Ideal time:1-2 days.

The best way to get there

  • Via air: The closest airport is Lohegaon Airport in Pune, about 70 kilometres away. Mumbai's Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport is the next nearest airport (90 km).
  • Via train: Mumbai and Pune are easily accessible from Lonavala Railway Station. The daily trains that run between Pune and Mumbai make a lot of halts in Lonavala.
  • Via Road: Lonavala is traversed by National Highway no. 4 and the historic Mumbai-Pune Road. Regular state buses, private buses, and taxi services are accessible on this route.
  • The daily average cost for lodging and meals is around Rs. 800-Rs.

20. Shimla:

  • Shimla continues to be one of the most enchanting hill towns in the nation. Its colonial and heritage structures relate to historical events. Fans of Victorian architecture won't want to miss Shimla's Viceregal Lodge, Christ Church, Gaiety Theatre, Gorton Castle, and Barnes Court for a distinctive experience.
  • All cultural events occur in Shimla at The Ridge, located along Mall Road. Shimla is a dreamer's paradise, encircled by cedar, rhododendron, and Himalayan oak forests. Shimla's city will be covered entirely with snow if tourists visit during the winter. During the coldest winter months, Shimla's tallest peak, Jakoo Hill, is usually covered in a thin coating of snow, and Kufri, nearby, is well renowned for its snow-skiing slopes.
  • The two finest vacation seasons are from December to January and April to October.
  • Ideal time: 3 to 4 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: The Indira Gandhi International Airport in New Delhi is 359 km away, while the Chandigarh Airport is 122 km away.
  • Via Train: The closest train station is at Kalka, which is 96 kilometres away. From Kalka, travelling by rail to Chandigarh and New Delhi is simple.
  • Via Road: Shimla is connected to all the main towns by road. Shimla has regular bus service to Chandigarh and New Delhi.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals ranges between Rs 1000 and Rs 1500.

21. Digha:

  • Digha is a Popular beach resort town in West Bengal, located between the East Midnapore district and the northern end of the Bay of Bengal. Over seven kilometres of shallow sand beach make up Digha's most notable feature. At Digha's low-gradient beach, the water begins approximately a mile from where tourists touch the sand, providing a large area of lovely sand to unwind on.
  • The coastal casuarina plants contribute to the beach town's dreamy allure. The little fishing community was once known as Beerkul under Warren Hastings' rule and was founded by the British in the late 18th century. Like Kanyakumari, Digha offers the unusual experience of seeing the sunrise and sunset over the saline seas of the Bay of Bengal.
  • Ideal time: one day
  • The most incredible time to travel is from October to February.

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata (192 km) is the closest airport. Most of India's main cities are easily reachable from Kolkata.
  • Via Train: Three direct trains travel between Howrah and Digha Railway Station. The majority of Indian cities may be reached from Howrah Railway Station easily.
  • Via Road: A highway connects Digha with Kolkata and Howrah (a distance of 168 km). From Kolkata's Dharamsala Bus Station (180 km), regular buses travel to Digha.
  • The daily average cost for lodging and meals is around Rs. 800-Rs.

22. Goa:

  • Anyone who has visited Goa, a tiny state on the Western Coastal Plain, will agree that the beach is paradise. Goa, formerly a province of Portugal, is known for its spectacular beaches, towering forts, bizarre churches, Portuguese architecture, laid-back lifestyle, and delicious cuisine.
  • Numerous people, mainly young ones, visit Goa yearly to enjoy the beach. Goa is a popular location for beach party enthusiasts and stages many music events every year. Visit Goa for a feel of Europe in India.
  • The ideal time to travel is from October to June.
  • Best time: 4-5 days.

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Take a flight to the Goa Dabolim Airport. The distance to Panaji, the state's capital, is 31 kilometres.
  • Via Train: The two major railway stations in Goa are Madgaon and Vasco-da-Gama. These train stations provide excellent connections to Mumbai and other important Indian cities.
  • Via Road: Goa, particularly its capital, Panaji, has excellent road connections to other cities.
  • The daily average for lodging and meals is around Rs. 800-Rs.

23. Khajuraho:

  • Who hasn't heard of the romantic stone sculptures from Khajuraho? But only some people realize that the beautiful sculptures seen in the three groups of temples at Khajuraho make up only 10% of the whole collection. The historic temples of Khajuraho are praised for being the last remaining examples of some of the best temple art in the world. The Khajuraho Group of Monuments consists of 85 Hindu and Jain temples, 20 of which have survived the tests of time.
  • All of these temples were constructed of sandstone. The Khajuraho temples, which had been abandoned for centuries, were rediscovered in the 1850s and have since attracted visitors worldwide. Khajuraho, which is in Madhya Pradesh, is one of India's most affordable vacation destinations because the city's tourist attractions are its temples and sculptures.
  • October to February is the best season to visit.
  • Ideal time: 2 to 3 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: The main town is only 5 kilometres from the Khajuraho Airport. The Kanpur Airport is the following nearest airport ( 229 km)
  • Via Train: The Delhi-Chennai-Bangalore-Trivandrum route connects Jhansi (114 km) with the small Khajuraho railway station. Satna is the next nearby railway station (118 km).
  • Via Road: Regular buses go from Satna and Jhansi to Khajuraho. However, there are no direct buses from Jhansi; thus, to get to Khajuraho, you would have to change buses in Chhatarpur. Many North Indian cities and towns, including Harpalpur, Chhatarpur, Mahoba, Sagar, Jabalpur, Bhopal, Indore, Gwalior, Panna, Agra, Allahabad, and Varanasi, are connected to Khajuraho by long-distance buses.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals varies between Rs 1000 and Rs 1500.

24. Alleppey:

  • India's most fabulous and affordable travel destinations are all in Alleppey, sometimes called the "Venice of the East." The iconic Kerala backwaters are best seen at Alleppey, also known as Alappuzha, located in the centre of Kerala's extensive network of canals.
  • Houseboat excursions and lodging are also quite popular in Alleppey. Spend a day relaxing on one of Kerala's charming houseboats while floating through its picturesque backwaters. Occasionally, Tourists will come across a small village along the direction where they can stop for a drink of toddy before continuing their journey through the enthralling system of canals and backwaters.
  • September to March is the best season to visit.
  • Ideal time: 3 to 4 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Trivandrum International Airport is the second-closest airport (82 km), behind Cochin International Airport (162 km).
  • Via Train: The distance between the city centre and Alappuzha Railway Station is roughly 4 kilometres. Almost all of India's major cities can be reached easily from Alappuzha.
  • By Road: Alappuzha has an excellent KSRTC bus network that connects it to the surrounding cities.
  • The daily average for lodging and meals is around Rs. 800-Rs.

25. Wayanad:

  • Wayanad is Kerala's green paradise, with a wealth of natural variety. Wayanad, a region tucked away in the Western Ghats highlands, is well-known among tourists for its natural environment. The Wayanad experience is enhanced by the unspoiled beauty of nature, breathtaking trek routes, the enormous expanse of lakes, and the region's rich history and tradition.
  • While tourists are here, go to Wayanad's Chembra Peak to witness the well-known heart-shaped lake surrounded by lovely hills and valleys, enjoy Banasura Sagar Dam, Asia's second-biggest earth dam and India's largest, and tour Edakkal Caves to see ancient pictorial paintings on the cave walls.
  • October to May and December to February are the best months to visit.
  • Ideal time: 3 to 4 days

The best way to get there

  • Via Air: Karipur International Airport in Kozhikode is the nearest airport (98 km).
  • Via Train: Kozhikode is the closest railway station (85 km). Kozhikode has good rail connectivity to important cities like Bengaluru, Chennai, and Kochi.
  • Via Road: There is a decent KSRTC bus network connecting Wayanad to the surrounding cities.
  • The daily cost of lodging and meals varies between Rs 1000 and Rs 1500.
