Tourist Places in Shimla

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is one of the most well-known hill towns in North India. The mountain and beautiful surroundings of Shimla are well-known throughout the nation. Shimla, among the most stunning tourist destinations in the world and a popular destination for lovers, is at 2200 metres.

The scenic habitat and vibe of this place, which served as the former capital town of British India, will encourage visitors to revisit. The colonial-style structures and Shimla's mediaeval temple fascinate visitors from all across the world by his attraction. This article will detail the best tourist destination available here in Shimla.

1. Kufri in Shimla

Kufri is a popular destination for travellers 17 kilometres from Shimla Hills station. This hill station is a favourite of nature lovers and adventure seekers. It is situated at a height of 2510 metres near the Himalayan foothills. The most significant aspect of visiting Kufri is that there won't be a large tourist population, even though there are numerous breathtaking vistas to witness. Kufri is a must-visit if you're coming to Shimla because there are so many amazing sights to see.

2. Jakhoo Hill in Shimla

The topmost point of this hill station is Jakhu Hill which is situated only 2 kilometres from Shimla and offers a stunning perspective of the town and the snow-capped Himalayan Mountains. A well-known tourist destination in Shimla Hills Station, which is a favourite destination for devotees and nature enthusiasts, is the 8000-foot-high Jakhu Hill. On this hill sits an old temple called Jakhu Temple, the residence of a massive Hanuman idol. One of the top attractions in Shimla is this temple.

3. Mall Road in Shimla

Shimla's Mall Road, located below the hill, is home to a wide variety of supermarkets, cafés, eateries, bookstores, and tourist hotspots. From this route, you may also admire Shimla's natural splendour. One such location that draws a large number of visitors to Shimla is this Mall Road.

4. The Ridge Road in Shimla

The Ridge Shimla is a wide, open road that runs beside Mall Road in the city's heart. You can take in a variety of sights at The Ridge. Stores here offer unique artefacts, and the snow-covered mountain peaks give breathtaking views. The Ridge's most unique feature is that, during the British era, it was the most exclusive spot to stay during the warmer months. Many people travel to Shimla because it is a stunning location. The Ridge serves as a market and a city's community space. Shimla always used to be crowded with locals and tourists. This avenue also has a lot of popular cafés, clubs, boutiques, stores, and eateries.

5. Kalka in Shimla

Shimla Railway is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site in India's hill railway station. Along with other railway lines in India, the British constructed this rail line to Shimla in 1898. It travels via several popular tourist destinations, including Solan, Summer Hill, and many more, from Kalka (a town in Haryana) to Shimla. If you're travelling to Shimla, you should take this train. This train will provide you with an unforgettable tourism experience with several tunnels, bridges, and spectacular vistas.

6. Green Valley in Shimla

On the route from Shimla to Kufri, the Green Valley mountainous region is a stunning view. This Valley is encircled on all sides by barren hills covered with deep deodar and deodar forests. Yaks are frequently spotted wandering and grazing in the Valley. Despite the area's lack of tourism development, travellers are nonetheless drawn to it by the abundant deodar and deodar trees that border the gorgeous hillsides. Rare creatures can also be observed here sometimes. Green Valley has also appeared in several Bollywood films for its beauty and elegance.

7. Tara Devi Temple in Shimla

Even though you consider yourself religious or not, you should visit the Tara Devi Temple when you're in Shimla. This temple, located at the top of Tara Parvat hill, is roughly 250 years old. The principal spirit here is also Goddess Tara, a Tibetan Buddhist deity who is one of Goddess Durga's nine sisters. According to local belief, the wooden idol placed in the shrine was imported from West Bengal. At the same time, the temple has a peaceful vibe about it. Tourists are drawn to this location by its architectural beauty.

8. Solan Mushroom City in Shimla

Solan, known as the Mushrooms City and the Red Gold City of India, is well-known for producing tomatoes and mushrooms. Tourists that visit this place love it very much because of Solan. Since the British government masterminded the original economic growth of this location, they deserve credit for Sonal's development. The best feature of this location is that it offers pleasant temperatures all year long. This location's stunning natural surroundings will never fail to astound you.

9. Indian Institute of Advanced Studies in Shimla

The Indian Institute of Advanced Studies is a magnificent Victorian-style building on a Hill in Shimla. It is currently being used as a college that provides post-doctoral courses. The British government once held important political meetings at the building. The structure, a must-see in Shimla and Manali, was originally constructed as Lord Dufferin's home. He was the Viceroy of India from 1884 to 1888. Its previous name was Viceregal Lodge. Before Shimla would utilise electricity in any way, the structure had an electrical connection as early as 1888. Before becoming the Indian Institute of Advanced Studies, this location served as Rashtrapati Niwas, the president of India's summer residence following the country's independence.

10. Manali Hill Station in Shimla

Manali, a well-known hill town in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh, is well-known for being the home of the Hindu god "Manu." The mountain city of Manali, one of India's most popular hill resorts, is located at the height of 2,050 metres above sea level in the northern Kullu district's Beas river valley. Manali was once inhabited by nomadic hunters and herders who travelled there from the Kangra Valley.

11. Kullu in Shimla

With Manali, an open valley with amazing views and imposing hills clothed with luscious green trees, Kullu is a well-known tourist attraction in India. Due to its great views, Kullu, at an elevation of 1230 metres, is one of Himachal Pradesh's most visited tourist destinations. Let's say that most visitors to this area like exploring both Kullu and Manali simultaneously. This little village, which is surrounded by beautiful scenery, will captivate any tourists that come to visit. Additionally, you may go to various significant temples in Kullu, such as the Raghunath Temple and the Jagannath Devi Temple.

12. Toy Train in Shimla

This four-toy train journey, which offers spectacular scenery of the hills covered with pine trees, is an absolute must-see place for your Shimla trip. The toy train will take you through 20 stops, 103 caves, 800 crossings, and over 900 spectacular bends on your 96 km ride. The most beautiful portion of the trip is somewhere between Barog and Shimla, when the train begins to slow because of a hard climb, and you have an opportunity to see some stunning sights. You must make reservations in advance to ride this train. The cost is between Rs 65 to Rs 500.

13. Christ Church in Shimla

In Shimla, Christ Church is a highly well-liked location. This church, situated on The Ridge, was built in 1857. It took over 3 years to finish the architectural masterpiece. The clock tower, frescoes, and window panes are some of Christ Church's features. Additionally, this cathedral is home to the biggest musical instrument in India, which you've probably seen in several Bollywood films like 3 Idiots.

14. Summer Hill in Shimla

A settlement called Summer Hill, commonly referred to as Potters Hill, is located outside Shimla. Potters used to assemble here in the past to create ceramics. This hill's elevation of 1283 metres above sea level offers breathtaking views of the region and the surrounding vegetation. Summer Hill is five kilometres from the well-known Ridge, a major tourist destination. Don't miss the breathtaking views from Summer Hill if you're visiting Shimla.

15. Chail Hill Station in Shimla

Chail, a beautiful hill resort built by Maharaja Bhupinder Singh of Patiala, is located 44 kilometres from Shimla and 45 from Solan. Let us inform you that this location also has the world's highest cricket field. Chail is renowned for its pristine woods and beautiful beauty.

16. Kali Bari Temple in Shimla

Although some temples draw visitors because of their structural splendour, others do so because of their religious or cultural importance. However, certain temples are ideal in both circumstances. The Kali Bari Shrine in Shimla, which is devoted to Goddess Kali and features an alluring statue of the Goddess, is one such temple. The temple, constructed in 1845, is also referred to as Shyamala. Shimla is derived from the Goddess Shyamala, and it is the capital of Himachal Pradesh. Between 7 am and 6 pm, the temple is open.

17. Arki Fort in Shimla

The architecture of the Arki Fort, constructed in 1660 AD, combines Rajput and Mughal styles. This location might prove to be significant for you if you adore history. The Kangra sculptures that cover the fort are what makes it famous. Even though the sculptures in this fort are said to be almost 200 years old, they nevertheless appear stunning. Include this fort on your list if you are a history enthusiast.

18. Johnnie's Wax Museum in Shimla

The biggest draw for tourists is the Johnny Wax Museum, sometimes referred to as the first wax museum in Himachal Pradesh. The Statues of several well-known movie stars are on display at this museum. The museum's wax figures of Harry Potter, Steve Jobs, Mahatma Gandhi, and Tony Stark are among the most popular ones. The best thing is capturing the moment on camera while posing next to your favourite star. The entrance charge to this exhibition is about Rs 250 for seniors and Rs 5 for kids, and it is open from 10 am until 10 pm.

19. Naldehra in Shimla

Naldehra, a stunning hill station close to Shimla, is 2044 metres above sea level. A golf course had already been built here by Lord Curzon. The ambience in this location is quite alluring because of the numerous deodar trees and the lovely vegetation. This is a fantastic location to view the Foothills Of the Himalayas. You can hear the voice of the breeze coming here due to the quiet environment. You may ride a horse to get across this area. If you visit, you may find it alluring to view the dawn and sunset in Naldehra. Said, this is the ideal hit spot for you if you're seeking peaceful surroundings.

20. State Museum in Shimla

The Himachal State Museum and Library, popularly known as the Shimla State Museum, was founded in 1974. Let us explain that the purpose of this museum was to protect the rich cultural heritage and preserve the past. You may get a detailed glimpse at this city's illustrious past thanks to the colonial-style building that is placed there. Various artworks, drawings, antiques, and coins may be seen at the Shimla State Museum.

21. Chadwick Falls in Shimla

Chadwick Falls is one of Shimla's top tourist destinations, flowing from a height of 1586 metres. The surrounding area is covered in deep forest, rich green deodar, and luscious green trees, and the sight is just magnificent. Due to the monsoon, the waterfall's water level rises, enhancing the area's beauty with dazzling waters. Chadwick Falls is a visual delight because of its alluring beauty and atmosphere. It is unquestionably among the top Shimla tourist attractions. One of Shimla's most picturesque locations is Chadwick Waterfall, located within the Glen Forest. You can swim around if you aren't frightened by the prospect of dipping your toes in the chilly water.

22. Daranghati Sanctuary in Shimla

The Daranghati Sanctuary, which spans 167.40 km and is situated 150 kilometres from Shimla, is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the area. This reserve area is situated in Shimla's upper levels, which served as the Rampur Bushahr royal family's hunting grounds in the past. This location, designated as a sanctuary in 1962, is now home to various animals.

Best Time to Visit Shimla

Shimla is at its most enjoyable in around March and June, when temperatures vary from 15 to 30 degrees Celsius. The winter season of November to February is the ideal time to see snowfall in Shimla. It is the perfect time to enjoy snow-covered sports like ice skating and discover the area. Shimla experiences rising tourists throughout the year, except for the monsoon season from July to September.

Famous Food of Shimla

Shimla is one of India's most stunning hill towns and is a must-visit location when planning a trip to Himachal Pradesh. In addition to all these things, Himachal Pradesh's capital city is renowned for its delectable Himachali food, a delicious fusion of Tibetan and Punjabi food. In Himachal, most of the diet comprises wheat bread, lentils, pulses, rice, and red meat. The food is primarily nutritious as well as rich and flavorful. Shimla offers a wide variety of delectable cuisine, from street sellers to fine restaurants and traditional cafés.

  2. Sidu
  3. Chicken Anardana

Way To Reach Shimla

The capital of Himachal Pradesh, Shimla, is one of the most stunning tourist spots in the nation and is home to many magnificent landmarks. Shimla is so unique that visitors rush to all the hill towns all year, making it impossible to escape the crowds. The first thing that comes to mind while planning a trip to Shimla is how effortlessly Shimla can be accessed by air, rail, and road.

1. By Air

The closest airport to Shimla is Jubbarhatti, roughly 23 kilometres from the city. If you're travelling there, remember, there are frequent flights from Chandigarh and Delhi to Jubbarhatti. You may get a cab to take you to Shimla after arriving at this airport.

2. By Rail

A narrow-gauge rail line connects Shimla's little railway station, which is only one kilometre from the city centre, Kalka. The well-known toy train from Shimla travels 96 kilometres between Kalka and Shimla, covering the route in around 7 hours. Shimla's closest train station is Kalka, with regular trains running from Delhi and other major states.

3. By Road

Shimla is well linked by road to towns like Chandigarh, which is 117 km away, Manali, which is 260 km away, and Delhi, which is 343 km away. In addition to Himachal Road Transport Corporation, several private buses operate out of important North Indian towns. Several luxurious buses leave ISBT Kashmere Gate overnight if you travel from Delhi to Shimla.
