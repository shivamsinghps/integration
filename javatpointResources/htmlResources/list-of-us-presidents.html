List of US Presidents

According to the constitution of the United States of America, the President in the US is "Head of the government" as well as "Head of the state". The President of the United States is elected for a working tenure of four years through an Electoral College system. The working period has been restricted to a limit of two terms after the adaptation of the Twenty-second amendment in 1951.

Some facts regarding President of US

  • The US president's office was initiated in 1789, and since then, 45 personalities have served to the designation of the United States President.
  • George Washington was the person who was honoured as the first president of the United States.
  • Grover Cleveland is the only President who held the designation for two non-sequential terms in the office.
  • William Henry Harrison was the shortest working President due to his demise just after 31 days of taking the responsibility.
  • Franklin D. Roosevelt became the longest-working President of the United States who served for more than two terms. He held the position for more than 12 years and died during the starting years of his fourth term.
  • The last presidential election that was conducted in 2020 was the 59th presidential election in which Joseph Robinette Biden won the elections. Joseph Robinette Biden Jr. is currently serving as the President of the United States.

Eligibility criteria to become the US president

The person who wants to serve as the President of the United States should meet the following three important attributes:

  1. The individual should be a natural-born native of the United States.
  2. The person should be a resident of the US for 14 years at least.
  3. The individual must have attained the age of 35 years.
