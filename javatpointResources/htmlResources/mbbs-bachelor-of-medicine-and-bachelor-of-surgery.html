MBBS - Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery

The full form of MBBS is Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of surgery. In Latin word MBBS is Medicinae Baccalaureus Chirurgiae. This course is provided and conducted by MCI (Medical Council of India). It is a professional degree course for Medical Science students.

MBBS is the Undergraduate Degree Course in which the student can complete this course within 5.5 years only. In these 5.5 years, 4.5 years is for the academic session and one year is for the internship. MBBS is the most popular degree designated for the degree of doctor. For the medical candidate or who wants to become the doctor, MBBS is a reputed degree or the gateway to their dream profession. This is the single bachelor degree course that holds the two-bachelor courses in a single domain i.e.,

  1. Bachelor of Medicine
  2. Bachelor of surgery

In this course students learn about the below-listed subjects;

  1. Human anatomy
  2. Human cytology
  3. Medicine Chemistry
  4. Pharmaceutical
  5. Chemistry
  6. Drugs Formulation &
  7. Effect and method of surgery

What are the necessary abilities for MBBS?

Each medical and health sciences professional should have the following skills:

  1. Ability to work in a basic and dynamic environment
  2. Efficient responsibilities and medical ethics
  3. Desire for information and learning new things
  4. Logical innovative working capabilities
  5. Sharp memory and concise functioning
  6. Correspondence relational abilities
  7. Guiding and caring abilities
  8. Friendly and compassionate abilities
  9. Medical writing abilities
  10. Persistence and relentlessness

Difference between MBBS from Abroad and MBBS from India

The student can do the MBBS in India or in Abroad, both are having so many similarities than differences. National Eligibility-cum-Entrance Test (NEET) is required to apply to all medical schools abroad just as in India. In India, students can complete their MBBS degree course within 5.5 years in which 1 year is included for the medical internship. But if the student is doing their MBBS degree course from abroad then they can complete their course within 6 yrs. In which 1 year of medical internship is included or we can say the temporary job.

Famous place and more about the MBBS in Abroad

Applicants looking for MBBS abroad will only need the best for themselves. Various variables go into making a country famous among Indian students. The cost of studies, everyday cost, MCI's perceived college and strong educational program-based instructions are among the top calculations that help in choosing the best medical college abroad.

There are so many popular places or destinations where the Indian people can easily go for the MBBS course, such as Bangladesh, the people's republic of China, Nepal, Ukraine, and Russia. Doing MBBS abroad furnishes understudies with more worldwide openness, admittance to enormous college grounds, unrivaled research center access, and better understudy instructor proportion. A candidate who chooses to study in abroad they can also practice in the host nation and they can settle down there later.

Students who want to pursue their medical studies in abroad have to appear for Medical Council of India Screening Test, otherwise called Foreign Medical Graduates Examination (MCI FMGE) to practice in India. However, those students who have pursued their medical examinations from Australia, Canada, the United Kingdom, the United States of America, or New Zealand, and are considered for recruitment as clinical specialists in a different country are excluded from appearing for MCI FMGE.

Course and Duration of MBBS

MBBS is the Undergraduate (UG) degree program. It is the 5.5 years program in which 4.5 years is for academic education and 1 year is for an internship that is mandatory for all the student who is doing MBBS. The MBBS is one of the most needed courses or the program for the 12th standard students with a science background in the medical field. For perusing MBBS course there are a lot of colleges and universities all over the country, but there are certain criteria which the students have to fulfil i.e. the students those who have cleared their 10th &12th examination and the students who are having Physics, Chemistry and biology subjects in 12th standard can go for MBBS in any country. This is the only course by which you can become a Medical Doctor. There are many subjects in the MBBS course these are: Physiology, Biochemistry, Microbiology, Anatomy, pharmacology, and Pathology. In this course, you will not only study the theory part of all the subjects but there are also many practical bits of knowledge about the diseases or the courses. In the of 5.5 years, you will be going to interact with the patient for practical knowledge and the students will get much training regarding the same.

About MBBS Entrance Exam for the Indian Institutions

For taking the admission to MBBS course in India the students have to appear in one important exam i.e. (NEET) National Eligibility cum Entrance Test. This is the only exam or test for the students who want to do the MBBS course from any university or any college. This test is accepted by both the types of institutions or the colleges i.e., Government institutions and Private institutions. There are two other exams by which the students can take the admissions for the MBBS course that is JIPMER MBBS Exam and AIIMS MBBS Exam. However, these exams are not existing as of now for MBBS students because these exams were scrapped in 2019 for increasing the importance of the NEET exam and making it the most important medical entrance test in India.

Exams required to study MBBS abroad

In 2019, an order was passed by the (MCI) Medical Council of India that NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) exam is compulsory for all the students who want to do the MBBS course from abroad. If the candidate is going to give this exam, then the students must know about the general guidelines i.e., the candidate's highest limit for appearing in NEET is 25 Years for only the General candidates and 30 years for the SC/ST/OBC candidates. The NEET score is valid only for the 3 years after that you have to appear again in the same entrance exam. Another test that is required for those students who want to study abroad i.e., an English proficiency test to know that how much the student knows the English language. This English proficiency test is very important for all Indian students. Many English tests differ from the English language requirements of different universities. The candidates can opt for any of these (TOEFL, IELTS, PTE, OET) courses to clear their English proficiency test.

Eligibility criteria of Abroad and Indian Universities for MBBS?

In the world, each country is having its own rules and regulation or eligibility criteria for those international candidates who want to go for an MBBS course. There are so many top countries all over the world for Indian students who want to do the MBBS course. So, there are a few and important eligibility criteria for the candidates i.e., Age Requirements, Academic Requirements, Medical Requirements.

i) Age Requirements: The students must be having a minimum of 17years of age while taking the admission in MBBS course from abroad. There is no maximum age limit for taking admission but in India, the maximum age limit is 25.

ii) Academic Requirements: There are major and important academic requirements that are needed to fulfill by every candidate who is interested in taking admission abroad.

  • The candidates must have completed their 10th&12th from any authorized board.
  • The candidates must have at least a total of 50% marks in 10th&12th with the subjects Physics, Chemistry, and Biology and English. (40% marks is compulsory in case of reserved category)
  • There is one test which the students need to qualify for taking admission which is the National Eligibility cum Entrance Exam (NEET) without qualifying for this exam no students are eligible to peruse the MBBS.
  • The students are also required to fulfill the eligibility criteria that are issued by the Medical Council of India while taking admission to Foreign Medical Universities or in the Indian universities.

iii) Medical Requirements: The candidate needs to submit their medical certificates while perusing the admission in abroad universities and in Indian universities. In that certificate, it should be mentioned that you are not suffering from any diseases.

There are so many Top Universities in India for MBBS in which the Indian candidates can take admission. These are as follows:

  • All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS)
  • Armed Forces Medical College (AFMC)
  • Jawaharlal Institute of Post Graduate Medical Education and Research (JIPMER)
  • Christian Medical College (CMC)
  • Maulana Azad Medical College, New Delhi
  • King George's Medical University, Lucknow

Top universities or medical colleges for MBBS in Abroad

Admission Process for MBBS in Aboard Universities

If the candidate has satisfied their eligibility criteria, then the students can move on to the next step that is an application process to study MBBS Abroad. Candidates are free to select his interest of MBBS University and he/she can fill out the International Student Application form for starting his admission process. The candidate is required to upload their important documents because it is part of the application procedure to make your academic profile strong. The documents include:

  1. Class 10- and 12-mark sheets
  2. School Leaving & School Migration Certificate
  3. NEET Score Card
  4. Personal Financial Statements
  5. Statement of Purpose (SOP)Resume (CV)
  6. Letter of Recommendation (LOR)
  7. Language Proficiency Certificate (if any)
  8. Passport Size Photographs
  9. Medical Certificate

If your documents have been successfully updated or the application process is completed along with the fees, then students will get their offer or Admission letter from the universities. After this process, the candidate needs to apply for the visa along with their original documents, passport, and their letter of acceptance from the same University.

In foreign countries, the admission fees are too high and everyone cannot afford this. As we know that if we are going to take the admission abroad then they are providing the scholarship to those students who have scored high marks in USMLE. So, this is the best way to get the scholarship by applying for the online form for the same. The scholarship is providing to international students. Career after MBBS course

In all students' minds, this question arises after completing the MBBS course that what we can do after this course. So, there are many opportunities that you can go for as you all know that in the final year, all students are getting internship opportunities. The students can work with hospitals, health care centers as consultants, physicians, or medical assistants in critical care units. The students can join or work in health campaigns organized by the government to create awareness related to general public health issues about different diseases, medicines, and fitness through their campaigns or conferences.

After completing their internship, the students can also register themselves as a doctor with the Medical Council of India (MCI). They can also apply for further studies such as post-graduation in medical science. While perusing higher studies in medical science, the students can also associate with pharmaceuticals as a research associate. They can apply for the central government, state, or local organizations, including hospitals, the defense sector, railways. The students can do practice as a physician and can earn up to INR 7,16,338 per year.

Jobs after MBBS

There are many jobs that students can do after completing their MBBS course, but the one option is that they can for further studies also. The students who are interested in the jobs after the MBBS course can get jobs in government sectors like in Indian railways and jobs in the Indian army. These are the highly payable and most reputed jobs as compare to other professions or the other fields.

About these jobs:

Indian Railway Jobs for MBBS students: In this job, the students need to qualify for a few exams like the PPB entrance exam to get the job in railways fields only. The students must be MBBS graduates, or sometimes post-graduation students or M.D. students are required. The average salary of the MBBS in Indian railways is rupees 9, 50,000 per year and it may differ as per contract basis or permanent basis.

Indian Army jobs for MBBS students: Indian army is the big word for everyone, and in this field, the requirements of doctors are a must because, with the help of a qualified doctor, the army man can do more for us. The candidate can get the job of medical officers in the armed forces, i.e., the Indian army, navy, or air force. The average salary of medical officers in the Indian army is between rupees 9,70,000 to 13,20,000 per annum.
