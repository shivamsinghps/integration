Tourist Places in Udupi

A charming town in Karnataka named Udupi, also known as "the temple city", is frequently visited by tourists. Udupi cuisine, which gave its name to one of the Indian cuisines, is now universally recognised as vegetarian food. There are more intriguing places to see in Udupi despite the town's reputation as a pilgrimage centre. Udupi, which sits on the Indian coast, is home to some tranquil beaches, islands, and other attractions. Discover the greatest tourist attractions in Udupi by reading on.

The city, which started as an ancient old town, has changed into one of India's fastest-growing towns thanks to Manipal's colleges and global culture. Everyone may find something amazing in Udupi to satisfy their travel needs, whether it is the historically significant buildings' exquisitely crafted architecture or an opulent resort vacation by the beach, making the city truly a traveller's heaven.

In this article, we discuss the best tourist places to visit in Udupi:

1. Mary's Island - A Seashell Heaven

The most well-known of Malpe Beach's four rocky islands is St. Mary's Island. St. Mary's, often called Coconut Island, is renowned for its uncommon crystalline basalt rock formations, which were created by volcanic activity millions of years ago.

The Island's distinctive landscape is a result of these rock formations, which also make it a sanctuary for those who enjoy geology and wildlife. This geological treasure trove, which is still underdeveloped but has enormous tourism potential, is also home to one of India's 26 geological museums.

The Island is situated approximately 14.5 kilometres distant from the Udupi Bus Stand.

Location: Karnataka, 576118 Malpe

Time: Ferries leave Malpe Beach for St. Mary's from 9:30 am to 5:30 pm. The Island is inaccessible from June to September because it is rainy season.

Cost: There is no entry cost.

Known for: The calm atmosphere

Activities: Watch the sunrise and sunset, take pictures, and eat local seafood.

2. Malpe Beach

You will undoubtedly adore Malpe beach if you are a beach lover. It is recognised as one of Karnataka's top beaches and is yet completely undiscovered. So, this is an excellent beach to visit if you're looking for unusual spots to visit in Udupi.

When you want to get away from the city's noise and bustle, this place is fantastic. The beach is spotless and unspoiled because it is uncharted terrain. Enjoy sunset here with your significant other. You can also explore the Daria Bahadurgad fort, which is located on this beach.

The distance between Malpe Beach and the Udupi Bus Stand is approximately 10 kilometres.

Location: Karnataka, Udupi

Time: Always open

Cost: No entry fee

Known for: Seductive beauty

Activities: Taking nature hikes and watching the sunset

3. Padubidri Beach - Bask in the Sun

About a kilometre separates Padubidri Beach from the National Highway. This beach is one of the area's most pristine and is excellent for unwinding and relaxing. As the sun starts to set, the Suratkal and Kapu lighthouses nearby start to sparkle.

Sunset and moonrise can be seen in opposite directions at certain times. Swimming in the water is possible because the sea is calm outside of monsoon season. The farthest point of Padubidri Beach, also known as Padubidri Endpoint, is where the river empties into the ocean.

About 31.5 kilometres separate this beach from the Udupi Bus Stand.

Location: 574111 in the Udupi District of Karnataka

Time: Always open.

Cost: There is no entry cost.

Known for: Breath-taking views

Activities: Relax, go for a nature stroll.

4. Theertha Waterfall - Obtain Refreshing Power

Beautiful Kudlu Theertha Falls is nestled close to Hebri in the vast Western Ghats region. This location, also known as Sita Falls, is fantastic for trekking. These falls have a 150-foot drop into a sizable pool of water.

One of the top tourist destinations in Udupi is this gorgeous waterfall, where you may swim safely in the pool below the falls. When the water is not too choppy; one can hike close to the base of the falls. Another cascade, Manga Teertha, is located above Kudlu Theertha but cannot be reached directly.

The distance between these falls and Udupi Bus Stand is 57.7 kilometres.

Location: 576112 Nadpalu in Karnataka

Time: 9 a.m. to 4 p.m.

Cost: INR 50 per person is the entry cost. Overhead camera charges are INR 200.

Known for: Attractive waterfall

Activities: Going for walks in nature, taking a bath, having a picnic, and taking pictures.

5. Sri Krishna Temple

The 13th-century Sri Krishna Shrine, also known as Sri Krishna Matha, is a Vaishnavite temple founded by Saint Jagadguru Sri Madhvacharya. The Navagraha kindi, a distinguishing aspect of this temple, is a silver-plated window with nine openings through which the God is worshipped. The Ashta Mathas offer daily prayers to God, and worshippers from all over the nation come to this temple to show their respect. This temple, which is well-known across the world for its customs, ceremonies, and Dvaita philosophy teachings, draws tourists who are interested in learning more about this way of life.

The temple is located within the town of Udupi and is roughly 4.5 kilometres from the bus stop.

Location: Thenkpete, Maruthi Veethika, Sri Krishna Temple Complex, Temple Car St., Udupi, Karnataka- 576101

Time: 4 a.m. to 9 p.m.

Cost: There is no entry cost.

Known for: A place of worship

Activities: Praying and worshipping.

6. Daria Bahadurgarh Fort

On one of the four rocky islands of Malpe Beach, Basavappa Naik of Bidanur built the Daria Bahadurgad fort. This amazing Island, which is only two kilometres from Malpe, is also home to factories that produce Mangalorean tiles close to the fort, making a day trip there to learn how these tiles are made an educational excursion.

The fort, which is now mostly in ruins, has a spectacular outlook, and sunset, when the entire horizon changes to a range of pink hues, is the best time to come.

About 10 kilometres separate the fort from the Udupi Bus Stand.

Location: Laccadive Sea.

Time: Always open.

Cost: There is no entry cost.

7. Barkur Village - Discover this Cute Village

In Barkur village, the Barkur temples are remarkable and have unique qualities that distinguish them from other temples. Although they are constructed with sloping terracotta roofs similar to Keralan temples, they are distinct since they lack gopurams.

In this short region, there are over eleven temples, each with a special meaning. Additionally, there is a fort called Barkur that houses artefacts from the kingdom's past.

Udupi Bus Stand is 15.7 kilometres from Barkur, which is located in that general area.

Location: 576210 Udupi, Karnataka

Time: Always open

Cost: There is no entry cost.

Known for: Architecture and temples

Activities: Stroll through the charming village and go to temples.

8. Coin Museum Corp Bank - Collectors of Coins

Corporation Bank is another name for Coin Museum Corp Bank. The Heritage Museum includes a sizable collection of coins dating back to 400 BC from different Indian rulers. Additionally, visitors can have a thorough overview of the background history of banking in India since 1906.

The heritage museum was renovated to be a "Financial Temple" in the home of the Corporation Bank's founder. The gallery is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Udupi since it is home to about 1400 coins with a current market value of more than 4 million rupees.

The Udupi Bus Stand is only 4.5 kilometres distant from the museum.

Location: Corporation Bank Road, Thenkpete, Maruthi Veethika, 576101, Udupi, Karnataka.

Time: The time is from 10 am to 5 pm. (Sundays are off-limits).

Cost: There is no entry cost.

Known for: Huge collections of coins

Activities: Visit a museum and know the history.

9. Chaturmukha Basadi

In the town of Karkala, there is a historic symmetrical Jain temple called the Chaturmukha Basadi. Due to its four symmetrical faces, this historical temple is known as "chaturmukh" and was built by Immadi Bhairarasa Vodeya in the 16th century on a small rocky hill from the Santara dynasty.

This temple, also known as Tribhuvan Tilaka Jina Chaityalaya, is entirely carved out of granite. The Basadi has excellent sculptures of Mallinath, Tirthankara Aranath, and Munisuvratnath in addition to facing the large Bahubali monument in the town.

This magnificent site, which took 30 years to create, currently has 108 beautifully crafted pillars in its complex.

The Udupi Bus Stand is roughly 38 kilometres from the temple.

Location: Dhana Shala Road, Fisheries Office, Karkala, Karnataka 574104

Time: From 8 a.m. to 6 p.m.

Cost: There is no entry cost.

10. Kodi Beach - Best for Beach Games

The Kodi Beach, sometimes called the Delta Beach, is adjacent to Kundapur Town. Kodi, which in Kannada means "shore", is particularly well-known since it is one of the best spots to visit in Udupi because it is surrounded by water on three sides.

The River Suvarna and the Arabian Sea meet at Kodi Beach, which is also well-known for its seafood and a favourite weekend getaway spot for Manipal University students.

About 36 kilometres of distance separate the Udupi Bus Stand and Kodi Beach.

Location: Maravanthe

Time: Always open.

Cost: There is no entry cost.

Known for: Natural beauty

Activities: Take a bath, enjoy in the area's atmosphere, and taste local eateries' traditional fare.

11. Manipal lake - A Calm Side of the Town

Manipal Lake is one of the best tourist attractions in Udupi despite being a little lake that locals appreciate. It is situated in the centre of the town and provides visitors with boating amenities. Additionally, it is a favourite hangout for university students. In addition, it's a fantastic area to meander around and eat some delectable street cuisine.

The distance between Manipal and Udupi Bus Stand is about 4.5 kilometres.

Location: Manipal Lake is located in Udupi, Karnataka, 576104.

Time: 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.

Cost: About 10 INR on entry.

Known for: Calm lake

Activities: Boating, unwinding, and photography Cost: INR 30.

12. City Centre - A one-stop shop for you

A renowned shopping and lifestyle attraction in Udupi is its City Centre. This commercial centre, which has a number of shops, restaurants, and service locations, is situated on Mosque Road in the middle of the city. Originally constructed to accommodate the traffic between Udupi and Manipal, this is today a well-liked tourist destination and one of the top attractions to see in Udupi.

The shopping centre is around 3.7 kilometres away from Udupi Bus Stand.

Location: Maruthi Veethika, near the District Bus Terminus, next to the Jamia Masjid, in Udupi, Karnataka 576101

Cost: Entry is not charged.

Known for: Shopping

Activities: Shop, sample the cuisine of the neighbourhood, and take in the mood.

13. Mangroove Plantation

Visit the mangrove plantations to see Udupi's lovely and environmentally conscious coastal districts if you're looking for natural hotspots. Around the gangolli, Kodi, and Swarna riverine areas, there is an extensive amount of mangrove plantations. Visit the Parampalli, which has acres of mangrove forest and is close to the Kodihole river. As a lover of nature and wildlife, you'll appreciate it and surely consider it to be among the best Udupi tourist attractions.

Location: 576104 Parampalli, Udupi, Karnataka

Cost: NA

Known for: Mangrove plantations

Activities: Sight-seeing, photography, and nature hike.

14. Koup Beach - Unknown Udupi Gem

One of the nicest locations to visit in Udupi is Kapu, or Kaup Beach, a town in the district of Udupi that is situated halfway between Mangalore and the town of Udupi. This is because of the crystal-clear water that breaks against the sand beachfront that is lined with palm trees. A historic lighthouse built in 1901 and located on this beach is open to visitors from sunrise until 6:30 am.

It is incredibly amazing to watch the sun gently rise over the vast ocean, and one could easily spend hours doing so. Although the seas of Kaup Beach are rougher than those at Malpe Beach, they are nonetheless beautiful.

The distance between Kaup Beach and the Udupi Bus Stand is around 22 kilometres.

Location: 574106 Padu, Karnataka

Time: 5:30 a.m. until 6:30 a.m. for the lighthouse. The beach is open every day.

Cost: Entry is INR 10 for the lighthouse and INR 0 for the beach.

Known for: Enlivening sights

Activities: Taking nature hikes and watching the sunset.

15. Anantheshwara Temple

One of the oldest temples in the Tulu Nadu region, the Anantheshwara Temple, is situated next to the well-known Shri Krishna Matha Temple. This ancient Hindu temple, built during the Alupas' rule in the eighth century, is devoted to Lord Shiva.

The Anantheshwara Temple, which is connected to the Dvaita philosopher Madhavacharya as well, is the ideal location for anyone looking for serenity. One can visit the temple in the early morning and meditate here.

About 5 kilometres of distance separate this temple from the Udupi Bus Stand.

Location: Sri Krishna Temple Complex in Thenkpete, Maruthi Veethika, Udupi, Karnataka 576101.

Time: 6 am to 12:30 pm, and 6:30 pm to 8 pm are the hours.

Cost: Entry is not charged.

Known for: A place of worship

Activities: Praying and worshipping.
