Best Movies on Netflix

Finding the best Netflix movies may be difficult, but there will always be fantastic films to watch. It could be challenging to choose the perfect Netflix film to watch. We've all been there. You have chosen to watch a certain program. You have full access to Netflix, including a streamlined list of the movies you've already marked for later viewing, but there is also the matter of choosing. There are numerous alternatives accessible if you're seeking the best action movies, horror movies, comedies, or old movies on Netflix. You need to choose something that fits your mood or something you can agree on with your friend, lover, or couch mate. You finally find something after hours of surfing, but it's too late, you're too tired out, and indecision has prevailed.

So, do not worry; we will provide you with the list to assist you in locating the best Netflix Movies titles available. We've browsed the collection and put up a selection of some of the greatest movies streaming, including classics, recent releases, and more. We've tried to make it easy for you to choose the best titles to Watch on Netflix with both new releases and underappreciated movies, saving you the time of browsing through genres in search of the perfect movie to watch.

1. The Irishman

I Heard You Paint Houses, a Scorsese version of Charles Brandt's biography of mafia enforcer Frank Sheeran, took a while to make and takes some time to see. Robert DeNiro stars in the production as a former truck driver who joins a Pennsylvania criminal family under the leadership of Russell Bufalino, played by Joe Pesci. The ensemble cast is nothing short of spectacular, and because of that, the three-and-a-half-hour classic Scorsese film The Irishman, which explores the loneliness of a lifelong criminal, is all the better. Al Pacino plays Jimmy Hoffa, the leader of the Teamsters union, while Pesci and De Niro each have two of their most significant parts to date. The CGI de-aging effects in the film will wow you.

2. Tell Me Who I Am

In the opening scene of this documentary, Alex Lewis is involved in a motorbike accident and awakens in the hospital without remembering who he is. He has no memory of anything?not even the definitions of a bicycle or a TV, much less who his mother or father is?but he does recall his identical twin brother, Marcus. Alex is filled with questions as he returns to his boyhood home, and Marcus is equally filled with solutions.

As if this documentary wasn't already a crazy tale, Marcus omits one crucial element from Alex's life. However, Marcus gradually becomes aware of his ability to alter Alex's account of their history. Although the film is tragic, it is also intriguing and, in the end, humanizes the brothers' experience.

3. Saving Private Ryan

Steven Spielberg's Saving Private Ryan, one of the best World War II films ever produced, debuted on Netflix in April 2022, giving your watchlist a touch of Hollywood history. The five-time Oscar winner centers on a squad of troops charged with locating and returning a paratrooper (Matt Damon) stationed deep within occupied France.

One of the most famous opening scenes in movie history is the one from Saving Private Ryan, which is a graphic recreation of the D-Day landings. Tom Sizemore, Edward Burns, Barry Pepper, Giovanni Ribisi, Vin Diesel, Adam Goldberg, and Jeremy Davies make up the rest of Tom Hanks' team in this one, and their camaraderie and sacrifice make for constantly moving to watch. Spielberg's epic is must watch if you enjoy war dramas.

4. Beasts of No Nation

Beasts of No Nation is difficult to watch; we won't beat around the bush about that. This terrifying film, directed by Cary Fukunaga of No Time to Die, chronicles the story of a young orphan (Abraham Attah) who is coerced into serving as a child soldier by a ruthless warlord (Idris Elba) during an unspecified African civil war. Idris Elba, who plays the commandant, gives an outstanding performance in this fascinating and terrifying film. He commits several war crimes, including grooming a youngster for battle, yet you still want to support him. The young Abraham Attah performing the role of Agu is also a revelation.

Beasts of No Nation, a film version of the same-titled book by Uzodinma Iweala, is a superbly photographed account of the human cost of combat and puts the terrible reality of the war front. Although it's not a movie for families, sitting through its two-hour story is an educational and, dare we say, essential cinema experience.

5. The Conjuring

James Wan is at least an above-average horror filmmaker by any objective standard. The creator of popular television franchises like Saw and Insidious has a talent for writing populist horror that retains a hint of his unique aesthetic personality. He has a Spielbergian aptitude for writing for multiplex audiences without completely losing characterization.

If this list were to be enlarged, some of his films would rank just outside the top 100, but The Conjuring is unquestionably the scariest of all of Wan's feature films, making it the clear Wan representative. The Conjuring has a way of defying when and where you anticipate the horrors to occur.

6. RRR

RRR, an Indian epic that first aired in theatres, has found a new home on Netflix thanks to Western consumers. The Telugu-language film, which S. S. Rajamouli directed, is set in the 1920s and concentrates on two rebels named Alluri Sitarama Raju (Ram Charan) and Komaram Bheem (N. T. Rama Rao Jr.) as they violently oppose the British Raj. There's a reason RRR has been hailed as one of the all-best time's action films.

First, what more do you need if none of it strikes your interest? It lasts for 182 minutes, which is a lengthy period, but RRR doesn't waste any time by including motorbike chases, violent bow and arrow battles, and men battling alongside bleeding tigers. Furthermore, there is a love story, a sweet bromance, and a few musical pieces, so don't worry. This is one of the best Netflix movies currently accessible because of all these elements.

7. Apollo 10½: A Space Age Childhood

A Space Age Childhood is the most recent movie Jack Black, and Oscar-nominated director Richard Linklater created. After two NASA scientists, played by Zachary Levi and Glen Powell, learn that they mistakenly made modules that are too tiny for an adult, Stanley, a fourth-grader, is launched into space. The performers have worked together multiple times, with the movie School of Rock possibly being the most notable. The debut of their latest picture is similarly joyful.

Apollo 1012, although being about a child, is firmly rooted in the past, with its fictionalized events taking place in 1969?the year when the very real Apollo 11 became the first spacecraft to send humans to the moon. Additionally, the non-fantasy components of the movie are heavily inspired by Linklater's personal history, making it partially autobiographical. Black portrays an older Stanley in the meanwhile. Not only is the plot of Apollo 1012 noteworthy, but the whole production is animated with the actors rotoscoped over, resulting in some creative passages.

8. Tick, Tick... Boom!

Wiz Hamilton Tick, Tick... Boom! An upbeat rendition of a lesser-known piece by Rent creator Jonathan Larson marks Lin-Manuel Miranda's feature film debut. The mostly autobiographical plot follows aspiring theatrical composer Jon (Andrew Garfield) as his post-college fantasy and reality clash. In 1990, while working in a New York Cafe, he was driven by a strong desire to realize his musical aspirations before turning 30. Garfield performs admirably during his first singing role, and Miranda shoots with vigor, escaping the usual stage-to-screen challenges while nailing the many musical sections.

9. Okja

A sci-fi adventure film that Bong Joon-ho directed makes overt allusions to the contemporary food market. At the time of its release, Okja, which starred Tilda Swinton, Paul Dano, and a cast of phenomenally gifted performers, generated a lot of conversation and controversy, particularly around the morality of meat production. It also demonstrated that businesses like Netflix might succeed spectacularly with out-of-the-box artistic decisions as long as they do them with assurance. Bong Joon-ho and company have enough of that.

This is a fun movie because of its daring and original plot, fantastic action, and stunning cinematography. Bong Joon-ho is involved; when this Academy Award-winning filmmaker is involved, you know you're in excellent hands. Who needs a justification to watch anything that Tilda Swinton is in?

10. Django Unchained

The 2012 Western epic Django Unchained, directed by Quentin Tarantino, is the director's most successful and profitable work to date. It is set in 1858 and tells the story of a formerly enslaved person (Jamie Foxx) who enlists the help of a German bounty hunter to free his wife Broomhilda (Kerry Washington) from the control of a cruel plantation owner (Leonardo DiCaprio).

Quentin Tarantino's most action-movie-style blockbuster film may be the high-octane modern western Django Unchained. It merits the advertising blitz; before attempting to save Django's wife from the villainous Calvin Candie, former slave Django (Jamie Foxx) links up with Dr. King Schultz (Christoph Waltz) to pursue criminals throughout the American South (meme king Leonardo DiCaprio).

It is very much an Inglourious Basterds-style rewrite of history that is brutal, humorous, and successful. The candid look into enslaved people's lives in America (and the violence done upon them) makes Django Unchained incredibly unpleasant. Still, it also has that Tarantino touch that makes it wonderfully entertaining?a pairing that may seem strange or in poor taste to some.

11. The King

Timothée Chalamet plays Henry V in The King, another Netflix original film. After being abruptly crowned king of England in the 15th century, Henry V is a young man who must navigate the realms of politics, battle, and deceit. With a stellar ensemble that includes Sean Harris, Joel Edgerton, and Robert Pattinson, this film has all the pomp and circumstance you'd expect from a contemporary medieval film. Director David Michôd created one of the most captivating and artistically spectacular historical dramas available on a claimed $20 million budget.

12. Stree

Chanderi serves as the backdrop for this unique dark comedy based on a story from South India. According to local mythology, the ghost of a prostitute named Stree is claimed to visit the locals during yearly religious festivals and capture local males. Rajkummar Rao's character Vicky, a tailor, reveals all of this to Shraddha Kapoor, his new client, after finding love with her during the festival. Vicky's friends start to believe that Stree herself could be the mysterious object of his infatuation, so they set up traps for both living things and ghosts to learn the truth and save the town.

It boasts a compelling plot and stars Rajkumar Rao, a national award winner, and Shraddha Kapoor. Additionally, actors like Abhishek Banerjee, Pankaj Tripathi, and Aparshakti Khurana have excelled in supporting parts. Regarding box office earnings, the film made 280.76 crores against a meager 25 crores in spending.

13. Drishyam

Based on the immensely popular Malayalam film of the same name, Drishyam tells the tale of a father who would stop at nothing to protect his family. Ajay Devan plays the main character, and he crushed the job. Just be aware that it's one of Bollywood's finest suspense films ever.

14. Guru

Abhishek Bachchan's performance in Guru demonstrates that he is not the amateur everyone believes him to be. The life of Dhiru Bhai Ambani, the man who founded Reliance, is based partly on the film. Although it isn't a biography, it draws heavily on his life. It chronicles the rise of a peasant who immigrated to Bombay in 1958 and became the nation's most illustrious industry mogul.

The casting is excellent, and the story is well-written. You have a compelling plot portrayed by skilled actors and actresses; when you combine Mithun Chakraborty, R. Madhavan, Vidya Balan, and more talented supporting actors, both Aishwarya Rai and Abhishek Bachhan excel in their roles as Gurukant Desai, the major character.

The music composed by A. R. Rahman with lyrics by Gulzaar is the icing on the cake. It enhances the film rather than detracting from it. The music of Guru will bring you joy, tears, and smiles. You'll go on an adventure thanks to it.

15. 3 Idiots

One of the greatest films on Netflix is 3 Idiots, based on Chetan Bhagat's most well-known book, Five Point Someone. If you haven't already, you must watch the Bollywood film 3 Idiots. The film, directed by Rajkumar Hirani, examines life in an engineering college. Rancho, the main character, is a creative troublemaker with eccentric answers to every issue. The story opens with two friends searching for a friend from college. While waiting, we listen to them reminisce about the heyday of their college years, which were full of unforgettable occasions, lessons that changed their lives, and some key ideals that most of us should follow.

After graduation, he terminated all ties with everyone for an unknown reason. Throughout the film, Raju, and Farhaan, his two closest pals he persuaded to change their minds and look for him. This film has drama, suspense, a compelling plot, and humor. Your perception of Aamir Khan's portrayal as a brilliant college student would undoubtedly change after watching the film. It reminded the Indian audience of the importance of knowledge over information and the need to follow our dreams and put our passions above material possessions.

16. Rang De Basanti

Probably one of the all-time greatest Bollywood films is Rang de Basanti. The film is a masterpiece of the art of filmmaking, in addition to having a compelling plot and top-notch performances. This film has an amazing transition between its two primary timeframes. Every sequence fits well, and you never feel out of place.

This movie is for you if you enjoy seeing movies that get you to reflect on your decisions in life. In terms of the story's narrative, it tells the tale of young Indian people who are reconnected to their history. Six teenage Indians play a significant role in the narrative as they help an English woman shoot a documentary on independence warriors. As they document the story, they become aware of the hardships endured to create this nation and the duties entrusted to us to advance it. Since the famed Mr. A. R. Rahman composed the soundtrack, you can be sure that the music will be fantastic.

17. Zodiac

The brilliant tagline of David Fincher's crime thriller claims that there are many ways to be killed by a killer. While the Zodiac Killer's murders in the 1960s and 1970s in San Francisco are at the center of the story, Jake Gyllenhaal's newspaper cartoonist, Robert Downey Jr's. crime reporter, and Mark Ruffalo's police inspector are the ones who become obsessed with solving the case, which was infamously never solved.

The explanations keep eluding them as they dive further into the murderer's potential identities, creating a serial killer film that is both intellectually and physically gripping (the reconstructed death sequences are truly nightmarish). It all adds to one of Fincher's finest accomplishments, told over 157 unhurried minutes.

18. The Big Short

This film, based on Michael Lewis' nonfiction book "The Big Short: Inside the Doomsday Machine," follows a few astute traders as they discover the housing market bubble that contributed to the financial crash in 2007-2008 before everyone else.

The film is renowned for its deft explanations of complex financial products, such as Selena Gomezexplaining synthetic CDOs over a poker game or Margot Robbie explaining mortgage-backed securities in a tub filled with champagne.

Hollywood movie The Big Short, which made over USD$130 million in the United States alone and won many Academy Awards, was likewise seen as such. This film provided insightful insights about human behavior, Wall Street greed, and how it can negatively impact everyday Americans' lives. Anyone interested in the stock market should see this film without a doubt.

19. Moneyball

Billy Beane (Brad Pitt), the general manager of the Oakland A's, thinks that baseball's usual method of doing things is incorrect since no one bothered to do the math to discover what works. Beane is compelled to find value for the club's money because of his budgetary constraints in Oakland and the owner's desire to manage the team like a company rather than as a money-losing hobby.

To concentrate on what counts most in winning games, base hits, and pitching, he hires Ivy League graduate Peter Brand (Jonah Hill) to use his laptop to crunch the statistics for draught picks and players. He seeks for and selects undervalued players with shortcomings who have the potential to win games. You will enjoy this movie if you appreciate baseball and Jonah Hill.

20. Forrest Gump

Nearly 30 years have passed since the world was equally moved to tears and wonder by the presentation of the life of an intellectually challenged man. After receiving acclaim from critics all around the globe, gaining a devoted following, and winning six Academy Awards, Forrest Gump is still included on every list of the greatest films produced in modern times. Tom Hanks portrays the title character in Forrest Gump, based on the same-titled 1986 novel.

The film's plot follows a man with a low IQ over several decades. His outlook on life and method of living is an encouragement to all of us. In addition to teaching Elvis Presley to dance, becoming a football star, meeting John F. Kennedy, serving in Vietnam, and speaking at an anti-war demonstration at the Washington Monument, Forrest pulls off several other incredible accomplishments that the rest of the world would find impossible. These are but a few illustrations.

This gorgeous film, only recently released in 2019, has been garnering praise and making waves worldwide because of its motivational plot. The narrative is set in Malawi, an African nation experiencing a severe drought that has left thousands of citizens without access to clean water. William Kamkuamb, who is just fourteen years old when he first notices the crisis, later studies and develops an original plan to save his home community from being thirsty.

22. Into the Wild

When things are difficult, we frequently fantasize about running away and embarking on an adventure, but then it occurs to us that making something that spontaneous genuinely would take a lot of work, so we dismiss the idea and resume watching films like "Into The Wild." This one is one of the finest movies on Netflix to watch to unwind and think about what it means to be a wanderer.

The story of Christopher Johnson McCandless, a man who chose to live in the Wild after giving all his money to charity, is told in the film Into the Wild. He then takes rideshare to Alaska. Into the Wild is one of the best Netflix movies, with a flowing background score that will help you relate to the joyful attitude of each scene.

23. Dangal

Dangal, an Amir Khan epic, is also a must-see Bollywood movie to watch on Netflix. It is based on the true tale of veteran wrestler Mahavir Singh Phogat and his struggles to teach his two wrestler children to find their way to the Commonwealth Games. It won many people over with its energizing plot and emotional teachings. Dangal provides a glimpse at the unflinching dedication in the face of social injustice.

This moving Netflix film depicts how Geeta Phogat and Babita Kumari went on to win gold and silver medals at the 2010 Commonwealth Games with a significant lesson. This film restores your confidence in girls' empowerment and the power of pure resolve and persistent hard work to realize unattainable goals.

Due to it being an urban pastoral, Netflix's finest romantic film is also the winner of the Oscar for best foreign language movie. 25. The Departed

Martin Scorsese comes to mind whenever we discuss legendary thriller films. Martin Scorsese has directed several suspenseful films, but it wasn't until this crime classic, which some criticized for being too traditional and uninspiring, that he finally received his Oscar. With actors like Jack Nicholson, Matt Damon, and Leonardo Dicaprio, The Departed is a protracted but masterfully made gangster film that offers a fascinating glimpse into the life of an undercover spy.

Martin Scorsese proved with this film that he is the master of urban storytelling and intensely suspenseful cinema. The film's focus is Leonardo DiCaprio, who plays a police officer while being someone else. The reality that corruption begins in early childhood is cruelly hinted at in parts of the film by Scorsese.

26. Shutter Island

Martin Scorsese produced this outstanding psychological thriller, and Dennis Lehane's book of the same title inspired the script. The film's narrative centers on the 1954 missing of a person from the Ashecliffe Institution for Violent and Mentally Ill Criminals. Two detectives, played by Leonardo DiCaprio and Mark Ruffalo, then get in touch with the island to inquire about a patient's whereabouts. People viewing Shutter Island must pay close attention to become invested as the characters unravel the island's mysteries.

While never resorting to poor startle techniques, Scorsese maintains a high level of anticipation throughout the whole movie. The soundtrack enthralls the viewer even in passages that shouldn't be stressful. Because Scorsese wants his viewers to be in that exact scenario, his films are great experiences that keep you anticipating until the end.

27. Schindler's List

Netflix has a fantastic documentary about the time when the Nazis persecuted Jews. Oskar Schindler, a German entrepreneur, tried to save his Jewish workers at that time. The Oscar-winning picture "Schindler's List," among the greatest film available on Netflix, is both the most lauded and the most harshly attacked in the Spielberg canon. In Schindler's List, there is an inherent optimism and confidence in hope among those who are oppressed, imprisoned, and tormented.

28. Jack Reacher

The 2005 book One Shot by the Child served as the inspiration for the Jack Reacher plot. This espionage film on Netflix offers a clever debut to the Reacher franchise character, which Tom Cruise ably supports. Jack Reacher is more contemplative drama than a current, extremely quick-action thriller as a spy film.

It is a practical design that allows for a few fascinating twists and instances of shock for watchers, but viewers who regularly follow the story and are meticulously weighing the evidence while attempting to solve Jack Reacher's mystery before it is supposed to be solved will probably predict the majority of the predicted turns. For a chance to fall in love with Tom Cruise once more, watch this Netflix espionage series.

29. The Siege of Jadotville

One of those excellent war films, Siege of Jadotville, depicts the brave warriors. This 2016 historical war film on Netflix, which Richie Smyth directed, tells the story of how an Irish commander and his 150 men fought against 3,000 Congolese soldiers under the leadership of French and Belgian mercenaries. They battled with the force of 3,000 Katangese invaders on a peacekeeping operation in 1961 when some were still minors.

31. The hand of God

Paolo Sorrentino, a popular Italian filmmaker, makes his filmmaking comeback with The Hand of God, which depicts the semi-autobiographical story of a young man (Filippo Scotti, playing a youthful Sorrentino) who must deal with the rigors of having to grow up in the 1980s Naples. The film's title references a sad and life-affirming occurrence that compels its character to mature sooner than he'd want, in addition to the historic goal scored by Argentine footballer (and legendary Napoli player) Diego Maradona at the 1986 World Cup.

Even if the film's stunning settings, mesmerizing cinematography, and larger-than-life characters assure that it rates among Sorrentino's greatest work, saying more runs the danger of giving away some of its most heartfelt moments. Add this to your watchlist if you like Luca Guadagnino's Call Me by Your Name.

Why not return to the Breaking Bad universe with this feature-length spin-off El Camino if, like the rest of us, you're anxiously awaiting the release of Better Call Saul's concluding episodes? El Camino picks up immediately after the Breaking Bad season finale and centers on Jesse Pinkman's journey to freedom and a new life.
