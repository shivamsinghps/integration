Best Mileage Car

When it comes to buying a car, the vast majority of consumers in India consider the vehicle's mileage as well as its low cost of maintenance.

A car's mileage is one of the most important factors in determining whether or not it will be the first choice because it opens up the possibility of using the vehicle for business purposes too. Additionally, fuel efficiency is unquestionably an essential component.

So, if you are looking for a vehicle with the best mileage, the following list will help you narrow your search. These vehicles are among the best in their class and won't strain your finances.

1. Hyundai Venue

Sport utility vehicles or SUVs are not particularly well-known for their remarkable fuel economy, but this is not the case with the Hyundai Venue. The Hyundai Venue has astoundingly good fuel efficiency estimates, ranging from 18.15 to 23.7 kilometers per litre. This has helped the brand become a runaway success in the lucrative sub-four-meter SUV class.

The Venue may have arrived on the scene a little too late, but it is loaded with various contemporary engine choices. The first car has a direct-injection 1.0-liter GDI engine and a 6-speed manual gearbox. The engine is paired to the transmission by default.

This vehicle's engine and gearbox combination achieve a fuel economy rating of 18.27 kilometers per liter. Thus, making Venue the most fuel-efficient gasoline-powered SUV available in India. The powertrain system achieves ARAI-rated fuel consumption of 18.15 kmpl when coupled with a 7-speed automatic dual-clutch gearbox. This is quite a respectable statistic for an automatic petrol-powered SUV.

It is significant to note that Hyundai does not provide an automatic transmission option for the 1.2-liter petrol engine option in the Venue. According to the manufacturer, the fuel economy of the petrol-powered Venue 1.2 is a decent 17.52 kilometers per litre. It is the 1.4-liter CRDi engine that is responsible for the Venue's exceptional fuel economy.

The Hyundai Venue is undoubtedly worthy of being rated first as the best mileage SUV in India that costs less than 10 lakhs. This recognition is well deserved.

2. Honda City E: Hev

The new City e: HEV hybrid model that Honda has developed specifically for the Indian market was recently made public. It is said that the new model would have a certified fuel economy of 26.5kmpl, which would place it in second place among the most fuel-efficient cars in India (Petrol Segment).

The new City e: HEV is powered by a 1.5-litre Atkinson Cycle petrol engine with four cylinders and naturally aspirated combustion, and it is teamed with two electric motors. The electric motor produces 109 horsepower and 253 newton-meters of torque from a standstill, whereas the petrol engine is rated at 98 horsepower and 127 newton-meters of torque.

The lithium-ion battery in the City Hybrid has a capacity of 0.734 kWh and weighs 14.5 kg. This variant only comes with one gear ratio that is always the same.

There are three driving modes: Engine Drive, EV Drive, and Hybrid Drive. Engine Drive runs only on the petrol engine, while EV Drive runs solely on the electric motors, and Hybrid runs on a combination of both.

3. Mahindra XUV300

The Mahindra XUV300 is equipped with a stylish exterior design, an interior that is packed with features, and fuel-efficient engines. According to the ARAI test cycle results, the petrol model of the Mahindra XUV300 is said to have a fuel economy of 17.0 kmpl, while the diesel model is said to have a fuel mileage value of 20 kmpl.

This vehicle was designed specifically for the current consumers in India. The engine of the Mahindra XUV300 has been fine-tuned to roll out the highest performance and fuel efficiency statistics possible. The start-stop system is designed to automatically switch off the engine when it is not being used to prevent the unnecessary use of petrol.

The car's sixth gear was developed specifically to reduce fuel consumption in addition to its other functions. It is possible that it will make the engine spin more slowly when you are cruising at high speeds on the highway, which will help you save a significant amount of money on fuel consumption.

Under the bonnet of the Mahindra XUV300 are two engine choices: a 1.2-liter turbocharged petrol motor that generates 110 BHP and 200-newton meters of torque, while the other is a 1.5-liter turbo-diesel mill that generates 115 BHP and 300 newton-meters of peak torque. Both of these engines are turbocharged.

The XUV300 is equipped with a 6-speed manual transmission as the standard gearbox; however, a 6-speed automatic transmission is available as an option for the diesel engine.

4. Tata Nexon

The Tata Nexon is undeniably one of the most attractive automobiles available in its price range. Nevertheless, you might not be aware that the Tata Nexon is also one of the most fuel-efficient cars in the Indian market. When it comes to the amount of money spent on the amount of fuel consumed, a Tata Nexon is unquestionably a good buy in India.

The Nexon has the highest ARAI-rated fuel efficiency level of any compact SUV. Moreover, it complies with the BS6 standard, which makes it the most fuel-economical SUV in that category. When equipped with a petrol engine, the Tata Nexon can achieve a mileage of 17.4 kilometers per liter (km/l). In contrast, diesel models achieve a mileage of 22.4 kilometers per liter (km/l), independent of whether they are manual or automatic.

When it arrived in 2020, the Tata Nexon had a BS6-ready 1.2-litre 3-cylinder turbo-petrol engine that generated 120 horsepower and 170-newton meters of torque.

The diesel engine with a capacity of 1.5 liters produces maximum power and torque figures of 110 PS and 260 Nm, respectively, per the new norms.

5. Honda BRV

The Honda BRV can be purchased with diesel or petrol-powered engines. The power for the petrol model comes from a 1.5-litre i-VTEC engine that has a capacity of 1497 cc. Furthermore, it can generate 118 horsepower at 6,600 revolutions per minute (rpm) and 145 Nm of torque at 4,600 revs per minute (rpm). The Automatic Petrol model gives an average of 16.0 Km/liter.

The Diesel model achieves a mileage of 21.9 kilometers per litre. On the other hand, the diesel model is propelled by a motor with a displacement of 1498 cc and a capacity of 1.5 liters i-DTEC. This engine generates 98.6 horsepower and a maximum torque of 200 Nm.

The Honda BRV has a maximum speed of 190 kilometres per hour. The total curb weight of the vehicle is 1306 kilograms, and the fuel tank has a capacity of 42 litres. This automobile can be purchased in India for approx. 8.75 lakh Indian rupees.

6. Honda City 1.5 E MT

The Honda City 1.5 E MT is the entry-level petrol model in the lineup of Honda City automobiles. It is driven by a 1497 cc, 1.5-litre petrol engine that is capable of producing 119 PS of maximum power at 6600 rpm and 146 Nm of peak torque at 4600 rpm. The engine is powered by petrol.

The Honda City 1.5 E MT strikes the ideal balance between luxury and performance while maintaining exceptional mileage. A five-speed manual transmission gearbox is responsible for transferring power from the engine to the front wheels of the hatchback. Additionally, this engine achieves an exceptional fuel economy of 16.8 kilometers per litre.

It features a fuel tank that has a capacity of 40 liters and an overall curb weight of 1165 kilograms. The Honda City 1.5 E MT has a maximum speed of 171 kilometers per hour.

This motor vehicle can be purchased in India for a price of 8.21lakh Indian rupees. So, if you want to purchase a sedan, this is a good option.

7. Hyundai Creta

Hyundai Creta is available in India at Rs. 10.2 lakh. It is propelled by a 1.6-liter petrol motor and a 1582 cc engine, both of which produce 123PS of power and 151Nm of torque, respectively. Due to its impressive combination of power, performance, and good aesthetics, the Hyundai Creta quickly became the most popular automobile in India.

The mileage of a Hyundai Creta begins at 17 kmpl and may reach a maximum of 21 kmpl. The Creta has a mileage of 17 kilometers per liter for its petrol model, while its mileage varies from 19 kilometers per liter to 21 kilometers per liter for its diesel model, with its maximum speed of 165 kilometers per hour.

It has a fuel tank that has a capacity of 50 liters, and the entire curb weight of the vehicle is 1665 kilograms. This vehicle has a total of six gears for enhanced acceleration.

The Hyundai Creta is undoubtedly one of the very finest automobiles available in the sub-10-lakh pricing bracket for SUV vehicles.

8. Hyundai Aura S

The Hyundai Aura S is a vehicle that can operate efficiently on either petrol or electricity. It is both easy on the wallet and friendly to the environment. The stated mileage is completely ARAI-rated, and it provides a mileage of 20.5 km/L at its best.

When compared with diesel and CNG models, the Aura CNG shows reduced fuel consumption. Thus resulting in a fifty percent reduction in the cost of fuel. Unquestionably, it is one of the best CNG cars with the best mileage in India as of the year 2022.

The engine in the Aura S has a displacement of 1197 cc and has the capacity to produce 68 bhp of power and 95 nm of torque. This vehicle has a manual transmission with five different gear ratios. The emission standard meets BS6 requirements.

The trunk space that is provided is a whopping 402 liters, which is a significant amount, and it has a fuel capacity of 65 liters.

Since it has a grade of two stars from the NCAP and has airbags for both the driver and the passenger, its level of safety is quite high. In addition to that, there is a sophisticated entertainment and communication setup in the vehicle.

9. Maruti Vitara Brezza

The Maruti Vitara Brezza is a well-known brand name in the world of subcompact SUVs. When we think about the factors that have contributed to the success of the Vitara Brezza, the car's excellent gas mileage stands out as particularly significant.

The redesigned 2020 Maruti Vitara Brezza will continue to provide an astoundingly high degree of fuel economy, despite the changes that have been made to the vehicle.

The new petrol-manual transmission Vitara Brezza achieves a fuel economy of 17.03 kilometers per liter (km/l), while its petrol-automatic counterpart achieves a mileage of 18.76 kilometers per liter (km/l), which places the Vitara Brezza name in the list of best mileage cars in India.

The BSVI-compliant 1.5-liter K-series petrol engine with mild-hybrid technology is one of the options available in the facelift for the 2020 Maruti Vitara Brezza. This engine is capable of producing 105 maximum horsepower and 138 maximum Newton-meters of torque.

All of the models come with a manual gearbox that has five gears as standard, and buyers will have the option of selecting a torque converter with four speeds.

10. Ford Ecosport

The long-running and venerable Ford Ecosport is the next vehicle on our list of the most fuel-efficient cars available in India. The Ecosport has a fantastic appearance, wonderful features, an amazing engine, and excellent driving characteristics.

The Ford Ecosport offers purchasers the choice between two different engine configurations: a 1.5-liter 3-cylinder petrol engine that generates 118 horsepower and 149 Newton-meters of torque, or a 1.5-liter 4-cylinder diesel engine that generates 99 horsepower and 215 Newton-meters of torque. Even in its BSVI form, the Ecosport continues to be a model of excellence when it comes to its ability to save fuel.

The Ecosport's petrol engine achieves 14.8 kilometers per liter for automatic models and 15.9 kilometers per liter for manual models. Alternatively, the ARAI estimates that the Ecosport diesel has a superior fuel economy of 21.7 kilometers per liter.

11. Honda WRV

When compared to other younger SUVs that compete in the same market, the Honda WR-V may be an SUV that is undervalued. When comparing its fuel economy to that of its competitors, the WR-V, on the other hand, does not seem to ever come in second place.

The ARAI-certified mileage of 17.5 km/l for the petrol versions and even 25.5 km/l for the diesel variants demonstrates that the Honda WR-V is very economical. The petrol-powered WR-V has a city mileage rating of 15.35 kilometers per liter, while the diesel model has a rating of 13.29 kilometers per gallon. Surprisingly, the WR-V petrol is rated to achieve 25.88 kilometers per liter on the highway.

The present 1.2-liter i-VTEC petrol engine and the 1.5-liter i-DTEC diesel engine will be carried over. However, they will be redesigned to conform to BSVI standards.

In terms of the transmission, the petrol-powered Honda WR-V is mated to a 5-speed manual gearbox (MT), whereas the diesel-powered Honda WR-V is mated to a 6-speed manual transmission (MT) unit.

12. Maruti Suzuki Baleno1

Maruti Suzuki Baleno has been one of the hatchbacks that have sold the most units in the nation, and it just continues to get better.

The Baleno is propelled by either a 1.2L Naturally-Aspirated petrol engine that provides 83Ps of power and 113 Newton meters of torque. Or a 1.2L mild-hybrid Dualjet petrol engine that generates 90Ps of power and 113 Newton meters of torque.

The naturally aspirated 1.2-liter petrol engine may be purchased with either a 5-speed manual or continuously variable transmission (CVT) system. The 1.2L Dualjet petrol engine is only offered in conjunction with a 5-speed manual gearbox configuration.

The Maruti Suzuki Baleno has a mileage that varies from 22.35 to 22.94 km/l (kilometers per liter). The Baleno Petrol Manual is rated with a mileage of 22.35 Kmpl according to ARAI. The Baleno Petrol has an ARAI-estimated economy of 22.94 kilometers per liter when equipped with an automatic transmission.

Push-button start and stop, keyless entry, and automatic climate control are some of the new features that have been added to the Baleno. Other new features include LED projector headlamps, 16-inch alloy wheels, and a rearview camera. It also has a 7-inch touchscreen infotainment system that comes with connectivity for both Apple CarPlay and Android Auto.

Other safety features, including ABS with electronic brake distribution (EBD), ISOFIX child seat anchors, rear parking sensors, and twin front airbags, are also included on the Baleno.

13. Renault KWID

The Renault KWID is one of the greatest automobiles. It has some of the most up-to-date specs and features, and it gets excellent mileage.

If you choose the 0.8-liter model, the KWID has 54PS of power and 72Nm of torque; the 1.0-liter model has 68PS of power and 91Nm of torque. In terms of transmission, the 0.8L petrol model is only offered with a 5-speed manual gearbox, whilst the 1.0L model may be purchased with either a 5-speed manual or an automated gearbox combination of the same capacity.

On highways, the Renault Kwid's petrol-powered manual and automatic transmissions both provide between 23.01 and 25.17 kilometers per liter of fuel economy. The mileage for the Manual Petrol model comes in at 25.17 kilometers per liter. The Automatic Petrol model has a mileage of 24.04 kilometers per liter (kmpl).

A manual air conditioning system, keyless entry, a 12V charging outlet for passengers in the rear, and parking cameras in the rear are some of the most recent features that have been added to the new KWID. Other features include an 8-inch touchscreen infotainment system that comes with connectivity for Android Auto and Apple CarPlay.

The KWID places a strong emphasis on safety, as seen by the fact that it comes standard with a driver-side airbag, parking sensors in the back, and ABS with electronic brake distribution (EBD).

14. Maruti Suzuki Ignis

The Maruti Suzuki Ignis is a potent alternative that offers the highest possible fuel efficiency. Moreover, it comes standard with all of the most recent technological advancements and amenities.

The most up-to-date version of the Ignis is propelled by a 1.2-liter petrol engine that has the potential to produce 83 horsepower and 113 newton-meters of torque. The Ignis may be paired with either a manual transmission with 5 speeds or automatic transmission with 5 speeds.

The fuel economy of the Maruti Suzuki Ignis is the same whether it is equipped with a manual transmission or an automatic transmission; it is 20.89 kilometers per liter. However, one's actual mileage will differ based on their driving style, the location in which they drive, whether or not they utilize air conditioning, and other variables of a similar kind.

The Ignis is undoubtedly one of the greatest choices available in the affordable category since it comes with a long-range of exceptional features.

In addition, the Ignis is loaded with goodies like an infotainment system with a 7-inch touchscreen that is compatible with both Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, LED headlights with daytime running lights, automatic temperature control, and alloy wheels that measure 15 inches.

Maruti Suzuki has always emphasized safety, and the Ignis is equipped with several safety features, including ABS with electronic brake distribution (EBD), parking sensors in the back, and twin airbags in the front seats.

Conclusion

So, if you want to purchase a new car. You may acquire any of the cars listed above. These will assist you in lowering your costs while increasing efficiency and performance. These best mileage cars assure to provide unparalleled fuel efficiency, making them excellent choices in India.
