Tourist Places in Vijayawada

A well-known city in Andhra Pradesh, Vijayawada, is situated in a beautiful area next to the Krishna River. Some of the best sites to visit in Vijayawada include the hills, canals, and numerous mediaeval temples surrounding this lovely city. One can visit places like Undavalli Caves, Prakasam Barrage, Bhavani Island, Sri Durga Malleswara Swamy Varla, Kanaka Durga Ammavari Temple, Raghavaiah Park, Bapu Museum, Moghalrajpuram Caves, Akkanna Madanna Caves, and many more.

The tolling bells of notable temples like Sri Subrahmanya Swami Temple and Kanaka Durga Ammavari Temple will greet you as you explore Vijayawada. The lavish green beauty surrounding Rajiv Gandhi Park, Gandhi Hill, and Raghavaiah Park will titillate you.

Visit Undavalli Caves, Akkanna Madanna Caves, and Bhavani Island if you're interested in ancient caves. Don't overlook the Bapu Museum and Hinkar Tirtha, two significant Vijayawada landmarks. Each location is significant to the city's religious and scenic traditions. The top tourist attractions in the city are listed below for your convenience.

1. Undavalli Caves

The Undavalli Caves are unquestionably a wonder of mediaeval architecture, and each rock carved tells a different tale. The monolithic construction in these caverns is often associated with Indian rock-cut architecture. These caverns are said to have been created in the fourth century and were carved out of a single piece of sandstone on a hillside.

It is thought that these caverns are the first example of Gupta architecture that predominated in the nation in later centuries. The building has attained national significance today. The cave is separated into three layers, each depicting a historical event that history buffs are sure to find fascinating. One of the best attractions in Vijayawada, the caves' lush green surrounds complements the historical significance of this site.

Location: Penumaka-Vijayawada Rd., close to Prakasam, Undavalli, Barrage, Andhra Pradesh 522501

Cost: No entry fee

Times: 9:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.

Optimal Season: October through March.

2. Bhavani Island

The Bhavani Island is a famous landmark in the city because it is one of the biggest islands of its sort to be located in a river. It has established itself as the ideal site for guests to visit and unwind due to its geographic location and construction. This is the ideal weekend getaway spot for folks who find adventure appealing and appreciate activities like water slides.

To get to the island, you will need to take a boat, which is an exciting journey in and out itself, taking you by picturesque vistas of the lush green surrounds and the lovely Krishna River. In addition to relaxing on the island or sleeping in a hammock, you may also enjoy other sports like water skiing, parasailing, kayaking, and fishing.

Location: Vijayawada, India, on Bhavanipuram Bhavani Island

Cost: No entrance fee

Time: Anytime during the day.

Optimal Season: October through March.

3. Prakasam Bridge

One of the top attractions in Vijayawada is the Prakasam Barrage, which features an iconic building and contributes to the city's fame. The building connects the Krishna and Guntur districts with its beautiful 1223-meter length along the Krishna River. The barrage is illuminated at night in a symphony of lights, offering the most breathtaking perspective of the building.

As a result, it attracts people from nearby and visitors who stop to see the wonder from a distance. The barrage offers a breathtaking view of the Krishna River and space for irrigation infrastructure in the surrounding districts.

Location: 523105 Prakasam Barrage Road, Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh

Cost: No entrance fee.

Time: Anytime during the day.

Optimal Season: October through March.

4. Kanaka Durga Ammavari Temple

A stunning shrine to the goddess Durga is located inside the temple, surrounded by spectacular Dravidian-inspired architecture. The idol present on the grounds of the temple is thought to be one of the nation's many shakti peethas. The temple is situated alongside the Krishna River, on top of the Indrakeeladri hills.

Given that it is mentioned in numerous religious scriptures and ancient literature, the temple is held in high regard by its adherents and is given even more notoriety. It is advised that you pay attention to the temple hours, the Seva schedule, and the darshan guidelines if you are a fervent devotee visiting this temple to worship the Supreme Being to get the most out of your trip.

Location: Mallikarjunapeta, Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh 520001, India, Indrakeeladri, Durga Temple Ghat Road

Cost: No entrance fee.

Times: Monday through Wednesday, 5:00 AM to 1:00 PM, and Thursday, 5:00 PM to 9:00 PM.

Optimal Season: October through March.

5. Kondapalli Fort

The magnificent Kondapalli Fort is situated in the Guntur district, close to Vijayawada, in the Kondapalli village. The magnificent fort is a popular tourist destination since it is thought to have had a significant historical impact. Due to its close vicinity to Vijayawada, which is only 23 kilometres away, locals frequently visit the location for a full-day picnic with friends and family. Awe-inspiring views of the valley should be noted as you tour the majestic fort. Particularly the Kondapalli Dolls, wooden toys from the hamlet, are well known. If you're fortunate, you might even come across a vendor selling these lovely dolls for you to bring home as mementoes.

The Musunuri Nayaks in the area built Kondapalli fort, also known as Kondapalli Kota. Many believe that this imperial fort had roots in many historical periods. It was used as a military stronghold by the British and is thought to have been constructed in the 14th century as a hub for trade, business, and entertainment. A reservoir, Golconda Darwaza, Dargah of Gareeb Saheeb, Tanisha Mahal, and other historical sites that are now in ruins are among the major characteristics of this architectural wonder. The entrance gate, known as the Dargah Darwaja, is carved out of single granite rock. When travelling near Vijayawada, one must not skip visiting the Kondapalli Fort.

Location: Vijayawada's Ghat Road, Kondapalli.

Cost: Adults pay INR 5, while children pay INR 3.

Time: 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

Optimal Season: Between November and February.

6. Rajiv Gandhi Park

The Rajiv Gandhi Park is a well-kept park full of flowers, thick grass, and lush foliage, making it the perfect place to unwind and laze around in the city. The park also features a small zoo and musical show to keep you entertained.

Location: India, 520001, NH65, Krishnalanka, Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh.

Cost: No Entry Fees.

Time: 2:00 pm to 9:00pm.

Optimal Season: Whole year.

7. Amravati (Capital City)

Amaravathi, also known as the capital of Andhra Pradesh, is a planned community on the banks of the Krishna River. Amaravathi is well known for being the location of a Buddhist Stupa, a semi-hemispherical building housing Buddhist relics and frequently referred to as the Abode of God.

Amaravati, which is 32 kilometres north of the Guntur District, is a significant centre for pilgrimage and tourists. The ancient building, which is now a city meditation site, was built during the reign of Emperor Ashoka.

The city was planned to have 51% green space due to its 217 km riverfront location. Earlier this year, on October 22, 2015, Prime Minister Narendra Modi laid the building's cornerstone. Hyderabad was designated as the state of Telangana's capital following the division of the country into two parts in the year 2014.

As a result, Andhra Pradesh picked Amaravathi as its capital. The Satavahana dynasty's historic capital is where the word "Amaravathi" originates. Due to the numerous temples and shrines that beautify the small hamlet in various locations, pilgrims visit this town virtually all year long.

Location: The Coromandel Coast is located in the Coastal Andhra area of Andhra Pradesh, India, 10 kilometres (6.2 mi) south-west of Vijayawada, 25 kilometres (16 km) north of Guntur, and 45 kilometres (28 mi) south-east of Tenali.

Cost: No Cost.

Time: N.A

Optimal Season: November - March.

8. Gunadala Matha Shrine (Church)

The St. Mary's Church is located among the hills of the Krishna District. A sacred relics museum and an iron cross that has been placed on the top of a hill can be found here. When the Annual Feast of Our Lady of Lourdes is held there, the region becomes a hub of great celebration and devotion.

Location: In Andhra Pradesh's Krishna District, in the Vijayawada neighbourhood of Gunadala.

Cost: No Entrance Cost.

Time: 7:00AM to 7:00 PM.

Optimal Season: Anytime

9. Subramanya Swami Temple

The Subramanya Swamy Temple, situated in Vijayawada (Andhra Pradesh) at the base of the Indrakiladari Hills, is dedicated to the Lord of Serpents Kartikeya. The temple honours Lord Subramanya in all three of his incarnations: as a kid, as Sri Valli Devayanai, his original form, and lastly, as a serpent. It is sculpted from gleaming white stone, and the facade is ornately decorated with stonework. A Garuda pillar with a silver covering is also housed in the temple, and its worshippers attach significant religious value to it. The believers worship the nearby anthill, a snake's natural habitat, with the same fervour and conviction.

Visits to this temple are unexpected encounters with nature as it is surrounded by stunning mountains, forests, and rivers. Before entering the temple gates, one must take a sacred plunge into the Kumaradhara River. You can go around the deity from the temple's back entrance, which is where the entry is made.

Beyond the aforementioned Garuda, the pillar houses the temple's main sanctum, where Subramanya and Shesha serve as the chief deities. Daily pilgrims from all over the nation come to this temple to worship these gods, which is quickly rising in popularity.

Location: It is on Indrakeeladri Hills, next to Goddess Kanaka Durga at Vijayawada.

Cost: Free Entry.

Time: 6:30 AM-1:30 PM and 3:30 PM-8:00 PM.

Optimal Season: Anytime

10. Victoria Museum

With its countless sightseeing opportunities, Vijayawada draws visitors from across the nation. The Victoria Museum, which is on Bandar Road, is a must-see attraction in Vijayawada for those who enjoy archaeology. One of the top attractions in Vijaywada and one that everyone should see is the Victoria Museum, which houses a diverse collection of artwork and antiquities.

For those who enjoy archaeology and historical and prehistoric items, Victoria Museum is a must-visit tourist attraction. The museum's varied exhibits are well-maintained and attract visitors with their distinctive collections. The different exhibits at Victoria Museum can be viewed without charge, and it is conveniently located in one of the busiest areas.

On Fridays and federal holidays, the museum is closed. Victoria Museum is unquestionably a top Vijayawada attraction that is worthwhile for both young and old visitors.

Location: Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh, Krishna District, M G Road, 520013, India.

Cost: Rs 50 per person.

Time: 10:30A.M. To 5:00 PM and every Friday, it is closed.

Optimal Season: Whole year.

11. Hazartbal Mosque

Here is a distinctive mosque that retains a long-standing place of significance for Muslim devotees if you are considering the greatest Vijayawada sights to see on your vacation to Andhra Pradesh. This mosque stands out for its superior architectural splendour compared to other mosques you may have seen. The mosque is supposed to have a holy object that belonged to the Prophet Mohammed.

The mosque appears to be a splendour of white beauty that is a pleasure for the eyes because of its sparkling attractiveness. Beyond its religious significance, people visit the mosque to take in the abundance of beauty and architectural splendour. The mosque sees a lot of traffic throughout the year because it is also thought that looking at the relic can solve all of a person's troubles.

Location: India, Vijayawada 520001

Cost: No entrance fee.

Time: 9:30 AM to 5:30 PM

Optimal Season: Any time of the year.

12. Mogalarajapuram Caves

Vijayawada has become one of the most popular tourist attractions in Andhra Pradesh because of its flawless fusion of traditional and contemporary India. This Andhra Pradesh city, which is the third largest, is renowned for its parks, museums, temples, and caverns, in addition to its historical significance.

The Mogalarajapuram caverns, discovered in the fifth century, are located around 5 kilometres from the centre of Vijayawada city. Large numbers of people are drawn to these caverns by its exquisite carvings and sculptures. These caverns are conveniently located close to Vijayawada city.

The Mogalarajapuram caverns are a must-see tourist attraction because of their exceptional sculpture. The approach to these caverns gives stunning views as it is situated in a luxuriant verdant environment. The hilltop's panoramic vista is also captivating. Anyone visiting Vijayawada should not skip a visit to the Mogalarajapuram caverns, a site of significant archaeological and sculptural value. It is worthwhile to appreciate the remarkable carvings and the care that the artisans took in making them.

Location: Moghalrajpuram Caves, 522503, Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh, India.

Time: 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM.

Cost: Rs 3 per individual.

Optimal Season: Any time of the year.
