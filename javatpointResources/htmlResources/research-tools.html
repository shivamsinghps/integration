Research Tools

What is Research?

The term 'Research' means a proper study regarding a specific topic or concern using specific methods. According to Earl Robert Babbie, the American sociologist, "Research is defined as a systematic analysis to describe, explain, predict and control the phenomenon observed." Inductive and deductive methods are involved in the research.

In order to analyze an observed event, inductive methods of research are used. The inductive methods are related to the qualitative. For the verification of the observed event, deductive methods are used. Deductive methods are related to quantitative research.

Characteristics of Research

There are various characteristics of research:

  1. For precise data, a systematic approach must be followed. Rules and procedures are essential part of the process which set the goal. Researchers must practice ethics and codes of conduct when making observations or conclusions.
  2. Research depends on sensible thinking, which includes inductive and deductive methods.
  3. The information or knowledge is obtained from actual observations in natural settings in real time.
  4. An in-depth analysis of all the data collected so that there are no discrepancies.
  5. Research creates a way to generate new questions. The data which is existing supports to create more research opportunities.
  6. In nature, research is analytical. It uses all available data so that inference is not ambiguous.

Types of Research

There are various types of research:

  1. Basic research
  2. Problem-solving research
  3. Problem oriented research
  4. Applied research
  5. Qualitative research
  6. Quantitative research

1. Basic Research: - Data collected to improve knowledge is a basic research definition. Knowledge expansion is the core motivation. It is a non-commercial study that does not make anything easier to create or invent. For example, an experiment to identify a simple fact.

2. Problem-Solving Research: - Problem solving research is directed by organizations to comprehend and resolve their own issues. This type of research utilizes applied examination to discover answers for the current issues.

3. Problem-Oriented Research: - As the name recommends, problem-oriented research is led to comprehend the specific idea of an issue to discover applicable arrangements. The term 'problem' refers to several options or issues when examining a condition.

4. Applied Research: - Applied research emphasizes on analysis and solutions to real-life issues. Applied research refers to a study that supports solve practical issues with the help of scientific methods. In solving issues, study plays an essential role that affects the entire well-being of humans.

5. Qualitative Research: - Quality research is a process which is related to the inquiry. It helps to create a thorough understanding of problems or problems in their natural language. Qualitative research is a non-statistical method.

Qualitative research depends a lot on the researchers' experience and the questions used to examine the sample. The size of sample is generally restricted to 6-10 people. Open-ended questions are asked in a way that gives rise to another question or set of questions. The objective of asking open-ended questions is to collect the information from the sample.

1. Quantitative Research: - Quantitative research is an organized method of gathering information and examining it to make determinations. Quantitative data is related to the numbers.

In quantitative research more people are involved-more people means more data. With more information to examine, you can get more precise outcomes. This method of research uses close-end queries because researchers usually want to collect statistical data. There are various tools which are used in quantitative methods in order to gather the data such as polls, questionnaires, surveys.

Types of Research Methods

There are following types of research method:

  1. Qualitative research
  2. Quantitative research

1. Qualitative Research: - Qualitative research is a research method in which data is gathered using conversational methods. The participants are free to ask open-ended queries. The responses which are gathered are non-numerical. This technique not just enables a specialist to comprehend participants' opinions yet additionally why they think with a specific goal in mind.

Types of qualitative research:

  • Focus groups
  • Ethnographic research
  • One-to-one interview
  • Case study
  • Text analysis

2. Quantitative Research: - Quantitative research is another type of research which deals with numbers and measurable forms. We use quantitative research to answer questions in terms of justifying relationships with quantifiable factors in order to either describe, forecast or handle an event.

There are various methods of quantitative research:

  • Descriptive Research
  • Survey Research
  • Correlation Research
