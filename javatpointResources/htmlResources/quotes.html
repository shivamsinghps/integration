Quotes

Quotes are defined as the quotation from the words or text attributing to the creator. The purpose of using Quotes is to provide direct information from work.

Or

Quotes are the sentences or words written by another person.

Quotes are enclosed within quotation marks in written text.

For example,

Practice makes a man perfect.

Quotes represent well-known statements, which are used all over the world. Quotes are used for motivation, inspiration, love, etc.

Quotes Categories

Here, we will discuss the following categories of Quotes. Each category will include 15 quotes with their moral. At the end of this article, we will discuss types of Quotes.

  • Inspirational Quotes
  • Motivational Quotes
  • Life Quotes
  • Christmas Quotes
  • Father's Day Quotes
  • Positive quotes

Types of Quotes

There are three types of quotes, which are listed below:

  • Direct Quotes
  • Indirect Quotes
  • In-text Quotes

Direct Quotes

Direct quotes are the quotes that we have taken directly from the source without modifying it. Such quotes depict direct meaning and require no further explanation.

The direct quotes are generally enclosed within quotation marks.

For example,

A room without books is like a body without a soul."          - Marcus Tullius Cicero

Indirect Quotes

Indirect quotes are used to extract ideas from the source. These quotes are long and are useful for the abstraction of the main idea.

Indirect quotes do not require any quotation marks.

In-text Quotes

In-text quotes are the short quotes that are used as a part of a sentence. It means that these quotes can easily fit in a sentence to make it a complete sense. It further adds delight to our writing.

For example,

So many books, so little time"          - Frank Zappa

The above quote can be written as:

There are so many books to read, but little time to cover them.
