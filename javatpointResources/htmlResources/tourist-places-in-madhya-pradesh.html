17 Best Tourist places in Madhya Pradesh

Major Tourist Destinations of Madhya Pradesh: Madhya Pradesh, which is called the heart of India, is famous worldwide for its tourist destinations. Many historical monuments, mosques, temples, forts, and palaces attract tourists. Traveling to Madhya Pradesh will be no less than a dream for the tourists. It also contains numerous national parks and wildlife refuges, home to multiple threatened species of flora and fauna.

Madhya Pradesh is a state situated in the middle of the country. This is why it is also very easy for tourists visiting India from anywhere in India or the world to come to MP. We will tell you about those tourist places in Madhya Pradesh, where once you come, you will want to visit again and again.

If you are going to Madhya Pradesh or want to know about the tourist attractions of Madhya Pradesh, then definitely read this article, where we will introduce you to the major tourist places and attractions of Madhya Pradesh.

1. Khajuraho

Khajuraho is a very special city and tourist destination in Madhya Pradesh located in the middle of India, which is famous for its ancient and medieval temples in the nation and worldwide. Khajuraho, the curious land of Kamasutra in Madhya Pradesh, has drawn visitors worldwide. This little village in the Chhatarpur district is well known for its good suggestive set of memorials, which has made its standing on the list of UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

The renowned Khajuraho Temple is a group of Hindu and Jain temples in Madhya Pradesh.

Khajuraho is the foremost choice of visitors due to its famous temple; there are many places to visit where you can have them on your Khajuraho trip.

2. Pachmarhi

Pachmarhi is a beautiful hill station located in India's Hoshangabad district of Madhya Pradesh. Pachmarhi is the center of attraction for the country's tourists and abroad for its beauty; it has everything that a tourists expects, such as peace, blooming mountains, evergreen greenery, flowing waterfalls, silver cascading waterfalls, colorful shades, and more. The enchanting views of the beauty of the four moons of Pachmarhi. Pachmarhi is very cheap compared to other hill stations; this tourist place situated in the hills of Satpura is also known as Kashmir in the central region.

3. Indore

Indore is the most crowded city of Madhya Pradesh, discovered on the southern boundary of the plateau Malba, 190 km from the state capital Bhopal.

Indore has been making the state proud by being ranked number one among the cleanest cities in India for four consecutive years under the Swachh Bharat Mission. Not only this, but Indore is also very popular known as the food capital and mini Mumbai tourist destination.

It is said that a beautiful temple of Lord Indra was built in this city in the 18th century, which inspired the British rulers to name the city Indore.

4. Gwalior

King Surajsen builds the famous city of Gwalior. The beauty of this historical city, the attractive monuments, palaces, and temples are less appreciated. The splendid architectural work has been done in the mosques, rock temples, and sculptural structures. Gwalior is not limited to palaces only; its hills and beautiful greenery attract many tourists. Let us tell you that Tansen was born in Gwalior, and his tomb remains the same.

5. Kanha National Park, Mandla

Kanha National Park Located in Madhya Pradesh, it is the largest national park in central India, famous for the national animal tiger and the habitat of many such wild animals. Kanha National Park is a city located in the Mandla district of Madhya Pradesh, which mesmerizes the tourists visiting here with its natural beauty. Kanha National Park was established in 1955, and since then, many endangered species have been protected here. Kanha National Park was carried beneath Project Tiger Reserve in 1974. The national park area is spread over 940 square kilometers, divided into two sanctuaries, Hallan and Banjar.

Considered to be one of the best parks in Asia, this national park is home to 22 species of large mammals, over 300 species of wildlife, and diverse bird species. The most special thing about Kanha National Park is that it inspired the well-known Rudyard Kipling's book - The Jungle Book.

6. Orchha

Orchha Fort is situated on the island of Betwa river at a distance of 16 km from Jhansi in Madhya Pradesh. Orchha Fort was constructed in the 16th century by Raja Rudra Pratap Singh. The main attraction of this fort is the Raja Mahal, which displays intricate architecture. Along with the Raja Mahal, many attractions like Sheesh Mahal, Phool Bagh, Rai Praveen Mahal, and Jahangir Mahal are located in this fort to attract tourists, which adds to the charm of this fort.

While a part of the fort was converted into a Ram Raja temple, this temple is the only place in the country where Lord Rama is worshipped as Raja Rama. The fort is an ideal destination for tourists and history and architecture lovers in Madhya Pradesh, attracting thousands of tourists every year.

7. Omkareshwar

Located at the confluence of the Narmada and Kaveri rivers, Omkareshwar has been given the state of the Hindu holy symbol 'Om' due to the merging of the two holy valleys and the river's waters Narmada. Its name is derived from 'Omkar,' which is the name of Lord Shiva. Located on Mandhata Island, Omkareshwar is believed to be one of the 12 Jyotirlingas that entice thousands of enthusiasts every year. It has two old temples; one is Omkareshwar, and the other is Amarkeshwar. This holy city also has architectural marvels and natural beauty, apart from pilgrimage sites.

8. Sanchi

Sanchi, one of the Buddhist pilgrimage sites in Madhya Pradesh, is famous for the oldest stone structures in India. The Buddhist Monuments at Sanchi symbolize the vast heritage of culturally rich India. Emperor Ashoka of the Maurya dynasty established the great stupa in the 3rd century BC. The stupas at Sanchi were built as homes to Lord Buddha and many important Buddhist relics. This place is surrounded by lush green gardens, which provide a different kind of joy and peace to the tourists visiting here.

9. Ujjain

Ujjain is the most religious place in Madhya Pradesh, which is called the city of Mahakal; in this post, information about the tourist places of Ujjain like Chintaman Ganesh Temple, Mahakaleshwar Temple, Siddhavat, Sandipani Ashram, and Vedashala (observatory Ujjain), etc. is given.

Ujjain is a great religious center like Banaras, Gaya, and Kanchipuram. Ujjain has carved a niche for itself as the city of Vaishnavism, Shaivism, and many other sects and sects such as Jainism and Buddhism.

Mother Shakti and her various forms are mentioned in the Avanti section of Skanda Purana. The crimes committed by the Tantriks of the Nath cult have flourished in Ujjain.

One of the 12 Jyotirlingas of India, Mahakal Jyotirling is in Ujjain; this Jyotirling is self-styled. This is a south-facing Jyotirlinga. Many tantric traditions are found in Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga itself. On the third floor, there is an idol of Lord Nagchandreshwar, whose darshan is visible only on the day of Nag Panchami.

10. Hanuwantiya

One needs to visit an amazing place to calm the mind, take a break from the daily lifestyle and spend some wonderful moments in peace. Although there are many tourist places to visit in Madhya Pradesh, after seeing Hanuwantia Island, located in the Khandwa district of Madhya Pradesh, you will also be convinced of the beauty of this place. One of the most popular tourist places in Madhya Pradesh, Hanuwantia Island, is famous for many exciting things like sports activities, floating, and trekking. Let us brief you that Hanumantiya Island is even known as Hanumantiya Island. So, if you are also going out to visit Madhya Pradesh, then definitely reach here.

11. Jabalpur

Jabalpur is a very beautiful city in the central state of India. The population of the city is estimated at 12.7 lakh, according to the 2011 census. In terms of area, Jabalpur city is spread over 367 square kilometers, and its height is 412 meters. Madan Mahal Fort, built on a rocky hill on the western outskirts of Jabalpur city, was built in 1116 AD. The age-old Pisanhari ki Madiya in the city gives a mesmerizing view of the Jain temple. Apart from this, Hanumantal Bada Jain Temple and Rani Durgavati Museum are some of the major tourist places in the city.

12. Bhimbetka Rock Shelter

If you are interested in history, this place is best for you to visit. Bhimbetka Rock Shelter is an archaeological site located in the Raisen district, considered the starting place of human development. More than 500 rock shelters and caves where paintings made thousands of years ago are still present today. The most aging painting here is assumed to be 12 thousand years old. Coming here, you will feel like you are in a distant world.

13. Maheshwar Temple

Maheshwar is located on the drifts of river Narmada and is known as the temple village of Madhya Pradesh. This city has its own mythological and historical significance. The city has also been mentioned in the epics of Ramayana and Mahabharata. Maheshwar was earlier the capital of the Holkar province of Queen Ahilyabai Holkar. She built many buildings in this city and beautified the city with Maratha architecture while carrying out public works. Maheshwar is quite famous for the production of Maheshwari Sarees.

14. Bhojpur Temple

There is an incomplete Hindu temple in Bhojpur village near Bhopal, the capital of Madhya Pradesh, dedicated to Lord Shiva. The most special thing about this temple is a 7.5 feet high linga in its sanctum. It is said that this Shivling was constructed during the reign of King Bhoj in the 11th century, and due to some reasons, the construction of this temple was left incomplete. If you visit this temple, you can also see the construction material there, which is of old times.

15. Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh National Park is one of the most important tourist places in Madhya Pradesh, which was the hunting ground for the Maharajas of Rewa in the olden times. This national park is known all over the world as Tiger Reserve. Bandhavgarh National Park is world-famous for Royal Bengal Tigers' density in India. This park is a very beautiful forest because of its abundance of wildlife and flora. More than 22 species of mammals and 250 species of avifauna are found in this park.

16. Amarkantak

Amarkantak is one of the major pilgrimage centers of Madhya Pradesh, which is also known as "Tirtharaj" (King of pilgrimages). Let us inform you that Amarkantak is a hill station situated at an altitude of 1065 meters, which is also famous for some of its exquisite temples. The most sacred and biggest river of Madhya Pradesh, Narmada, originates from Amarkantak, making this place very special. The dense forests of Amarkantak are rich in medicinal properties, making them ecologically important.

17. Shivpuri

Shivpuri is a famous city in Madhya Pradesh with special historical significance. It is around an area where Hindu and Mughal rulers have ruled for a long time. Shivpuri is a very quiet town situated at 478 meters above sea level, good enough for peaceful tourism. Named after Lord Shiva, the city served as a refuge for the Kachhwaha Rajputs until 1804 and was later ruled by the Sindhis. Let us inform you that the Han freedom fighter Tatya Tope was given the Shivpuri sentence of death.

Visiting Madhya Pradesh and exploring various tourist spots is interesting as all the locations have abundant historical, archeological, and spiritual significance. Therefore, whenever you plan a trip to Madhya Pradesh, do not forget to include these spots in your itinerary.
