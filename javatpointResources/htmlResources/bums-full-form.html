BUMS: Bachelor in Unani Medicine and Surgery

BUMS stands for Bachelor in Unani Medicine and Surgery. The Persian-Arabic medical system, founded on the ideas of the Greek physicians Galen and Hippocrates, is the subject of the five-year undergraduate curriculum known as BUMS. Jobs with titles like Unani paramedic, hakims, pharmacist, research officer, and associate research officer are accessible to BUMS hopefuls in both the public and private industries.

One of the traditional medical systems still used today in South, the Middle East, and Asia is Unani medicine. The famous physician "Hippocrates," who established this approach, did it in Greek culture. The "Hikmat" or Unani Tibb Medicine is another name for it. Its history dates back 6,000 years. This technique relates to the Roman drug regimen created by "Galen," a Roman physician. The Arabic and Persian doctors accepted and flagged this approach during the medieval ages due to the development of the system.

The Unani system of complementary medicine. The amazing healing capacity of the human body is strengthened to better care for patients. According to the Unani philosophy, the body can repair itself and regenerate. We only need to support its inherent healing ability.

And after allopathic, Ayurveda, and homoeopathy medical systems, Unani appears to be the fourth most widely used medical system in India. The four components of the human body, based on this concept, are blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile. In regards to the four elements, this is comparable to Ayurveda and is equally well-known.

From around the 14th century, Allaudin Khilji, the ruler of the Delhi Sultanate, introduced the Unani medical system to India. The correct diagnosis, in accordance with this system, is a prerequisite for treating any condition. Any disease must be diagnosed using clinical factors like signs, symptoms, laboratory findings, and mijaz. Not only in India but countries such as Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, and South Africa it is being widely used with the name of BCM Complementary Medicine: Unani Tibb.

The Unani system has shown remarkable success in treating conditions like:

Many colleges in India and overseas provide certificate and degree courses in the Unani medical system of treatment. Several young individuals in India today are drawn to the Unani philosophy. The youthful student generation is quickly embracing naturopathic medicine. There are numerous educational options in this area, including research programs and undergraduate, graduate, and diploma programs.

Duration of the course

One of the most well-known degrees in the field of Unani medicine is the Bachelor of Unani Medicine & Surgery (BUMS). A UG degree is nearly sufficient to land the job. This degree is granted after completing a 5.5-year academic program, which includes four and a half years of coursework and a one-year internship program with real-world applications. The Unani educational system's undergraduate program can be completed via correspondence courses.

Eligibility

The minimum prerequisite for admission to the BUMS program is passing the 10+2 exam with 50% marks. The applicant must have completed 12th grade with biology, chemistry, and physics. Students who studied Urdu in their 12th grade or who are fluent in Urdu have been given precedence for admission.

The Central Council of Indian Medicine (CCIM) in India is the governing organisation for Unani education. There are merely 35 medical institutions in India that provide the Unani style of medicine curriculum. Several reputable colleges offer admission via entrance exams at the federal and state levels. For admission to the BUMS course, students may sit for the NEET which is a National Level Test, and KEAM which is a State Level Test. The applicant must be at least 17 years old.

Jobs

After completing BUMS, there are professional opportunities both in India and overseas. Professionals in this discipline are needed by a large number of firms and research institutions engaged in production and research abroad. The applicant who has earned a Bachelor of Unani Medicine and Surgery degree (BUMS) is qualified to use the title of Hakeem (Doctor) and to engage in family practice. A physician of Unani medicine may consider a career as a doctor in a governmental or private hospital or as a medical representative.

The experts in this subject can work for firms that handle Unani preparations. They might land a position as a teacher or researcher in Unani colleges. Allopathic treatment is unpopular with a lot of people. This is where the alternative medicine system comes into role.

The standard for all other professions is the pay in the healthcare profession. This respect is owed to the Unani profession as well. Unani doctors get between $12,000 and $15,000 per month in the government sector, but their pay begins at $15,000 monthly in the private industry.

BUMS at Pakistan

It is an Oriental medical practice that was first introduced at Pakistan's Hamdard University and afterwards at Islamia University. The sole criterion for admission to the five-year bachelor's program/course is FSC (Pre-Medical).

According to UAH Act 2002-1965, NCT-licensed graduates may treat Eastern medicine as licensed Unani physicians after completing their degree program and a one-year internship. All higher learning institutions registered with the Higher Education Commission will accept able graduates. Additionally, they are eligible to participate in the Eastern Medicine program to earn an M.Phil. including Ph.D. degrees earned domestically and internationally.

2020 saw the National Council for Tibb (NCT) announce that graduates of the BUMS program would be considered "Doctors of Natural Medicines." Salary ranges for BUMS graduates in India depend on factors like education, amount of experience, region, and industry. Who can do BUMS?

The following criteria must be met in order for an applicant to be eligible to enroll in the BUMS program:
