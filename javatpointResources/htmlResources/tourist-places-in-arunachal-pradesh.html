Tourist Places in Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh has beautiful mountains, unexplored routes, serene lakes, and well-known monasteries. Arunachal Pradesh is a fantastic location for a wonderful vacation. It is the best place to learn more about tribal culture and experience its natural simplicity. The variety of plants and animals, high-altitude meadows, and subtropical forests enhance the beauty of the state. Famous tourist places in Arunachal Pradesh are described below.

1. Tawang

It is renowned for its several significant and stunning monasteries. Tawang is a lovely town that is also pronounced Dawang. Its beautiful scenery makes your journey beautiful. Tawang Monastery is the most well-known and most revered among Buddhists.

Famous tourist spots in Tawang

i. Sela Pass:?

One of the most stunning spots in the Himalayan vista is Sela Pass. For the residents of Arunachal Pradesh, Sela Pass is a lifeline because it is the only route that links the Tawang to the rest of the nation. Sela Pass is a gorgeous location that is always covered with snow and offers some stunning views.

ii. Tawang Monastery:

It is situated within lovely surroundings. It is acknowledged as the second-largest monastery in the entire world. It provides a wonderful view of the Tawang-Chu valley as it is nestled among the Himalayan peaks.

iii. Madhuri Lake:

There are many lovely lakes in Arunachal Pradesh, but none is as stunning and captivating as Madhuri Lake. This lake is also referred to as Sangetsar Lake. This lake has become popular due to its appearance in the Bollywood film Koyla.

iv. Nuranang Falls:

A pristine and lovely experience is provided by Nuranang Falls. The Nuranang Falls is a stunning white waterfall that cascades from a height of almost 100 meters.

2. Ziro

Ziro is a haven for those who want peace. The small town Ziro is located in Arunachal Pradesh. It is home to the Apa Tani people and renowned for its pine hills and rice farms. Ziro has pleasant weather all year, which makes it pleasant to travel during the year.

Famous tourist spots in Ziro

i. Kile Pakho:

It serves as a viewpoint as it provides unique sight to viewers from both sides. On one side of Kile Pakho, there is the Ziro plateau, and on the other, the snow-covered Himalayas.

i. Meghna Cave Temple:

Lord Shiva is the primary deity of this shrine, and it is situated near Ziro. It is a 5000-year-old temple.

iii. Dolo Mando:

It is one of Ziro's well-known attractions. Visitors climb to the top of the hill to enjoy a beautiful view of Hapoli city and old Ziro.

3. Namdapha National Park

It is the country's third-largest national park in terms of area. This park is a treasure for those people who love wildlife. The park is famous for its vibrant bio-diversities; the park is the house of endangered animal and plant species in the country. Visitors can see animals like the Clouded Leopard, Snow Leopard, Tiger, and Leopard in this area. This national park offers a stunning view of the adjacent peaks. It is an ideal place for relaxing as well as for some walking and trekking activities. Travelers can enjoy exciting elephant safaris while exploring the park's lush natural greenery.

Close to streams, travelers can go camping, where they can also enjoy boating. In this park, there is a Miao Museum where visitors can see actual forest creature species and learn about village life.

Tips for visitors:

  • Bring plenty of wool clothing with you when you come here and also wear trekking shoes.
  • In order to ensure a comfortable stay at Namdapha National Park, reservations for lodging must be made in advance.

4. Roing

Roing is one of the most renowned tourist destinations because of its deep gorges, furious rivers, numerous waterfalls, peaceful lakes, snow-capped mountains, and archaeological sites. It is a natural paradise with lakes and mountains.

Famous tourist spots in Roing

i. Mehao Wildlife Sanctuary:

It is referred to be the "real ocean of green heaven on earth." This sanctuary is home to an unnervingly large variety of species of animals, plants, birds, fish, butterflies, and reptiles.

ii. Mayudia:

It is a unique highland resort close to the Roing. Mayudia is surrounded by beautiful greenery and stunning Himalayan scenery. Large amounts of snow occur from December to March, which gives the area an additional allure. Mayudia is a great destination for honeymooners and those who enjoy hiking.

iii. Nehru van Udayan:

It is situated on the banks of the Deopani river. There is a fascinating and amazing cactus house at the Nehru Van Udyan, a beautiful garden and orchid area. Visitors can see the Deopani river very well from the Ejeez tower, which is near the garden.

iv. Hunli:

This is a little, picturesque town that can be reached from Roing to Anini in two hours. Hunli is renowned for its trekking.

v. Bhashmaknagar Fort:

One of the most significant and ancient archaeological sites in Arunachal Pradesh is this fort. Various artifacts, including ceramics, terracotta figurines, plaques, and decorative tiles discovered throughout its excavation procedure, give insight into the advanced state of civilization at that period.

5. Itanagar

It is a natural paradise. It is situated between two hills. Itanagar is well known for its festivals, diverse wildlife and plants, educational sites, and historical monuments. Itanagar is undoubtedly one of the top destinations to visit in Andhra Pradesh due to its attractive scenery and number of sites to explore.

Famous tourist spots in Itanagar

i. Ita Fort:

It is one of the ancient and enduring wonders of prehistoric religion. It is situated in the Papum Pare. Ita Fort literally translates to "the fort of bricks" because in the Ahom language, "Ita" stands for brick.

ii. Rupa:

It is located about 20 kilometers away from Itanagar. Rupa is a charming hill station. Rupa is situated beside the Tenga river and offers visitors the most stunning sights. This location is a fantastic example of the splendor of the area because of its lush greenery, blue skies, and fresh mountain air. The primary festivals in Rupa are conducted in the village of Shertukpen, where a magnificent celebration of color is held. Local festivals such as Lossar, Khik-Saba, and Chhe-Kor are celebrated with great enthusiasm and joy. Another significant festival, known as Khiksaba, is celebrated during winter. At this festival, people wore traditional attire and performed ritualistic dances.

iii. Gompa Buddhist Temple:

This is Itanagar's most popular tourist destination. The temple's distinctive feature is its vibrant yellow roof, which makes it easy to spot from a distance. The sculpture of Lord Buddha is the most significant feature for tourists and devotees.

6. Bomdila

Bomdila is a small town that offers a glimpse of the local customs and culture. Visitors enjoy the town's beautiful natural surroundings, including the apple orchards, valley, snow-covered hills, and stunning environment. Temples of both Buddhism and Hinduism are found here. Bomdila offers the visitors opportunity to experience the wildlife, culture, and tradition, as well as the wonders of nature, all in one location. It is undoubtedly among the top tourist destinations in Arunachal Pradesh.

The places to visit in Bomdia are Bomdila Monastery, Tipi Orchid Reserve, Upper Gompa, Apple Orchards, Eaglenest Wildlife Sanctuary, and Bomdila View Point.

7. Bhalukpong

Bhalukpong is located along the Kameng river's banks. Bhalukpong offers visitors the opportunity to experience wildlife, and a variety of activities complement the surrounding environment. The town has become more attractive to the Kameng river, which flows through the nearby forest. Visitors can enjoy camping, fishing, hiking, and other outdoor activities in Bhalukpong.

8. Anini

Clouds come down and touch the ground at Anini. Anini is the perfect place to go for a fantastic vacation because of the nice climate and the stunning splendor of pristine nature. It is breathtaking to see Anini's lush landscape covered in white fog; everyone must see this scene at least once in a lifetime. Trekking is the greatest way to experience the magnificence of the local wildlife and the beauty of nature. For the Arunachal Helicopter Service, Anini is the main stop.

9. Tirap

The Tirap is situated in the southeast of Arunachal Pradesh. Tirap never fails to amaze its visitors with flowing rivulets, green and colorful orchids, and the pure air that calms the restless mind. Tirap is a hidden treasure for nature lovers and adventurers who enjoy hiking, camping, and other outdoor activities in the midst of the forest.

Famous tourist spots in Tirap

i. District museum:

This museum has some magnificent items that showcase the diverse traditions of the Arunachal Pradeshi people.

ii. Khonsa Museum:

This museum is home to some magnificent artifacts that illustrate the diverse cultures of the people of Arunachal Pradesh. Visitors come here in large numbers to see exhibitions of various bamboo and cane crafts and vibrant handlooms. Tribal artifacts are very well-known in this museum. This museum gives visitors a glimpse into the lifestyle and culture of the Khonsa people. This place also displays swords and weapons.

iii. Khonsa waterfall:

This waterfall is situated in a large forest. This waterfall boasts a stunning view, and the stream's cool water is always refreshing.

10. Mechuka

Arunachal Pradesh's Mechuka is well-known for its diverse tribes and surrounding natural beauty. Mechuka is a well-known tourist site because of its historical and religious significance. It is one of the North East's best scenic locations. Mechuka is also known as Menchukha. Men-chu-kha translates as "medicinal water of snow," where "men" stands for medicine, "chu" for water, and "kha" for snow, while "kha" can also mean "mouth."
