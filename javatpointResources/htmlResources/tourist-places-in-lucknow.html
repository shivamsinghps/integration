25 Best Tourist Places in Lucknow

Brief about Lucknow

Lucknow is the capital of Uttar Pradesh and a big metropolis on the banks of the Gomti River. Lucknow is called the "City of Nawabs" and is well-known for its literature, culture, and architecture. With its appealing tourist spots, Lucknow is a city that puts a special smile on visitors' faces. From its rich colonial past to its museums, the city captures both the modern city's majesty and simplicity.

The old Lucknow city is famous for its noisy, colorful streets of kebabs, biryani shops, Lakhnavi Chickenkari Bazaar, marketplaces, and wholesale jewelry stores. At the same time, the new Lucknow welcomes people from all walks of life and has broad streets, retail malls, parks, and everything planned out.

Places to visit in Lucknow

Lucknow is also renowned for its Nawabi culture, royal food, and as a tourist attraction, with lakhs of tourists coming to the city each year to admire its beauty. So, let's talk about some of the most famous tourist places in Lucknow.

1. Marine Drive in Lucknow

Marine Drive in Lucknow, located on the banks of the Gomti River, is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Lucknow. You've probably heard of Mumbai's Marine Drive, but when you visit Lucknow, you must witness the magnificence of Marine Drive in the twilight moonlight.

Marine Drive, located between Ambedkar Park and the Gomti River, is a popular tourist destination in Lucknow. If you visit Lucknow, be sure to stop there.

2. Science City in Lucknow

It is located in Lucknow's Aliganj area and contains a museum and information about the universe. Various forms of technology from within and outside the country are tastefully shown within the Science City.

If you are interested in science or are a science student, you should visit this Science city at least once.

3. Janeswar Mishra Park in Lucknow

This park, located in Gomti Nagar, Lucknow, is sprawled across 375 acres and was built by the Samajwadi Party. The tourists that come here are enchanted by the gorgeous artificial lake created on 40 acres at the other end of the park.

It is also the largest park in the Asian continent, with construction beginning on August 6, 2012, and ending on August 5, 2014. London's Hight Park inspired this lovely park in Lucknow. You may take a boat trip to this gorgeous lake after arriving here, and the Indian Army's fighter aircraft Deer 21 is on display for tourists to observe.

4. Ambedkar park in Lucknow

This Park in Lucknow's Gomti Nagar was built with pink stones that seem particularly lovely in the glittering light at night.

Every year on Ambedkar Jayanti, Baba Bhim Rao Ambedkar's birth anniversary is commemorated at this beautiful architectural park with a great ceremony. Mayawati, the former Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh, built it in 2008. Inside the park, hundreds of gorgeous elephant statues appear fairly alive to view; this is a must-see if you consider a trip to Lucknow.

5. Bada Imambara in Lucknow

This wonderful structure was created in 1754 by Asaf-ud-Daula to provide work to laborers, and it took 14 years to complete.

This Imambara hall is the biggest hall on the Asian continent and also has:

  • Step Well
  • Garden
  • Maze
  • Picture Gallery

If you're planning a trip to Lucknow, put this historical landmark on your list of attractions to see. A thorough tour might take at least 2 hours. Imambada has a Rs 50 entrance fee.

6. Bhool Bhulaiya in Lucknow

The maze inside Lucknow's Bada Imambara draws many tourists. There are hundreds of entrances to enter the maze, but there is only one route out, which is why it is called a maze.

So, once you visit Imambara in Lucknow, be sure to hire a guide so that you may fully comprehend your excursion here.

7. Chandrika Devi Temple in Lucknow

One of Lucknow's most well-known temples. This temple is devoted to Goddess Durga and is the most well-known Hindu temple in Lucknow. It is thought that the statue in its proud home is a hybrid of Kali, Lakshmi, and Durga.

Inside the temple premises, there is a beautiful lake where Lord Shiva is enthroned in the shape of a big statue. There are often a large number of people visiting this shrine. If you plan a vacation to Lucknow, you should stop by this famous shrine at least once.

8. Gomti river boating in Lucknow

If we are touring the city of Lucknow and miss taking a boat ride on the holy river Gomti, we are missing out. If you are going to Lucknow, you must take advantage of the wonderful experience of boating here.

On the river Gomti, a victim memorial was created to commemorate the freedom fighters on August 15, 1957.

9. Anandi Waterpark in Lucknow

During your visit to Lucknow, you might consider going here if you love to play with the splash of water and see the historical heritage and temples.

  • Anandi Waterpark offers a variety of rides, with the major attractions being fun rides and sea waves.
  • When you go to the waterpark to purchase a ticket, you will be given a swimming outfit, breakfast, and other amenities for swimming.
  • It is located on Faizabad Road, near Lucknow's Indra Canal, one of Uttar Pradesh's major waterparks.
  • This Lucknow waterpark is highly attractive, and as a result, visitors frequently come here to enjoy it.

10. Rumi Darwaza in Lucknow

Asaf-Ud-Daula built this Rumi Darwaza in Lucknow in 1784. Asaf-Ud-Daula's Rumi Darwaza is an example of Awadhi architecture.

The unique feature of this door is that it was built without wood or iron, allowing the architectural design of this massive entrance to shine through.

11. Kathouta Lake in Lucknow

This lake, located in the Indra Nagar area of Lucknow City, is quite large and beautiful. The water from this clean and lovely lake is utilized for drinking in many parts of the city.

Lucknow's lake is gradually transforming into a tourist destination. So, if you like, you may view it whenever you have spare time.

12. Residency in Lucknow

If you travel to Lucknow and didn't see the Residency here, know that our trip to Lucknow is incomplete

The first liberation struggle in 1857, the British took over this old structure and began residing here; it has been known as Residency. At this remarkable location in Lucknow, India's freedom warriors battled bravely for their independence. And the epic narrative of courage continues to echo throughout this tenure.

13. Zoo in Lucknow

This zoo, located in Banarasi Bagh, about 4 kilometers from Char Bagh Railway Station, has a variety of animals and birds.

Tourists may ride a paddleboat on the lake in the zoo, and if you want to ride a train or an elephant, come to this zoo in Lucknow. Many extinct bird species can be found here.

14. Musical Fountain in Lucknow

The musical fountains at the PictureGallery pond- Shahi Talab, are functional again.

You may visit the musical fountain if you like. As the evening approaches, the multicolored fountains swaying to the melodies of varied music seem quite calming, adding to the city's splendor. This is the greatest in the evening after touring Lucknow throughout the day and viewing the locations to see in Lucknow.

15. Hussainabad Clock Tower

Husainabad Clock Tower, near the Rumi Darwaza, is another cultural monument dotting Lucknow's landscape. The Hussainabad Trust built this 221-foot tower in 1881 at the cost of 1.75 lakhs, and it is styled after London's Big Ben Clock Tower. It is known as the country's highest clock tower and has Gothic and Victorian architectural elements. The clock is shaped like a flower, with 12 petals and a 14-foot-long pendulum.

16. Saadat Ali Khan Maqbara in Lucknow

The monument of Saadat Ali Khan is particularly attractive from an architectural standpoint, and it is located in Kaiserbagh, Lucknow, about 4 kilometers from Charbagh. This mausoleum, made of black and white marble stones, resembles a vase and is rather lovely. In this mausoleum, there are two tombs: one for Nawab Saadat Ali Khan and another for his Begum Khurshid Jadi.

17. La Martiniere Main Building in Lucknow

The English Major General Clyde Martin created this structure for his house in the 18th century, and it is a rare example of European design. Paintings and chandeliers were two of Martin's favorite things. He lavishly embellished the castle with chandeliers and paintings. On the premise of the palace's lawns, there is also a lake.

Clyde Martin was buried in this castle upon his death in 1800. Martin stipulated in his will that 'La Martiniere School' be created in this mansion after my death. As a result, the La Martiniere School was founded in this mansion in 1840. Among India's renowned English schools, this institution retains a distinct place.

18. Phoenix Palassio in Lucknow

Phoenix Palacio is one of India's largest shopping malls. This mall is around 15 kilometers from Charbagh Railway Station, near the Atal Bihari Bajpai Cricket Stadium.

There will also be the Inox Megaplex in this Mall. This is the city's first IMAX theatre. This mall is particularly popular among Lucknow's youth for hangouts, shopping, and entertainment.

19. Dilkusha Kothi in Lucknow

Dilkusha Kothi, located on the banks of the Gomti River, has a distinct place in all of Lucknow's tourist attractions. It's the ruins of a home from the early nineteenth century. The Nawabs used to spend their summers in this ruin. The outside walls and magnificent gardens are all that is left of this tower today. Due to its historical significance and architectural splendor, Dilkusha Kothi is one of the essential destinations to visit in Lucknow.

20. Hazratganj Market

Hazratganj is a lively market in Lucknow located in the city's center. Hazratganj is well-known among visitors visiting Lucknow for its shopping. With several showrooms, shops, retail complexes, malls, and theatres, the area is shopping heaven. Aside from that, visitors may enjoy a wide range of delectable foods. Hazratganj Market is one of Lucknow's major shopping and sightseeing destinations.

21. Jama Masjid in Lucknow

Jama Masjid is one of Lucknow's oldest historical landmarks. It is not just a popular tourist site but also an important religious center for the Muslims in Lucknow. Nawab Mohammad Ali Shah constructed this mosque in 1839. Everyone is mesmerized by the majesty of its artistry, made of golden sandstone. The mosque's rich history typically entices past buffs. This mosque is a living monument to the splendor and magnificence of a bygone period.

22. Satkhanda in Lucknow

Satkhand is a nice spot to visit in Lucknow because it is one of the key historical sites. Muhammad Ali Shah initiated the building of Satkhand, a four-story incomplete tower dating back to the 1800s. However, the construction of the building was incomplete because of the king's death, and only four floors could be completed. It is an incomplete yet beautiful piece of architecture that continues to draw tourists and is one of the most well-known tourist attractions in Lucknow.

23. Amrapali Water Park in Lucknow

Amrapali Water Park is a key part of Lucknow's tourism. Compared to other parks in Lucknow, this park has many attractions for people of all ages, and it draws most tourists. Amrapali Water Park is a great destination to visit in Lucknow if you're searching for some excitement.

24. Chota Imambara

'Imaambara of Hussainabad' is another name for Chota Imambara. Muhammad Alishah, the third Nawab of Awadh, constructed this magnificent structure in 1840. From the standpoint of interior and exterior design, this structure is stunning. The tombs of Muhammad Ali Shah and his father are here. This structure is notable for having a royal hammam, a one-of-a-kind feature. It gets its water from the Gomti River. When the water reaches the two cisterns, it gets hot in one and cold in the other. Tourists are drawn to this building because of the chandeliers.

25. Samta Mulak Chowk

Even in the late hours of the evening, Samta Mulak Chowk, the heart of Lucknow's ice cream vendors, causes a flurry. Though most people come here to eat ice cream with their families, many others become a part of the late-night economy.

Local Food in Lucknow

Lucknow's local dish is well-known in India and around the world. Lucknow is known for its spicy food. If you visit Lucknow, make sure to taste some of the native cuisines. Rahim's Kulcha Nahri, Raja Thandai, Makhan Malai Chowk, Chappan Bhog ki Mithai, Nilkanth Chat, Ratti Red crispy, mutton seekh kabab, and other Lucknow specialties are all delicious.

Best Time to Visit Lucknow

Although Lucknow may be visited at any season, the months of September to March are outstanding. During the summer, it is quite hot, with temperatures reaching 45 degrees Celsius and beyond in May and June. Winter begins in October and lasts until March, with temperatures ranging from 5 to 25 degrees. During the monsoon, it rains often, and the temperature stays between 25 and 35 degrees.
