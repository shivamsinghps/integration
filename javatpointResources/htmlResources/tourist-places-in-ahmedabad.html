25 Best Tourist Places in Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad is Gujarat's fastest-growing city. The city is situated on the banks of the Sabarmati River. Ahmedabad, the historic capital of Gujarat, is the state's biggest city.

It is well-known for its ancient sites as well as its modernity. Its delicious food, vibrant culture, and warm hospitality draw many visitors throughout the year. Several locations are designed, from large malls to parks, where you may learn about important personalities in Indian history. Here is the list of Ahmedabad's most popular tourist places.

1. Sabarmati Ashram

Our Father of the Nation, Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, and his wife Kasturba Gandhi lived in the Sabarmati Ashram in Ahmedabad. Sabarmati Ashram, located on the Sabarmati River, is also known as 'Gandhi Ashram' or 'Mahatma Gandhi Ashram.' It is historically significant since it is where the Salt Satyagraha march started. In the Mahatma Gandhi Ashram, there are other additional establishments. The Gandhi Memorial Organization museum is the most well-known, including some of Gandhi's letters and images on show. The museum was originally housed at Hriday Kunj, Gandhi's ashram hut, but was moved here in 1963 when the museum was established. The Gandhi Memorial Museum is now housed at the ashram. Nandini, Vinoba Kutir, and Upasana Mandir are some of the other buildings and places inside the Gandhi Ashram. These structures have the names of Gandhi's close associates.

2. Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel National Museum

The Sardar Vallabhai Patel National Museum, located in Shahibagh, Ahmedabad, is a memorial to India's first Deputy Prime Minister, Sardar Vallabhai Patel. This museum, surrounded by a lovely park, also includes various museums and images related to the Indian National Movement.

3. Sidi Saiyyed Mosque

The Sidi Saiyyed Mosque, also known as the Sidi Saiyyed Ni Jali Mosque, was built in 1573 and is one of Ahmedabad's most famous and magnificent mosques. The structure is one of the final mosques built by the Gujarat Sultanate and finished in the year before the Mughals came and destroyed Gujarat. Sidi Saiyyid, a former soldier of Bilal Jhajjar Khan, a general in the army of Shams-ud-din Muzaffar Shah III, the final Sultan of the Sultanate of Gujarat, is credited with the construction of this beautiful mosque. The mosque is especially well-known for its 10 stone lattice windows, commonly known as the side and back arches of the jaalis. This location is unquestionably ideal for photographers and history fans.

4. Historical Place Lothal

If you are a history fan who wants to gain an insight into the life of previous generations, you must go to Lothal since it is a destination that perfectly blends the past and the present. Lothal, a famous Indus Valley site and a 4500-year-old city discovered in 1954, is located 85 kilometres from Ahmedabad on the banks of the legendary ancient river Saraswati (which has now dried up). Lothal was not only one of the first successful civilizations, but it was also a hub of industrialization bordering several nations. For exciting excavations and findings, geologists, historians, and archaeologists come to Lothal.

5. Kankaria Lake

Kankaria Lake, formerly known as Hauz-e-Qutub, is the second-largest lake in Ahmedabad. Kankaria Lake was constructed in the 15th century and renovated in 2008. Kankaria Lake is a magical site where entertainment, culture, wisdom, and skill come together perfectly. From balloon safaris and zoos to toy trains and amusement parks, Kankaria Lake has it all. It's ideal for a late-night walk. Kankaria Lake provides activities like archery, gymnastics, and water rides to adventure lovers. Kankaria Carnival, the most frightening celebration, is celebrated with zeal and devotion. It also provides you with an excellent opportunity to demonstrate your abilities in numerous contests.

6. Swaminarayan Akshardham

Shri Swaminarayan Mandir is a well-known temple dedicated to Narayan Dev in Ahmedabad's Kalupur neighbourhood. On the orders of Swaminarayan, the founder of the Swaminarayan branch of Hinduism, this majestic temple was erected in 1822. This temple is a superb example of nineteenth-century architecture, ornate with kaleidoscopic hues and detailed decorations. The goddesses are beautifully decorated and dressed in gorgeous clothing inside the sanctum sanctorum. On the day following Diwali, one lakh people attend the temple.

Swami Narayan Temple is a one-of-a-kind spiritual experience that reflects numerous colors of spirituality. For the convenience of visitors, the temple complex contains a multi-story guesthouse and a medical centre on its grounds. This temple also serves as the headquarters of Nar Narayan Gadi, making it a highly revered institution among worshippers. The morning aarti, or prayer ceremony, is also well-known at the temple.

7. Calico Textile Museum

The Calico Textile Museum, one of the world's top textile museums and a recognized institution in Indian textiles, is known for its garment collection. While popular pieces contain sequins, mirrors, jewels, silk threads, and other decorations, you may also discover collections of 17th-century attire. The museum is a fascinating tourist attraction housed in Gujarat's gorgeous wooden Havelis and has lush gardens and fountains.

8. Jama Masjid

The Jama Masjid in Ahmedabad is one of India's greatest mosques, established in 1424. Emperor Sultan Ahmed Shah, the creator of Ahmedabad, created this magnificent structure as part of the old walled city. In addition to being a well-known shrine, the Jama Masjid houses the tombs of Ahmad Shah I, his son and grandson, and the king's queens. A wonderful blend of Hindu and Muslim architectural traditions may be seen here. It took 13 years to build and was meant to be a private temple for the Sultans. It's worth noting that the mosque was constructed completely of stones.

9. Hathi Singh Jain Temple

The Hathi Singh Jain Temple, dedicated to Lord Dharmanath, the 15th Jain Tirthankara, was established in 1848. There are 52 temples in total here. The architecture of these temples is impressive, and the entire area is known as Hathi Singh Wadi. In Hathi Singh Wadi, there is also a 78-foot-tall Mahavir Stambha. This pillar's quality and elaborate carvings are amazing. Hathi Singh Jain Temple is one of Ahmedabad's most popular tourist attractions. If you happen to be in Ahmedabad, you should visit the Hathi Singh Jain Temple.

10. Sabarmati Riverfront

Riverfront is a lovely public park created along the Sabarmati River's banks. Sabarmati River Front Park is the name of this park. Once a year, the Sabarmati Riverfront has a flower show that is well worth viewing. In addition, a variety of events are hosted here. Inside the Sabarmati Riverfront, there are numerous attractive locations to explore that are the focus of attention for travellers visiting the Sabarmati Riverfront. The Sabarmati Riverfront in Ahmedabad is a renowned tourist site. If you ever go to Ahmedabad to visit the places. So take a trip to the Sabarmati Riverfront.

11. Sarkhej Roza in Ahmedabad

Sarkhej Roja, an amazing complex of tombs and pavilions, is just a short distance away from the huge complex. The compound is based on an artificial lake created for Gujarat's rulers. The graves of Sheikh Ahmed Khattu, Ahmed Shah's spiritual counsellor, are among the most important tombs here. Here you'll find beautiful carvings and brass latticework. This is one of the best locations to see in Ahmedabad since it is steeped in history and beautifully embellished.

12. Shah-e-Alam's Roza

Ahmedabad has many tombs that surprise you with their elaborate designs and beauty. The Roza of Shah-e-Alam, also known as Rasulabad Dargah or Shah Alam no Rojo, is one such mausoleum that should be on every tourist's list. This mausoleum is a popular Sufi temple that attracts people from all over the world.

The tomb's dome was formerly adorned with gold and precious stones, adding to its splendour. The tomb complex comprises a small tomb with twenty-four domes that hold Shah Alam's family's tombs.

13. Vastrapur Lake

Vastrapur Lake is a historic lake in western Ahmedabad that was repaired and enhanced in 2002. It contains an open-air stage where many of the city's cultural events are held. A large statue of Narsingh Mehta (a prominent Gujarati poet-saint) was built here, and the lake was renamed Bhakta Kavi Narasimha Mehta Sarovar in his honour.

When the Vastrapur lake dried up in 2016, local officials ensured that the fish and other water creatures were relocated to a secure location. However, many fish had died by that time. In 2019, the AMC (Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation) made it feasible for the Narmada River to flow into the lake, which was previously impossible.

14. Kite Museum

Bhanubhai Shah created the Kite Museum on the campus of Sanskar Kendra in Paldi, Ahmedabad. The museum, which opened in 1954 and was designed by renowned architect Le Corbusier, features an intricate exhibition of distinctively carved kites such as mirror-work kites, block print kites, Japanese kites, etc.

15. Manek Chowk

In the Gujarati city of Ahmedabad, Manek Chowk is a lively city plaza. It is located in Old Ahmedabad and is surrounded by historic structures. This is the place to go if you have a large shopping appetite because Manek Chowk serves three purposes throughout the day. It's a vegetable market in the morning. It's a bullion market in the day and a foodie's dream in the evening. It's the second-largest and busiest market in India. If you spend a full day here, you'll see how the afternoon changes the vegetable market, and evening transforms the bullion market into a foodie's heaven.

16. Law Garden Night Market

The Law Garden Night Market in Maharaja Society's Law Garden is well-known in Ahmedabad. Traditional clothes, wall hangings, bandhej sarees, long colourful skirts (chaniyas), antique jewellery, traditional footwear, and home decoration goods are among the greatest purchases here, kept in many unique Kutch and Saurashtra stores.

Food trucks fill the streets of the night market, selling great local specialties and street food. It's best to go between 8 and 9 p.m. since it's handy to go to Happy Street afterwards. The Law Garden night market is especially beautiful during Navratri.

17. Vaishnodevi Temple

The Vaishnodevi Temple is a stone-built Hindu temple on the Sarkhej Gandhinagar Highway, also known as the Vaishnodevi Circle. The temple is built into an artificial rock with a deep carve of roughly 50 feet in diameter. The temple can accommodate roughly 5000 worshipers at a time, according to a 1350-square-foot hall.

The Vaishnodevi Temple in Ahmedabad is a scaled-down replica of the original in Katra, Jammu and Kashmir. It is devoted to Vaishnodevi, a form of Maa Durga, the Hindu feminine deity. The heads of three more Hindu goddess statues are within the temple, Lakshmi, Kali, Saraswati, and a Shaiva shrine.

18. Alpha One Mall

The Alpha One Mall, popularly known as Ahmedabad One Mall, is located in the centre of Ahmedabad. Alpha One Mall, which covers 706,000 square feet in Ahmedabad's bustling Vastrapur neighbourhood, is the city's largest mixed-use city centre.

The mall's three levels are peppered with various national, international, and local companies. There are many stores, ranging from single-location shops to franchise operations. Alpha One Mall is a fantastic spot to spend a full Sunday with family, a Friday afternoon with friends, or simply a window-shopping stroll anytime you choose, with entertainment options such as a movieplex and a good food court to enjoy after the show.

19. ISKCON Temple

The ISKCON Temple in Ahmedabad, close to the Gujarat Samachar Press, is the best place to experience spirituality and mental delight. This temple, also known as the 'Hare Krishna Temple,' is ideal for meditation because of its serene ambience.

Hare Rama Hare Krishna chantings may continuously be heard within the Hare Krishna Mandir. Devotees, especially those with a spiritual mind, benefit from many devotional lectures and talks. Followers hold seminars at institutions, corporations, and other settings to promote spiritualization practices. The persistent chanting of the holy names is a unique characteristic at the centre.

20. Gujarat Science City

Gujarat Science City is a unique project taken up by the Gujarat government to foster general knowledge and scientific temperament, especially among the country's young generation. The facility, which covers 107 acres, also hosts regular lecture presentations and seminars for students and scientific lovers. It is located on Science City Road in Ahmedabad.

21. Nehru Bridge in Ahmedabad

The Nehru Bridge in Ahmedabad connects the Real Old City (Kot Vistar) with West Ahmedabad, spanning the Sabarmati River. The bridge was dedicated to India's first Prime Minister, Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru, and opened to the public in 1962. The bridge is recognized as one of the most attractive sites in the city.

22. Vechaar Utensils Museum

The VECHAAR (Vishalla Environmental Centre for Heritage of Art, Architecture, and Research) Utensils Museum, constructed in 1981 near the Vishalla Village Restaurant in Ahmedabad, was created by Mr Surender C, a well-known architect. The museum houses an extensive collection of kitchenware that dates back to hundreds of years.

23. Sanskar Kendra

Sanskar Kendra is a city museum in Paldi, Ahmedabad, founded in 1956. The museum is a collection of displays that showcase the city of Ahmedabad's art, culture, history, and architecture. It also claims to have the world's longest incense stick. Another museum area is dedicated to the Patang Kite Museum, a one-of-a-kind kite museum.

24. Dhanvantari Garden

Dhanvantari Garden, located in Dungarshinagar, Paldi, is another lovely garden in Ahmedabad. There is more than enough green in the garden and several benches where you may rest. Aside from the vast running and walking paths, the park is frequented by local children who come to participate in outdoor games and activities in the evenings.

25. World Vintage Car Museum

The Auto World Historic Car Museum, located in the Dastan of Kathwad, displays about 120 historic cars, including old utility vehicles, motor vehicles, and ancient carriages. The museum also has a cafe and a museum store and abundant parking and restrooms. It is organized and maintained by India's Vintage & Classic Car Club.

Shri Pranlal Bhogilal Patel established the Auto World Vintage Car Museum by transforming his collection into a renowned museum. Mr. Nitin Dosin, the Director, now administers it. The museum also has a collection of royal carriages and cars from 1900 to the 1970s.

How to reach Ahmedabad

  1. By Flight: If you are flying to Ahmedabad, the airport is Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport, connected to major cities in India.
  2. By Train: If you are travelling by train to Ahmedabad, you should know that the Ahmedabad railway station is connected to major cities around India.
  3. By Road: If you are travelling to Ahmedabad by road, you should know that NH 8 connects Ahmedabad to places like Bhavnagar, Surat, Mumbai, and Pune. You may also take the bus from any of these cities.

Famous Street Food in Ahmedabad

Most people are unaware that Ahmedabad's heart is in its street food. You may get a great selection of street food to satisfy your sense of taste. Ahmedabad's food selections, particularly it is street food, have their distinct flavour. There are numerous vegetarian options, but the non-vegetarian options are very appealing. Khakhra, Dhokla, Kulfi, Pani Puri, Dal Vada, and Maska Bun are some of the top street food options in Ahmedabad.

These are some of the best street foods in Ahmedabad that you should not miss. Before leaving Ahmedabad, make sure you've tried all of these delights' popular and well-known tastes. These food products are reasonably priced, so you can simply purchase them.

Best Time to Visit Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad is not ideal for summer vacations. During these days, the temperature here remains between 43 and 47 ?. Tourists going here benefit greatly from the monsoon season. However, the Sabarmati River frequently floods owing to heavy rains. The winter season enjoys comfortable temperatures ranging from 5 to 20 ° C. Winter is the greatest time to visit Ahmedabad due to the weather conditions.
