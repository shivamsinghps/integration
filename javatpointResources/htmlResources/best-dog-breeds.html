Best Dog Breeds

Adopting a dog means one would have a great adventure before them, and they are going to have a new loyal best friend; and apart from this, becoming the dog owner for the first time is a truly rewarding experience in itself for the owners and the whole family. But along with all these good points mentioned above, one should also understand and realize the responsibilities that come while adopting a dog. It is truly said that from a truly rewarding experience, adopting a dog can become a miserable experience, and the owners would be remiss if they didn't acknowledge that this process of adopting a dog comes with some little curveballs. An owner or the family who is adopting a dog will become more familiar with all the difficulties that they have to face while adopting a dog as they start learning the ropes of pup parenthood. Most people usually consider the size of the dog before buying or adopting a dog, but they should focus more on their personality. Be it a giant breed, medium breed, or toy breed, the personality of the dog should be given more importance rather than the size of the dog. The personality of the dog decides how smoothly a relationship will be maintained between the owner and the dog. Let's have a few examples to understand this more:

  1. If one is looking for dogs for their kids, they should look for the dog breeds that are proven to be the best companion for younger ones
  2. If one is looking dog for seniors or their parents, they should look for the dog breeds that are the best companion for elders
  3. If one doesn't like much excitement in life or for introverted personalities, calm dog breeds proved to be the best companion for them

Marissa Sunny, who is a cannie behaviour specialist, advised all the dog adopters that before adopting a dog, they all should ask themselves how their life would look like after having the dog as their companion or what they envision their life with a dog pet companion. She further explained how the breeds of the dog should affect the decision. She explained that one should go with a high-energy working breed if they want a dog companion to go running with and get out of the house. Opposite this, an adult senior-breed dog can work for someone who is looking for a Netflix buddy. And other than this, one thing she mentioned is that not only the purebreds but many mixed breeds available in the animal shelters are also good companions and options for first-time dog owners.

Here, a complete list of the best dog breed is provided in this article so that anyone who is looking to adopt a dog for the first time can make their decision easily about which breed they should go with. The list of dog breeds given here includes some of the best breeds of dogs that are easily trainable and bondable.

1. Cavalier King Charles spaniel

The Cavalier King Charles spaniel is a high-spirited, athletic, tender, and sophisticated little puppy. They are a charismatic combination of a spaniel and a small toy breed. These cute little puppies are amongst the best choices for owners to adopt due to their smartness and adaptability. These absolutely attractive pups are keen on amusing and entertaining their humans, making them great and sterling for a wide range of owners, including families, couples, individuals, and seniors. Besides, they are also renowned for being effective therapy dogs. These puppies need a little more grooming in comparison with other puppies, like an ear- checks a week, cutting nails once a month, and daily brushing.

Breed review
Life expectancy 12 to 15 years
Height 12 to 13 inches
Weight 13 to 18 pounds

2. Labrador retriever

The Labrador retriever is considered among the most popular dog breeds and is one of the well-behaved dog breeds. They are most notable because of their friendly attitude and unreserved nature. This even-tempered, fun-loving, and companionable dog comes in yellow, chocolate, and black colour. The neighbourly behaviour of these adorable pups not only allows them to connect with family but also with other animals as well. Labrador retrievers are one of the uncomplicated breeds of dogs to teach and train as they love to make their humans happy. To make them look best, occasional brushing and baths are all needed.

Breed review
Life expectancy 10 to 12 years
Height 24 inches
Weight 55 to 80 pounds

3. Papillon

Papillon is a French word that means butterfly. This is another teeny puppy that weighs only 10 pounds. This little pup is a very adoring breed of dog and easily gets along with kids. Even being so small, papillon is amazingly athletic and lively and benefits highly from playtime. They are not much interested in sticking out with other animals. Although, these pups are amazingly not difficult to groom all because of their absence of an undercoat. This dog needs monthly grooming sessions and a good bath periodically.

Breed review
Life expectancy 14 to 16 years
Height 8 to 11 inches
Weight 5 to 10 pounds

4. Golden retrievers

Golden retrievers are the adorable, notably affable pup that is dedicated to their humans. These dogs are very much obedient and can be easily trained; hence teaching them to sit, fetch and stay is likely to be extremely easy, and this is the reason a large number of service dogs are golden retrievers. Their clubbable and approachable personality makes them a great choice for family dogs. Golden retrievers need a shedding spree two times a year and a good brushing each week.

Breed review
Life expectancy 10 to 12 years
Height 24 inches
Weight 55 to 75 pounds

5. Bichon Frise

Bichon Frise is famous for its adorable and playful nature. They are alluring and brilliant lapdogs who be friendly with everyone they meet. As they are great with children and easy to train, they are amongst the best dog to adopt. The fluffy white coat of these pups is non-allergic and prevents them from dog allergies. They need a monthly bath and a good brushing numerous times a week. Every four to six weeks, they need to go to a groomer that helps them to look stunning.

Breed review
Life expectancy 14 to 15 years
Height 9.5 to 11.5 inches
Weight 12 to 18 pounds

6. Pugs

Ask any Pugs owner, and they will always tell you that these cute pups are one of the perfect family companions one can have. Pugs are so adjustable and sociable that they get along with everybody, including seniors, kids, and other animals. Training them is easy as they enjoy making their humans happy. Another benefit is their coat which doesn't require high maintenance and needs brushing once a week to control light shedding.

Breed review
Life expectancy 13 to 15 years
Height 10 to 13 inches
Weight 14 to 18 pounds

7. Yorkshire terrier

The Yorkshire terrier is an adorable and small-sized pup whose weight is only seven pounds. Even so, being tiny, they possess major personalities and is known for being tenacious, brave, and energetic. Besides, they are extraordinarily friendly. The breed's long, hypoallergenic coat imitates human hair more than the fur of a dog, making them one of the famous dogs that can deal with pet allergies. This little pup needs high maintenance to look tip-top such as their long hair needs daily brushing, bathing once a week, and professional grooming routinely.

Breed review
Life expectancy 11 to 15 years
Height 7 to 8 inches
Weight 7 pounds

8. Basenji

One might not be too familiar with this pup, but Basenji is an adjustable, quiet, and smart breed of dog. They are amongst the best choices for owners because of how low-key and easy-going this breed is. Some people also call them "cat-like". As these dogs are not overtly friendly like other breeds, they make a perfect choice for first-time owners who prefer a dog that is not always at their ankles. Basenji is simple to care for because of their short coat and need a once-over every week or two. They don't require bathing unless they get into anything.

Breed review
Life expectancy 13 to 14 years
Height 16 to 17 inches
Weight 22 to 24 pounds

9. Standard poodle

Poodles are remarkably athletic and smart family companions that are known for their fluffy and pillow-like anti-allergenic coats. Due to these positive traits, poodles have been bred with various types of breeds to acquire designer breeds like Golden doodle, Capavoo, Labradoodle, and Cockapoo. Just as puppies, poodles can be highly energetic and can become exhausted and lose their energy. They need daily brushing and professional grooming in a month or two to prevent tangling and keep their coats shining.

Breed review
Life expectancy 12 to 15 years
Height 18 to 24 inches
Weight 45 to 70 pounds

10. German Shepherd

The kingly German shepherd is an outstanding dog breed for numerous reasons. They are extraordinarily smart puppies that are easy to train, and this is one of the reasons they are employed and used in k-9 units. Additionally, these dogs are gentle and always loyal to their humans, which makes them great watchdogs. German shepherds are easy to groom as they need brushing a few times a week to get rid of loose hairs and need bathing occasionally.

Breed review
Life expectancy 7 to 10 years
Height 22 to 26 inches
Weight 50 to 90 pounds

11. Bernese Mountain dog

Bernese mountain dog is a sweet-natured and powerful breed of dog. These dogs can be easily trained, notably gentle with everybody, and get friends with other people and animals. Their big size may frighten someone, but they are big softies who always love to stay nearby their humans. As a matter of fact, these dogs can be a little bit shy. This breed has an issue of shedding repeatedly and need a good brushing more than twice a week.

Breed review
Life expectancy 7 to 10 years
Height 23 to 27.5 inches
Weight 70 to 115 pounds

12. Irish setter

The Irish setter is an adorable pup who bonds and get along easily with everybody they meet, including adults, kiddos, old-aged person, and other animals. If people are in search of a friendly and lovable pup, this might be a better match for them. They have a tendency to be boisterous a little bit, so an active and playful setup is perfect. Irish setter needs brushing twice a week, monthly nail trims, and bathing from time to time. To make them look stellar, moderate grooming is also required.

Breed review
Life expectancy 12 to 15 years
Height 25 to 27 inches
Weight 60 to 70 pounds

13. Great Dane

Great Dane's menacing stature can be intimidating to people, but in reality, this pup is an absolute gentle giant. This patient, enjoyable, super-friendly dog bonds with its humans easily and are unfailingly loyal to them. Although, this gentle dog also makes for a sharp-eyed and brave watchdog. With regards to training, this breed reaps benefits from professional obedience training in an effort to make use of its full potential. These dog needs to be brushed weekly, bathed every once in a while, and have their nails trimmed every month.

Breed review
Life expectancy 7 to 10 years
Height 28 to 30 inches
Weight 110 to 175 pounds

14. Whippet

One might get confused between a whippet and a greyhound as they look alike. Though, the whippet is its own breed and is a little bit smaller. This graceful and lean dog is a fast runner that enjoys having a good chase in the gardens or backyards. Another bonus is that this pup barks barely. Besides, their short coat can be easily cared for and needs to be brushed once a week and bathed occasionally. Whippet is smart and has a bit more of a naughty and troublesome personality that can be a little problematic to control when training.

Breed review
Life expectancy 12 to 15 years
Height 19 to 21 inches
Weight 25 to 40 pounds

15. Mixed breeds

It doesn't matter what mix of breeds; mutts are always amazing. It is believed that mixed breeds have less genetically inherited health issues in comparison with purebred ones. Additionally, if one adopts them from the shelter, these dogs will already be neutered/sterilized, vaccinated, and microchipped, and also, the one adopting them will be helping with the problem of pet overpopulation.
