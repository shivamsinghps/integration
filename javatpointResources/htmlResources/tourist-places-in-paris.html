Tourist Places in Paris

You may have read or heard a lot of things about Paris. You may have visited the location more than once. However, Paris is a city bursting with attractions; from world-class museums to lesser-known hidden jewels, there are many experiences to be had there.

1. Disneyland:

One of the most well-known and greatest locations to visit in Paris is Disneyland, a vivid, colourful, happy, and always busy amusement park. Both children and adults like visiting Disneyland in Paris. One of the most popular tourist destinations in Paris, formerly known as Euro Disney Resort, is recognized for its incredibly entertaining attractions, shows, animated films, rallies, and thrilling opportunities to interact with your favourite characters.

There are a variety of activities on the list, such as going to the princess pavilion, touring the Disney arcade, riding in horse-drawn street cars, learning "the little word," and getting photographs taken with your favourite cartoon characters, like Mickey Mouse or Winnie the Pooh. If tourists want to have a good time with their kids in Paris, tourists should stay here.

  • Don't miss The countless exhilarating rides, such as Buzz Lightyear Laser Blast, Autopia, and Arcade. Beating the Disney parade, pyrotechnics, crazy selfies with your favourite Disney character, Mad Hatter's Tea Cups, Dumbo the Flying Elephant, and Orbitron are all ways to win.
  • 10 a.m. to 11 p.m. in the opening time.
  • Entry fee: A one-day pass is £36 for adults and £30 for children. There are yearly passes and vacation packages in addition to daily tickets.

2. Eiffel Tower:

The Eiffel Tower, the most recognizable landmark in Paris, was built for the 1889 World Exposition and towered an astounding 300 meters above the city. You may take the view from the top of the tower and see Gustave Eiffel's private apartment on exhibit after taking the elevator there.

  • 9 a.m. to 11 p.m. in the opening time, except July 14.
  • For children under the age of 4, there is no entrance cost to the Eiffel Tower.
  • As for the remaining rates:
  • Lift Entrance to Second Floor: For adults, INR 861 (€11) and Children (12-24 years old) receive €8,50.
  • Lift Entrance to Top Floor: €17.00 for adults, €14.50 for kids (12-24) and €8.00 for kids (4-11).
  • Stair Entrance to the Second Floor: €7 for adults, €50 for children (12 to 24 years old), and €3,00 for children (4 to 11 years old).

3. The Louvre:

The sleek glass pyramid at the entrance of the Louvre, the most popular museum in the world, is its defining feature.

As soon as you step inside, you'll be impressed by the museum's huge collection of art, which includes some of the most famous works in the world, like Michelangelo's "Dying Slave" and the "Mona Lisa" by Leonardo da Vinci.

  • Open from 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. every day, except Tuesday.
  • Entry fee: €12.

4. Notre-Dame Cathedral:

Built in 1163, the Notre Dame Cathedral (also known as Notre Dame de Paris) is a Gothic cathedral with mesmerizing architectural details, including rose windows, magnificent statues, and Gothic woodwork.

  • 8 a.m. to 6:45 p.m. is the opening time of Notre Dame Cathedral.
  • The Hunchback of Notre Dame, a masterpiece of literature by Victor Hugo, is also nearby.

5. Arc de Triomphe:

It is one of Paris' most iconic sights in Paris. The Arc de Triomphe (Triumphal Arch), the most famous and recognizable triumphal arch in the world, is situated at the western end of the well-known Champs-Élysées.

Some historical French victories, wars, and notable military leaders are engraved on the surface.

  • Except for January 1, May 1, August 8, July 14, and December 25, the business is open daily from 10 a.m. until 9.45 p.m.

6. Montmartre:

Montmartre is most known for its creative community, a significant hill northeast of Paris. Some well-known painters, such as Monet, Picasso, and Renoir, stayed during the Belle Époque.

  • Don't forget to stop at the Wall of Love in Montmartre, where 311 different languages have written "I love you" on the wall.
  • It is open from 8:30 a.m. to 8 p.m. from May to September, and from October to April, it is open from 9 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.
  • Entry is free. For the audio and video guides, fees are required.

7. Sacré:

One of Paris's most beautiful tourist destinations is the Sacré-Coeur Basilica, also known by its common name. It is located on Montmartre's highland. This basilica, which has Romano-Byzantine characteristics, is of great spiritual importance. The entryway, arches, and walls feature nationalist elements, even if the meditation garden and the fountain are excellent for tranquillity and mental serenity. Inside the basilica, the use of cameras is strictly prohibited.

  • Don't miss the Roman-Byzantine architecture, the biggest mosaic ceiling in France, or the 360-degree panorama of France from the dome's pinnacle.
  • 6 a.m. to 10:30 p.m. is the opening time.
  • $5 entry fee.

8. Palace Of Versailles:

One of the most well-liked world heritage sites and interesting places to visit in Paris is the Palace of Versailles, constructed as a symbol of French Supremacy. This enormous and magnificent building includes beautiful gardens, a unique design, a stunning terrace, and a wonderful collection of Renaissance-era artwork. This breath-taking location has been designated a World Heritage Site for thirty years. This is also regarded as one of the shining examples of 17th-century French painting. The palace now has approximately 2,300 rooms, each uniquely designed. It's difficult to miss the palace's magnificence.

  • It's difficult to miss the palace's magnificence.
  • Remember to see the Garden, the Queen's Chamber, the Chapelle, the Royale, The Grand Trianon, the Hall of Mirrors, the Museum of French History, and the Musical Fountain Shows.
  • The opening times are 9 a.m. to 6:30 p.m.
  • Entry fee: €18 on non-show days or €25 on days with a musical fountain and garden show.

9. Palais Garnier:

Previously known as Salle des Capucines, the Palais Garnier is a historic opera theatre in Paris. This was the main location of Paris Opera, a famous ensemble of opera performers in the city, also famous as Opéra Garnier. Currently, it mostly presents mesmerizing ballet performances. The main entrance, steps, stage fly tower, grand foyer, and pavilions all include exquisite and entrancing woodwork carvings, paintings, and artwork, which make Paris a popular tourist destination.

  • Don't miss any amazing ballet performances, the Grand Escalier, Foyers, Bassin de la Pythie, Salon du Glacier, Auditorium, or the Library.
  • The times are 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. from September to June and 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. from July to August.
  • €9 for entry.

10. Quartier Latin:

The most popular tourist destination in Paris is Quartier Latin, situated on the Seine shores. There are numerous prestigious educational institutions, including the Schola Cantorum, Ecole Polytechnique, Ecole Normale Superieure, and Ecole des Mines de Paris. The private landscape park known as Luxembourg Park, located around the Senate Building and Palais du Luxembourg, is noted for its lovely fountains. Numerous sporting events, notably the France Tennis Championship, are held on the grounds.

The beautifully designed Garden, apple orchards, Medici fountain, Orangerie, Pavillon Davioud, and bronze Statue of Liberty miniature are not to be missed.

  • The entrance is free.

11. Moulin Rouge:

The Moulin Rouge, a historical and traditional cabaret establishment at the foot of the Montmartre highland, is where the French Cancan originated. Previously a venue for courting dances, Moulin Rouge is today a lavish cabaret establishment with lovely decorations. Of course, it's also one of the fascinating tourist destinations in Paris. It's one of the nicest places to visit in Paris at night because of the brightly illuminated structure, hot music, and romantic ambience. Additionally, if you're looking for interesting spots to visit in Paris, France, Moulin Rouge may be at the top of some tourists' lists.

  • Don't forget A fantastic and thrilling cabaret show.
  • Open from 9 p.m. until 1 a.m.
  • €9 for entry.

12. Musee D'Orsay:

One of the most well-known museums in Paris is Musee d'Orsay, which houses some great sculptures and works of art. Here, famous works by artists including Berthe Morisot, Jean Auguste Dominique Ingres, Eugène Carrière, and Johan Barthold Jongkind are shown for tourists. Many people interested in art, culture, and history visit this place. And undoubtedly one of the most well-known tourist destinations in Paris.

It is important to see the works of Georges Seurat, Edgar Degas, Edouard Manet, Georges Auguste Renoir, Bal du Moulin de la Galette, and Auguste Renoir.

  • Everyday opening times are 9:30 a.m. to 6 p.m., while Thursdays are open until 9 p.m. On Mondays.
  • Entry is free of cost.

13. Pont Alexandre III Bridge:

According to many, this bridge is among the most beautiful on the entire globe. It was built in 1896 and linked the Champs-Élysées with Les Invalides. It was constructed for the Exposition Universelle de 1900, which also witnessed the debut of the most powerful microscope in the world, radio, escalators, talking movies, and other technological innovations. It honours the partnership between Russia and France in 1892 and bears the name of the Russian Tsar Alexander III. Due to his ability to speak the language like a native, the Tzar was well-liked in France.

  • Don't miss the many sculptures carved into the bridge and its surroundings.

14. Louis Vuitton Foundation:

The Louis Vuitton Foundation, owned by the LVMH group, focuses on advancing art and culture. It is one of the sites in Paris that you must see, not just for the fancy label but also for the amazing design. It is one of the destinations you shouldn't miss out on when visiting Paris and is located on Mahatma Gandhi Avenue.

  • Don't miss: The structure contains 11 galleries, each of which has a different size. You may also view the big auditorium, which has roughly 350 seats.
  • Open from 11 a.m. till 8 p.m.
  • €2 is the entry fee.

15. Le Manoir De Paris:

Do you find the concept of haunted houses appealing? You'll like Le Manoir de Paris if that's the case. With a surface area of 1000 m², it is one of the most beautiful places to visit in Paris at night. 14 Parisian legends, most of which are based on actual events, are brought to life by the 30 artists here. The visitor can pick from one of five levels of fright in each of the 23 rooms' horror stories. To the collection of "horror" things, three animatronics, a violent dog, and a garish gargoyle have been added.

  • Remember Halloween, Valentine's Day, Christmas, and every Friday the 13th.
  • Opening times are 6 p.m. to 10 p.m.; weekends only. (Fri-Sun)
  • Adults pay €29, children ages 10 to 15 pay €20, and fast pass costs €37.

16. Musée Jacquemart-André:

This privately administered museum is located in Paris's 8th arrondissement. It is a part of the Institut de France, and the museum was designed in 5 parts by the renowned French architect Henri Parent. It was once the home of Édouard André and Nélie Jacquemart, a well-known social painter, before becoming a museum. They spent their whole life collecting great works of art, which they then displayed for everybody to see.

  • Don't forget: Works by Jean-Baptiste-Siméon Chardin and Sandro Botticelli Rembrandt, Pietro Perugino, Neri di Bicci, and many others.
  • Arrive for 30 minutes before closing; open from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.
  • Entry fee: €7 to €11.

17. Ile De La Cité:

In the heart of the city, this island is situated. The early Parisian tribes established the city here. Just 981 people live on the island, mostly in the northeast and west. Several canon residences in the neighbourhood were built in the 16th century. In World War 2, victims deported from Vichy to Nazi extermination camps are remembered at the Memorial des Martyrs de la Déportation.

  • Memorial of the Deportation Martyrs should not be missed.

18. Seine River:

Seine River, commonly referred to as the heart of Paris, is one of the most stunning and well-liked tourist destinations in Paris. The city's main water supply comes from the 777 km long river. There is a lot to see at the river's shores even if there isn't much to do there.

  • Entry is free.

19. Catacombs Of Paris:

This is another popular tourist destination in Paris. Because there are several ossuaries and a sizable collection of bones there, the location is significant. The area was created in the 18th century when overcrowding in cemeteries was becoming more and more of an issue. Over 6 million individuals were transported to the ossuary. Tourists are strongly advised to visit. This is the place to go if visitors want an unusual travel experience. Skeletons and bones can be seen in underground tunnels and caverns open to the public. All adventure seekers would love this location.

  • In Paris, France, the address is 1 Avenue du Colonel Henri Rol-Tanguy (75014).
  • Open from10 a.m. to 8:30 p.m.

20. Galeries Lafayette:

The Galeries Lafayette is the only place to go if you want to enjoy the best possible shopping experience in Paris. Lafayette's glimmering galleries are nothing less than heaven for those who love to buy. The location is in the wealthy Opera district. Three blocks of buildings are known to make up the enormous department store. You may explore the stores of some of the most well-known French companies here. You may look at major labels like Hermes, Chanel, and Louis Vuitton. You may also buy items from other brands. A fantastic view of the city is also available from the top floor.

In conclusion, this location in the city of Paris is well seen. Come here if you wish to enjoy a lavish lifestyle.

  • Location: Paris, France, Boulevard Haussmann.
  • Open from 9:30 a.m. to 8:30 p.m.

21. Le Marais:

There are many things here that you may investigate. Le Marais's historic streets are interesting to explore. This area has unique little boutiques, cool cafés, cutting-edge galleries, and eateries. One may say that this is a fantastic area with lots of things to do where you can enjoy time with your loved ones. For all tourists, this area is truly a must-see. It is well recognized that the location has an avant-garde reputation. This is the perfect place to go if you want to spend a day in Paris with friends or family. Therefore, be careful not to exclude this location from your list.

  • Activities: Take advantage of the area's various stores and coffee shops.

22. Musee Rodin:

Another must-see museum in Paris is Musee Rodin, situated in the city's centre. The French artist Auguste Rodin is honoured at this specific museum. His magnificent creations, such as The Kiss, The Thinker, and The Gates of Hell, are displayed within the museum for visitors to view. Tourists may surely be surprised by the outstanding sculptor's creations at this destination. If tourists want to appreciate art, here is the place for them. There are many wonderful works to discover all across the museum. Learning about Parisian sculpture in this way is helpful.

  • Address: 77 Rue de Varenne, Paris, 75007, France
  • Time: 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.
  • 12 Euros for entry.

23. Musee Picasso:

The Musee Picasso, sometimes known as the Picasso Museum, is one of Paris' most well-known museums. The museum's major attraction is the presence of thousands of archival items in addition to more than 5000 works of art. It is well known that each of these works provides an example of Pablo Picasso's creative process. Tourists must visit if they enjoy art and have always appreciated Picasso's work. Additionally, tourists may go through the artist's many creations. Tourists can see everything here, from drawings to sculpting and engraving. The museum is famous for having a unique collection of works by outstanding painters.

  • Visit this site if tourists want to see all of Pablo Picasso's artwork.
  • You may discover a lot about the artist by going to this destination.
  • Address: 5 Rue de Thoringy, Paris, 75003, France
  • Open from 9:30 a.m. to 6 p.m.
  • 50 euros; free entry on the first Sunday of the month.

24. Saint Germain Des Pres:

The Seine River is also close to this location. This Parisian district is mostly recognized for its galleries, luxury stores, hip cafés, and restaurants. The location has a lot to offer. Make sure to stay here for a whole day if you want to come. There are many things to discover nearby. After several hours of exploring, you may relax at one of the neighbourhood cafés or restaurants. Visitors need to know that the region is also highly recognized for its exciting nightlife. So, come here if you want a fantastic evening in Paris. This is a must-see location in Paris.

25. Jardin Du Luxembourg:

Luxembourg Park is a lovely area to visit in Paris and is situated on the left side of the River Seine, and the destination has a unique appeal that never fails to surprise tourists. Tourists appreciate the breathtaking views at this location. Here, a picnic with the family would be ideal. The finest spot to escape the bustling city life is in this location's natural green surrounds. Tourists will get relaxation from the bustling city life thanks to the area's tranquil beauty. Tourists can travel here to enjoy a relaxing afternoon with their loved ones. The Palais du Luxembourg, located inside the park, is also interesting. Overall, this is a wonderful location to spend time with your family.

  • The address is 75006 in Paris, France.
  • 7:00 a.m. to an hour before sunset.
  • No entrance fees.

26. Tuileries Garden:

Another destination in Paris that is great to visit is this one. This is a stunning location in the city to visit that is close to the Louvre. Most tourists arrive at the Garden in the afternoon to take in the sun and wonder at the French formal garden design. The yard provides a lovely view that will help you relax. Come to Tuileries Garden to meaningfully spend time with your loved ones. A small amusement park is also situated in the Garden, making it the ideal place for you to spend time with your children. Families taking vacations here will love this location. You have to go here once while you're in Paris.

  • 7 a.m. to 9 p.m. are the times.
  • The address is 113 Rue de Rivoli ,75001 Paris, France.
  • Activities: You may have a nice time in the Garden or have fun with your children at the indoor amusement park.
