List of Finance Minister in India

What is a finance minister?

A finance minister is one of the top ministers of the central cabinet ministers of India. The Finance minister of the country controls major financial activities of the country. He has the authority and power to make the annual budget and taxation law of the country.

History of finance minister

The ministry of finance was founded on 29th October 1946. At that time, Liaquat Ali Khan (a statesman and a lawyer of Pakistan) was the first finance minister of the interim government of Louis Mountbatten. He had been in charge from 29th October 1946 - 14th August 1947. After the independence of the Nation on 15th August 1947, Sri R.K Shanmugham Chetty became the 1st finance minister of India. He presented the first budget of the country on 26th November 1947.

Role and responsibilities of finance minister

  • Making annual budget
  • Making financial rules and regulation
  • Doing financial sanctions for all ministries and offices
  • Giving advice on government expenditure proposals and account matters
  • Making funds of all the departments
  • Giving advice on fiscal policy and public finances

List of Finance Minister in India

From the formation of the ministry of finance in India till the present time, there are a total of 28 Finance Ministers in India. However, some of them became 2 to 4 times the finance minister, so Nirmala Sitharaman, the present finance minister, is the 46th finance minister of India. The details of all finance ministers are listed below:

1. R.K Shanmukham Chetty

Mr. Shanmukham Chetty was the 1ST (15th August 1947-17th August 1948) finance minister of India. His full name was Ramasamy Chetty Kandasamy Shanmukham Chetty. He was a lawyer, economist, and politician. Mr. Chetty was born in British India in Madras on 17th October 1892 and died on 5th May 1953. He was a member of the Swaraj Party and Justice Party. He has done his education at Dr.Ambedkar Government Law College and Madras Christian College. Apart from the finance minister, he was also President of the Central Legislative Assembly and Diwan of Cochin kingdom. He made the Nation's first budget and presented it in parliament on 26th November 1947.

2. Jawahar Lal Nehru

Jawahar Lal Nehru was the 1st Prime Minister and 2nd (5th February 1949- 5th April 1949), 5th (24th July 1956-30th August 1956), and 7th (14th February 1958-22nd march 1958) Finance Minister of free India. He was born on 14th November 1889 in Allahabad and died on 27th May 1964. He did his B.A from Trinity College and Law from Inner Temple. He was a Barrister, writer, and politician, and a member of the Indian national congress. Nehru got Bharat Ratna in 1955. He served the country variously as two times the finance minister of India, three times the Defence Minister of India, the minister of external affairs, vice president of the executive council, Member of Parliament, Lok Sabha, and member of the constituent assembly of India.

3. Dr. John Mathai

Dr. Mathai was an economist and the 3rd (22nd September 1949-1st June 1950) finance minister of India. He was also the first railway minister of India. He was born on 10th January 1886 in madras and died in February 1959. He was a member of the Indian National Congress. He has done his education at Madras Christian College and Madras Law College. He got the Padma Vibhushan in 1959. Apart from these, he variously served the country as he was the first Chairman of the State Bank of India as well as the Vice-Chancellor of the University of Kerala (first Vice-chancellor) and of the University of Mumbai.

4. Dr. C. D. Deshmukh

Mr. Deshmukh was the 4th (1st June 1950-24th July 1956) finance minister of India. The full name of Mr. Deshmukh was Sri Chintaman Dwarakanath Deshmukh. He was born on 14th January 1896 in Bombay and died on 2nd October 1982. He studied at the University of Cambridge as well as got Padma Vibushan in 1975. Apart from being a finance minister, he served the county variously. From 11th August 1943 - 30th June 1949, he served as the Governor (3rd) of Reserve Bank of India. From 1956 - 1961 he served as the Chairman of the University Grants Commission. And he was the Vice-Chancellor of the University of Delhi from 1962 -1967.

5. T.T. Krishnamachari

Mr. Krishnamachri served the country two times as a finance minister of India as he was the 6th (30th Aug 1956-14th Feb 1958) and 9th (31th Aug 1963-31st Dec 1965) finance minister of India. He was born in 1899 in madras and died in 1974. He was a politician and a member of the Indian national congress, as well as he was an entrepreneur of the TTK group. He also served the country as a member of parliament, Lok sabha for madras (1951 - 1957), and member of parliament, Lok sabha for madras south (1957 - 1962). But despite so many respectable designations, he was the first minister of independent India who has resigned due to his involvement in a scam.

6. Morarji R. Desai

Morarji Ranchhodji Desai was the 4th prime minister as well as the 8th (23rd March 1958-31st August 1963), 11th (13th Mar1967-16th July 1969), 15th 24th March 1977-26th Mar 1977), and 18th (16th July 1979-28th July 1979) finance minister of India. He was born on 29th February 1896 in Bombay and died on 10th April 1995. He studied at the University of Mumbai. He was an activist and a politician. He was a member of the Janta Party. He got Nishan-E-Pakistan in 1990 and Bharat Ratna in 1991. He also served the country as the home minister, 2nd deputy prime minister, 2nd chief minister of Bombay state, and Member of Parliament, Lok sabha.

7. Sachin Choudhary

Sachin Choudhary was the lawyer and politician of India. He was the 10th (1st Jan 1966-12Mar 1967) finance minister of India. He was born on 24th February 1903 and died on 12th June 1992.

8. Smt. Indira Gandhi

Indira Gandhi was the 3rd prime minister and 12th (16th July 1969-27th June 1970) finance minister of India. Her full name was Indira Priyadarshini Nehru, and she was the daughter of the first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru. She was born on 19th November 1917 and died on 31st October 1984. She was dropped out of Visva-Bharti University and Somerville College, Oxford. She was a member of the Indian national congress and got Bangladesh freedom honor in 2011 and Bharat Ratna in 1971. She served the country as prime minister, two times minister of external affairs, minister of defense, minister of home affairs, and minister of information and broadcasting.

9. Y.B.Chavan

Full name of Y.B.chavan was Yashwantrao Balwantrao Chavan. He was the 13th (27th June 1970-10th Oct 1974) finance minister of India. He was born on 12th March 1913 and died on 25th November 1984. He was a politician, writer, and social activist. He was the leader of the Indian national congress and also called the leader of the common man. He serves the country variously as the 5th deputy prime minister, two times as a minister of home affairs, minister of external affairs, minister of defense, 1st chief minister of Maharashtra, and the 3rd chief minister of Bombay state.

10. C. Subramanian

S, known as Chidambaram Subramanian, was the 14th (10th Oct 1974-24th Mar 1977) finance minister of India. He was born on 30th January 1910 and died on 7th November 2000. He was a politician, writer, and social activist. He was a leader of the Indian national congress and studied at the University of Madras. For playing an important role in the green revolution in India with M.S. Swaminathan, he was awarded Bharat Ratna in 1998. Apart from the finance minister, he served the country as minister of agriculture, deputy chairman of the planning commission, defense minister, and the Governor of Maharashtra. He also wrote some books like; Hand of Destiny, Eventful Decades, etc.

11. H.M. Patel

Hirubhai Mulljibhai Patel was the 16th (26th March 1977-24th January 1979) finance minister of the country. He was born on 27th August 1904 in Bombay and died on 30th November 1993. He was the leader of the Swatantra party and played a very important role in internal and national security during and after the partition of India. He was a civil servant as well as a politician. He served the country variously as a home minister, defense secretary, and two times the Member of Parliament, Lok sabha in sabarkantha. Some books written by him were Rites of Passage, the First Flush of Freedom: Recollections, and Reflections.

12. Charan Singh

Chaudhary Charan Singh was the 5TH prime minister and the 17th (24th January 1979-16th Jul 1979) and 20th (19th Oct 1979-14th Jan1980) finance minister of India. He was born on 23rd December 1902 in Hapur and died on 29th May 1987. He studied at Dr. Bhimrao Ambedkar University, Agra. He was the leader of the Indian national congress. He also served the country variously as 3rd deputy prime minister, home minister, and two times chief minister of Uttar Pradesh.

13. H.N.Bhuguna

Hemwati Nandan Bhuguna was the 19th (28th July 1979-19th Oct 1979) finance minister of India. He was born on 25th April 1919 in Garhwal and died on 17th March 1989. He was the leader of Bharatiya Lok dal. He studied at Allahabad University. He served the country as the finance minister as well as the 9th chief minister of Uttar Pradesh.

14. R. Venkataraman

Ramaswamy Venkataraman was the 8th president and 21st (14th January 1980-15th January 1982) finance minister of India. He was born on 4th December 1910 in Tamilnadu and died on 27th January 2009. He studied at Loyola College, University of madras. He was the leader of the Indian national congress as well as a lawyer and activist. He played a role in the Quit India movement for the independence of the country. He served the country variously as the 7th vice president, home minister, and defense minister.

15. Pranab Mukherjee

Pranab Kumar Mukherjee was the 13th president and 22nd (15th January 1982-31st December 1984) and 38th (24th January 2009-26th Jun 2012) finance minister of India. He was born on 11th December 1935 in Mirity and died on 31st August 2020. He was the senior leader of the Indian national congress and awarded Bharat Ratna (2019) and Padma Vibhushan (2008). He did his B.A., M.A., and LL.B. from the University of Calcutta. He served the country variously as defense minister, two times minister of external affairs, leader of Lok sabha, deputy chairman of the planning commission, leader of the Rajya Sabha, Member of Parliament, Lok Sabha and Member of Parliament, Rajya sabha.

16. V.P. Singh

Vishwanath Pratap Singh was the 7th prime minister and 23rd (31st Dec 1984-24th Jan1987) finance minister of India. He was born on 25th June 1931 in Prayagraj and died on 27th November 2008. He was the 41st Raja Bahadur of Manda and was the only prime minister who was a monarch. He had been the leader of different political parties like the Indian national congress, Janata dal, and Jan morcha. He studied at Allahabad University and the University of Pune. He served the country variously as minister of external affairs, defense minister, leader of the house, Rajya sabha, 12th chief minister of Uttar Pradesh, Member of Parliament, Rajya Sabha, four times the Member of Parliament, Lok sabha. His biography was Vishwanath Pratap Singh: politician, painter, poet, every time I wake up.

17. Rajiv Gandhi

he was the youngest 6th prime minister and 24th (24th January 1987-25th July 1987) finance minister of India. He was born on 20th August 1944 and died on 21st May 1991. He was dropped out of trinity college, Cambridge, and imperial college London. He was awarded the Bharat Ratna in 1991. He was the leader of the Indian national congress and served the country variously as the president of the Indian national congress, leader of the opposition, and the Member of Parliament for Amethi.

18. ND. Tiwari

Narayan Datt Tiwari, an Indian politician, was the 25th (25th July 1987-25th June 1988) finance minister of India. He was born on 18th October 1925 in Nainital and died on 18th October 2018. He was the leader of the Bharatiya Janata Party and also a member of the Indian national congress. He studied at Allahabad University. He served the country variously as the 21st governor of Andhra Pradesh, 3rd chief minister of Uttarakhand, 15th minister of external affairs, and three times as a chief minister of Uttar Pradesh.

19. S.B. Chavan

Sankarrao Bhavrao Chavan was the 26st (25th June 1988-2nd December 1989) finance minister of India. He was born on 14th July 1920 and died on 26th February 2004. He was a member of the Indian national congress. He served the country variously as two times the home minister of India, two times the chief minister of Maharashtra, and as the president of the Bharat Scouts and Guides.

20. Madhu Dandavat

Mr. Dandavat was the 27th (6th Dec 1989-10th Nov1990) finance minister of India. He was born on 21st January 1924 in Ahmednagar, Bombay, and died on 12th November 2005. He was a politician, physicist, and member of Janta dal. He also served the country variously as two times the deputy chairman of the planning commission, railway minister, Member of Parliament, Lok sabha, and member of Maharashtra legislative council. He studied at the institute of science, Mumbai. His books are Jayaprakash Narayan: struggle with values: a centenary tribute, quest of conscience, etc.

21. Yashwant Sinha

Yashwant Sinha was the 28th (10th November 1990-21st June 1991) and 34th (19th March 1998-1st July 2002) finance minister of India. He was born on 6th November 1937 in Patna, Bihar. He is a member of the All India Trinamool Congress from 2021 to the present. Earlier, he was a senior leader of the Bhartiya Janata Party. He also served and serving the country variously as a member of parliament, Rajya Sabha and Lok Sabha, minister of external affairs, and now as the vice president of all India Trinamool Congress. Yashwant Sinha was awarded the Officier de la legion d'honneur in 2015.

22. Dr. Manmohan Singh

Dr. Manmohan Singh was the 13th prime minister and 29th (21st Jun1991-16th May 1996), 37th (30th November 2008-24th January 2009), and 39th (26th June 2012-31st Jul 2012) finance minister of India. He was born on 26th September 1932 in Gah, Punjab (now in Pakistan). He is an economist, academic, and politician. He did B.A and M.A from Punjab University, B.A from the University of Cambridge, and Dphil from the University of Oxford. He was a member of the Congress party. He served the country variously as 16th minister of personnel, public grievances and pensions, and the leader of the opposition in the Rajya sabha. He was awarded the Padma Vibushan and Adam Smith Prize.

23. Jaswant Singh

Jaswant Singh was the 25th (1996) and the 30th (16th May 1996-1st June 1996), and the 35th (1st July 2002-22nd May 2004) finance minister of India. He was born on 3rd January 1938 in Barmer, Rajasthan, and died on 27th September 2020. He studied at the Indian military academy Mayo College. Jaswant Singh was one of the founding fathers of BJP. He served the country variously as a major in the Indian army and fought the Indo-Pakistani war of 1965. He also served the country as deputy chairman of the planning commission, as a defense minister, and the minister of external affairs. For his significant services, Jaswant Singh got the Outstanding Parliamentarian Award in 2001. A movie on him was Jungle Cry.

24. P. Chidambaram

Palaniappan Chidambaram was the 31st (1st June 1996-21st Apr 1997) and 33rd (1st May 1997-19th Mar1998) and 36th (20th May2004-30th Nov 2008) and 40th (31st July 2012-26th May 2014) finance minister of India. He was born on 16th September 1945 in madras. He is a member of the Indian national congress party from 2004 till now. He did his BSc and LLB from the University of Madras, ma from Loyola College, and MBA from Harvard University. He is an economist and served the country variously as the Member of Parliament, Rajya sabha, home minister, minister of personnel, public grievances and pensions, and the Member of Parliament, Lok sabha. By profession, he was also a senior advocate.

25. I. K. Gujral

Inder Kumar Gujral was the 12th prime minister and 32nd (21th Apr 1997-1st May 1997) finance minister of India. He was born on 4th December 1919 and died on 30th November 2012. He served the country variously as leader of Rajya Sabha, member of Lok Sabha, minister of external affairs ambassador of India to Soviet Union, etc.

26. Shi Arun Jaitely

Arun Jaitley was the 41st (26th May 2014-14th May 2018), 43rd (23rd August 2018-23rd January 2019), and 45th (16th February 2019-30th May 2019), and the former finance minister of India. He was born on 28th December 1952 and died on 24th August 2019. He did LL.B. and B.COM from the University of Delhi. He was a member of the Bharatiya Janta Party and served the country variously as defense minister, minister of corporate affairs, minister of information and broadcasting, leader of the house, Rajya Sabha, leader of the opposition, Rajya Sabha, law and justice minister, minister of commerce and industry. By occupation, he is a senior advocate of the Supreme Court and a politician. He was awarded Padma Vibhushan in 2020. His books are A New India: Selected Writings 2014-19, Articles & Speeches by Arun Jaitely: A Compilation.

27. Piyush Goyal

Piyush Ved Prakash Goyal was the 42nd (14th May 2018-22nd August 2018) and 44th (23rd January 2019 to 15th February 2019). He was born on 13th June 1964. By profession, he was a Chartered Accountant. He served and serving the country variously as former railway minister, coal minister, minister of mines, minister of renewable energy, minister of textiles, minister of commerce and industry, etc.

28. Smt Nirmala Sitharaman

Sitharaman is the 46th (30th May 2019-present) finance minister of India. She was born on 18th August 1959. She is the member and former Spokesperson of the Bharatiya Janata Party. She also served and serving the country variously as she was a member of parliament from 2014 -2016, minister of commerce and industry from 2014-2017, defense minister from 2017-2019, as a minister of corporate affairs from 2019 till now. In Forbes 2020 list of most powerful women in the World, she was at 41st rank.
