31 Best Tourist places in Kullu Manali

Kullu Manali, one of Himachal Pradesh's most popular tourist places, attracts visitors of all kinds. It is no surprise that natural attractions rank among the top places to visit in Kullu Manali, given their variety of snow-capped peaks, charming rivers, and green valleys.

There are numerous areas where visitors may engage in activities like paragliding, skiing, rafting, and trekking. With its year-round pleasant weather, snow-capped mountains, undulating green valleys, and flowing rivers, Kullu Manali is a popular destination for honeymooners and families. Here is a list of some of the top Kullu Manali attractions that will make your vacation special.

1. Jogni Waterfall

The stunning Jogini falls in Manali are only a short distance from the Vashisth hot springs. The principal travel option is hiking from the Vashisth temple. One of the most well-known Kullu Manali tourist destinations, the stream into which these falls flow eventually runs into the Beas River.

The water slides from over 150 feet, dropping from several stages and forming a pool at the bottom that many people enjoy relaxing in. The Jogini Mata Temple, recognized as a spiritual location for several local towns, is located at the base of the falls.

These waterfalls, which indicate the northern boundary of the Vashisth Village, can be reached after a 3-hour hike along one of the most picturesque paths in the area.

  • Location: Vashist is located in Himachal Pradesh.
  • Distance: 46 km from the Kullu bus stop.
    4 km from the Manali bus stop.

2. Bhrigu Lake

It is located approximately 4,300 meters above sea level, close to Gulaba Village, and in the Pir Panjal Mountain range east of Rohtang Pass. Maharishi Bhrigu, a well-known yogi, is said to have meditated on the side of this lake, which carries his name. One of the reasons the lake never freezes even at such a high altitude is because the area was considered holy as a result.

Most people describe the meadows they come across on the walk to Bhrigu Lake as their inspiration for going on the hike, instead of the lake itself or the temple on its shores. Another reason people enjoy visiting this location is the breathtaking views of the adjacent valleys from such a high elevation.

  • Location: The Kullu district of Himachal Pradesh
  • Distance: From Kullu Bus Stand, it is 53 kilometres away.
    18 kilometres away from Manali Bus Stand

3. Parvati Valley

The Parvati Valley of the river Parvati, one of the nation's most picturesque regions, is undoubtedly the Himachal Pradesh attraction that will capture the hearts of visitors the most. The valley's slopes are lush, with gorgeous greenery, and several waterfalls have made their way through the area's seams to increase the splendour.

The best and most popular activities in Parvati Valley, where there are plenty to choose from, are trekking and camping. The Parvati Valley, which has the well-known tourist site of Kasol at its centre, is quickly becoming one of the most popular vacation spots in the region.

  • Location: In the Himachal Pradesh's town of Kullu.
  • Distance: 60 km from the Kullu bus stop.
    100 kilometres from the Manali bus stop

4. Old Manali

Old Manali, a peaceful and charming tiny village, is located about 3 kilometres distant from Manali and is slightly hilly. Old Manali, a favourite destination for travellers, is separate from New Manali.

The area is surrounded by various charming cafés and restaurants that provide some of the most delicious dishes. The location, home to the well-known Hadimba temple, is a trendy shopping destination for clothes and cherries.

  • Location: Himachal Pradesh's Old Manali, Manali.
  • Distance: 44 km from the Kullu bus stop.
    Two kilometres from Manali's bus stop.

5. Kasol

The Himachal Pradesh town of Kasol is a paradise which affordable for tourists. Kasol, a little village in the Parvati Valley, is more than just a popular destination for hiking and camping adventures; it is simple to declare that it is a complete journey.

The Parvati Kund or Parvati hot springs are located in Kasol. Tosh is a tranquil tiny town with an exciting trekking trip. Tosh can find the most excellent views of the Dhauladhar range of the Himalayas Kasol.

6. Malana Village

Malana, a paradise in the Himachal Pradesh state, is sometimes called an ancient village. The daunting Chanderkhani and Deo Tibba mountains separate the isolated village in the Malana Valley of the Malana River from the outside world.

The best method to get to Malana is to hike from the well-known tourist destination of Kasol. Everyone should go to Malana at least once because it is surrounded by some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the region.

  • Location: The Himachal Pradesh State
  • Distance: 45 kilometres from the Kullu bus stop.
    86 kilometres from the Manali bus stop.

7. Hampta Pass

The Hampta Pass, also known as Hamta Pass, is a narrower route that connects the Kullu Valley to the Lahaul Valley at an elevation of 4,270 meters above sea level.

The pass is well-known for its vivid wildflowers that grow between elevations of 3000 and 3800 meters and its fascinating waterfalls. These ice caps hold mountainsides, high-altitude lakes, and several peaks over 6000 meters clustered together to construct this picturesque paradise.

  • Location: Himachal Pradesh, in the valleys of Kullu and Lahaul.

8. Rohtang Pass

The Rohtang Pass, a high-altitude mountain pass of the Pir Panjal Himalayan range, is 3,978 meters above sea level and connects the Kullu Valley with the Lahaul and Spiti Valleys of Himachal Pradesh. Numerous other factors are essential for Rohtang Pass in addition to this.

Many people come here each year, especially in the summer, to escape the extreme heat of the plains because it is a region where they can find snow all year round.

The pass is essential for promoting travel to Ladakh, Spiti Valley, Lahaul Valley, and Kullu Valley. It is also a significant section of the Manali Leh Highway.

  • Location: Himachal Pradesh's Manali-Leh Highway.
  • Distance: The distance from the Kullu bus stop is 92 kilometres.
    The Manali bus stop is 51 km away.

9. Solang Valley

It is a side valley on the highest points of the Kullu Valley and falls on the route of the Rohtang pass, where it gets its name from the neighbouring town. The adventurous sports and activities in Solang Valley and the change from season to season are particularly well-known.

The Solang Valley features paragliding and other sports in the summer. At the same time, during the Winter, this location transforms into a fantastic mountain resort with all the necessary facilities.

ATV rides, ropeways, and trekking are other popular activities in Solang in addition to these. Solang is a must-see among the Kullu Manali tourist attractions for a wonderful experience while in the valley.

  • Location: India's Manali
  • Distance: 54 kilometres away from Kullu Bus Stand.
    Fourteen kilometres away from Manali Bus Stand.
  • Price: Entry costs for the ropeway are Rs. 500.0 per person, Rs. 1200.0 per person for paragliding, and Rs. 500.0 per person for zorbing.

10. Manikaran Sahib

The Manikaran Sahib Gurudwara is a worship destination because it strongly connects to Guru Nanak, the first Guru and creator of the Sikhism religion. Myth has it that when Guru Nanak and his follower needed a "Langar" but had nowhere to cook, the Guru instructed the follower to put a stone where the hot springs appeared to prepare their food. This area is well known for its natural hot springs.

Because the Pilgrims now make sacrifices to the god in the area's many springs, they also consider taking a bath in these holy waters to be lucky. Manikaran Sahib, one of the top destinations for Kullu places to visit, is a must-see.

  • Location: Kullu District, Manikaran Road, Hari Har Ghat, Himachal Pradesh
  • Distance: 19 km apart from Kullu Bus Stand.
    24 kilometres away from Manali Bus Stand
  • Timings: Always open.

11. Beas River

The Beas is a north Indian river that rises in the renowned Beas Kund and travels for about 500 kilometres before reaching the River Sutlej in the Punjab province. The river serves as a water supply for the people of Kangra, Manali, and Kullu.

Numerous rituals and legends from Hindu mythology are linked together, notably those of the sage Vyas, after whom this river is called. The river, which travels through numerous narrow valleys and hill slopes covered with greenery, is still honoured in many cultures and receives a lot of year-round pilgrimages.

A tiny group of Indus Dolphins also call Beas home. Another truth is that the river flows into Pakistan after joining the Sutlej in Punjab.

  • Location: In the Himachal Pradesh town of Manali
  • Distance: 40 kilometres apart from Kullu Bus Stand.
    3 km away from Manali Bus Stand

12. Hidimba Temple

The Hidimba Devi Temple goes back to the Mahabharata period and is dedicated to Hidimba Devi, the wife of the epic's Bhima. The complex of cave-like temples is located on a rock extending from the forest, and cedar trees encircle it. Before Bhima defeated her brother and she married Bhima, it is said that Hidimba used to meditate here with her brother, so ritual purity the area.

Since the locals worship her as their goddess, the Navratri is the ideal time to visit her temple and ask for blessings. The temple was constructed in 1553 and still stands today with its inside woodwork sculptures unchanged.

  • Location: Hadimba Temple on Old Manali Road in Manali, Himachal Pradesh
  • Distance: From Kullu Bus Stand, it is 44 kilometres away.
    From Manali Bus Stand, it is 2 kilometres.
  • Time: every day from 8:00 am until 6:00 pm

13. Vashisht Hot Water Spring & Temple

The village of Vashisht's natural hot springs are not too long from Manali and are situated above the Beas River. Due to these springs, the popularity of this modest village has made it to the top among people worldwide.

With a separate pool reserved for female visitors, the water's temperature is naturally regulated between 43° and 56° Degrees. Daily visitors come into the village in vast numbers to swim in the warm waters, which are also considered medicinal and have numerous other health benefits.

  • Location: Hot Water Spring in Bashisht, Himachal Pradesh.
  • Distance: 44 km distances it from the Kullu bus stop.
    From the Manali bus stop, it is 2.4 kilometres away.
  • Time: Daily hours are from 7 am to 10 pm.

14. Great Himalayan National Park

This Indian National Park was created in 1984 and has a total size of 1,171 sq km, with elevations ranging from 1500 m to 6000 m above sea level. It is home to more than 375 species of wildlife, all of which are protected, making hunting activities illegal there.

This location, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is well-known for eco-tourism in the Himachal Pradesh state and is a haven for trekkers and backpackers who have a deep passion for the natural world. Tree after tree of Deodar and Pine line the area, one of the most significant spots to see some stunning wildlife, including snow leopards.

  • Location: Himachal Pradesh's Kullu District, Shamshi
  • Distance: 57 kilometres apart from Kullu Bus Stand.
    98 km away from Manali Bus Stand.
  • Timings: Always open.
  • Price: For Indians, the cost or entry charge is INR 50.

15. Museum Of Himachal Culture And Folk Arts

The museum, which was opened in 1998, keeps Himachal Pradesh's historical past. It has various items representing the state's historical culture, principles, customs, and art. All of the significant historical artefacts that are rare and in worry of disappearing can be seen by visitors.

Visitors can anticipate traditional clothing, kitchenware, musical instruments, woodcrafts, and more, which reflect the people of Himachal Pradesh's vanished lifestyles. Additionally, there are presentations of models of numerous monuments, and it is one of Kullu Manali's main tourist attractions.

  • Location: Utopia Complex, close to Hadimba Temple, Hadimba Temple Road, Siyal, Manali, Himachal Pradesh
  • Hours: 8 am to 8 pm.

16. Gulaba

A lovely and picturesque village along the path to Rohtang Pass is well-known for being recognized and maintained by the National Green Tribunal Act's guidelines. The town has all the essential systems, computerized response systems, and a highly efficient trash collection system to maintain the village's pure beauty.

The picturesque village is an essential link in the rules for Rohtang Pass and is located at 4300 meters above sea level in the Pir Panjal Mountain range. During the colder months of November to March, Gulaba has difficulties setting up to stop vehicles from entering Rohtang Pass. Gulaba stands as the platform for the renowned Bhrigu Lake Trek.

  • Location: Himachal Pradesh's Leh-Manali Highway
  • Distance: 55 kilometres away from the Kullu bus stop.
    14 miles away from the bus stop in Manali
  • Time: 6:00 am to 6:00 pm, every day of the week.

17. Arjun Gufa

It connects to Mahabharata mythology and is named for one of the Pandavas, Arjun. Along the Beas River is one of Kullu Manali's attractions. The stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys from the cave draw tourists here.

A small opening into the mountain leads to the cave, where light is stopped after a certain distance. The exciting element is having to travel the challenging cave passageway with only a torch or lantern. Visitors can explore the cave inside before learning how to exit.

  • Location: Near Prini Village in the Indian district of Manali.

18. Rahala Waterfalls

Rahala Waterfall is created when melted ice flows through rocks. Since the waterfall is liquifying ice, the water is so cool. The fall is breathtaking at the height of 8,500 feet above sea level. Around may hear it singing birds in the deodar and silver birch trees that represent the forest.

The view is excellent enough to urge tourists travelling to Rohtang Pass to stay. In Kullu Manali, visitors can take pictures of various tourist attractions and spend time admiring the waterfall and the surrounding valleys.

19. Chandrakhani Pass

One of the top tourist places in Kullu Manali for trekking is this pass, located in the Kullu Valley at 3,600 meters. While travelling, visitors can observe a variety of plants and animals. It will be a fantastic experience to hear the birds chirping and see the distinctive, vibrant flowers decorating the track.

When visitors reach this point, they can wonder at the stunning views of Deo Tibba Peak, Pir Panjal, and the Parbati mountain range. It is a lovely combination of the outdoors and adventure.

The primary route to all the Kullu Manali tourist destinations is through Bhuntar. Unlike others, it is still a top-rated tourist destination but much less populated. 21. Kothi Village

One of the best places to see some of the highest snow-capped peaks of the Pir Panjal and Dhauladhar ranges is from the Kothi Village, a charming village on the way to Rohtang Pass. Aside from that, Kothi Village is renowned for its views of neighbouring mountains and the River Beas as it flows through a narrow and deep valley. Located in the foothills of the famous Rohtang Pass at 2500 meters above sea level.

Visitors will recognize this location as an artist's inspiration as soon as they enter the village. The picturesque beauty of this location will undoubtedly discover numerous movie screenshots shot on the hills of the stunning valley.

22. Jana Falls

The small village of Jana, which is close to Manali's Naggar region, is home to the unique but well-known Jana Falls. This location, surrounded by apple orchards, deodar and pine forests, and a great view of numerous snow-covered peaks, has become a prominent picnic location due to its simple yet appealing beauty.

When visitors want to travel some off the roads to locate incredible Kullu Manali tourist places, in that case, the wooden bridge near the falls allows for fantastic photo chances, making the location even more interesting for a visit.

Visitors can enjoy swimming and take a bath in the small pond created at the base of the falls, or they can walk up to the top for a better view of the area.

  • Distance: 34 kilometres apart from Kullu Bus Stand.
    33 km away from Manali Bus Stand.

23. Tibetan Monastery

One of the oldest and most well-known tourist attractions in Kullu Manali is the Tibetan monastery, which is relatively close to Manali's bus station. The monastery was established due to a large Tibetan population living in the area. The monastery's work and operations are managed by locals who raise all funding through contributions.

The location is well-known for its markets that buy genuine Tibetan handicrafts and hand-made items, in addition to being a centre of worship and spiritual awareness. The monastery is where a statue of Lord Buddha is located; it is built in the ancient Buddhist architectural style.

  • Location: Himachal Pradesh's Manali.
  • Distance: 39 kilometres apart from Kullu Bus Stand.

25. Ragunath Temple

One of the best locations to visit in Kullu Manali is the Raghunath temple, which is dedicated to Lord Rama of Hindu mythology and is located 2,056 meters above sea level. The Kullu Valley is considered the Kullu Valley of Gods because of the abundance of temples there, and this one, in particular, connects to the previous Kullu monarchy.

King Raja Jagat Singh built this temple in 1660 as punishment for his offences. Even though Ayodhya is considered the location of the god's birth, he carried back an idol of Lord Ram.

The temple is in a stunning location, and the surrounding scenery and natural spirituality of the surrounding area combine to make for a great experience.

  • Location: Sultanpur Post Office in the Himachal Pradesh State.
  • Distance: 0.55 km from the Kullu bus stop.
    43 km from the Manali bus stop.

26. Manali Sanctuary

This Himachal Pradesh wildlife sanctuary, inside the source region of the renowned Manalsu Khad, is just 2 kilometres from Manali. The Sanctuary is surrounded by Deodar, Kail, Horse Chestnut, Walnut, and Maple trees and covers an area of 32 sq. km.

This area has incredible wildlife views, including Brown Bears, Leopards, Musk Deer, and Snow Leopards roaming freely. In the summer, visitors can also see several mountain goats wandering around in wild areas in the park's mountain regions.

  • Location: Circuit House Marg in Manali, Himachal Pradesh is the location.
  • Distance: 45 kilometres from the Kullu bus stop.
    Three kilometres from the Manali bus stop.

27. Nehru Kund

This area, a short distance from the city and located on the Manali Rohtang Highway, takes its name from Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru, the country's first prime minister. The Prime Minister used to drink this area's crystal-clear spring water whenever he visited the city, earning the moniker Nehru Kund.

The crystal clear water and the sound of the water flowing out amongst the absolute peace of this location are two of the most excellent experiences one may have here. It is thought that the water source in the springs is Bhrigu Lake.

The experience is made even more beautiful by the river Beas flowing through the Kund/Pond. Over the years, its popularity has only increased, and many visitors now want to stay out here.

  • Location: the Himachal Pradesh town of Bashisht.
  • Distance: 47 km from the Kullu bus stop.
    5 km from the Manali bus stop.

28. Sultanpur Palace

A number of the most precious artefacts associated with the royals and the history of Kullu are now housed at this palace, which was once the royal mansion of the Kullu region's royal family. The castle is decorated with magnificent paintings in a combination of the regional Pahari and Colonial style.

These paintings depict the local way of life and its citizens wearing various traditional outfits. The palace has a well-known history of being destroyed by an earthquake and then lovingly restored by the locals to look just like the original.

Although the old palace, also known as Rupi Palace, was built in 1600, its breathtaking location amid rising mountains and snow-capped peaks is what properly makes it stand out.

  • Location: State of Himachal Pradesh, Sultanpur, Kullu
  • Distance: 1.2 kilometres from the Kullu bus stop.
    43 kilometres from the Manali bus stop.
  • Time: 7:00 am to 7:00 pm daily.

29. Basheshwar Mahadev Temple

This stone temple, designed in the shape of a pyramid, is located in one of the most breathtaking settings ever, on the banks of the Beas River in the Kullu Valley. The temple has idols for numerous deities, including the gods Vishnu and Ganesh and the goddesses Durga and Laxmi, in a small village named Bajura. Outside the temple, there is another Ganesh temple constructed in the form of a three-sided temple.

Numerous tourists from across the nation visit the complex each year to view the magnificent sculptures and works of art from the temple, which date to the ninth century and are currently maintained in good condition.

  • Location: Bajaura, Himachal Pradesh, on Bishweshwar Temple Road
  • Distance: Sixteen kilometres from the Kullu bus stop.
    It is 56.5 kilometres from the Manali bus stop.

30. Pandoh Dam

One of the leading large dams built on the Beas River to create hydroelectricity is located in the Mandi district of Himachal Pradesh. Additionally, before the river is released into the Sutlej River, the dam transfers a part of the river's flow to the Dehar Power House.

Its fame is primarily due to the lake that was created by the dam, though. Many people visit this dam to capture some of the most incredible images and bring back memories of the beautiful lake, as well as the lake's setting.

  • Location: Himachal Pradesh's Mandi District.
  • Distance: Fifty km away from Kullu's bus stand.
    From the Manali bus stop, 90 kilometres away.

31. Bijli Mahadev temple

The Bijli Mahadev Temple in Kullu Valley, another well-known temple in Himachal Pradesh, has a remarkable history every two years according to the Hindu calendar. A myth says Lord Shiva once consumed all the electrical current in the air to preserve humanity.

The Kullu community today thinks that a repeat of this event occurs every two years, during which the environment becomes the hottest due to a constant display of thunder and lightning. Another curious truth is that lightning strikes the precious Shiva lingam inside the temple every year. The Bijli Mahadev temple has interesting geography in addition to its legend.

The Himachal Pradesh temple is located at a significant elevation and provides beautiful views of the Parvati and Kullu valleys.

Conclusion

These picturesque places are a haven for those who value serenity, adventure, and the outdoors.
