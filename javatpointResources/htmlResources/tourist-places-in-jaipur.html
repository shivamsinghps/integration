13 Best Tourist Places in Jaipur

A tourist place is a place of attraction and interest that exhibits natural or built beauty, historical significance, natural and cultural value, and that offers amusement and leisure.

Importance of Tourist Place

  • It generates revenue for the economy, spurs growth, creates huge employment opportunities, and gives a major boost to the infrastructure development of the nation.
  • It becomes a medium of cultural exchange between the local people and foreigners.
  • It is a major source for the Government to earn from Foreign Exchange.
  • It helps the country to establish itself as a brand in the tourism sector.
  • It helps in the development of connectivity for the area.

Sites to see in Jaipur

Jaipur is Rajasthan's state capital. No tour in India is finished without spending and traveling time in Jaipur. People affectionately have given the nickname "The Pink City." It is called Pink City due to the blushing colors of its buildings.

Jaipur is brimming with architectural gems and is a wonderful land of heritage and culture. Jaipur is also known for India's famous Golden Triangle, a widely popular tourist circuit. The city of the Palace of Jaipur will give you a sense of the lavish lifestyle that Royal members of the family used to have. The astronomical instruments present in the Jantar Mantar Observatory to solve the mysteries of space and will make more awareness among people about space. If you have enough time, you can also see the beautiful festival held according to Jaipur's annual calendar, like Elephant Festival in early spring and the Internationational Kite Festival in January. Are you ready to take an exciting, thrilling, and amazing trip to "The Paris of India"?

1. City Palace

Address: Tulsi Marg, Gangori Bazaar, J.D.A Market, Pink City, Jaipur

City Palace has been situated in the center of the ancient city of Jaipur for close to 300 years. It has a sprawling complex of structures and buildings constructed around a series of landscaped garden courtyards overlooking lake-palace and Jaipur. Huge guard walls defend the palace, and the fairytale-like structure is still home to some of the Jaipur's modern-day royal family. It is more enchanting and extravagant than your imagination. It is a stunning and magnificent representation of Mughal and Rajasthani architecture styles. Chandra Mahal is an eye-catching building with curved eaves and domed rooftops. Other worth seeing places are Mubarak Mahal (Welcome Palace), which has dazzling costumes and textiles; Diwan-i-Am (Hall of Public Audience), which displays exclusive collections of miniature paintings.

After touring places, explore Friends of the Museum Bazaar: it contains some of Jaipur's amazing souvenirs.

2. Jantar Mantar

Address: Gangori Bazaar, J.D.A Market, Pink City, Jaipur

Official Site: http://www.jantarmantar.org/

Looking at it the first time, you will sense that these are just a bunch of larger-than-life abstract sculptures. Do not imagine it as an art gallery; it's a remarkable collection of astronomical tools. It was begun by the Rajput ruler Jai Singh II to calculate the heavens around 300 years ago.

Samrat Yantra, the massive observatory's sundial, is a striking feature. It is 27 meters tall and casts a huge shadow that accurately calculates the time to two seconds.

Free Advice: if you want to know each structure in greater depth, hiring a private guide would be worthwhile.

3. Hawa Mahal

  • Timings: 9:00 am to 4:30 am (every day)
  • Entry Fee: ₹200 for Foreign Tourist
    ₹50 for Indian Citizen
  • Architect: Lal Chand Ustad

Address: Hawa Mahal Road, Badi Choupad, J.D.A Market, Pink City, Jaipur.

It is an iconic landmark of Jaipur. Its literal meaning is Palace of Breeze. It is one of the most famous buildings in India and contains all the noticeable features shown in the city's postcards,

From the honeycombed facade, built to resemble the crown of Hindu god Krishna, salmon pink, to the geometric accents and rows of tiny windows;

This magnificent structure was made in 1799 for the Royal Ladies to oversee the festivals so that the public could not see them. 953 Windows of the Hawa Mahal allow smooth passage of breeze, making it a suitable summer palace.

The structure is a perfect mixture of Hindu and Islamic traditions. Tourists can see the complete magnificence of the structure from the outside. Archeological Department, Rajasthan Government, manages the Hawa Mahal.

4. Albert Hall Museum

Address: Museum Road, Ram Niwas Garden, Kailash Puri, Adarsh Nagar, Jaipur.

The structure has derived its name from London's Victoria and Albert Museum. Sir Swinton Jacob has designed the museum taking styles from Indo- Sarcenic architecture. The foundation laying stone was done by the Prince of Wales in 1876. The museum displays:

  • wood crafts, a wide range of metal objects, stones, carpets, arms and weapons, and stone and metal sculptures, natural stones, and ivory goods.
  • It also has huge collections of miniatures from Jaipur schools of art, Bundi, Kota, Kishangarh, and Udaipur.
  • It also houses a massive collection of art and important artifacts.

Rudyard Kipling, an English Writer, visited the museum and wrote: "rebuke to all other museums in India from Calcutta downwards." Such high praise came from the renowned author for this prestigious institution. If you are fond of Egyptian Mummy and would like to see antique coins of a different era of Indian History, clay figures showing all yoga positions, and costumes worn by different castes, you must visit this place.

Free Tip: Try to visit this place at night; when multicolored light hits the museum, it illuminates the alluring structure from the outside.

5. Birla Mandir

The Birla Temple, also popularly known as Lakshmi- Narayan Temple, is situated at the base of Moti Dungari. It is constructed on an elevated platform and is relatively modern; it is built with white marbles. The temple was built and commissioned in 1988 by eminent industrialists, the Birlas. The temple is devoted to the Lakshmi, the Goddess of good fortune and wealth, and his companion, Lord Vishnu, also called Narayan. The temple is a superb display of exquisite sculptures and carvings covering different mythological features, and it is a great work of art. The temple's top consists of three domes: featuring three different religions practiced in India. It was built in order to honor secular India. Apart from the temple, the complex also stores one museum that displays Birla's family's belongings. The spectacular structure emits a soothing, peaceful, and quiet vibe; it gives you the correct opportunity to applaud intricately designed marble carvings, beautiful sunset views, and shrines to gods.

6. Nahargarh Fort

Address: Krishna Nagar, Brahamapuri, Jaipur.

It is also known as Tiger fort, and it is approximately 300 years old. Nahargarh is present on the ridges of the Aravalli Mountains and provides an outstanding northern backdrop to the city. Jai Singh, during his reign in 1734, constructed it and later increased it in 1868. Nahargarh, an abode of tigers, acted as a formidable barrier and guarded Jaipur against its adversaries. Within the boundaries, the fort has Madhavendra Bhawan, which was a summer spot for the Royal.

Tourists can also visit the sculpture park and the fort's wax museum. If you get hungry, you can swing to the fort's amazing restaurants and relish the traditional Indian cuisine.

  • Timings: 10:00 am to 5:30 pm- Every Day
  • Entry Fee: ₹200 for foreigners, ₹50 for Indians, ₹25 for Students
  • Restaurants: Once Upon a Time, Padao
  • Architectural Style: Indo- European architecture

7. Galta Ji

It is a prehistoric Hindu Temple Site and is present on the outskirts of Jaipur City. It has pavilions, shrines, holy kunds, and natural springs. The magnificent temple is located at the heart of hilly terrain, surrounded by a wonderful gorge that brings tourists every year. Temple is constructed with pink sandstone and has a huge temple complex consisting of different shrines. The architecture is unique and built like a palace. The wonderful structure has been beautified with round roofs, pillars, and painted walls. This pilgrimage site also has temples of Lord Hanuman, Lord Ram, and Lord Krishna. The shrines take pride in having seven holy kunds and natural freshwater springs. Galta Kund is more sacred and supposed to be; it never gets dry. Different tribes of monkeys are believed to be found here, and natural settings and religious hymns and chants make the atmosphere bliss.

  • No Entry Fee
  • Timings: Monday to Sunday- 5:00 am to 9:00 pm

8. Amrapali Museum

Official website: https://amrapalimuseum.com/

  • Address: Ground Floor, K, 14/B, Ashok Marg, Panch Batti, C Scheme, Ashok Nagar, Jaipur.

Amrapali Museum is present in Jaipur is one Site to visit; the museum displays a huge collection of Jewelry in varied forms of precious metals. The museum conducts remarkable Jewelry exhibitions of different stones and pearls of the ancient period. Among the jewels, you will witness embellished shoe covers, silver bands for horses' legs, chariots fully covered in silver, and a glorious crown for a groom from Himachal Pradesh. The museum houses 4,000 different kinds of artifacts brought from different parts of the country. Tourists get thrilled by seeing such intricate designs of the artifacts. Every exhibition opens up new surprises for the visitor. Tourists who have been to the museum experience it like a movie of treasure. While these items are only for the exhibitions, you can avail an extensive range of bracelets, necklaces, and other wearables from the museum shop.

9. Jal Mahal

It is also known as Water Palace and is one of the remarkable sights in the Jaipur. At once, it might look like a mirage for you. When it was constructed, Maharaja Madho Singh I did not wish to use it as a Palace. But, in fact, it was constructed as a hunting lodge for the royal and his entourage during their duck hunting trips. It is situated in the middle of Man Sagar Lake in Jaipur city. It is a very popular tourist spot for sightseeing. The building is built in the Rajput style with pink sandstone in a symmetrical style. People who come to witness such beauty are unaware that innovative design and technology were used to create such incredible structures.

Regular tourist is not permitted to enter the Jal Mahal, and entry of the public is restricted into that area.

  • Timings: Morning to Evening (Every Day)
  • Entry Fee: It is not open for the visitor. Visitors can take a glimpse of the Lake Shore.
  • Location: Amer Road

10. Markets

Jaipur is considered to be the mecca for souvenirs. Markets in Jaipur are filled with everything from precious gemstones to embroidered textiles, precious gem and costume jewelry, Rajasthani puppets, and cobalt blue pottery. Just directly move to the Handicrafts Emporium from Ajmeri gate to experience a hassle-free shopping experience. The Government administered stores sell sets of wooden carvings representing Rajasthani characters, blue pottery decorated with yellow flowers, and hand-printed sarees. They have a fixed price, so you can't get any discount, and you can also avoid bargaining.

Johari Bazar is a paradise for the Jewelry lover. The market lit from top to bottom with dozens of stores selling everything from fine silver and gold to costume Jewelry.

11. Jaigarh Fort

Address: Above Amber Fort, Devisinghpura, Amer, Jaipur, Rajasthan, India-302001

The Jaigarh Fort is a majestical fort constructed by the Sawan Jai Singh III. The main reason for the construction was to shield Amer Fort and the structures which are within the complex. It gives a panoramic view of Jaipur City. It is also popular because the renowned treasure was buried inside the palace, and the Government of Rajasthan confiscated it in 1970. The fort is constructed with thick walls of red sandstone and is spread over 3Kilometer in length and 1 kilometer in width. The fort also has " Jaivana Cannon" and a huge complex. It includes Aram Mandir, Lalit Mandir, Laxmi Vilas, and Vilas Mandir. No one has conquered the Jaigarh fort in a battle, and it remained one of the most powerful forts in the Jaipur.

12. Panna Meena ka Kund

There are 2,000 step-wells in India, and 100 are found in Rajasthan. You can visit by taking a 10-minute walk from the Amber Fort. It is a unique architectural presence in India and tourists come in large to witness such remarkable architecture. People call with other names like Panna Meena Bawri, Panna Meena Baori, and Panna Mian Kund.

Panna Meena ka Kund is present in Amer Town. No one knows the reason for the name, but several stories are there which try to tell the reason behind the naming of the structure. This Baori has helped locals by providing access to fresh drinking water. The traces of this structure can be found when there was no piped water. It remains the sole source of water when there is low rainfall.

  • No Entry Fee
  • Timings: Monday to Sunday- 12:00 am to 12:00 am

13. Akshardham Temple

At first glimpse, it might look like an ancient site temple, but do not get swayed by this attraction; it was constructed in the 21st century. The temple is popular for its carvings, magnificent idols, beautiful architecture, and sculptures, and the temple is dedicated to the Narayan (Lord Vishnu), the Hindu God. It provides an Indian version of the architecture, Hindu gods' sculptures, and cultural heritage to the visitors. It is situated in the Vaishali Nagar, Jaipur. The idol of Lord Vishnu is decorated with silver and gold ornaments.
