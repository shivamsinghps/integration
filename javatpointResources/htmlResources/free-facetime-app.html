Free Facetime App

Face time is one of the most famous communication apps for Apple users. If we are users of iPhone, iPad, or MacBook, then the application is already installed on our device. If we want to start the app, then we have to type the username and password; after that, we can use it.

Apple/iOS, on the other hand, can only use FaceTime. It is not available on the Google Play store. So, if we want to switch to an Android device or are already using it, we cannot use FaceTime.

So, in this tutorial, we will discuss some FaceTime alternatives on the market that we can use on our Android device. These apps are available for free and have a large number of users.

Types of Free Facetime App

There are various types of free facetime app:

  1. Google Meet
  2. Whatsapp Messenger
  3. Skype
  4. Viber
  5. Signal Private Messenger
  6. Snapchat
  7. Facebook Messenger
  8. Google Duo
  9. JusTalk
  10. Zoom
  11. JioMeet

1. Google Meet

Google is developing enterprise-grade video conferencing, which is available to everyone. Anyone with a Google Account may now host an online meeting with up to 100 people for 60 minutes.

Advanced highlights are available to businesses, schools, and other organizations, including meetings with up to 250 internal and external participants and live broadcasting to up to 100,00 people inside a domain.

While Google Duo is a one-to-one service, Google Meet is a video calling alternative that sits alongside Google Chat. Although it has its own application, the most of those who use it are G Suite users, including Gmail, Calendar and Docs for business. We can start meeting from Gmail or Google Calendar, and talk with individuals, hold a conference, or simply text chat.

Features of Google Meet

There are various features of Google Meet:

  • Meetings with Live captioning
  • Unlimited numbers of meetings
  • Screen sharing with participants
  • Adjustable layouts and screen settings
  • Messaging with participants
  • Cross-platform compatibility
  • A preview screen for video and audio
  • Integration with Google and Microsoft Office Apps

2. WhatsApp Messenger

Price: Free

Google Play Rating: Multi-Platform (Android, iOS, etc.)

Support: 4.3/5

WhatsApp is the most popular and used messaging app globally and it's a terrific alternative to FaceTime. It's also absolutely free. We can use WhatsApp to send instant messages, share photos, documents, videos, as well as make video and audio calls similar to Facetime.

Features of WhatsApp Messenger

The following are the features of WhatsApp Messenger:

  • Instant messaging
  • Chat in Private or in Groups (up to 256 people)
  • We can also use WhatsApp our desktop via WhatsApp.
  • Using WhatsApp messenger, we can share documents, videos, photos, and also our location.

Usage:

We must validate our phone number after downloading WhatsApp (for Android) then we have to verify our phone number. After entering the phone number then we will receive a 6-digit authentication code on our phone number, and after entering it then we can use WhatsApp.

3. Skype

Price: Free

Google Play Rating: 4.3 / 5

Support: Multi-Platform

Another alternative to FaceTime is Skype. It is part of Microsoft that was launched in 2003. Users of Skype, like those of WhatsApp and FaceTime, may send instant messages, make audio/video calls, and do a variety of other things. Skype is also supported both on iOS as well as Android devices, desktops, and tablets.

Features of Skype

The following are the features of Skype:

  • SMS Connect
  • Video and Voice Calls
  • Screen Sharing
  • Supports photos, documents, videos, and much more.
  • Share photos, documents, and videos.
  • Private or Group Chat

Usage

After downloading Skype, we have to create a Skype account; if we don't have, then we have to sign-in by entering our Skype username/email and password to begin using it.

4. Viber

Viber has been around since roughly 2010. We may also use Viber to send instant messages, video chat, and make voice calls, and much more. We can also make low-cost calls to genuine phone numbers using Skype.

Feature of Viber

The following are the features of Viber:

  • Send stickers and gifs
  • Video and Audio calls
  • Share photos, documents, and videos
  • Build Groups and Communicate
  • Instant text, video, and voice message

Usage,

After downloading Viber, (for Android), we have to enter and verify our phone number. After entering the phone number, we get a 6-digit authentication code to the phone, which we must enter before using Viber.

5. Signal Private Messenger

Price: Free

Google Play Rating: 4.6 / 5

Support: Multi-platform

Signal private messenger is a type of app which offers the user a secure connection and privacy using end-to-end encryption. It is also an excellent alternative to Facetime because it supports multi-platform.

Features of Signal Private Messenger

Signal Private Messenger has the following features:

  • Private and group chats
  • Share picture, gif, location, etc.
  • End-to-end encrypted messaging
  • Video and Voice calls.

Usage

When we download it, Signal asks for our phone number and then sends us a verification code. We may then set-up our profile after inputting it, and we will need to generate a six-digit PIN for added security.

6. Snapchat

Price: Free

Google Play Rating: 4.3 / 5

Support: Multi-platform

Snapchat is another famous communication app. Popular among teenagers. Snapchat now allows for one-on-one or group video calls of up to 16 people. One unique aspect of Snapchat that set it apart from other applications is that messages we send or receive disappear once they have been read or seen.

Features of Snapchat

The following are the features of Snapchat:

  • Instant text, video, and voice message
  • Build groups and Communities
  • Video and audio calls
  • Share photos, videos, documents, etc.
  • Send stickers and GIFs

7. Facebook Messenger

Price: Free

Facebook messenger is undoubtedly the most user-friendly alternative to FaceTime on Android. Facebook is used by many people, including most of the individuals we know (probably). That implies we can do video calls with individuals instead of talking to a vast group of people to test a new service. Even though it is bloated and inconvenient, the app itself is useful. It also works cross-platform so our Android, iOS, and computer-toting friends can join the fun. However, we hope Facebook; we hope Facebook would concentrate on making the app a little less awful. Video chat has recently been added to messenger lite. We recommend that you start with the Lite version.

Features of Facebook Messenger

The following are the features of Facebook Messenger:

  • Live video char//video calling
  • Photo/video sharing
  • Instant messaging
  • Groups chats- users can chat with their Facebook friends as well as phone book contacts.
  • Ability to record voice messages

8. Google Duo

Price: Free

Google Duo is basically FaceTime on Android. It's a straightforward live video chat service. Simply, we imply that it's all this application does. We open it, ties to our mobile number, and afterward, we can get to calling individuals. Whoever we're calling should utilize Duo as well. The fact that this application is cross-platform is the most outstanding feature. That implies our iPhone friends can get on board too. It has a couple of nifty highlights, for example, knock-knock, which allows us to see callers before we get their video chat call. It's free, multi-platform, simple, and functions very well.

Features of Google Duo

The following are the features of Google Duo:

  • Using Google duo, we can share our phone's screen.
  • We can also use Google duo from a web browser
  • It also offers Picture-in-Picture (PiP) Mode.
  • Send messages to our contacts.
  • In the Google duo, we can also use effects, filters, and Portrait mode.
  • In the Google duo, we Pin our contacts to our Home Screen.
  • Using the Knock-Knock feature, we can see who is calling.

9. JusTalk

Price: Free with in-app purchases

JusTalk is a surprisingly good video chatting app. Except for 2G, it has good video quality on all connections. It also allows you to do things like a doodle on the screen while talking, sharing pictures, and even customizing the look of your screen. There is also cross-platform support, so that we may share it with your iOS buddies. Make sure you are not fooled by the price. It is completely free to use this application. Themes and other customization elements are available for purchase in-app. They are absolutely optional. In comparison to FaceTime on Android, it's a fantastic option.

Features of JusTalk

The following are the features of Justalk:

  • Free voice and video calls
  • Lively Messaging
  • Secure Data Encryption
  • Memories recoding
  • Endless fun while calling

10. Zoom

Zoom is not actually a substitute for FaceTime, but it is a better, cross-platform video chat app. It advances itself as a commercial or educational platform, yet anyone may use it. It allows video chats with up to 100 participants and incorporates encryption, screen sharing, and other highlights. For one-on-one discussions, you certainly need somethings like Facebook Messenger or Google Duo. Although, those who need the capability to scale up must undoubtedly consider this one.

Features of Zoom

The following are the features of Zoom:

  • Collaboration: - Permits collaboration with up to 300 UR and external non-UR peoples, with chat, whiteboarding, annotation, and breakout rooms.
  • Scheduling: - Browser, client and plugin scheduling options, with the delegation for co-hosts, and schedulers.
  • Recording: - With the help of the Zoom, we can record meeting locally and upload to OneDrive, YouTube, or blackboard learn.
  • Screen and Application Sharing: - Zoom presenter allows users to share their entire desktop or a specific application.
  • Mobile Devices: - Zoom is a feature-rich mobile app for Android and iOS.
  • Large Room and Webinar: - We can extend the zoom webinars to permit bigger groups, up to 500 interactive participants, large rooms, or 10,000 viewers through Zoom webinars.
  • Dynamic Voice Detection: - In zoom, the primary camera view will automatically switch to the active speaker when zoomed in.

11. JioMeet

JioMeet is one of the newer substitutes to FaceTime, and it's really somewhat better in few circumstances. JioMeet just does not offer one-on-one video conversations but also multi-person video chats with up to 100 people simultaneously (for a maximum of 24 straight hours). This app also contains a pleasant, basic UI and a few security highlights such as password-protected chats, and there is a driving mode for security. It's a little bit newer than most options and might have a few bugs, but it worked fine in our testing.

Features of JioMeet

The following are the features of JioMeet:

  • JioMeet provides an endless number of free HD (High-Definition)
  • Like the Zoom video conferencing App, JioMeet offers one-to-one video The clients can also host meetings with up to 100 people.
  • Meetings on JioMeet can be password protected, similar to how Zoom supports a waiting area for security reasons.
  • In JioMeet, Clients can also send text, voice messages, images and files to one another.
  • The App's USP is that there is no limit. The free call can go on for up to 24 hours without interruption.
  • The multi-device login support
      up to 5 devices at a time that can be switched during ongoing calls.

The meetings on JioMeet can be scheduled, have an active speaker layout, password-protected meetings, screen-sharing feature, waiting room, safe driving mode to video call while driving, multi-device support, and the ability to switch to another device with ease.
