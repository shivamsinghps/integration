Kota University

Kota University is spread across Bundi, Karauli, Jhalawar, Kota, Baran, and Sawai Madhopur. It is a well-established university in the state of Rajasthan, and 175 + colleges are affiliated with the University. The University came into existence with the enactment of a state legislative act called the University of Kota Act, 2003. The first vice-chancellor of the University was Prof. B.L. Verma. The University Grants Commission well recognizes Kota University. The University provides UG (Undergraduate) and P.G. (postgraduate) courses to the students. The faculties of the University are Law, Education, Commerce, Social Sciences, Arts, Science, and Management.

Governor of Rajasthan, Shri Kalraj Mishra, is the present chancellor of Kota University, and the vice-chancellor of the University is Professor Neelima Singh. The official website of the University is www.uok.ac.in. The official website can find all the latest updates and university information.

The University has signed several Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with several national and international parties for research and development in several disciplines. For example, DCM Sri Ram Consolidated Limited, Kota, Centre for Climate Science and Policy Research, and Linkoping University, Sweden. The University has sent a proposal to the UGC to develop a rainwater system, girls' hostel, dispensary, Central Library, Sports Complex, sewerage line, boys and girl hostels, and computer science on the campus.

University believes in the motto of "Satyam, Shivam, and Sundaram". The motto means the search for truth, the quest for welfare, and the urge for the best. The University has made an effort to conduct national and level workshops, host conferences, seminars, and publishment of articles, journals, and book chapters on national and international standards.

Vision

To make this institution a center of excellence and a source of learning, the Department for teaching and research in various faculties and interdisciplinary courses in which students can express their hidden potentials in society and contribute to the nation-building and development of our motherland and humanity as a whole.

Mission

  • To give jobs-oriented education to the student.
  • Inculcation of entrepreneurial skills in the students for innovation and taking risks in real life.
  • Empowering the youth to power the nation.
  • To develop self-reliance in young minds by stimulating their intellectual response to difficulties of the contemporary century.
  • Imparting quality education with an affordable fee structure
  • Promote and Facilitate Technological Developments.

Some of the widely famous courses offered by the University are B.Pharma, MBA, M.P.Ed, MBA, and M.Sc.

University holds an entrance exam for the admission of students into the college other than M.P.Ed. Students are admitted to courses like B.Pharma and Bsc based on the score in class 10+2 board level examination. Appearing candidates have to attain minimum marks to get admission into the U.G. and P.G. courses. Some relaxation of 5% is provided to students coming from S.C. (Scheduled Caste), S.T. (Scheduled Tribes), OBC (Other Backward Caste), SOBC, and P.H. backgrounds.

The institution offers many internship opportunities to worthy and deserving students to acquire and develop new skills for getting jobs in off-campus placement programs. University placement opportunities are very few for the student. The highest salary offer received to any student is 3.5 lakh/annum.

Kota University Admission Process

Students who want to be admitted into Kota University must register online on the official website, submit all the necessary documents, and pay the application charge. Steps are given below:

  • Log in to the official portal of Kota University.
  • Go to the online application from the drop-down menu.
  • Fill out the application form and submit all the scanned documents as required.
  • Pay the application charge of INR 500 by credit/debit card or net banking.
  • Take a screenshot of the registration number and save the application receipt.
  • Shortlisted students must visit the University premises with the necessary documents and a photocopy of the application form.

Required Documents

  • Transfer Certificate
  • Character Certificate
  • Date of Birth Certificate
  • Class 10 and Class 12 Marksheet
  • Passport Size Photograph
  • U.G. mark sheet (if applicable)
  • P.G. mark sheet (if applicable)

Common Asked Questions

Q- Explain the refund policy of Kota University in case a student wants to take back his admission.

Ans: The refund Policy of Kota University states that if a candidate decides to withdraw from University, they should apply for a refund within 15 days before closing the admission or after taking admission, whichever is earlier. 70% of the tuition will be given back to the student, but it is subject to the condition that the seat so vacant has to be occupied by another candidate. The admission Fee is non-refundable.
