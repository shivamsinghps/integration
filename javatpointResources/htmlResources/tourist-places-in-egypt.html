58 Best Tourist Places in Egypt

Egypt is a great nation that is rich in many treasures for tourists. Egypt offers various locations for a fun sightseeing trip, from historical beauties to culturally significant landmarks.

There are many pleasant locations to go to in Egypt to make your vacation unforgettable, regardless if you are:

  • A lover of history
  • An adventurer
  • A family is on holiday
  • A honeymooning couple

People of all ages may fulfill their vacation desires in Egypt, an excellent combination of culture, adventure, and enjoyment. In addition to the pyramids, Egypt has many more breathtakingly beautiful tourist destinations that you must visit.

There are plenty of museums, stunning valleys, and impressive temples you can visit for a trip. The fascinating thing about this is that the incredible architecture of ancient Egypt still makes you wonder how these locations came to be. Here is the list of tourist places in Egypt

1. Luxor Temple:

The pharaohs Ramses II (1279-1213 BC) and Amenhotep III principally built the Luxor Temple, one of Egypt's most magnificent and finest temples (1390-1352 BC).

This temple, also known as the Southern Sanctuary, was altered at various points in history by Alexander the Great, the Romans, and King Tutankhamun. It features stunning sandstone walls that light wonderfully at sunset and has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Place: Luxor Governorate, Luxor City, Egypt
  • Time: 6 am to 10 pm every day.
  • Entry Fee: 160 EGP per person; students pay just 80 EGP per person.

2. Great Pyramid of Giza:

The Great Pyramid of Giza, one of Egypt's most well-known tourist attractions, is the oldest survivor of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. The Great Pyramid of Giza is one of three magnificent pyramids representing the Giza pyramid complex. It is also the oldest and most significant.

According to Egyptologists, this pyramid houses the burial of Egyptian king Khufu from the Fourth Dynasty. For more than 3,800 years, the Great Pyramid of Giza kept the record for the highest artificial structure on the planet.

  • Location: Giza, Egypt's Giza Governorate
  • Time:Hours are 8 am to 5 pm every day.
  • Entry Fee: 1,700 INR per person.

3. Valley of the Kings:

A popular tourist attraction in Egypt for history enthusiasts, the Valley of the Kings, also known as the Valley of the Tombs of the Kings, is situated on the western side of the River Nile. The New Kingdom's rulers and prominent nobles were buried in this valley from the 11th to the 16th centuries.

It is located in the Theban Necropolis next to Thebes. East Valley and West Valley are combined at the location. The second one contains the majority of the royal graves. Writing carved on the walls generally used Greek and Latin language. More than 63 rooms and tombs can be found here, and they are all decorated with fascinating pieces of art inspired by mythological Egyptian figures.

  • Location: Luxor, Egypt's Luxor Governorate
  • Hours are 6 am to 5 pm every day.
  • Entry Fee: 380 INR for three tombs of your choosing

4. Nile River Trip:

The Nile River is among the most picturesque spots to visit in Egypt, defining the country's natural attractiveness. The view of the azure waters and the stunning sunrise and sunset impress any nature admirer. Furthermore, the Nile River offers fantastic options for vacation, including boat journeys.

The visitors may see various of the nation's most famous sights on their Nile River tour trips, including the Temple of Kom Ombo and the Temple of Horus at Edfu.

5. Egyptian Antiquities Museum:

The Egyptian Antiquities Museum, commonly known as the Egyptian Museum and the Museum of Cairo, is located in the city's center. This lovely museum is home to numerous one-of-a-kind and unique artifacts that date back to the prehistoric past, giving visitors a glimpse of the golden age of Egypt when monarchs controlled the nation.

The Egyptian Antiquities Museum, which houses more than 120,000 historical artifacts, is one of the top destinations in Egypt for history enthusiasts.

  • Place: Cairo, Egypt
  • Time: Thursday and Sunday from 9 am to 9 pm, Monday through Wednesday, and Friday to Saturday from 9 am to 5 pm,
  • Entry Fee: 460 INR per person

6. The White Desert:

The White Desert in Egypt is one of the most exciting destinations. It is characterized by substantial chalk mountains that give the impression of a white landscape in the middle of barren land. The entire area is covered in stunning peaks that resemble icebergs and substantial white stones.

Travel to the unusual White Desert to take in the area's breathtaking natural splendor while touring Egypt's temples and tombs.

  • Place: Egypt's Farafra.

7. Siwa Oasis at Libyan Border:

Siwa Oasis, one of Egypt's most pleasant vacation destinations, is situated in the nation's western region, away from the hustle and bustle of the cities. This delightful little oasis, surrounded by enormous palm trees and several lovely water springs, is one of the most beautiful tourist sites in the Western Desert.

A spectacular mud-brick fortification's ruins may be seen all around this location. The Siwa Oasis, the most significant starting point, is where you organize your adventure journeys to the vast surrounding desert.

8. Sakkara Pyramids:

Though the Giza Pyramids are undoubtedly well-known, Egypt is also home to several other magnificent pyramids that may visit. Only a few kilometers outside of Cairo, the Sakkara Pyramids offer a glimpse of the impressive architectural prowess of the ancient Egyptians.

The majestic Sakkara Pyramids are made up of the Red Pyramid, the Bent Pyramid, and the Step Pyramid. Along with the pyramids, several ancient tombs with richly decorated inner walls will captivate your eye.

  • Place: Giza, Egypt's Giza Governorate.

9. Mosque of Mohamed Ali:

The Mohamed Ali Mosque, often known as the "Alabaster Mosque," is a mosque in Cairo's Citadel. This mosque, built in the 19th century, is said to be among the top locations in Egypt for architecture and spiritual lovers.

This mosque is considered the most prominent building constructed in the 19th century, with its twin minarets and active silhouette (a subject seen against a light background). The Mohamed Ali Mosque has an artistically decorated interior and Ottoman-style architecture.

  • Place: Cairo Governorate, Al Abageyah, El-Khalifa, Egypt
  • Hours: Every day from 8:00 am to 5:00 pm.
  • Entrance Fee: 40 EGP per person

10. Zamalek:

The Zamalek area, located on Gezira Island's northeast shore, is an isolated place filled with high-end restaurants, interesting antique shops, and lovely boutiques.

Zamalek is one of the most attractive sites to visit because of its architecture, which is greatly influenced by European styles. A floating ship with ten restaurants is called the Le Pacha 1901, and you must go there.

Here, you may enjoy a wide range of cuisines and have a good time in the bar with karaoke (a method of entertainment where a machine constantly plays the music of well-known songs so that visitors may sing along with the lyrics) and a DJ playing music till 2 am.

Then you may tour the numerous art museums and galleries, including the Museum of Modern Egyptian Art and the Palace of Arts, among others.

  • Location: Cairo, Egypt, is the location.
  • Time: 24-hour period

11. Great Sphinx of Giza:

On the western bank of the Nile is a sculpture known as the Great Sphinx of Giza, which is 66 feet tall and features a lion's head and a human body. Egypt's earliest known structure is believed to be the Great Sphinx of Giza, which was carved out of limestone.

The Sphinx is still one of the top tourist destinations in Egypt for those interested in history and mythology.

  • Place: Giza Governorate, Egypt, Al Giza Desert
  • Hours are 8 am to 5 pm.

12. Gezira Island:

Gezira Island, which floats on the emerald Nile River and is situated toward the western edge of downtown Cairo, is one of the most lovely locations in Egypt. Both the Gezira and Zamalek districts are found on this island's northern and southern sections, respectively.

Four bridges cross Gezira Island: the 15th of May Bridge, the Al-Galaa Bridge, the 6th of October Bridge, and the Qasr EL Nil Bridge. The name "Jardin des Plantes," which means "Garden of Plants" in English, was given to the island in the 19th century due to the remarkable diversity of imported plants.

13. Tiran Island:

Tiran Island is a magnificent and ethereal area of desert land in the clear Red Sea near Saudi Arabia's sea border.

The island is a well-protected Biosphere since Ras Mohammed National Park covers it. You may experience some of the most incredible underwater natural treasures, including marine life and naturally beautiful coral. It is one of Egypt's most highly regarded destinations, particularly if you enjoy swimming and snorkeling.

14. Ras Muhammad National Park:

One of the most fantastic places to travel in Egypt for diving is Ras Muhammad National Park, the most visited national park in the nation. This park is tucked between the Red Sea's magnificent mangroves, colorful coral reefs, and the internal Sinai Desert.

Divers may see the vibrant corals and vibrant reef fishes in this area's crystal clear waters. White Storks in their countless numbers and Risso's Dolphins live in the Ras Muhammad National Park.

  • Hours are 7 am to 4 pm every day.
  • Entry Fee: 350 INR per person

15. King Tutankhamun Museum:

King Tutankhamun of Egypt is the most well-known in the entire world. In Cairo's Grand Egyptian Museum, also known as the King Tutankhamun Museum, his coffin has been stored in a highly well-preserved condition.

It is one of the most highly recommended destinations to visit in Egypt and is spread across 480,00 square meters of ground just a few kilometers south of the Pyramid of Giza. The King Tutankhamun Museum has one of the most comprehensive histories of ancient Egyptian rule in ancient Egypt.

The monarch's throne has also been reconstructed by archaeologists and preserved in the museum's preservation facility since August 2019.

16. Aqua Blue Water Park:

One of the top vacation spots in Egypt for thrill seekers is Aqua Blue Water Park, which has 44 exciting water slides and a total of 9 massive pools. The Tube Free Fall, Twisters, Black Hole, Rafting Slide, and Kamikaze are some well-known rides that thrill-seekers who visit the park may enjoy.

Aqua Blue Water Park has specific kid-friendly areas, tennis courts, massage rooms, basketball courts, and pool rooms.

  • Time: All days from 10:00 am to 5:00 pm.

17. Old Market of Sharm:

Due to the many lively and fascinating antique shops that line its picturesque streets, Sharm's Old Market is one of the most important sites in Egypt for shopping.

Tourists may enjoy shopping for traditional fabrics, decorations, souvenirs, spices, traditional ahwa (coffee) joints, shisha pipes, and saffron at this vibrant bazaar. After you've finished shopping, enjoy your taste with some regional specialties, including seafood meals and Egyptian barbecue.

  • Location: South Sinai Governorate, Sharm El Sheikh, Egypt
  • Time: It is open daily from 9 am to 11 pm.

18. Karnak Temple:

One of Egypt's most well-known historical tourist destinations is the Karnak Temple, home to several prominent buildings, including the Great Temple of Amun, the Festival Temple of Tuthmosis III, and the Temple of Khons. The famed Konark Open Air Museum is found inside the compound of this well-known temple.

Several ancient towers, chapels, and the remains of ancient temples may also be seen here, which is extremely interesting to visitors. The Karnak Temple is regarded as the second-largest old religious structure in the entire globe.

  • Location: Egypt, Luxor Governorate, El-Karnak
  • Hours are 6 am to 5.30 pm every day.
  • Entrance Fee: 200 EGP for each person as an entry fee

19. Temple of Medinat Habu:

The Temple of Medinat Habu, commonly referred to as the "Mortuary Temple of Ramses III," is one of the most well-liked destinations for religious purposes in Luxor, Egypt. King Ramses III's tomb is in the Valley of the Kings. Still, the Medinet Habu was built in his honor.

The southeast corner of this enormous building provides the most incredible view of the entire complex. The Medinet Habu's primary attractions are the Chapels of the Votaresses, the Sacred Lake, the Second Pylon, the Nilometer, the First Pylon, and the Hypostyle Hall.

  • Location: Egypt, Luxor Governorate, Al Bairat, Al Qarna
  • Hours are 6 am to 5 pm every day.
  • Entrance Fee: The fee of 25 EGP per person

20. Khan-el-Khalili:

Khan-el-Khalili, one of the largest markets in the world, was built in the 14th century and is regarded as one of the most significant locations in Egypt to visit if visitors want to buy. The atmosphere at this colorful open market is quite upbeat and vibrant, with merchants offering a variety of regional goods and customers bargaining aggressively.

The items sold in this region's stores and shops, which are dispersed across a large area, include semi-precious stoneworks, small pyramids, toy camels, jewelry, gold artifacts, stained glass lamps, copperware, antiquities, and perfumes.

  • Location: Cairo Governorate, El-Gamaleya, El Gamaliya, Egypt
  • Hours: 12 am to 12 pm.

21. The Egyptian Museum:

Auguste Mariette created the Egyptian museum in 1857, which was transferred to its present location in 1897, making it one of the fascinating places to visit in Cairo. Tourists will be repeatedly drawn back to this location by the massive volume of exhibitions and collections, and the enormous display of artifacts will astound anyone who enjoys history.

The exhibition, which features almost 120,000 antiquities, shows the magnificent rule of the ancient Egyptians. The Tutankhamun Galleries is one of the most notable marvels. It also features exquisite jewelry, mummies, sarcophagi, ceramics, and, most notably, the treasure of King Tutankhamun.

22. Old Cairo:

Old Cairo gives you a glimpse into Egypt's illustrious past because of its extensive ancient history and countless churches. It is among Cairo's must-see locations, and it is possible to see evidence of the Greco-Roman era at several historical places.

The white and red bricks still show signs of this Roman stronghold. You may visit several tourist attractions, including the Coptic Museum, the Church of the Virgin Mary, the Ben Ezra Synagogue, and many others. You will comprehend Egyptian culture much better due to these structures.

23. Al Azhar Mosque:

The Al-Azhar Mosque, one of several best-preserved even now mosques from the Fatimid era, is located in the center of the Islamic Cairo District. It is among the most spectacular tourist locations in Cairo due to the architectural exhibition and the serene atmosphere. The Barbers' Gate is where the mosque's primary entrance is located.

As you enter the mosque's central courtyard, have a look around. The mosque's five minarets and the El-Taibarsiya Medrese may both be seen from the mosque's central location. You will then encounter the sizable prayer hall.

  • Location: Al-Azhar Street is located in the Islamic Cairo District.
  • Time: 9 am to 5 pm.
  • Fees: The cost per adult is 156.60 Egyptian pounds.

24. The Hanging Church:

The renowned Cairo Hanging Church, often called the "Church of Saint Virgin Mary," features a stunning stone exterior with elaborate inscriptions in Coptic and Arabic languages. The Hanging Church is one of the oldest structures, founded in the third century AD.

The church is known as the "Hanging Church" because that was built on top of the gatehouse of the Babylon Fortress and seems to be swinging in midair.

  • Hours: 8:00 am to 11:00 am.

25. Museum Of Modern Egyptian Art:

The Museum of Modern Egyptian Art is a vital component of Gezira Island's National Cultural Center, sometimes referred to as the Gezira Center for Modern Art. This magnificent museum, which has a remarkable collection of contemporary Egyptian artwork produced throughout the 20th and 21st centuries, is one of the most well-liked tourist destinations in Egypt for art lovers. Additionally, visitors may view magnificent pieces of art by other renowned creators, including Gauguin, Monet, and Van Gogh.

  • Location: Egyptian Governorate of Cairo, Zamalek
  • Hours: Tuesday through Sunday, closed on Monday

26. Statue of Ramesses II:

The statue of Ramesses II, which dates back 3,200 years, shows the emperor standing. Giovanni Battista Caviglia uncovered an 11-meter-tall red granite statue in the Great Temple of Ptah in 1820.

The statue was broken into several pieces, and, over time, it was brought back to its former height using various restoration techniques. The Ramesses II Statue is displayed in the Grand Egyptian Museum on a three-meter pedestal. The Statue of Ramesses II is one of Egypt's most enormous statues and weighs around 7.25 tonnes.

27. Cairo Tower:

One of Egypt's most popular tourist destinations is the 187-meter-tall Cairo Tower, which is placed in Sharia Hadayek Al Zuhreya Gezira. This tall structure, which has a remarkable architectural design, provides fascinating, incredible views of the whole of Cairo city from its top floor.

The tower, constructed between 1954 to 1961, boasts stunning latticework that provides a glimpse into the top-notch Arab quality of earlier times. Currently, Cairo Tower is home to a large number of exclusive restaurants and nightclubs.

  • Location: Cairo, Egypt, is the location.
  • Hours: Every day from 9:00 am until 1:00 am
  • Entry Fee: 70 EGP per person

28. Grand Egyptian Museum:

The Grand Egyptian Museum, often known as the "Giza Museum," is a beautiful archaeological museum created in Giza. Artifacts from ancient Egypt, including King Tutankhamun's, will be kept at this museum, which is regarded as the most significant archaeological museum in the world.

The museum will be erected on a land area of around 480,000 square meters, with architecture resembling a slightly bent triangle.

  • Time: 9 am until 5 pm, Monday through Saturday.
  • Entry fee:Egyptians must pay 60 EGP for entry; students must pay 200 EGP, and others should pay 400 EGP.

29. Pyramid of Khafre:

The pyramid of Khafre, regarded as the second-largest and second-tallest of the three Pyramids of Giza, is home to the tomb of the Fourth-Dynasty pharaoh Khafre. This magnificent pyramid, which has a 215-meter base and a 136-meter height, is covered in Tura limestone.

The interior of the building has fascinating chambers that were carved out of limestone. There are two entrances to the Pyramid of Khafre, one at ground level and the other at 11 meters above.

  • Time: It is open daily from 7 am until 3 pm.

30. Giza Solar Boat Museum:

Following King Cheops's passing, the Giza Solar Boat Museum was created to shelter boats. After being broken and buried, the ship was later discovered and rebuilt.

In his afterlife, it is thought that he utilized this 44-meter-long boat built of cedar wood to go down the Nile.

  • Hours: Monday - Sunday, 8.30 am to 4 pm

31. Red and Bent Pyramid:

The Bent Pyramid, one of the oldest pyramids ever constructed, was designed by Sneferu, a founding figurehead of the Pharaohs. The numerous other pyramids created in the following centuries contrast sharply with the exceptionally distinctively designed pyramid.

This architectural masterpiece, also called the Red Bent Pyramid of Dahshur, features three funeral chambers, the third of which has an extraordinary feature. The progressive overhangs of the red stones on the roof create a highly distinctive pattern that is not present in any other Pyramids.

32. Egypt Papyrus Museum:

The Egypt Papyrus Museum is one of the must-see destinations in Egypt for any visitor looking to purchase some exceptional precious items before departing from the country. It displays and sells impressive works of art created on paper made from the cyperus papyrus plant.

You will be astounded by the superb regional artistry as you glance over the bright hand-painted images carved on the papyrus tree bark.

  • Hours: Every day from 8 am until 6 pm

33. Pyramid of Djoser:

The pyramid of Djoser is one of the less frequented tourist destinations in Egypt and allows you to examine a classic Egyptian pyramid without the regular crowds. The Djoser Pyramid, one of the oldest stone-cut structures on the planet, is located near Saqqara Town, south of Cairo.

This step-style pyramid, which belongs to the 27th century BC, was built before the Pyramids of Giza, which have a smooth-sided construction.

  • Time: Morning 8 to evening 5 Monday through Sunday.

34. Luxor Museum:

Ancient artifacts from the time between the Old Kingdom and the Mamluk period are displayed at the Luxor Museum in an impressive collection. This museum delights your eyes with masterpieces from the Theban necropolis and temples.

A limestone sculpture of Tuthmosis III, an alabaster statue of Amenhotep III, and a carved figure of Tuthmosis III are just a few examples of the Luxor Museum's remarkable collection.

  • Time:Hours are 9 am to 4 pm and 5 pm to 10 night, Monday through Sunday.
  • Entry Fee:For students and adults, the entrance fee is 50 EGP and 100 EGP, respectively.

35. Mortuary Temple of Hatshepsut - Deir el-Bahari:

The stunning Hatshepsut Mortuary Temple may be found on the western side of the Nile, at the base of the Libyan plateau. This limestone temple, which honors Amon-Ra, the sun deity, features a classical style of architecture.

Senimut, a highly skilled architect of the time, built the Hatshepsut Mortuary Temple to honor the remarkable accomplishments of Queen Hatsheput. Three spectacular terraces, courts, a hypostyle hall, and towers are all present in the temple.

  • Time:Hours are 6 am to 5 pm every day.

36. Avenue of Sphinxes:

The Avenue of Sphinxes, which sits between the Karnak Temple complex and the Luxor Temple, used to hold several commercial processions. The Avenue of Sphinxes dates back to 380 BC, is around 2.7 kilometers long, and is lined with 1,350 sphinxes.

The Great Sphinx at Giza has cat-like characteristics and is the most well-known sphinxes. The other sphinxes along this route have rams' heads.

37. Valley of Artisans:

One of the best spots to visit the famous Egyptian tombs in Egypt is the Valley of the Artisans. It is sometimes referred to as Dier el-Medina, elegantly constructed with worker residences and expertly decorated tombs. It is only a short distance from Medinet Habu and the Colossi of Memnon and is placed along the western bank of the River Nile.

Visitors will discover beautifully decorated, vibrantly colored scenes from everyday life. While touring the Valley of Artisans, you may arrange to stop by several tombs nearby, including Shuroy, Roy, Anker Ka, and Sennutem.

38. Colossi of Memnon:

The two majestic sculptures known as the Colossi of Memnon, which once protected the temple of King Amenhotep III, are one of Egypt's most well-known historical tourism destinations. These sculptures of the king are 59 feet tall and weigh over 700 tonnes.

The quartz sandstone sculptures have exquisite carvings depicting King Amenhotep III's mother and wife next to their legs. Even though both sculptures' faces have been damaged, their intimidating stature ensures they continue to be a big draw.

  • Hours are 6 am to 5 pm every day.

39. Temple of Seti:

The Temple of Seti is one of Egypt's least visited tourist destinations and is located on the outskirts of the city of Luxor. This temple is named after Seti I, an Egyptian monarch who started building it but could not finish it.

Despite some current damage to the temple, the lovely sculptures of Seti I, Ramses II, and the gods still adorn its walls.

  • Time: Every day from 7 am to 6 pm is the operating hours.
  • Entrance fee of 78 EGP per person

40. Coptic Orthodox Church of Alexandria:

The Coptic Orthodox Church of Alexandria, an Oriental Orthodox Christian church, was previously home to the Pope of Alexandria, the head of the Coptic Orthodox Church. This church is considered to be where Saint Mark, an apostle, and evangelist, first established a church in the first century. The church features exquisite Coptic engravings together with gorgeous Basilique-style architectural architecture.

  • Time: It is accessible from 6 am to 10 pm. It closes at midnight on Saturday.

41. Bibliotheca Alexandrina - Library in Alexandria:

The Bibliotheca Alexandrina, established in 2002, is an exciting collection of knowledge. One of the most fantastic destinations for readers and students to visit in Egypt is the library of Bibliotheca Alexandrina, which now houses over 1.5 million educational objects.

Millions of valuable books, a planetarium, four museums, and six specialized libraries are all housed in this enormous educational gallery.

  • Time: The services are 10 am to 7 pm from Sunday through Thursday, 2 pm to 7 pm on Friday, and 12 pm to 4 pm on Saturday.
  • Fees: 70 EGP for each person is the entry fee.

42. Citadel of Qaitbay or The Fort of Qaitbay:

Built-in the 15th century, the Citadel of Qaitbay is a strong castle on the Mediterranean Sea coast. The court, erected upon the ruins of the famous Pharos lighthouse created by Mamluk ruler Qaitbey, has thick walls and a robust structure.

The stronghold, converted into a maritime museum in 1952, provides insight into Egypt's rich architecture and history and depicts preserved marine life.

  • Time: 9 am to 4 pm, Monday through Sunday.
  • Fees: 15 EGP for each person is the entry fee.

43. Alexandria National Museum:

The stunning Alexandria National Museum, which is housed on three levels, provides details about the famous past and rich heritage of the Egyptian city of Alexandria. Around 1,800 artifacts that tell the story of Alexandria through the Islamic, Pharaonic, Roman, and Coptic centuries are housed in this museum, which opened in 2003.

Silverware, glass, china, and other antiques from the nineteenth century are priceless and displayed in the museum.

  • The times are Monday through Sunday from 9 am to 4:30 pm.
  • Entry Fee: EGP 35 per person

44. Stanley Bridge in Alexandria:

The stunning Stanley Bridge, constructed over Stanley Bay, is a significant landmark in Alexandria. The bridge has six spans and reaches a maximum length of around 400 meters.

The Stanley Bridge is an engineering masterpiece that was the nation's first to be erected into the ocean.

45. The Blue Hole of Dahab:

The Blue Hole of Dahab, one of Egypt's most adventurous tourist destinations, is not recommended for those who are weak of heart. Some few kilometers north of Dahab, in the southeast of Sina, sits The Blue Hole, the most challenging diving site in the world.

It is difficult to skip out on this experience because of the spectacular underwater and above views of the Red Sea. It is formerly a marine sinkhole with a depth of nearly 328 feet (100 meters), making it a popular location for free diving among tourists looking for adventure and excitement in Egypt. It is a well-known spot for snorkeling and scuba diving if you want to watch corals, reef sharks, and rare turtle species.

46. Mount Sinai:

Mount Sinai is well-known because it was where God gave Moses the ten commandments. This spectacular, rugged mountain offers fantastic hiking options and is one of the top Egypt destinations recommended for thrill seekers.

When the hikers reach the peak, they may take in expansive views of the charming South Sinai mountain range. Additionally, Mount Sinai is renowned for its breathtaking dawn and sunset vistas.

47. Catherine's Monastery:

One of Egypt's earliest religious structures is St. Catherine's Monastery, built between 527 and 565 AD. This monastery, at the foot of Mount Sinai, preserves St. Catherine's name. St. Catherine was murdered and tortured because she believed in Christianity.

This stunning desert monastery is home to an incredible collection of sacred texts, artwork, manuscripts, and the fabled burning bush. After paying your respects to St. Catherine's Monastery, you may also hike Mount Sinai and take in the beautiful views of the sunset or dawn.

  • Hours: Monday through Friday, 9 am to 11.30 pm, closed on Saturday and Sunday

48. Dahab's Eel Garden:

One of the most captivating Egypt tourist destinations is Dahab's Eel Garden, especially for those who enjoy the ocean. Its name is derived from the abundance of garden eel that can be seen on the bottom just a few meters from Assalah Shore's diving entrance point, at a depth of between 10 and 20 meters.

The garden eels at this diving location are a highly uncommon sight and an outstanding beauty you cannot miss. It is the best place to witness many resident barracudas in addition to the eels. Hard and soft corals, ghost pipefish, unusual seagrass, and even enormous coral rocks are all available for your enjoyment.

49. Coloured Canyon:

On the Sinai peninsula, close to the village of Nuweiba, lies a small slot canyon known as the Coloured Canyon. The stunning variety of colors that make up the Coloured Canyon gives it its name and an intriguing and distinctive appearance. Over millions of years, river erosion led to the development of this 800-meter-long canyon.

Sandstone walls that are 40 meters high surround the canyon on each side. With fair-skinned yellow and red to dark brown and black, the walls here have a variety of colors. Iron and magnesium oxides are present because the colors are current.

50. Great Sand Sea in Siwa:

The Great Sand Sea is a vast dune region between western Egypt and eastern Libya. One of the most significant sand fields in the world, the Great Sand Sea spans a vast expanse of over 72,000 square kilometers.

Barchan, Seif, and Crescent dunes, three of the most enormous dunes in the world, may be found here. Visitors may enjoy various activities, including fossil digging, dune driving, and sand sledging, among these stunning dunes.

51. Mit Ghamr Dovecotes:

Dovecotes, a highly distinctive architectural style exclusively seen in Egypt, are located in the Mit Ghamr area of the Nile delta. The primary purpose of these Dovecotes, which are earthen buildings that rise tall and resemble long brownish-red mud chimneys, is to raise pigeons.

If you desire to see something rare and out of the ordinary, it is one of the greatest sites in Egypt to discover. It was done as a portion of urban planning, which is crucial in providing food for the vast majority of people in the nation.

52. Samir Lama Memorial:

The Samir Lama Memorial, honoring the late actor and well-known desert adventurer Samir Lama, is one of the most well-liked tourist attractions in Egypt's southwest. One of the main factors contributing to his popularity in Egypt is the discovery of the Lama- Monod pass, located in southwest Egypt.

Several memorials are dispersed in the center of the Sahara desert's Egyptian section. The memorial also provides a breathtaking perspective of the monuments' location on the Gilf Kebir Plateau.

53. Magic Lake in Fayoum:

The charming Magic Lake in Fayoum's Wadi El Hitan is breathtaking. The lake is referred to as "Magic Lake" due to its capacity to change color frequently throughout the day in response to the amount of sunlight and the time of day.

The Magic Lake, surrounded by a sandy desert, is also said to have essential minerals. Along with providing much-needed relaxation, swimming in this lake can help you with your rheumatism.

54. Fjord Bay:

Fjord Bay is one of the well-liked destinations in Egypt for water sports like swimming and snorkeling. It is situated about 15 kilometers south of Taba, Coral reefs are well-protected, and an amazingly colored subterranean environment surrounds the bay. It's available from 8 am until 4 pm.

55. Fortress of Shali in Siwa Oasis:

The stunning 13th-century mud-brick Fortress of Shali is located in the center of the Siwa region. The once-protective fortification, built of kershef (a combination of clay and salt rock), is now in ruins.

The stronghold was once a four- to five-story structure to protect the area against outside assaults. Today, many people visit the Fortress of Shali to take in the breathtaking sceneries of the Nile river.

56. Philae Island:

The famous temple island Philae Island is located on the tranquil Nile River. Several ancient Egyptian temples and shrines may be found at this important archaeological site, which is situated between the Aswan High Dam and the old Aswan Dam.

Due to its significance as a hub for selling ivory, it is also known by the Greek name "Elephantine." In this little landmass, the ancient Egyptians built the spectacular and well-known Philae island temple complex devoted to the Egyptian goddess Isis. This location has earned a significant place among the UNESCO World Heritage Sites. The complete structure is thought to have developed as early as 600 BC.

57. Mahmya Island:

Mahmya Island, located in Hurghada, is one of Egypt's most fantastic tourist destinations for beachgoers. The spotless sand beaches and the clear sea on this unique island are popular among visitors. While visiting Mahmya, you may discover a wide range of pubs and eateries and the opportunity to take in the spectacular nightlife.

One of the top places for swimming and snorkeling is Mahmya Island, which is highly recognized for both activities. This island, a protected ecological reserve, boasts some of Egypt's most beautiful underwater scenery.

  • Hours: 8 am to 8 pm.

58. Sannur Valley Cave:

The Sannur Valley Cave, located about two hours outside Cairo, is renowned for its fantastic stalactite and stalagmite geological formations. There is also a tonne of quarries, which are older than ancient Egypt.

The cave here covers an area of around 700 meters, is 15 meters wide, and is 15 meters deep. The Eocene limestone allowed groundwater to percolate through it, creating the cave.

  • Timings: Always open.
