What is the full form of TET

TET: Teacher Eligibility Test

TET stands for Teacher Eligibility Test. Teachers play an essential role in our society. They help us succeed and develop into responsible members of society. Thanks to the teachers who help us prepare for challenging and difficult times. By receiving education when we are young, we set the stage for the rest of our life. Teachers are indeed one of the essential components who shape a person's future. The government has acquired several certified educators in this regard by passing various exams. One of these is the Teacher Eligibility Test, functioning successfully since 2009. (TET).

The Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET), is also referred to as the Teacher Eligibility Test (TET). It is the prerequisite for prospective teachers to continue a career in education. It is a requirement for a candidate to be qualified to serve as a teacher for classes ranging from first grade to eighth grade. With this certification, candidates can apply for teaching positions, particularly in Indian Government institutions.

The Teacher Eligibility Test, or TET, is the absolute minimum criterion in India to be eligible for the appointment as a teacher for Classes I through VIII. The test fulfills the Education Act. In Indian government schools, the test is required for teachers.

Similarly, Super TET is a new qualifying exam that the UP government introduced following UPTET. India's state and federal governments both administer the TET. Most states carry out their own TETs. There is no expiration date for the TET certificate.

Purpose of TET

The Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) is the minimum qualification in India to apply for a class I-8 teacher position. The purpose of the test is to advance the objectives of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education, a 2009 initiative by the Indian government.

History

The Indian government instituted the Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) to raise teaching standards in 2011. Teachers already employed were anticipated to complete the exam in two years.

The Test

The National Curriculum Framework for Teacher Education of the Indian government serves as the basis for the test that new teachers for grades I through VIII must pass.

The B.T.C. (D.El.Ed), B.Ed, and B.El.Ed is an example of professional teaching credentials they can use to take the test. To pass, candidates must receive more than 60%. CBSE oversees the test administration and manages the TET records.

Central Teacher Eligibility Test

Notifications dated August 23, 2010, and July 29, 2011, were delivered to the National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE) following the sub-section of Section 23 of the R.T.E. Act, outlining the minimal conditions for eligibility to teach Classes I through VIII. This is directed to the structure of the CTET. An essential condition for employment as a teacher in any institution referred to in clause (n) of the Education Act is passing the TET, which is administered following NCTE Guidelines (R.T.E. Act). 2022 Teachers.

The Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET), conducted twice a year by the CBSE, Delhi, is administered by the national Ministry of Human Resource Development. There are twenty Indian languages used for CTET. A total of 14 lakh applicants submit applications to take the test. On December 8, 2019, 110 Indian cities hosted the thirteenth round of CTET.

To qualify for the CTET, candidates must receive a minimum score of 60%. 1-14% of candidates who took the examinations to date qualified, and the certificate is valid for life. The National Capital Territories, in addition to other schools managed by the union territories (U.T.s) of Chandigarh, Dadra and Nagar Haveli, Daman and Diu, and the Andaman and Nicobar Islands are some of the institutions where qualified candidates may apply for employment as teachers of Classes I to VIII (N.C.T.). The exam is complicated as the qualifying rate is 1-14% in the exams held.

To achieve the following goals, the TET was declared a requirement for hiring a teacher:

  • To develop a nationwide standard for teacher recruitment and quality.
  • To promote the continued advancement of student and institution performance standards in teacher preparation programs.
  • To convey the government's emphasis on teacher quality in particular.

State-wise TET

The weighting for the District Selection Committee (D.S.C.) teacher selection entrance exam for Andhra Pradesh and Telangana is 20%. The 2012 test attracted almost 4 lakh applicants. The Andhra Pradesh TET exam was administered in numerous test centers in February 2019 to thousands of applicants.

Uttar Pradesh

The TET exams (UPTET) are administered by the Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Board once a year to choose primary and junior teachers. Two papers make up the UPTET written exam, and either candidate may take both papers.

On November 28, 2021, the UPTET 2021 exam was administered by UPBEB in 75 districts throughout Uttar Pradesh in two shifts. But later, the exam got postponed because of paper loss. The second exam took place on January 23, 2022, and the results were announced on April 9, 2022. In 2016, there were two shifts on December 19 for the UPTET written exam. In 2017, the written test was held on October 15 after UPBEB published its examination announcement on August 22.

The UPBEB notification was published in 2018 on September 15, with applications opening up on September 17 and the written test taking place on October 28.

Rajasthan

The Rajasthan Board of Secondary Education conducts the RTET, or Rajasthan Teacher Eligibility Test, for prospective teachers in the state of Rajasthan (RBSE).

Two times a year, Level 1 and Level 2 Teacher Eligibility Tests are administered.

Candidates must answer one hundred fifty questions in two hours and thirty minutes. Five sections comprise the Level 1 examination paper, and the Level 2 examination paper comprises four sections.

Haryana

The Board of School Education Haryana (BSEH) periodically administers the Haryana Teacher Eligibility Test (HTET) for the Primary (P.R.T.), Trained Graduate Teacher (T.G.T.), and Post Graduate Teacher categories of school personnel (P.G.T.).

Until 2009, the examination was known as the State Teacher Eligibility Test (STET). It was renamed the HTET in 2011 as per the R.T.E. Act, 2009, following the NCTE's notice.

Three categories make up the examination:

Level I: For teaching Classes I-V(P.R.T)

Level II: For teaching Classes Six through Eight (T.G.T.)

Level III: For teaching graduate-level courses (P.G.T.)

The HTET qualifying certification is accurate for seven years from the examination date.

There is no cap on the number of times a candidate may take the exam, and those who have already earned the HTET/STET qualification are eligible to retake it to increase their score.

Punjab

PSTET, also known as the Punjab State Teacher Eligibility Test, is administered by the Department of School Education, Punjab. For applicants seeking to become teachers with the Punjab School Education Board, the PSTET is divided into two separate exams:

Paper I: for hiring teachers from Grades 1 through 5.

Paper II: for hiring teachers for classes sixth through eighteen.

Through the Punjab School Education Board's official website, qualified individuals submit applications to take the PSTET.

Maharashtra

A teacher eligibility test (TET) at the state level is administered by the Maharashtra TET (MAHATET). Teachers interested in teaching classes 1 to 8 in Maharashtra schools must pass the MAHA TET, commonly known as the Maharashtra TET (Teachers Eligibility Test), administered by the Maharashtra State Council of Examinations (MSCE).

The MAHA TET test has two levels: primary (paper 1) and upper primary (paper 2). Candidates must adhere to the MAHA TET paper 1 if they wish to teach classes from 1 to 5. Candidates who want to teach grades from 6 to 8 must appear for MAHA TET exam paper 2. Candidates must appear for Papers 1 and 2 if they intend to teach classes from 1 to 8.

How do I apply to take the TET?

1) The individual must go to the TET's official website.

2) A registration link will then appear on your desktop. You'll be taken to the page containing your personal information if you click there.

3) After carefully reviewing the data and filling out the personal information page, you can submit your MAHA TET registration form.

4) The final step is to log in using the registered username and password you created in the previous step.

5) Complete the application that is placed in front of you.

6) Upload all the files essential for successful submission.

7) Most importantly, remember to save the form before submitting it.

Still, it does need that you have at least a basic understanding of the disciplines for a qualified instructor to provide the foundation for the future of Indian residents.
