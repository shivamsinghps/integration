30 Best Tourist Places in Bangkok

Brief about Bangkok

Bangkok has everything you expect from the Thai capital, as it is crowded, congested, colourful, intriguing, infuriating, and stunning.

Everyone immediately catches the attention of Bangkok's alluring nightlife and famous Thai massages when the city's name is spoken. Bangkok, Thailand's capital, is not just known for this. Bangkok is one such city that attracts visitors from all over the world. Due to its tourist attractions, this city is also among the most visited worldwide. Bangkok is undoubtedly on the wish list of young people who desire to travel. In this segment, we will notify you about some of the most well-known sites to visit in Bangkok if you intend to travel to Bangkok.

1. The Grand Palace in Bangkok

This should be your top historical tourist site in Bangkok. The royal compound lives up to its name, with spectacular structures that would disgrace even the wealthiest modern rulers. The majestic palace, built in 1782 and still used for important events and entertaining heads of state, served as the royal residence for numerous generations. You should dress modestly when visiting the Grand Palace, covering your arms and legs and avoiding loose apparel.

To minimize difficulties and make the most of your trip, take the Grand Palace and Wat Phra Kaew Tour. This half-day sightseeing tour includes transportation from your hotel and a local guide who will explain the significance of the locations you see. With a guide, it's more accessible to grasp the importance of what you are witnessing.

2. Wat Pho in Bangkok

If your legs are ready for additional walking, adding a visit to Wat Pho, situated just south of the Grand Palace area, to your palace tour is a great idea. The temple is the oldest in Bangkok and was constructed by King Rama I. It has a long history of being revered as a site of healing and was formerly renowned for its pharmacy and being Thailand's first "university"?both of which were founded by King Rama III. The traditional medical school on the site provides Thai and foot massages, though the prices are far higher than those in other city sections.

3. Lumbini Park in Bangkok

The most well-known park in Bangkok is Lumbini Park, which covers an area of 142 acres. It offers boating access to an artificial lake where you can rent a boat and enjoy it, as well as walking opportunities. You can enjoy this place, and a 2- to 2.5-kilometre stroll is nearby. Walking here in the morning and evening will make your walk more enjoyable. You may also go riding here.

4. Pattaya city in Bangkok

Bangkok's Pattaya City became well-known in the early 1980s, and every year since then, millions of visitors have travelled there. Fun has long been a signature of Pattaya City. The beaches of Pattaya are a never-ending celebration of life from dawn to sunset since water sports enthusiasts may participate in their favourite underwater activities. People rush to the city's supercharged nightlife scene when it darkens, where partying and drinking go on until the sun rises. Pattaya offers something for everyone, whether travelling as a couple, a family, or on business. Pattaya is adjacent to Thailand's capital's major beach resorts and is only 147 kilometres from Bangkok.

5. Siam Paragon in Bangkok

Siam Paragon, one of the important shopping centres in all of Thailand, is a vast space with various sights and activities besides shopping. The complex is home to an art gallery, an aquarium that displays Thai art, and an opera concert theatre that hosts some spectacular events in addition to its never-ending selection of stores and eateries. Siam Paragon is the best blend of culture, food, and entertainment since it has karaoke, bowling, and 15 massive cinema screens.

6. Wat Suthat in Bangkok

Wat Suhat, one of only ten first-class royal temples in Bangkok, is so enormous and grand that it must be seen to be appreciated. The biggest in the nation, it has an elaborate ordination hall. The temple is massive in every way. Its bottom base is lined with 28 Chinese pagodas, whose exquisite carvings and embellishments perfectly display extraordinary Thai craftsmanship. The interior is also stunning, with unique paintings illuminating the Buddha's life. A significant historical and religious item is the bronze Buddha statue Phra Sri Sakyamuni, which stands eight meters tall.

7. Terminal 21 in Bangkok

Terminal 21 offers the desire to see the world to downtown Bangkok with its cutting-edge concept that combines numerous world-famous destinations under one roof?wandering around a maze of stores in Tokyo, strolling along London's Carnaby Street, snagging a bargain in an Istanbul zouk, or seeking something to feed your grumbling belly at San Francisco's Fisherman's Wharf.

Terminal 21, housed in the same building as Grande Centre Point Sukhumvit, features a curved glass and steel façade that mimics a contemporary airport, and the motif continues after you enter. The Terminal 21 retail experience includes floor signs that indicate arrivals in specific locations, a crisscrossing network of escalators, electronic display boards that look like flight information screens at airport terminals, and information desk receptionists dressed in air hostesses' outfits.

8. Wat Mahathat in Bangkok

Wat Mahathat, the headquarters of Thailand's most excellent monastic order and a Vipassana Meditation centre, is an important hub for studying Buddhism and meditation. The temple was initially constructed to store a Buddha relic and is one of Bangkok's oldest temples. Although most of the events are in Thai, some are in English, and the temple has become a popular location for learning Vipassana Meditation (Insight Meditation).

You may even get your fortune told while inside the 'wat' (temple). Every Sunday, immediately next to the temple, is Bangkok's largest amulet market, where religious amulets, charms, talismans, and traditional medicine are strewn out on the ground for consumers to view in the hopes of finding one that will bring them good luck or ward off evil.

9. Assumption Cathedral in Bangkok

The Assumption Cathedral, which is more than 200 years old, is one of Bangkok's most famous tourist attractions, and it is the epicentre of Catholicism in Thailand. This lovely church is located off Charoen Krung Road near the Chao Phraya River and is well worth a visit.

Since its construction, the Assumption Cathedral has been Thailand's most prominent and famous Catholic cathedral. In 1984, Pope John Paul II visited this place, confirming villagers' faith in this sacred sanctuary.

10. Wat Intharawihan in Bangkok

Wat Intharawihan, on the northern outskirts of Banglamphu, is defined by a 32 metrehigh standing Buddha. The building of the Luangpor Toh, a brick and stucco figure, began in 1867 during the reign of King Rama IV. It took more than 60 years to complete and is the highest structure in the world, adorned with glass mosaics and 24-karat gold. The Ubosot was erected at the end of the Ayutthaya period and contained several remarkable Buddha figures, raised frescoes on the walls, and beautifully gilded window shutters.

11. Princess Mother Memorial Park in Bangkok

There are many places to visit in Thailand, but this memorial park offers a tranquil setting in a verdant garden and insight into the life and works of Thailand's renowned Princess Mother. In two exhibit halls, you may learn about her life and work.

The park includes a life-size replica of the home where the Princess Mother was born and resided, located in a beautifully designed garden. The Princess Mother died in 1995, just two years after the memorial park was completed, and photographs from her royal cremation in 1996 are also on display.

12. Erawan Temple in Bangkok

Erawan Temple in Bangkok, which is not Buddhist, gets more tourists than many of the city's sanctuaries. It was built in the mid1950s after the Thai government decided to make the luxurious Erawan Hotel in this neighbourhood.

A magnificent figure of the Brahma God was specifically cast and plated, and The Erawan Hotel opened to worldwide renown and appreciation for three decades. In the end, the building couldn't compete with more modern offices and was replaced in 1991 by the luxurious Grand Hyatt Erawan Bangkok. Because the temple was designed to mirror the grandeur of the ancient Erawan Hotel, the location became known as the Erawan Temple.

13. Lumphini Park in Bangkok

Lumpini Park is an inner-city oasis of peace, fresh air, and shade, providing inhabitants with the ideal link to nature. The park is named after Lord Buddha's birthplace in Nepal and is home to various plants and animals.

Lumpini Park has evolved into an endearing centre for outdoor leisure activities for people over the years. It is one of the most prominent tourist sites in Bangkok.

Lumpini Park never fails to impress when it comes to food, with various street vendors dishing up classic Thai street meals like Pad Thai and others. Rowing, paddleboats, and a fun outdoor gym are among the colourful pastimes available in the park. The park is home to increasingly rare indigenous plants, a forest park, and some unusual residents, such as water monitor lizards.

14. Thai Elephant Museum in Bangkok

The elephant is still a national symbol and a highly adored animal in Thailand. A white elephant, according to tradition, should belong to the monarch and become their sole possession. The white elephant is an albino Siamese elephant that is considered a symbol of dignity and luxury. It may be viewed on the grounds of the Dusit Palace. Originally utilized as the royal elephants' stables, the structure has become a museum.

The first building will teach tourists about the history of royal elephants and how the animals are classified. The first building also has several antiques, pictures, and figurines. The second structure houses a replica of a white elephant dressed in full royal regalia and numerous antiquities. Another attraction of the museum is the magnificent collection of elephant tusks, the largest of which is claimed to be 290 cm long and previously belonged to the lucky

15. MBK Shopping Centre in Bangkok

The MBK shopping centre is one of Bangkok's most well-known shopping centres. Not only tourists but the majority of locals prefer to run errands at the mall. The mall has eight floors and approximately 2,000 stores selling everything from clothing, handbags, leather products, and accessories to electronics, mobile phones, and furniture.

The mall also has some delectable restaurants serving authentic Thai cuisine and cuisines worldwide in its food court.

16. Chao Phraya River in Bangkok

Every Bangkok tourist attraction list is complete if the famed 'Chao Phraya River is mentioned. This river, which runs south to the Gulf of Thailand, will give you several experiences. While the fantastic dinner cruises and enjoyable boat tours are a few reasons to visit, its magnificent grandeur against the backdrop of stunning architecture makes visits here even more satisfying, making it one of the best Bangkok tourist spots. Visiting here from December to February is excellent because winter in Bangkok, particularly in this region, is admirable.

17. Safari World in Bangkok

Safari World is a significant tourist attraction in Bangkok, with visitors and locals visiting the park to spend time with the animals. It is not only a place to learn about the habits and lifestyles of wild animals, but it also hosts various exciting events and activities.

Safari World debuted in 1988 as a zoo, with ambitions to expand to add a bird park. The following year, however, it grew into an amusement park by offering a variety of exciting activities within the open zoo. Soon later, a marine park with various underwater species was added. Safari World in Bangkok is a renowned tourist attraction in Thailand where visitors can get up close and personal with exotic species from all over the world. It is a zoo with a difference, offering a range of entertainment options such as feeding and caring for the animals.

18. Sea Life in Bangkok

Sea Life Bangkok is an underwater aquarium beneath Bangkok's Siam Paragon mall. Sea Life Bangkok is Southeast Asia's largest aquarium, spanning 110,000 square feet. The aquarium houses various marine species rescued from the world's most isolated locations, including crabs, octopuses, penguins, sharks, and fish.

Sea Life Bangkok has themed parts, including Rocky Hideout, Shark Walk, Coral Reef, and the Seahorse Kingdom, where visitors can see various aquatic animals. Through its Aquatic Quarantine and Nursery Centre, the aquarium also provides the option to participate in a live ocean experience.

19. Art in Paradise in Bangkok

Art in Paradise is an interactive 3D art museum in Bangkok's Esplanade mall. Shin Jae Yol created it, and it features numerous rooms packed with excellent artwork by Korean and Thai artists. The primary goal of this cultural sanctuary was to provide the general public with the opportunity to experience 3D and illusion art.

This popular interactive attraction initially debuted in Pattaya, Thailand, and subsequently expanded to Bangkok, where it was a smashing hit. Art in Paradise, often known as the Trickeye Museum or the 3D Museum by locals, is a delightful spot to escape Bangkok's heat and glare or seek cover from the rain. This two-story gallery is filled with brilliantly done 3D paintings that visitors may participate in by taking pictures with 3D illusion backgrounds.

20. Chinatown (Yaowarat) in Bangkok

Yaowarat, Bangkok's Chinatown, hosts one of the most exciting Chinese New Year events. This time of year, families of Chinese heritage congregate to enjoy street activities and banquets. Crowds of followers, bursting firecrackers, and dragon dancers bring the entire street to life.

In other areas of Bangkok, Chinese restaurants and retail centres have unique Chinese New Year celebrations, which include anything from deals to free cultural performances. The Chinese Lunar Calendar is used to compute the exact dates for this yearly event, which generally takes place in January or February.

21. Sky Bar in Bangkok

Sky Bar provides panoramic views from the top of State Tower, one of Bangkok's tallest skyscrapers. The bar, with its conspicuous golden dome, is tucked away in a nook of the award-winning, open-air Sirocco restaurant on the 64th floor. The Sky Bar counter is lit and changes colour every few minutes.

Sky Bar is home to some of Bangkok's best live jazz. The extract is a DJ bar, smoking lounge, and restaurant that serves exquisite single-malt Scotches like Macallan and Glenmorangie with a tiny carafe of authentic Scottish water. Another popular hangout is Distill, where everyone wants to be seen at the Pyramid.

22. Asiatique: The Riverfront in Bangkok

Asiatique: The Riverfront is a brilliant blend of Bangkok's most popular shopping experiences: a night market and a mall. Once a major international trading port, it has become a massive replica warehouse complex with over 1,500 shops and 40 restaurants. The Saphan Taksin BTS Skytrain Station is about a 10-minute walk away.

An evening spent here is ideal for perusing the stores and purchasing gifts or something for you. The Calypso ladyboy cabaret, a Muay Thai show, and a vintage Thai puppet show are all performed nightly.

23. Vertigo at Banyan Tree in Bangkok

Vertigo at Banyan Tree is a 61-story-high open-air bar and restaurant positioned 61 stories above Bangkok's congested streets. Indulge in some of the world's most extraordinary champagne, exciting cocktails, wine-by-the-glass, or non-alcoholic drinks in the bar area, or go for a fantastic seafood feast.

The excellent environment is stylish and romantic, making for an unforgettable evening. A telescope is accessible for stargazing, and live jazz is performed most nights.

24. Sathorn Unique Tower in Bangkok

The Sathorn Unique Tower, with its 49 stories (including two dungeon-dark basement levels), is one of Bangkok's skyscraper constructions that was never completed due to the Asian Financial Crisis of 1997. As you scale the Sathorn Unique Towers one by one, the vistas of the city get more spectacular. But be cautious because there have been reports that the location is "Haunted"!

Frequent reports of persons being discovered dead on various building floors have only added labels like "paranormal" and "spooky" to the structure's popularity. As a result, whether you are an adventurer who enjoys exploring such locations, or someone who enjoys reading and seeking ghosts, the Sathorn Unique Tower is the place to be.

25. Madame Tussauds in Bangkok

Madame Tussauds Bangkok Wax Museum is unlike any other Wax Museum in Thailand. Anyone from Albert Einstein to Tom Cruise, Barack Obama to Tiger Woods may be touched here. However, you are not permitted to touch any figures in the most ornamented chamber, which houses the Thai royal family. The quality of the 70 wax figurines will take your breath away.

This museum not only displays wax reproductions of world-famous personalities in 8 themed parts, but you can also discover how these sculptures are manufactured. You may also learn about Madame Tussaud, who made her first wax sculpture of French writer Voltaire in 1777. So, pose with your favourite celebrities and historical figures, learn more about its past, and even escape the Bangkok heat! You may conclude your tour with a one-of-a-kind 4D viewing of an Ice Age short film.

26. Wat Traimit in Bangkok

Wat Traimit, located near Hualampong Railway Station at the end of Chinatown's Yaowarat Road, is the ideal location to observe the brilliant gold Buddha statue. It is one of Bangkok's most influential royal temples and is famous for housing the pure gold figure of Lord Buddha. It is known as 'The Temple of the Golden Buddha' because it holds the world's giant gold statue, is roughly 5 meters tall, and weighs 5.5 tonnes.

This 13th-century temple holds the famed Golden Buddha statue of Wat Traimit, constructed in beautiful Sukhothai style and discovered by chance or accident around 40 years ago. The figurine was dumped off in the disposal while hidden within the stucco/plaster exterior. However, the idol's origin still needs to be discovered, presuming it was created during the Sukhothai or Ayutthaya periods when the Burmese attacked the city.

27. Wat Chana Songkhram in Bangkok

Wat Chang Songkhram, formerly known as Wat Ratchaburana, contains one of Sukhothai's biggest Sri Lankan-style bell-shaped chedis. Around the main chedi, other lesser chedis get smaller as they get closer to the summit. You may stroll a little north of the temple to see the King Ramkamhaeng statue, which is popular among locals.

Wat Chana Songkram was formerly known as 'Wat Klang Na,' which translates to "temple in the centre of paddy fields." After the Thai defeated the Burmese three times between 1785 and 1787, it was ultimately christened Wat Chana Songkram, which means "victory of the battle temple." It is free to visit every day.

28. Rot Fai Park in Bangkok

The Rot Fai Park is one of Bangkok's largest parks and was once a golf course. With a few modest hills and rich green trees, this attractive park is ideal for getting away from the city crowds. The two-kilometre cycling track is available for use, and other sports like tennis, swimming, basketball, and kayaking are available.

Rot Fai Park in Bangkok is a large 150-acre park filled with beautiful greenery, fascinating flora, and wildlife. It is a hidden jewel among the concrete jungle of Bangkok and is ideal for getting away from the city's hustle and bustle. The State Railway formerly utilized the property as a golf course. Nonetheless, it was later transformed into this lovely park, complete with cycling paths, picnic areas, a butterfly park, a Buddha Garden, a swimming pool, a boating lake, a children's golf course, a student camping site, a botanical garden, and a variety of fun and exciting activities such as tennis, basketball, petanque, sepak takraw, kayaking, and so on. The park also keeps historic steam engines and carriages as heritage items.

29. Flow House in Bangkok

The Flow House Bangkok, Bangkok's only urban beach club, gives you an excuse to skip the long road trips to the countryside to surf the sea waves and enjoy the surfing experience at its indoor sea wave simulator ride. Flow House, located in the middle of Bangkok, is an ideal tourist destination that combines the Californian beach lifestyle with Thai culture and food.

Aside from family gatherings, Flow House Bangkok provides a setting for aspiring surfers who want to practice surfing in a comparatively safer environment before hitting the actual waves. After you've finished battling the artificial waves on the ride and taking some fantastic pictures, you can fill your tummies with food at the dining and bar area before leaving with adventurous and unforgettable memories.

30. H Gallery in Bangkok

H Gallery, located in Bangkok's Bang Rak District, is a renowned centre for modern regional and international art. The gallery, which opened in 2002, is housed in a charming and exquisite 125-year-old timber Anglo-Thai home. H Gallery specializes in developing modern Asian art and has hosted numerous controversial exhibitions. It displays a variety of beautiful treasures, such as photography, paintings, textiles, and sculptures.

The gallery has included works by local and international artists such as Mit Jai Inn, Sopheap Pich, and Michael Lee and is curated by Brian Curtin, an Irish-born art critic. The H Space on the building's second level is dedicated to experimental installations by artists.

How to Reach Bangkok From India

Tickets to this Southeast Asian city range in price from 8,000 to 30,000. Flights to Bangkok from India are the cheapest from Kolkata or Chennai. Aside from that, regular and direct flights to Bangkok are available from other major cities like Bangalore, Delhi, Hyderabad, and Mumbai. However, it also depends on the airport in India and the flight. Bangkok's main airports are Don Mueang International Airport and Suvarnabhumi Airport. Before travelling to Bangkok, you should obtain information on the ticket charge from each airport since there is a seasonal discount on the ticket to Bangkok.

Best Time To Visit Bangkok

Bangkok is located in a tropical zone and features a tropical monsoon climate with three seasons: hot, wet, and cold. Although Bangkok is a year-round destination, the ideal months to visit are November through February.

The hot season lasts from March to June, the rainy season lasts from July to October, and the cold lasts from November to February. The wettest months are September and October, whereas the warmest months are April through June.

Famous food in Bangkok

Pad Thai is a stir-fried rice noodle dish with a significant component of the chicken, shrimp, or tofu. Rice noodles are stir-fried with your protein of choice (tofu, beef, or eggs). On the side, there are garlic chives, red chilli pepper, chopped toasted peanuts, turnips, and lime. It is a uniquely Thai dish that you must eat at least once.
