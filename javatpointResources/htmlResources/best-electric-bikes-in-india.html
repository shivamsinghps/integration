Best Electric Bikes in India

You get out of bed and take a stroll to reenergize yourself for the day. As you leave your home and breathe in the crisp morning air, all you can smell is the dust and fumes spewing out the passing cars. It seems familiar. The numerous vehicles that travel the roads cause great harm to our environment by blatantly releasing poisonous gases into the atmosphere. A new two-wheeler called an electric bike entered the market to lower pollution levels. These plug-in electric bikes are of a newer generation and offer an affordable and practical means of transportation.

Advantages of Electric Bike in India

Rechargeable: Electric bikes and motorbikes run on nickel-metal hydride or lithium-ion batteries. The batteries can be easily charged because they can be plugged into any wall socket. They require eight hours on average to fully recharge.

Low Maintenance: Compared to regular bikes, electric bikes require less maintenance. An electric bike spares you the hassle of maintaining ordinary bikes, which require routine inspection, lubrication, and adjustment. But you must still be mindful of the fluid flush, brake pads, and tyres.

Low Fuel Cost: Low fuel costs are another benefit of electric bikes. Twenty minutes of driving consumes about 250 watts of power. Electric motorcycles are relatively reasonable because the power price is around 0Rs. 5 per unit. Besides, the petrol prices are getting high day by day.

Does not add to the Noise: Indian roadways are constantly loud. The petrol-diesel bikes add to the noise as their engines make so much noise. However, electric bikes cruise across the city streets without making noises by their engines. Some bikes now incorporate a gadget that generates artificial noise to alert commuters to an oncoming car.

With the sharp increase in gas prices, a bigger population is gradually shifting to budget-friendly purchases of electric bikes. Many homes now own electric bikes since they are inexpensive, need little maintenance, and are fuel-efficient. Increased adoption of these vehicles in place of those that run on diesel and gasoline will reduce air pollution greatly. Therefore, it is our responsibility to shift to electric bikes (or other electric vehicles) and prod others to do the same.

Best Electric Bike in India

Not every bike on the market has the same attributes. From one manufacturer to the next, range, speed, and price features might differ. Companies like Hero, Oben, and Revolt offer the best electric bikes in India. We provide you in this post with the specifications of the top five electric bikes in India.

1. Revolt RV 400

The starting price for the Revolt RV 400 electric street bike in India is around Rs. 98,485. There are 2 variations and 3 colours available, with the highest variant costing around Rs. 1,15,038. The motor of the Revolt RV 400 produces 3000 W of power. Revolt RV 400 creates a combined braking system for each wheel with front and rear disc brakes.

The Revolt RV400 is the first electric motorbike in the nation with AI capabilities. Along with a fairing that protects parts like the motor and battery, it gets a muscular storage tank/ compartment. It has a one-piece seat and a bolt-on sub frame at the back. A single disc and CBS brakes are present in the vehicle's both ends. A mono-shock in the back and inverted forks up front are responsible for the suspension.

A 3KWh motor that generates 5kW of power and 50Nm of torque powers this electric motorbike. A 3kW lithium-ion battery is used in it. The bike has three riding modes: Eco, Normal, and Sport, and the travelling range vary depending on which mode it is used. The bike can go 90 kilometers in the Sport mode, 85 kilometers in the City mode, and 120-kilometre in the Eco mode when rode at a constant speed of 65 km/h. The RV400 has a range of 156km while travelling in Eco mode, which allows the bike to go at a speed of 45kmph.

Regarding features, the RV400 has a full-digital instrument cluster with a full-LED headlight, tail lamp, and turn indication illumination. Additionally, it receives an app that can be used to start the motorbike, create a geofence, and receive alerts when the battery is running low. The company also provides a "Delivery on Demand" service that enables users to get a bike with fully charged batteries delivered to a specified location. The Revolt provides the RV400 with an onboard charger and a portable battery charger and will soon erect Switch Stations, which are locations where batteries may be swapped out. The best thing in this e-bike is the exhaust noise control, which enable users to turn on/ off the AI-based sound via the respective app.

Black and a dual-tone black/red paint job are the two color options that Revolt provides for the motorbike.

2. Tork Kratos

For a starting price of around Rs. 1,22,582, Indian customers can purchase the Tork Kratos electric street bike. The premium model begins at Rs. 1,37,499. It is offered in 2 configurations and 4 colours. The motor on Tork Kratos produces a 4000 W power output. Tork Kratos is equipped with a combined braking system for both wheels using both front and rear disc brakes. To compete with models like the Revolt RV400, Tork Motors introduced the new Kratos electric motorbike in India. Two versions of the new Tork Kratos-Standard and R-will be distributed progressively throughout India. The following cities are expected to be the first to receive an electric motorcycle for sale: Pune, Hyderabad, Bangalore, Chennai, Ahmedabad, and Delhi.

It has a bare-bones roadster style that is complemented with a headlamp with a triangle form, a split-type seat, and two-piece grip rails for the pillion. White is the only colour option for the standard model, but the R variation has four colour options: white, blue, red, and black. The electric motor in the standard model has a maximum torque of 28Nm and a peak power output of 7.5kW, or 10.05bhp. The R model, on the other hand, produces a peak torque of 38Nm and a significantly greater power output of 9kW, or 12.06 horsepower. The standard model's acceleration ranges from 0 to 40 kilometres per hour that takes four seconds, and its top speed is 100 kilometres per hour. The R version has a 105 kmph peak speed and a 3.5 second 0 to 40 mph acceleration time.

Both variations of Tork Kratos are IP67-certified, come with the lithium-ion battery, and are advertised as having an IDC (Indian Driving Cycle) range of 180 kilometres. The manufacturer states a real-world range of 120km per charge for both motorcycles. The R model also benefits from quick charging, and according to Tork Motors, recharging from 0% to 80% may be completed in one hour. Numerous riding modes, regenerative braking, reverse mode, mobile connectivity, USB charging, anti-theft, a front storage box, and OTA updates are some of the features that come as standard on both models. Other standard features include:

  • Full LED lighting.
  • An entirely digital instrument cluster.

Fast charging, two years of free use of the charging network, geofencing, locating my car, motor walk assist, accident alert, track mode, and smart charge analysis are additional advantages of the premium R model.

The battery pack is a strained element of the steel trellis frame that supports the motorbike. Telescopic front forks and a rear mono-shock are in charge of the suspension. Single discs are the anchor points on both ends, and CBS technology serves as the safety net.

3. Odysse Evoqis

The Odysse Evoqis electric commuter bike starts at around Rs. 1,58,471 in India. There is just 1 variant and 4 colours available. Odysse Evoqis' motor generates 3000 W of electricity. Odyssey Evoqis develops disc brakes for the front and rear. The two-wheeler company's Odysse Evoqis is a fully-featured electric motorbike. The motorcycle's peak speed is listed at 80 kmph, which is said to go from 0 to 50 kmph in 4.2 seconds. There are two degrees of speed available in assisted bike mode. It has a 170kg maximum load rating. According to the company, a bike may go over 100 kilometres with a single charge. It takes around five hours to fully recharge the battery.

The aluminium frame supports the electric motorcycle's mono-shock and telescopic forks. The Evoqis has a single disc at the back and two rotors up front for its braking system. It is mounted on alloy wheels with tubeless tyres measuring 17 inches. Its appealing design and clip-on handlebars emphasise its sporty image. A twin-pod headlamp, front turn indicators built into the fairing, and split-style seats are among the features included with the motorbike. The Evoqis has a music system, keyless entry, anti-theft lock, and complete LED illumination. Even shorter riders should be able to use it because the seat height is arranged at 750mm height.

The electric motorcycle is available in one shape and four colours: Fire Red, Lime Green, Magna Silver, and Candy Blue.

4. Oben Rorr

Oben Rorr electric bike has been introduced in India for about Rs 99,999 ex-showroom Maharashtra. This motorbike, which competes with the Tork Kratos, is the first produced by the Bengaluru-based business. The business also intends to introduce a new electric two-wheeler every six months over the following two years. Priced at Rs 99,999, ex-showroom Maharashtra, the Oben Rorr includes state discounts and FAME II. There is just one model of the electric motorbike.

The Rorr boasts a digital instrument cluster with a smartphone connection and an all-LED lighting system from Oben Electric. Users of the latter may have access to features like roadside assistance, geo-tagging, on-demand service, predictive maintenance, ride information, battery condition, and geo-fencing. CBS is a regular feature on the e-bike as well. A 10kW motor installed on the frame and connected to a chain drive powers this electric bike. It claims a peak speed of 100 kmph and an acceleration time of 3 seconds from 0 to 40 kmph. Eco, City, and Havoc are the three available modes. The City and Eco modes provide 120km and 150km of range, respectively, while the stated genuine range for Havoc is 100km. A 4.4kWh lithium-ion battery pack with a 200km IDC (Ideal Driving Conditions) range is what the manufacturer describes as the battery pack. Using a 15A socket requires 2 hours to charge this electric bike completely.

It has a mono-shock at the back and a telescopic fork up front. Both ends have disc brakes, and CBS is the industry standard. The e-bike features astonishing 230mm water resistant capabilities and a comfortable 200mm ground clearance. It will not be wrong if we say that there is a great competition between the Oben Rorr and the Tork Kratos.

5. Joy e-bike Monster

Joy e-bike, the Monster electric commuter bike in India, has a starting price of about Rs. 1,00,875. There is just 1 variation and 2 colours offered with this e-bike. The motor of the Joy e-bike Monster produces 250 W of power. Front and rear disc brakes are both included on the Joy e-bike Monster.

Eight models are available under the Joy e-bike brand, which is owned by Wardwizard Innovations & Mobility Limited. Particularly the Monster receives a motorcycle-like look that resembles the Honda Grom 125 mini-bike. The Monster should be simple for all riders because of its low seat height and light weight of around 75kg. Even though it is small, it includes several parts as of high-end bikes, like a front fork, turned upside down, 14-inch alloy wheels, and tubeless tyres.

A 72V/32Ah lithium-ion battery powers a 250-watt brushless DC hub motor that drives the Monster. The highest speed is only 25 kmph, despite the battery's remarkable 75 km range. It takes about 4.5 hours to charge the battery completely. There are two colour choices for this electric bike: black and red.

Additional Mentions/ Options

Revolt RV 300

The Revolt RV 300 electric street bike costs around Rs. 1,09,242 in India. There are just 2 colours and 1 variant available. The Revolt RV 300's motor generates 1500 W of electricity. The dual-wheel braking system on the Revolt RV 300's front and rear wheels include disc brakes. Revolt Motors has also unveiled its scaled-down counterpart, the RV 300, based on the RV 400. The RV 300, which comes in a single variation, has the same street bike attitude as its older variant and uses a similar design language. The RV 300 is offered in Neon Black and Smokey Grey tones.

A 60V/2.7 kWh lithium-ion battery powers the 1.5KW hub-mounted motor that drives the RV 300. Charge times for the battery are 3 hours for a 75% charge and 4.2 hours for a full charge. The RV 300 has three riding modes: Eco, Normal, and Sport from Revolt. Depending on the motorcycle's mode, the peak speed and range change. A computerised instrument cluster and all-LED illumination are features of the RV300. Along with many additional capabilities, it also receives an app that can be used with the motorbike and offers features like geofencing and low-battery notifications.

The RV 300's components include an adjustable mono-shock for the back and inverted forks up front. A disc-drum brake system with CBS is used for stopping. The motorbike has 90/80 front and 110/80 rear section tyres mounted on 17-inch alloy wheels.

PURE EV eTryst 350

The "eTryst 350" is the name of the first electric bike that PURE EV, an IIT Hyderabad-based manufacturer of electric two-wheelers, introduced in India. The EPluto 7G, EPluto, and ETrance+ are among the six electric scooter types the business sells in India. Additionally, it receives two electric cycles-the Ignite and the ETron+-and an electric step-through at the entry.

Later this year, the business plans to release its first electric motorcycle, according to its announcement. The eTryst 350 electric bike, which will be entirely created, built, and produced in India, is scheduled for sale in the first half of 2022. This compact and budget-friendly e-bike includes a battery with a 5-year guarantee. With a single charge, the 3.5kWh battery pack in the Pure EV eTryst 350 can propel the vehicle up to 85 kilometres per hour. The following three drive models are available for the electric bike:

  • Drive ( give range up to 60kmph),
  • Cross Over (give range up to 75kmph),
  • Thrill (give range up to 85 kmph)

This e-bike can reach 60 KMPH in 07.4 seconds and 40 KMPH in 04.4 seconds of acceleration. It has a top speed of 85 kmph and, according to the manufacturer, offers a driving experience comparable to that of vehicles powered by IC engines. The brand's first electric motorbike, the eTryst 350, is anticipated to cost between Rs. 1.20 and 1.50 lakhs. It will immediately compete in this price bracket with the Revolt RV400 and Tork Kratos.

According to the company, the eTryst 350 electric bike, which was supposed to be released on August 15, 2021, has been delayed due to several factors. As a result, the company will release its first fully electric motorcycle in the first half of 2022, with an expected price of Rs 1 lakh- 1.5 lakh.
