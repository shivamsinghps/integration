How to get hired in a company?

Finding a job can be challenging, and knowing how to get hired in a company is an important aspect for job seekers. In this article, we will discuss key tips and advice on ways to increase your chances of landing a job. We will also cover common interview questions that companies may ask during the hiring process. This information will be useful for candidates who are struggling to find employment.

There are primarily two methods of joining a corporation.

  • College Placements
  • Walk-in Interviews

College placements:

We can begin our conversation with college placements as it is a straightforward method of securing a job in a respected firm.

Corporations often conduct campus recruitment drives to recruit suitable candidates for various job positions. If you are presently studying for your undergraduate, postgraduate, or diploma program, you will have the opportunity to take part in on-campus placement drives, usually starting from your third year of studies.

There are three primary types of placements.

  • On-campus placement drive:
    It is the most common placements done by various companies, as they required enthusiastic and talented candidates for their company. In this placement, companies have tie-ups with the colleges, so they visit colleges, hire good candidates as per their requirements, and offer a Job.
    In on-campus placement, students have good chances to get placed in that company as there is a limited number of students and competition is comparatively easy.

Note: You can only attend the On-campus placement drive during your college days.

  • Off-campus placement drive:
    Off-campus placement drives are mass recruitment organized by well-established companies outside the college campus. As it is off-campus placement, so there is no role of college.
    In Off-campus placements, any candidate(with eligibility criteria) from any college can apply and attend the drive. One can attend Off-campus placements during college or after college.
    Off-campus placements are comparatively tough as competition becomes very high.
  • Pool-campus placement drive:
    In pool-campus placement drives more than one college affiliated from the same university conduct placement drive, where students more than one college can attend it and can get placed.

Walk-in Interview:

In today's job market, Walk-in interviews have become an increasingly popular way for job seekers to secure employment. These interviews are particularly beneficial for graduates who have not yet found work, or for those who are already employed but seeking a change. Depending on the company, there may be multiple rounds of interviews before a job offer is made, with the company selecting candidates based on their skills and abilities. Ultimately, Walk-in interviews offer an opportunity for job seekers to showcase their talents and secure employment with a reputable organization.

Preparation for an interview:

It is crucial to be well-prepared prior to an interview, regardless of whether it is a campus placement or a walk-in interview.

Guideline for preparing for an interview:

  • Create a powerful curriculum vitae
    The very first step for applying for an interview is to build a strong resume. Because a good resume helps you to get closer to your dream Job. A good resume increases your chances to get hired for that job.
    It will help you to get an interview and creating a positive impression on the interviewer.
    Following are some basic points to build a strong resume:
    • I cannot rephrase your content as there is no content provided. Please provide me with original content to rephrase.
    • You can discuss your skill sets, areas of expertise, and work experiences, including any previous internships or employment opportunities that have helped shape your career.
    • It is important to ensure there are no errors or mistakes in your resume to present yourself as a qualified candidate.
    • It is not recommended to use paragraphs in your resume. It is better to use bullet points or pointers instead.
    • It is recommended that the font size used in a resume stays within the range of 11-12 Arial, ensuring that it is easily readable.
    • Make sure that you don't include any falsified information on your resume.
  • Get ready to tackle the written or Aptitude test.
    Before going for a face-to-face interview with the company representative you need to clear some rounds, and a written aptitude test is one of them. It is also known as screening round.
  • Essential technical skills specific to a particular domain:
    Before attending an interview, prepare yourself for technical skills, as a company always looks for a skilled candidate. So when you are applying for a job, firstly check the requirements of the company and prepare yourself accordingly.
  • To be ready for effective communication, it is essential to have strong communication skills. Rather than copying someone else's words, it is important to articulate one's thoughts and ideas in a clear and concise manner. Practicing active listening and adapting language and tone to suit the context and audience can also enhance communication capabilities. Overall, honing communication skills is crucial for successful communication.
    A communication skill plays an important role during an interview. You must be able to confidently communicate with the interviewer to get placed in that company.
    You might have strong technical skills but unless you can't explain that skill you cannot get the job.

Recruitment procedure of a company:

The following text outlines the standard recruitment process used by most companies, although some details may vary from one organisation to another.

The process is widely used by several leading IT companies such as Accenture, Adobe, Capgemini, Cognizant, HCL, and Wipro.

Here is an alternative version: When recruiting new employees, companies typically conduct a series of interviews and assessments to evaluate candidates' skills and qualifications. As each round is completed, successful applicants move on to the next stage of the process. This ensures that the most suitable candidates are identified and considered for the role.

  1. Written/Aptitude test
  2. Technical interview round
  3. Hr interview round

1. Written/Aptitude test:

For many companies, the initial stage of their recruitment process involves a written round. This round is typically used as a way to screen candidates and is usually straightforward to pass. It usually involves four different sections:

  • English proficiency
  • Quantitative Aptitude
  • Verbal reasoning
  • Technical written test

There are multiple choice questions in each section, and you are given a specific amount of time to complete about 20-30 questions per section.

2. Technical interview Round:

The technical interview round is an important stage in the selection process, where applicants are evaluated based on their technical abilities. This is a face-to-face assessment, where candidates may be quizzed on different programming languages like Java, C++, C, HTML, JavaScript and data-structures. The interviewers use this round to gauge the technical proficiency of the candidate.

3. HR interview round:

The final stage of the recruitment process is the HR interview, where candidates are evaluated based on their confidence level, soft skills, communication skills, and other qualities.

This round is crucial as it can determine whether you will be accepted or rejected by the company. It is a straightforward process, but it requires a positive attitude and confidence. Your approach and demeanor can make or break your chances of success in this round.
