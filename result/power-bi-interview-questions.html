Power BI Interview Questions

Here is a compilation of frequently asked questions in Power BI job interviews for both new and experienced candidates. These questions are designed to help applicants ace their BI interviews.

1) What do you understand by BI? Or what is BI?

BI refers to a technique that uses technology to help people analyze and visualize business data from various sources. It enables users to share insights and make informed business decisions. BI is an essential tool for corporate executives, managers, and other users looking to gain crucial information about their organization.

2) What is Power BI?

Power BI is a software solution designed for Business Analytics and Data Visualization purposes, which has been developed and is continuously supported by Microsoft. With its capabilities, it allows users to transform information from various sources into interactive dashboards and reports used for Business Intelligence.

Power BI is an online platform that allows users to perform self-service business intelligence tasks. It offers a range of applications, software connectors, and services, with which users can transform raw data into useful information.

3) What is Power BI Desktop?

Power BI Desktop is a desktop application for analyzing business intelligence that is available for free on your computer. It is designed to work together with the Power BI service platform, enabling advanced exploration, modelling, and data shaping capabilities. Power BI Desktop offers interactive visualizations, enabling the creation of highly engaging reports. Users can save their work to their computer or publish it directly to the Power BI site for sharing with others.

4) What do you understand by self-service business intelligence (SSBI)?

SSBI (Self-Service Business Intelligence) is a method of analyzing data that allows non-technical business users to filter, segment, and analyze data without requiring a deep understanding of statistical analysis or BI.

The primary objective of SSBI is to enhance accessibility for end-users to analyze their data and generate diverse visualizations for improved business insights. This tool is user-friendly, and anyone with basic data understanding can create reports and intuitive dashboards that can be easily shared.

5) What are the different parts of Microsoft's self-service business intelligence (SSBI) solution?

The Microsoft Self-Service Business Intelligence Solution is composed of two main components.

The Excel BI Toolkit is a tool that enables users to generate interactive reports by importing data from various sources and modelling it to suit the report's specifications.

Power BI is a web-based tool that allows individuals to collaborate and share interactive reports and data queries created with Excel's BI Toolkit.

11) Is Power BI hard to Learn for a fresher?

Power BI is a software application developed by Microsoft for data analysis and visualization. It is designed to be user-friendly and does not require expertise in programming languages. Users can easily connect to any data source and generate reports summarizing their findings. An important feature of Power BI is the integration of Power Pivot, which allows users to create analytics measures using DAX query language developed by Microsoft.

12) How Important is Power BI?

Power BI is an effective tool for analyzing and visualizing data that is developed and maintained by Microsoft. It offers a variety of features including dashboards, datasets, and reports that enable users to customize their data viewing experience. With Power BI, businesses can combine both cloud and on-premises data to gain insights from a single dashboard. This tool is also useful for monitoring key data points from various business apps enterprise-wide. Overall, Power BI has a significant impact on business intelligence and data analysis.

13) Is Power BI free to use or paid?

Power BI offers both a free and a subscription-based version, with varying levels of features. While the free version is available to all users, the Pro subscription version can be purchased from the Microsoft Store to access enhanced functionalities. Businesses often prefer the subscription-based account, as it provides more data refreshers per day and other features not available in the free version.

14) Is Power BI better than Tableau?

Both Power BI and Tableau enable users to establish connections with various data sources. However, Tableau offers superior assistance for linking to multiple data warehouses compared to Power BI. Meanwhile, Power BI boasts tight integration with Microsoft's array of tools, including the Azure cloud platform.

15) Is Power BI a better option than Microsoft's Excel?

Power BI is a powerful tool that is known for its flexibility and ease of use, making it a preferred choice over Microsoft Excel. With its ability to handle data visualization and share dashboards to multiple users, it offers more advanced features than Microsoft Excel which is mostly effective for in-depth driver analysis.

16) Is there a way to export from power BI to Microsoft Excel?

Certainly! It is possible to export any dashboard or visual data from Power BI to Microsoft Excel. You can do this by selecting the ellipses (3 dots) in the top-right corner of any visualization and choosing the "Export data" icon. The exported data will be saved as a CSV file that can be opened and manipulated in Excel.

20) What is the cost of Power BI?

Power BI has three primary versions which include Power BI Desktop, Power BI, and Power BI Pro.

The usage of Power BI Desktop and Power BI is free of charge. However, if one wants to utilize the advanced features of Power BI Pro, they need to pay a subscription fee of $9.99 per month following a two-month trial period.

21) What is the difference between Power BI and Power BI Pro?

Power BI offers users with basic features to explore and analyze data in a new perspective. However, Power BI Pro provides more advanced capabilities including the ability to schedule data, access live data sources, storage space, full interactivity, and more.

30) What is the difference between SUM() and SUMX() in Power BI?

The Sum() function is used to aggregate data columns and calculate the total sum, while the SumX() function allows you to perform the sum calculation on a filtered subset of data.

31) What is the CORR function in Power BI, and when is it used?

CORR() is a statistical tool commonly used to measure the correlation between two different variables. This function provides a correlation value which can range from -1 to 1, indicating the strength and direction of the relationship between the variables.

32) What do you understand by Power View?

Power View is a powerful data visualization tool that allows users to create interactive visuals such as maps, graphs, and charts. This feature is available in various programs such as Excel, Power BI, SharePoint, and SQL Server. Using Power View, users can make their data more engaging and easier to interpret.

33) What are the main advantages of Power BI?

The list given below highlights the major benefits of Power BI:

  • It is possible to easily integrate Power BI with any current application.
  • Power BI provides customisable and feature-packed dashboards that can be personalised to meet individual needs.
  • One of the benefits of utilizing Power BI is the ability to securely share and publish reports.
  • Power BI enables swift data access, eliminating concerns over performance and memory limitations.

34) What are the biggest disadvantages or drawbacks of using Power BI?

Here are the primary limitations or disadvantages of Power BI:

  • The data imported into Power BI cannot be merged with data obtained through real-time connections.
  • The maximum file size that can be handled by Power BI is 1 GB. Any files exceeding this limit cannot be accepted.
  • The Power BI dashboard lacks the functionality to receive or transmit user or account parameters or any other entity parameters.
  • The sharing feature in Power BI has a restriction that limits the sharing of dashboards and reports to users who have the same email domain.

M language is a type of programming language utilized in Power Query. This is a practical and user-friendly language that is case sensitive. It operates as a functional programming language and shares similarities with other programming languages.

37) What do you understand by the term responsive slicers?

A responsive slicer is a tool utilized to conserve space on a report page, enabling users to easily resize it to various shapes and sizes. The data displayed on the slicer is automatically rearranged to fit the new shape. Furthermore, if the slicer becomes too small, an icon representing its functionality is displayed instead, freeing up even more space on the report page.

To create a map in Power Map, it is necessary to input location data such as city, state, country, or latitude and longitude. This is important because Power Maps are designed to display geographical visualizations.

41) What is the data source filter in Power BI?

A data source filter is a tool that enables the data to be filtered before being loaded into machines. It is essentially a criterion that is utilized to filter data.

42) What is the difference between older and newer Power BI?

Power BI has introduced a new tool called Power BI Desktop, which features a self-contained design tool comprising Power Pivot, Power View, and Power Query. This is a significant divergence from the previous version, which required Excel add-ins. The latest version of Power BI offers a variety of graphs, including treemap, line area chart, waterfall, and a combination chart.

43) What do you understand by the on-premise gateway in Power BI?

Power BI employs an on-premise gateway as an intermediary that enables secure transfer of on-premise data to the cloud.

44) What is the meaning of the term data alerts in Power BI?

Power BI's data alerts function operates by monitoring data that is refreshed. After refreshing the data, the system checks whether an alert is configured for that specific data. If the data has reached an alert threshold, an alert is triggered by the system.

45) What is the use of the split function in Power BI?

The split function in Power BI allows users to divide a string database based on a designated delimiter.

46) What are the main differences between Power BI personal Gateway and Data Management Gateway?

The Power BI Personal Gateway and the Data Management Gateway are two distinct tools used for deploying and refreshing reports. While the former is used specifically for reports that are deployed on Powerbi.com, the latter is an application that needs to be installed on the source data machine to enable report deployment on Sharepoint with automatic scheduling for refreshment.

47) What do you understand by Power BI Designer?

Power BI Designer is an independent application that enables the creation of Power BI reports and their subsequent uploading to Powerbi.com. It integrates different Power BI components such as Power View, Power Pivot, and Power Query to assist in the report creation process.

48) What are KPIs in Power BI?

KPIs, or Key Performance Indicators, are metrics used to assess an organization's success in different areas by analyzing measurable objectives and values. They involve measuring a base value against target values and comparing achieved performance with the target. KPIs offer graphical representations to aid in analyzing performance and determine if targets were met.

49) What is the difference between Distinct() and Values() in DAX?

The Distinct() and Values() functions in Power BI are quite similar in their purpose. However, the key difference is that the Values() function does not consider null values in its calculation, while the Distinct() function includes them.

50) What are the advantages of the direct query method in Power BI?

Power BI desktop is capable of generating data visualizations for smaller datasets with a limit of 1 GB. However, Direct Query method allows users to create and visualize much larger datasets without any limit. The Direct Query method also overcomes the 1 GB dataset limit of the Power BI desktop. With this method, there are no restrictions as to the size of the dataset that can be used.

51) What is the incremental refresh in the context of Power BI?

Incremental refresh is a method that refreshes only the newly added data without the need to truncate and load all the data again.

52) What is the difference between a Filter and a Slicer?

Filters and slicers are two functions in data visualization that serve different purposes. Filters help restrict user access to certain features within a dashboard or report, whereas slicers enable users to interact with the data and manipulate its display.

53) What are the different tabs in the Reports development Window?

You can find three primary tabs on the Reports development window.

  • Relationship tab
  • Data Modeling Tab
  • Report Tab

54) How many types of default Graphs (Visualizations) are available in Power BI?

Power BI offers a myriad of default visualization options, numbering at approximately 26 different types of graphs.

55) What is the difference between a new column and a new measure in Power BI?

Power BI provides two ways to create new data fields: Column and Measure. When creating a new Column, the data is physically stored and logic is applied to it. However, in the case of a Measure, calculations are performed in real-time based on dimensions, without any physical data storage.

56) What are the different joins in Power BI?

Power BI has two major categories of joins.

  • Horizontal Joins: Horizontal Joins are used to append data from multiple tables.
  • Vertical Joins: Vertical Joins are used to merge the data from multiple tables.

In order to use Power BI, it is necessary to have a business email rather than an email address from a telecommunications or consumer email service provider, as those types of email addresses are not supported by Power BI.

59) What is the usage of statewith() in Power BI?

The statewith() function, when used in Power BI, evaluates whether a sub-string is the beginning of a superstring. If it is, the function will return TRUE, otherwise it will return FALSE.

60) What is the Embed Code in Power BI?

The Embed code feature in Power BI allows users to create a shareable link address for their report. This link can be easily shared with other clients who can view the report.

61) What is the Time Intelligence function in Power BI?

The time intelligence function is a feature in Power BI that enables the manipulation of data based on time periods. With this function, users can easily analyze data across different time frames and obtain insights that would otherwise be difficult to uncover.
