1) What is the full form of NLP? / What is Natural Language Processing?

NLP, which stands for Natural Language Processing, is a computer science discipline that focuses on the interaction between humans and computer systems. It involves the use of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning to develop automated software that can understand and interpret human language, as well as extract relevant data from audio recordings.

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is an area of study that involves using computer systems to analyze and interpret natural language data. Its purpose is to develop algorithms that are capable of comprehending meaning from vast collections of text or audio data by utilizing machine learning algorithms. In essence, NLP is software that employs artificial intelligence and machine learning techniques to identify patterns and extract useful information from natural language data.

6) What do you understand by Dependency Parsing in NLP or Natural Language Processing?

Dependency Parsing is a crucial Natural Language Processing task that involves assigning a syntactic structure to sentences. This enables the identification of dependency parses, thus facilitating the comprehension of the relationships between the "head" words in the syntactic structure. It is also referred to as syntactic parsing.

Dependency parsing can become intricate when sentences have multiple dependency parses, also known as ambiguities. The goal is to accurately assign a syntactic structure to a sentence by resolving these ambiguities. This process is crucial not just for syntax but also for semantic analysis.

9) What is the use of TF-IDF? Why is it used in Natural language Processing?

TF-IDF is a popular technique used in Natural Language Processing to measure the significance of a word in a document or a set of documents. This numerical statistic is based on the frequency of a word in a document and the inverse frequency of the word in the entire collection of documents.
