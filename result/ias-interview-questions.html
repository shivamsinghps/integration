IAS Interview Questions

The Indian Administrative Service (IAS) is a government service whose candidates are recruited following a tough selection process by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC). The selection process includes a three-part examination comprising Prelims, Mains, and an Interview. The Interview section is known to be the most significant as it plays a crucial role in determining the best candidate for the job. Passing this exam guarantees placement in the IAS as one of the most respected government services in India.

The IAS Prelims and Main exams are designed to evaluate the knowledge and general awareness of candidates on various topics. In contrast, the IAS Interview is used to assess the personality and temperament of civil service aspirants.

The UPSC examination Pattern

To enter IAS, one must pass three exams, including preliminary, mains, and an interview round.

The Prelims is the first stage of the examination, comprising of multiple-choice questions. It is widely regarded as a rigorous test because the questions are original and not sourced from any material. Furthermore, each question carries the same weightage with a probability of 25% chance of being answered correctly.

The second stage of the civil service examination is known as the Mains. This round is subjective in nature and evaluates a candidate's writing style and knowledge presentation. Candidates who clear the preliminary round are eligible to appear for the Mains. The candidate's ability to provide detailed answers and demonstrate their knowledge is assessed in this paper.

In the third round of the IAS exam, candidates who have qualified mains must undergo an interview. The interviewers are typically experienced IAS officers. During the interview, candidates can expect to be asked questions that aren't covered by textbooks.

Common Interview Questions and Assessment

After being successful in both the preliminary and main examinations, candidates are required to take part in a personality or interview test. During this test, UPSC officials will ask questions related to the applicant's detailed application form.

One can inquire about a variety of topics during an interview such as place of origin, areas of interest, local issues, national concerns, and more.

During an interview, the questions asked can vary depending on the candidate's background, interests and experience. For example, if the candidate is from a certain state or region, they may be asked questions related to their local culture or politics. Similarly, if the candidate belongs to a famous tourist destination, they may be asked about the prominent places or attractions in the area. Personal interests and hobbies can also influence the questions asked during an interview. For instance, a candidate who enjoys gardening may be asked about soil types, various vegetables and herbs, and gardening techniques. Prior work experience can also influence the types of questions asked.

During a job interview, the interviewers may ask questions related to your job profile to assess your knowledge and experience in that field. For instance, if you have been employed as an engineer, they may inquire about your expertise in the area of technology, innovation or any other relevant topics. As you answer their queries, it gives them an opportunity to observe your interaction and response to each question.

In this interview round, the panel endeavors to evaluate whether your responses reflect a broader Indian perspective or are more representative of a particular region's mentality.

Situational Interview Questions

2) Where would Lord Rama have celebrated his "First Diwali"?

Determining whether Lord Rama celebrated Diwali or not requires both theoretical and practical knowledge. According to popular belief, Lord Rama may have celebrated his first Diwali upon returning to Ayodhya from his 14-year exile as the citizens welcomed him. However, historical evidence suggests that Diwali actually commemorates the slaying of Narakasura by Lord Krishna. As Lord Krishna's incarnation occurred after Lord Rama's, it can be inferred that Diwali celebrations did not take place during Lord Rama's time.

3) Which Indian temple disappears twice a day?

Stambheshwar Mahadev Temple is reputed for its unique feature of disappearing twice daily. The temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva, holds great historical significance in India and is among the oldest shrines in the country.

The Stambheshwar Mahadev Temple can be found in the village of Kavi Kamboi, situated in Jambusar in the state of Gujarat. Its unique location just a few meters away from the seashore means that it disappears when the tide is high and reappears when the water level recedes. As the sea level rises twice daily, visitors can witness the temple completely submerge underwater during high tide and then emerge again during low tide.

As such, candidates should prioritize developing their soft skills in preparation for this stage of the selection process.
