Most Asked BPO Interview Questions

Here are some common BPO interview questions that are frequently asked of both entry-level and experienced candidates, which can assist them in securing their ideal role.

1) What do you know about BPO and how it works?

BPO is short for Business Process Outsourcing and involves entrusting certain business functions to an external contractor. These functions can include support services such as technical assistance, or back-end operations like billing. This practice is employed when a company has a substantial workload and wishes to reduce costs by tapping into a cheaper labor market abroad. Thus, outsourcing non-core functions to a country with a larger workforce and lower wages can be a viable option.

BPO refers to the outsourcing of a company's complete business operations to a third party service provider for a contracted period. The primary goal of this is to ensure that the service provider offers top-notch customer service and optimizes the overall customer experience.

2) Why do you want to join a BPO?

How would you answer the question "Why do you want to work in a BPO?" during an interview? Provide a clear and concise response that conveys your professional goals and aspirations.

As a recent graduate, I've noticed the rapid evolution of technology and its significant impact on the outsourcing market. The continuous growth in the industry is providing numerous work opportunities and projects for BPO companies. As a result, the demand for skilled professionals in the BPO/IT field is on the rise, and one can gain valuable experience and knowledge to enhance their productivity over time. Many well-established brands in various industries also heavily rely on BPO services, making it a promising and exciting career choice for someone just starting out.

4) What are the major types of outsourcing?

Various forms of outsourcing exist, including the following:

  • Onshore outsourcing
  • Offshore outsourcing
  • Near-shoring outsourcing
  • Professional outsourcing
  • IT outsourcing etc.

The most profitable types of outsourcing in business are professional and IT outsourcing. These forms of outsourcing have been found to be highly successful in generating profits.

5) What is the difference between onshore and offshore outsourcing?

The interviewer may ask this question to assess how familiar you are with the BPO industry. Your response should explain the difference between offshore outsourcing and onshore outsourcing. Offshore outsourcing refers to assigning a task or project to a foreign country that is typically far away, whereas onshore outsourcing involves delegating work to a neighboring country.

6) What do you know about professional outsourcing?

Professional outsourcing is a commonly utilized practice in which specialized professional services, such as accounting, legal advisory, IT, administrative support, and education, are outsourced. The goal of outsourcing is to help businesses reduce overhead costs and access high-quality professional resources without the need for full-time staff. By paying only for the services provided, core businesses can save money while receiving top-notch professional support.

7) What do you understand by IT outsourcing?

IT outsourcing is a popular form of professional outsourcing that has gained momentum in recent decades. Many large IT companies have started to look for technology-related resources outside their organization from overseas countries where resources are more cost-effective. This allows them to outsource functions such as software development, infrastructure, maintenance, and support.

8) Why working in night shifts is important for BPO and IT sector?

The night shift plays a crucial role in the BPO and IT industry due to its significance in several aspects.

  • The BPO and IT sectors in India work on projects predominantly from developed countries such as the USA, Canada, UK, Australia, and New Zealand. Therefore, they must align their working hours to match those of their foreign clients.
  • The cost of production can be minimized if the company only rents offices for their night shift operations. During the day, other companies can make use of these offices, resulting in cost savings.
  • Working at night allows the BPO to increase its production output and cater to the needs of the market more effectively.

9) What is the difference between inbound and outbound call centers?

Inbound call centers and outbound call centers differ in their primary functions. Inbound call centers primarily receive calls while outbound call centers primarily make calls. Inbound call centers usually function as the service department of a company, while outbound call centers focus on the business department where they sell products or services.

10) What is multi outsourcing?

Multi-sourcing, a term coined by a market technology research firm in 2005, is a common approach to IT outsourcing and services used primarily by large enterprises. This strategy involves outsourcing IT operations and technology infrastructure to multiple vendors, sometimes combined with in-house IT elements.

11) What is manufacturer outsourcing?

Manufacturer outsourcing is a popular service used by companies involved in the production of goods. This strategy involves hiring individuals or businesses outside of the company to assemble or construct certain aspects or the entirety of a product. It is a valuable option for businesses lacking sufficient financial resources to purchase and operate large machinery. Utilizing outsourcing can provide these companies with a competitive edge against larger corporations.

12) What do you understand by process-specific outsourcing?

Process-specific outsourcing refers to a business practice in which a company delegates a specific operation to external companies or units who are experts in that field. This allows companies to take advantage of the expertise and knowledge of specialized external entities in a particular process.

An organization like Amazon can hire the services of courier delivery companies like BlueDart or Ekart to handle their package delivery needs. These companies handle everything from delivery timelines to customer communication and fees. This allows Amazon to focus on their primary business operations without worrying about the logistics of package delivery.

13) What do you understand by offshoring?

Offshoring refers to the act of delegating business operations to a foreign country. The main goal is to take advantage of lower costs in labour and other expenses. By offshoring, businesses can enter new markets and benefit from cheaper talent that may not be available within their home country. This strategic move can also help lower operating costs significantly.

14) What are the major advantages of a BPO?

Here are the key benefits of a BPO:

  • Flexibility: Companies choose to outsource non-core activities to a BPO, and that makes them more flexible. It also enhances the flexibility in resource management of the client company.
  • Improve Productivity: It improves productivity and human resource of the company because BPO can easily cope with the changing demand of the customers.
  • Cost-Effective: It enables call center owners to utilize the latest technology at a lower cost. The company does not have to invest in additionally fixed assets and can convert them to variable costs.
  • Speed: This is one of the biggest advantages of BPOs as they increase the speed of the business processes outsourced to them. They have a very good response time, and the client company can focus on the core activities.
  • Skilled Manpower: When a company outsources a business activity to a BPO, the company is insured for great services provided by the BPO's skilled labor.

15) What are the major disadvantages of a BPO?

Here are the key benefits of a BPO:

  • Communication Problems: There may appear a communication gap between customers and companies, creating further problems.
  • Different Time Zones: This is a very real problem because the different time zones between the client and the vendor companies create a calling problem.
  • Loss of Control: In this service, the customer care department is generally controlled by another company, so the customer may sometimes feel a loss of control in customer service.

16) What do you understand by a horizontal BPO?

There are two main types of BPOs that exist and operate in the market.

  • Horizontal BPO
  • Vertical BPO

Horizontal BPO is a type of outsourcing that primarily focuses on the outsourcing of specific functions across various industries. For instance, it includes outsourcing functions such as procurement, HR, payroll processing, facilities management, and ERP software maintenance. The outsourcing partner or vendor specializes in these specific functions to achieve better efficiency and productivity in the area of expertise.

17) What do you understand by a vertical BPO?

A vertical BPO is a type of outsourcing that concentrates on specific industries such as healthcare, financial services, manufacturing, and retail. It aims to provide services tailored to the unique needs of these industries.

One of the primary motives for companies outsourcing their tasks is the advantage of cost-saving and the ability to concentrate on their core business activities. 26) What is the full form of BPM?

BPM stands for Business Process Management which is a method of managing organizational procedures with the goal of improving client satisfaction. It focuses on optimizing operations within a business to achieve this objective.

ISO: 9000 refers to a collection of global standards designed to assist businesses in implementing effective quality management and quality assurance measures. 40) What is reshoring in the context of BPO?

Reshoring refers to the act of bringing back the manufacturing and production of goods to a company's country of origin. This process can also be referred to as back shoring, inshoring or onshoring.

41) What is the difference between back-office outsourcing and front office outsourcing?

  • Back-office outsourcing: When an organization decides to outsource the internal process of its working to another company, it is called back-office outsourcing. For Example, suppose a company has assigned all the responsibilities to manage its finances to a third party accounts firm. It would be called back-office outsourcing.
  • Front office outsourcing: When an organization decides to outsource the customer handling department, services and BPO call centers to another company, it is called front-office outsourcing. In this outsourcing, the vendor firm has to deal with and manage all the customer relationships and provide a contact point for customers of the company to address their grievances.

42) What do you understand by Procurement Business Process Outsourcing?

Procurement Business Process Outsourcing, commonly known as Procurement BPO, is a service provided by leading consulting companies which involves outsourcing the business functions of the purchasing or processing department of one company to another company. Although Procurement BPO is a relatively new and increasingly popular service, only a few big companies have opted to outsource their entire procurement activities through it. The reason being that Procurement BPO is a costly venture.

43) What is the difference between offshore, nearshore, and onshore outsourcing?

The primary distinctions among offshore, nearshore, and onshore outsourcing can be summarized as follows:

Offshore outsourcing refers to a business practice where a third-party company is contracted to carry out certain processes for a company, typically in a foreign country. This involves outsourcing work that is outside the company's own country. In essence, offshore outsourcing involves hiring an external firm to handle business functions in a location other than where the products or services are being developed or manufactured.

An instance of outsourcing is when US-based companies transfer their projects to Indian companies. This is a common practice among multinational corporations.

Nearshore outsourcing refers to the practice of hiring a company from a neighboring country to perform a specific process or project on behalf of another organization. This differs from offshore outsourcing, which involves contracting work out to companies located in more distant overseas countries.

A way for an American company to outsource work to a Mexican company is known as nearshore outsourcing.

Onshore outsourcing refers to the practice of a company hiring a third-party organization located within the same country to handle a particular process for them. This outsourcing approach allows the company to remain domestically while still benefiting from the expertise of the external service provider.

44) What are the main activities involved in a call center or a BPO?

The primary responsibility of working in a call center or BPO involves effectively addressing customer inquiries and resolving their issues. In addition, employees must persuade customers to purchase products and services and collaborate with colleagues to provide excellent service to clients.

BPO (Business Process Outsourcing) poses a significant threat of violating customers' confidentiality. BPO providers may accidentally or intentionally disclose customers' personal information, resulting in privacy breaches.

47) What is the full form of LPO?

Legal process outsourcing, or LPO, refers to a business model where legal tasks are outsourced by an organization to a third-party legal service provider. This allows the organization to focus on its core business activities, while the legal service provider takes care of all legal work.
