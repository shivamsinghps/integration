About the Company: SpaceX

SpaceX, a privately-owned American aerospace company, also known as Space Exploration Technologies Corp, was established in 2002 by the notable entrepreneur and CEO, Elon Musk. The company, headquartered in Hawthorne, California, designs and produces advanced rockets and spacecraft and is home to more than 6000 workers. Their primary objective is to lessen the expense of space transportation and ultimately make it viable for people to reside on other planets. Two of SpaceX's successful developments include the Falcon launch family and the Dragon spacecraft family, which currently operate to convey payloads into Earth's Orbit.

Achievements of SpaceX

  • The first privately funded liquid-fueled rocket to achieve orbit was created by SpaceX.
  • SpaceX produced a liquid-fueled rocket, which was the first privately developed rocket to be utilized for the purpose of launching a commercial satellite into orbit.
  • SpaceX has made history as the first privately-owned company to successfully launch and recover a spacecraft in orbit.
  • SpaceX made history by becoming the first company to successfully launch a spacecraft that docked at the International Space Station.
  • SpaceX achieved a historic milestone by successfully deploying a satellite in geosynchronous orbit, becoming the first company to do so.
  • SpaceX achieved a historic feat as the first privately-owned organization to successfully land the initial phase of an orbital rocket onto land.
  • In 2018, SpaceX successfully launched its powerful operational rocket called the Falcon Heavy, which is considered the most powerful of its kind.

Each node in the tree is assigned a color attribute, either red or black, which helps to ensure that the tree remains balanced. This attribute, combined with some clever algorithms results in a time complexity of O(log n), meaning that it is highly efficient even for large trees.

Here are the guidelines to be followed:

  • The nodes in a binary search tree will either be coloured Red or Black.
  • It is recommended that the topmost node of the tree should be colored black.
  • The instruction is to ensure that all terminal nodes are colored in red.
  • The color of a child node and its parent node must be different.
  • It is a fundamental property of a red-black tree that all paths starting from any node and ending at the NIL node have an equal number of black nodes.

14) Differentiate between TCP and UDP?

TCP and UDP are both types of protocols utilized in the transport layer of computer networks to facilitate packet transmission. While they share some similarities in function, there are also significant differences between the two.

TCP UDP
The term TCP stands for Transmission Control Protocol. User Datagram Protocol, commonly known as UDP, is a network protocol used for transmitting datagrams or small messages over the internet.
TCP is a protocol that requires a connection to be established before data transmission can occur. It uses a mechanism to ensure that each transmission is acknowledged before the next one can occur. UDP is a type of protocol used for data transmission that does not require confirmation of received packets, meaning it is connectionless.
TCP is considered to be a dependable protocol for data transmission. The protocol UDP is not a dependable means of transmitting data.
Compared to UDP, TCP has a slower speed since it prioritizes the secure arrival of data at its destination. UDP is considered a faster protocol than TCP because it does not have the same level of data reliability measures in place. UDP does not guarantee that data has been received, and this lack of reliability allows it to operate at a faster speed than TCP.
Data sequencing takes place within TCP protocol. UDP does not maintain the order in which data packets are sent or received, i.e., it lacks sequencing capability.
When some packets get lost during a transfer, TCP will retransmit them. The missing packets that were not successfully transmitted will not be re-sent.
TCP protocol is commonly used in situations that require dependable and flawless data transmission. An example of this could be when requesting a complex website that consists of various elements. UDP is commonly employed in situations where it is important to prioritize speed, and the transmission of information without errors is not essential. This type of protocol is often utilized in the context of online gaming, where rapid communication is necessary for optimal gameplay.
