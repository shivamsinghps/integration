Most Asked Scrum Master Interview Questions

Here are some common questions often asked in interviews for the Scrum Master role along with their ideal responses.

1) What do you understand by Scrum?

Scrum is an Agile methodology that facilitates the development, delivery, and maintenance of intricate products. Although it is predominantly employed in software development, it is also adaptable to other areas such as sales, marketing, and cutting-edge technologies. Its primary aim is to promote teamwork among members of a team.

Scrum is a project management methodology that is commonly used by small teams of up to ten members. The team sets achievable goals for themselves within time-bound iterations, known as sprints, which typically last for two weeks but can run for a maximum of one month.

2) What is the Daily Scrum?

The Daily Scrum is a short 15-minute meeting held by the Scrum Team to monitor their task progress on a daily basis. The team also conducts a sprint review to showcase their completed work and a sprint retrospective to constantly enhance their performance at the end of each sprint.

3) What is the main usage of Scrum?

Scrum empowers teams to develop a culture of continuous learning, self-management, and reflection. It grants teams the ability to learn from successes and failures, adapt to challenges, and make necessary improvements.

4) What is Agile in software development?

Agile is a methodology used in software development that emphasizes collaboration between self-organizing teams and iterative development. The process involves teamwork and communication to discover user requirements and develop solutions that best meet their needs. This approach allows teams to adapt to changes quickly and deliver quality products in a timely manner.

Agile methodology puts emphasis on being responsive to changes, promoting evolutionary development, early delivery, and continual improvement. It also encourages adaptive planning and flexible strategies.

5) What are the different roles in Scrum?

There are three distinct roles in the Scrum framework:

  • Product Owner: The first role is the product owner. This is an individual responsible for increasing the ROI by determining product features, prioritizing these features into a list, focusing on the upcoming sprint, and other things. These are constantly re-prioritized and refined.
  • Scrum Master: The Scrum Master is used to help the team learn how to apply Scrum to ensure optimum business value. The responsibility of scrum master is to remove impediments, shield the team from distractions, and make them able to adopt agile practices.
  • Scrum Team: The Scrum Team is a collection of individuals who work together to ensure the stakeholders' requirements on time.

6) What are the main responsibilities of the Scrum Team?

The Scrum Team is a group of individuals who organize themselves and work together on a project. This team usually consists of a minimum of five members and a maximum of seven members, but is always less than ten members.

Here are the main duties that Scrum Teams are accountable for:

  • Delivering functional products is a crucial aspect of every sprint and must be prioritized.
  • It is essential to make sure that the team members have clear ownership and transparency over their assigned tasks.
  • For a successful daily scrum meeting, the Scrum Team needs to provide precise and accurate information.
  • Effective teamwork relies on the cooperation and coordination of all team members.

Here's a paraphrased version: Agile and Scrum are two development methodologies that have some key differences. Some of the differences include: - Agile is a broader methodology that involves being flexible and open to change throughout the development process, while Scrum is a specific framework within Agile that focuses on iterative development and daily meetings called "standups." - Agile is more of a philosophy or mindset, while Scrum is a more structured and prescriptive approach to development. - Agile emphasizes collaboration and teamwork among all members of the project, whereas Scrum puts a lot of emphasis on the role of the Scrum Master, who acts as an intermediary between the development team and stakeholders. - Agile is often used in large-scale projects, while Scrum is more commonly used in smaller teams or projects.

8) What are some other frameworks of Agile rather than Scrum?

There exist various alternative Agile frameworks in addition to Scrum, such as feature-driven development, test-driven development, and kanban (which makes use of kanban boards).

9) What are the Artifacts of the Scrum Process?

The Scrum process involves three primary artifacts, which are discussed below:

  • Product Backlog: The Product Backlog is a list that contains new features, changes to features, bug fixes, changes to the infrastructure, and other activities to ensure that we can get an individual output.
  • Sprint Backlog: The Sprint Backlog is a subset of the Product Backlog. It contains tasks that the team has focused on to satisfy the sprint goal. Teams first identify the tasks that they have completed from the product backlog and then added them to the sprint backlog.
  • Product Increment: Product Increment is a combination of all product backlog items completed in a sprint and the value of previous sprints' increments. The output must be in a usable condition without minding it if the product owner doesn't release it.

15) What is Scrum-ban?

Scrum-ban is a hybrid methodology that combines the principles of both Scrum and Kanban. Its objective is to cater to the team's needs while minimizing work batching and adopting a pull-based system. Scrum-ban incorporates Scrum's structured approach and Kanban's adaptability and visualisation.

18) What is User-Story Mapping in Scrum?

Scrum methodology employs a technique known as User-Story Mapping, which involves organizing user stories in a manner that facilitates comprehension of system functions, backlog, release planning, and customer value. The prioritization of user stories is represented on the horizontal axis, while the vertical axis highlights an increase in sophistication.

21) What do you understand by 'Scrum of Scrums'?

22) What is the general age of the Sprints?

A sprint is a time-boxed period during which a product increment that can be released is developed. This period usually lasts for a month or less, although it can be shorter. Sprints can also be as brief as one or two weeks on average.

23) What do you understand by a Sprint Retrospective?

The sprint retrospective meeting is held post the sprint review, where team members reflect on their previous performance, identify errors, anticipate challenges, and brainstorm new strategies for surpassing them. This information is subsequently used to plan for the next sprint.

24) What are the main drawbacks or disadvantages of using Scrum?

Here are some major drawbacks or limitations of implementing Scrum methodology:

  • Scrum is a methodology that requires competent and skilled team members.
  • The success of a Scrum team relies on their ability to work together effectively and their dedication to delivering accurate results.
  • Insufficient experience in a team or a less experienced scrum master can potentially lead to the failure of the project.
  • It is crucial to clearly define the tasks involved in a project, as failure to do so could result in inaccuracies in the project outcome.
  • Scrum methodology is more effective when used on small project scopes, but it can become challenging to expand to larger and more intricate projects.

27) What is the difference between user stories, epics, and tasks?

The following are distinctions between user stories, epics, and tasks:

  • User Stories: User Stories are used to provide the team a simple explanation of the business' requirements created from the end user's perspective.
  • Epics: An epic is a collection of related user stories which are usually large and complex.
  • Tasks: A task is the smallest unit in Scrum that is used to track work. It is a breakdown of user stories further. Usually, a person or a team of two people work on a task.

28) What do you understand by Sprint and Velocity in Scrum?

Scrum involves breaking work down into manageable iterations called sprints. During each sprint, a specific module or feature of the product is created with a duration of one to two weeks. Velocity, on the other hand, is a performance metric that measures the amount of work a team can complete during a sprint. It's based on the number of user stories completed in a given sprint period.

30) What are a Burnup and Burndown Chart in Scrum?

A burnup chart is a valuable aid in monitoring progress and keeping track of the tasks completed in a project/sprint. It visually represents the amount of work completed and the total amount of work required, enabling the team to see the progress made towards meeting project objectives.

A burndown chart is a tool used to visually track progress in completing user stories. It displays the amount of work remaining against the total effort needed for each iterative cycle.

34) What are the full form of MVP and MMP? What is their usage?

MVP is an acronym that stands for Minimum Viable Product. The concept of MVP is often used in the Lean Startup methodology, highlighting the importance of learning while developing a product. By creating an initial version of their product and exposing it to potential customers or users, entrepreneurs can gather relevant data and gain insight into their target audience. This approach allows for testing and understanding of an idea before investing too much time and resources in its development. By observing how customers use and perceive the product, entrepreneurs can gain a better understanding of their needs and expectations.

The term MMP, or Minimal Marketable Product, refers to a description of a product that includes only the most essential features necessary to meet the needs of users. This approach helps businesses shorten their time to market while still satisfying their customers.

The "Definition of Ready" is an agreement that is established between the Product Owner and the team to determine what readiness means for the backlog items. Therefore, the team must identify the specific requirements for the User Story or PBI and the amount of work necessary to complete it. Each team and product may have its own unique Definition of Ready.

42) How can you coordinate between multiple teams?

There are many approaches to coordinate between multiple teams. One of the most common strategies for this is the Scrum of Scrums (SoS) meeting. In this meeting, the members representing each scrum team discuss the progress, performance, issues, risks, etc. The team should pre-define the frequency of these meetings. Generally, scrum masters would represent a particular scrum team, besides having the Chief Scrum Master (whose responsibility is coordination & collaboration among all the scrums) who facilitates these meetings.
