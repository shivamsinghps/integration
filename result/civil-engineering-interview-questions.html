Most Asked Civil Engineering Interview Questions

1) What is civil engineering?

Civil engineering is a branch of engineering that specializes in the planning, design, construction, and management of physical infrastructure. This includes structures such as roads, bridges, airports, pipelines, and buildings, as well as public works such as dams and sewerage systems. Civil engineers are responsible for ensuring that these structures are safe, efficient, and sustainable over their lifespan.

Civil engineering has a long history and is one of the oldest engineering fields, second only to military engineering. Within civil engineering, there are various sub-disciplines, including:

  • Coastal engineering
  • Construction engineering
  • Earthquake engineering
  • Environmental engineering
  • Forensic engineering
  • Geotechnical engineering
  • Materials science and engineering is a field that focuses on the discovery and development of new materials with specific properties and characteristics. It combines knowledge from physics, chemistry, and engineering to design materials that can be used in a variety of applications such as electronics, medicine, transportation, and more. This discipline is essential for the advancement of technology and plays a crucial role in improving our daily lives.

  • Structural engineering
  • Transportation engineering
  • Municipal or urban engineering involves the design and maintenance of infrastructure in cities or other urban areas, including water supply and sewage systems, transportation networks, and public works such as parks and buildings.
  • Water resources engineering deals with the study and management of water in our environment. This includes the design of water infrastructure, management of water resources, and the treatment of wastewater. It is an important field that works towards ensuring a sustainable supply of clean water for communities and protecting natural ecosystems that depend on water.

During the time of the industrial revolution in England, civil engineering became a significant engineering discipline, with many scientific innovations emerging. One of its most prominent figures is George John Smeaton, who is widely regarded as the father of civil engineering.

8) What is the meaning of grade in cement?

A grade is a numerical value that represents the minimum compressive strength achieved by a cement-sand mortar mixture after 28 days. Cement is labelled with different grades, such as OPC 43 and OPC 53, which indicate its maximum compressive strength after setting.

10) What is the blended cement, and what are the advantages of using blended cement?

Blended cement is a type of cement that is made by combining Ordinary Portland cement with mineral additives such as fly ash, slag, or silica fumes. The addition of these mineral admixtures enhances the properties of the blended cement, making it more superior to conventional OPC cement.

Blended cement offers a range of benefits that can be classified into two major categories:

  • Technical advantages
  • Environmental advantages

Blended cement offers several technical advantages over ordinary Portland cement. These include enhanced workability, increased durability, improved strength, and reduced permeability. Additionally, blended cement can help reduce greenhouse gas emissions and decrease energy consumption during production. It is a versatile alternative with properties that benefit the construction industry.

  • Blended cement offers a smoother texture compared to Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC), resulting in a finer mixture when combined.
  • Compared to Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC), using fly ash and slag cement as admixtures provides greater strength after complete setting, particularly exceeding 28 days, in terms of compressive and flexural stress. The strength outcome is dependent on the quality and proportion of the admixture utilized. Alternatively, silica fume cement undergoes setting within a shorter period, typically within 3 to 28 days.
  • Blended cement has a lower permeability compared to ordinary cement, which makes it more durable and resistant to water runoff compounds such as sulfates and chlorides that may have a detrimental effect as the cement ages. This is because blended cement has a longer useful life and hardness, contributed by the reduced penetration of aggressive water runoff compounds. Silica fume cement, which is a type of blended cement, has a permeability level of only 20% compared to Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC).
  • When utilizing OPC, it is possible for the concrete to become weaker and for cracking to occur if there are significant temperature variations between the surface and the interior. Therefore, it may not be ideal for regions with temperatures that dip below 40 degrees. Blended cement, on the other hand, can lower peak temperatures and minimize the likelihood of thermal stress.

Blended cement has several environmental benefits as compared to traditional cement. Its production requires less energy consumption compared to traditional cement. Additionally, it reduces greenhouse gas emissions, as it uses a mix of cement and supplementary materials like fly ash, slag, and pozzolanic materials. The use of these materials helps minimize the amount of clinker, which is the source of carbon dioxide emissions in traditional cement. Therefore, using blended cement helps reduce the carbon footprint of the construction industry.

  • Blended cement helps to reduce the amount of water required for construction.
  • Blended cement is an energy-saving alternative as it incorporates admixtures like slag and fly ash, which are byproducts of other industries.
  • Blended cement is an excellent demonstration of resource conservation practices. This type of cement utilizes by-products generated from steel and coal power plants, among other sources, which would typically be discarded as waste. By utilizing these by-products, the demand for other raw materials required in cement production, such as limestone, silica, and clay, can be reduced, thereby conserving these natural resources.

13) What is compacting, and why is it used?

Compacting is an essential process in concrete construction that involves increasing the density of the cement mixture to eliminate air pockets. Mixing different materials can result in air bubbles being trapped in the mixture, which affects the strength and durability of the concrete. The process of releasing these air bubbles is critical in creating a dense concrete mix that can withstand pressure and last long. Any voids left in the concrete will reduce its strength significantly, thereby rendering it vulnerable to damage and deterioration.

69) What is the difference between absorption, adsorption, and sorption?

Absorption and adsorption are two different processes in chemistry. Absorption occurs when an atom, molecule or ion enters any bulk phase (such as gas, solid or liquid) and takes up energy from a photon. Adsorption, on the other hand, happens when a gas or liquid solute collects on the surface of a solid. While both processes involve the transfer of matter, they differ in terms of where this transfer takes place: absorption occurs in a volume, while adsorption takes place on a surface.

Absorption and adsorption are two distinct processes, where the former is a bulk process while the latter is a surface process. Sorption is an umbrella term used to describe both absorption and adsorption.
