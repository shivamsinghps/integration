Most Asked Civil Engineering Interview Questions

1) What is civil engineering?

Civil engineering is a field of professional engineering that focuses on designing, constructing, and maintaining both natural and man-made elements of the physical environment. This includes a variety of public works, such as bridges, roads, pipelines, dams, airports, and railways, as well as various structural components within buildings and sewerage systems. The goal of civil engineering is to ensure the safety and functionality of our built environment for years to come.

2) Which are the different sub-disciplines of civil engineering?

The field of civil engineering is widely regarded as the second-oldest branch of engineering, with a history dating back to early military engineering. It encompasses several specialized sub-fields, each with distinct areas of focus and expertise.

  • Construction engineering
  • Earthquake engineering
  • Environmental engineering
  • Forensic engineering
  • Geotechnical engineering
  • Materials science and engineering involves the study of materials and their properties, as well as the development of new materials for specific applications. It is a field that is concerned with the improvement of existing materials and the creation of novel materials with improved characteristics and functionality. The discipline encompasses various areas such as metallurgy, ceramics, polymers, and biomaterials, and it plays a critical role in advancing technology and innovation in various industries.

This includes projects such as water supply and distribution systems, wastewater treatment plants, road networks, public transportation systems, parks, and city buildings. Urban engineers work to ensure that cities function efficiently, are resilient to environmental hazards, and provide for the needs of all residents.
  • Water resources engineering deals with the management and use of water resources for various purposes like irrigation, drinking water, hydroelectric power generation, and wastewater treatment. 10) What is the blended cement, and what are the advantages of using blended cement?

Blended cement is produced when Ordinary Portland cement (OPC) is mixed with mineral additives or admixtures such as silica fumes, slag, or fly ash. This enhances the quality of the cement beyond that of traditional OPC.

Blended cement is beneficial in various ways, which can be classified into two main categories:

  • Technical advantages
  • Environmental advantages

The use of blended cement offers many technical advantages. Blended cement is a combination of two or more types of cement, usually Portland cement and supplementary cementitious materials like fly ash, slag, or silica fume. When mixed together, these materials enhance some of the beneficial properties of cement while reducing some of its negative effects. These technical advantages include improved workability, higher strength and durability, reduced heat of hydration, and lower permeability. Additionally, blended cement can reduce the carbon footprint of the construction industry as it requires less energy to produce and emits fewer greenhouse gases compared to standard Portland cement.

  • Blended cement has a smoother texture compared to Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC), resulting in a finer finish for mixed construction materials.
  • Compared to Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC), using a combination of fly ash and slag cement in concrete provides greater strength, particularly after a full setting period of at least 28 days, according to studies. The strength and durability of the final product depend on the quality and proportion of these admixtures. When it comes to setting time, silica fume cement is among the fastest-setting, with a typical range of 3 to 28 days.
  • Blended cement has a lower permeability compared to ordinary Portland cement (OPC). This helps to increase its durability and strength by reducing the penetration of harmful water runoff compounds like sulfates and chlorides that become more damaging over time for OPC. Silica fume cement, for instance, has only about 20% of the permeability of OPC.
  • OPC can lead to weakened concrete with cracking if there are high temperature differences between the surface and interior. Because of this, OPC should not be used in areas where temperatures drop below 40 degrees. However, blended cement can alleviate thermal stress by reducing peak temperatures.

Blended cement has numerous environmental benefits as it reduces the amount of carbon dioxide emissions generated during production. It also reduces the use of raw materials, such as limestone and clay, that would otherwise be extracted at a considerable environmental cost. Additionally, the use of blended cement can improve the durability of structures, reducing the need for frequent repairs and replacements, thereby saving resources and energy. Overall, the adoption of blended cement can contribute to a more sustainable and environmentally friendly construction industry.

  • Blended cement is known to have a lower demand for water during construction.
  • Blended cement is energy-efficient as it incorporates additives such as slag and fly ash, which are typically generated as byproducts of various industrial processes.
  • Blended cement is a sustainable building material that effectively conserves natural resources. It is created through the use of by-products generated by steel plants, coal power plants, and other industries, which would otherwise go to waste. This process reduces the demand for natural components like limestone, silica, and clay, helping to protect these resources for future generations.

53) What is segregation of concrete?

Concrete segregation happens when the cement, sand, and aggregates in the concrete separate from each other during the handling and placement of the material. This usually occurs due to factors such as an uneven water-cement ratio or over-vibration/compaction during the pouring process. Essentially, the cement paste rises to the top while the aggregates settle at the bottom. Additionally, segregation is more likely to occur when the concrete is poured at a height of more than 1.5m.

70) What is the role of compacting in concrete?

Compaction plays a crucial role in the performance of concrete. When we incorporate different materials into cement, they create air bubbles that need to be eliminated in order to make the concrete denser and stronger. 71) What is the best way of compacting?

There are various methods for compaction, such as steel rods or paddling sticks. However, mechanical vibrators are known to be the most effective for compacting. The ideal compacting device should be compact enough to pass between reinforcement rods and reach the bottom of the form.

A good practice for curing slabs or roofs is to create ponds measuring 1m x 1m and pouring enough water gently. Beams and columns are kept moist by covering them with a wet sack.
