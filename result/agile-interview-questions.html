Top 20 Agile Interview Questions and Answers

Below are some of the most appropriate questions to ask during an Agile Scrum interview. These questions, along with their answers, can aid in your preparation for the Agile Scrum interview.

1) What is an agile or agile methodology?

Agile is a software development methodology that operates in iterative cycles lasting from 1 to 4 weeks. It facilitates the delivery of evolving business requirements throughout the development process.

2) What are some quality strategies of agile?

Here are a few effective approaches employed in agile methodology:

  • Iteration
  • Re-factoring
  • Dynamic code analysis
  • Short feedback cycles
  • Reviews and inspection
  • Standards and guidelines
  • Milestone reviews

3) What are an agile manifesto and its principle?

The Agile manifesto provides guidelines for efficient software development practices through collaboration and continual improvement. It involves 4 key principles and 12 guiding values that outline the best approach to software development.

  • The promotion of self-motivation and self-organization among individuals and their interactions should be encouraged.
  • A good practice in software development is to regularly showcase the functioning software accompanied by thorough documentation. This can aid in ensuring transparency and effective communication between the development team and stakeholders.
  • Collaboration with customers is valued over negotiating contact terms.
  • Being adaptable to changes rather than rigidly sticking to a predetermined plan.

The Agile Manifesto outlines a set of principles that guide agile software development processes. These principles were created to help software development teams deliver high-quality software quickly, while adapting to changing requirements and needs.

  1. Customer Satisfaction: Manifesto provides high priority to satisfy the costumer's requirements. Customer satisfaction is done through early and continuous delivery of valuable software.
  2. Welcome Change: Making change during software development is common and inevitable. Every changing requirement should be welcome, even in the late development phase. Agile process is used to increase the customer's competitive advantage.
  3. Create operational software that is functional and meets the requirements. Deliver the working software frequently, ranging from a few weeks to a few months with considering the shortest period.

4) Is there any disadvantage of the agile model (SDLC)?

The drawbacks of Agile Software Development Life Cycle are as follows:

  1. It is important for the development team to have a strong level of professionalism and prioritize the needs and goals of the client.
  2. It is possible that the introduction of a new requirement could pose a challenge to the current architecture in place.
  3. It is possible for the project to exceed its estimated time if additional adjustments and modifications need to be made.
  4. It can be challenging to predict the ultimate cost of the project since there may be constant iterations occurring.
  5. There is a lack of a specified need or request.

5) What are the burn-up and burn-down chart?

The burn-up and burn-down charts are tools used to track a project's progress. The burn-up chart shows the amount of work completed while the burn-down chart shows the remaining amount of work. Together, these charts provide an overview of the project's progress report.

6) What do you understand by Daily Stand-Up?

The daily stand-up is a brief everyday meeting that usually takes place in the morning. During this meeting, the entire team gathers for approximately 15 minutes to answer three questions. These questions help to keep everyone informed about the progress being made and any obstacles that need to be addressed.

  • Can you provide me with more context or information on what specific content you are referring to? I can assist you better if I have a better understanding of the content.
  • Can you please provide me with the details of your schedule for today?
  • Are there any hindrances preventing you from accomplishing your objective?

7) What do you understand about Scrum?

Scrum is a framework that facilitates the collaboration of agile teams to efficiently create, deliver and maintain a complex product within a considerably short time period. The resulting product delivered by the scrum team sprint is referred to as a sprint.

8) What are the different roles in Scrum?

Scrum has three distinct roles, which are the Scrum Master, Product Owner, and the Agile Development Team. Each of these roles has specific responsibilities in the agile development process.

  • Scrum Master: The Scrum Master is a team leader and facility provider who help the team member to follow agile practices so that they can meet their commitments and customers requirements.
  • Product Owner: The Product Owner is one who runs the product from a business perspective. He defines the requirements and prioritizes their values.
  • Agile Development Team: Agile development team provides the judgment on the technical feasibilities or any dependencies.

9) What are the responsibilities of the Scrum Master?

The primary duty of a Scrum Master is to...

  • Observing and overseeing the advancement of a project.
  • To effectively comprehend the requirements of the user.
  • Make an effort to complete the project accurately.
  • Enhancing the effectiveness of the group by increasing their efficiency, productivity, and overall success.
  • Conducted assemblies and addressed concerns to achieve resolutions.
  • Provide updates and feedback to both the customer and the development team.

10) What are different ceremonies and their importance in Scrum?

The main goal of Scrum ceremonies is to effectively communicate the Scrum planning, Scrum review, Scrum Daily stand up, and scrum retrospective. These ceremonies are crucial in tailoring the sprint to fit the needs of your specific project.

11) What do you know about Scrum ban?

Scrum-ban is a hybrid model of software development that combines the Scrum and Kanban frameworks. It's effective for projects that require ongoing maintenance, frequent programming errors, or sudden changes.

12) What do you understand by the term agile testing?

Agile testing is a software testing technique that is based on the principles of agile software development. It follows an iterative approach, where the collaboration between the product owner and development team results in the creation of user stories.

13) What are the major principles of agile testing?

Agile testing involves following specific principles that are necessary for effective implementation. Some of these principles include:

  • Customer satisfaction

  • Sustainable development
  • Continuous feedback
  • Provide prompt reactions to alterations.
  • Successive improvement
  • Self-organized
  • Focus on essence
  • Error-free clean node
  • Collective work

14) What are the skills of a good agile tester?

An agile tester is a professional who applies the principles of agile software development to software testing. The ideal agile tester possesses specific skills, including:

  • The individual should possess knowledge and understanding of the principles and philosophy of agile methodology.
  • Having outstanding communication skills is essential for a project manager as they need to effectively communicate with their team and clients.
  • He has the ability to prioritize tasks based on the needs and expectations of the customers.
  • One essential trait for a successful salesperson is the ability to comprehend the needs of the customer accurately.
  • It's important for him to be aware of the potential project risk caused by fluctuations in demand.

15) Name the agile frameworks.

Here are some examples of agile frameworks:

  • Scrum
  • Kanban
  • Feature Driven Development
  • Test Driven Development

16) Is it ever suggested to use waterfall over Scrum? If yes, explain when.

Occasionally, we opt for a waterfall model instead of scrum. This choice is based on the nature of the client's needs - specifically, if their requirements are straightforward, concise, clearly defined, and predictable. In these cases, we can anticipate that the project will remain relatively static throughout its entirety.

17) Name some methodologies and development where you have used the agile model.

When answering a question about methodologies, it is important to only discuss ones that you have experience with. Agile is a methodology that is commonly used in various contexts, so it may be relevant to mention it when appropriate.

  • Crystal methodologies
  • Lean software development
  • Dynamic development
  • Feature-driven development

18) What was the length of sprints/iterations in your project?

This is a frequently asked query among seasoned professionals. Its aim is to ascertain the kind of work environment you have experience working in. Additionally, you may be asked if you have ever worked with a duration shorter or longer than the one you have been accustomed to.

19) What is the difference between the agile & traditional way of working?

The traditional way of development is that which follow the sequential where design -> development -> testing etc. is performed whereas, in agile development, all of this is done in every iteration/sprint.

20) Why does Scrum encourage the use of automated testing for projects?

The Scrum development methodology promotes the use of automated testing for faster project delivery. This means that various automated testing tools should be utilized in the process. Can you discuss some of the automated testing tools you have worked with?
