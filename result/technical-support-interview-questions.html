Most Asked Technical Support Interview Questions

Here are some commonly asked technical support interview questions with suggested answers that can be helpful for anyone preparing for such an interview.

1) What do you think about the role of a technical support engineer?

A technical support engineer is responsible for ensuring the smooth functioning of an organization's computers and networks. They may offer remote assistance to clients of the organization as well.

A technical support engineer's job entails performing certain duties that include: - Providing assistance to customers with technical issues - Diagnosing and resolving technical problems - Maintaining and updating technical documentation - Collaborating with other teams to solve complex technical issues - Testing and evaluating new software and hardware technologies. These tasks are vital for ensuring that a company's technical infrastructure runs efficiently, and customers receive necessary technical support when required.

  • Set up and customize the operating system, hardware, programs, and online tools.
  • The task involves the supervision and maintenance of networks and computer systems to ensure they are in good condition and functioning optimally.
  • Access the system to view inquiries from both customers and employees.
  • Evaluate and uncover hidden problems.
  • Locate and fix any problems that may arise from either the computer's physical components or its programs.
  • Assessing novel innovations and appraising their effectiveness.
  • Conduct inspections to ensure safety measures are in place and functioning properly. Carry out necessary safety protocols and procedures to minimize hazards and risks.

Ping is a valuable tool for network administrators to ensure connectivity with an IP address. This computer software utility is used to verify the availability of a host on an IP network and is present in almost all modern operating systems that offer networking capabilities. It is also common in embedded network administration software.

An IP address serves as a distinct numerical label assigned to each device connected to a computer network. It consists of a series of numbers, such as 216.27.61.141, which is exclusive to a particular network. This feature provides a unique identity to each network connection.

13) What is a Data Cable? Why is it used?

A Data Cable is a slender cable made of plastic that serves to link various devices such as Hard Disk Drives (HDD), Floppy Disk Drives, and CD/DVD-ROM drives to the motherboard. It finds most of its application in the transfer of data.

14) What is a Microprocessor? What is its use?

A microprocessor is a type of device that is controlled by programs, and is used to retrieve data instructions from memory. The instructions are decoded and solved, and then executed in order to continue with the processing.

15) What is the disadvantage of microprocessors?

A microprocessor has certain limitations, with the most significant being the restricted size of data it can handle. Additionally, it is not capable of performing floating-point operations.

16) Why 8085 microprocessor is called an 8-bit processor?

The 8085 microprocessor is given the name 8-bit processor due to its 8-bit ALU (Arithmetic Logic Unit), which contributes to its computing capacity.

17) What do lights indicate on a Modem / LAN Card?

In most cases, a modem or LAN card has four lights that signify different things.

  • Power Light: The power light is used to show if the device (Modem) is getting Power Supply or not.
  • Link Light: The link light is used to indicate if the device is getting broadband/internet signals properly from the ISP.
  • Data Light: The data light is used to indicate whether the internet is working or not.
  • Connectivity Light: The connectivity light indicates that the Modem is connected to a PC or not.

The interviewer may ask you about the main components of desktop and laptop computers in order to assess your fundamental computer knowledge. These primary hardware components include the motherboard, processor, RAM, monitor, mouse, soundcard, keyboard, graphics, hard disk drive, power supply, and floppy disk drive, among others.

20) What do you understand by stack? Can we use ROM as a stack?

The stack is a vital section of the computer's memory, specifically the RAM, where the program counter and general-purpose registers are stored. It is not possible to use ROM as a stack since ROM cannot be written on.

21) What do you understand by Hard-Disk Partitions?

A Hard-Disk Partition is a segmentation technique applied to Hard-disk drives in order to create smaller sections as per user requirements. This technique is employed to enhance space management on the device.

22) What is the use of Latch?

A latch is a type of storage device that can temporarily hold a single binary value, either a 0 or a 1, and it's triggered by a timing signal. This device functions similarly to a D-type flip-flop as it's able to store digital information.

23) What do you understand by DHCP?

DHCP is a widely used networking protocol that automates the IP address assignment process. Hosts that are connected to a network can be dynamically allocated an IP address by the DHCP server.

24) What do you understand by the OSI model?

The OSI model, short for Open System Interconnection, functions as a standard description or conceptual model that outlines communication functions within computing and telecommunication systems. It offers a guide on how communication between two points within a telecommunication network should be carried out. The model is made up of different layers that provide services to the layer above it. The ISO, or International Organization of Standardization, developed the OSI model in 1984.

25) What are the seven different layers of the OSI model? Explain its architecture in detail.

The architecture of the OSI model is composed of seven layers that function together to transfer data from one individual to another anywhere in the world.

Here is the rephrased content: The OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) model consists of seven layers. These layers are arranged in a hierarchical structure that facilitates communication between different devices and networks. Understanding each layer's function is important in designing and troubleshooting network systems.

  1. Physical Layer: The physical layer is the lowest layer of the OSI reference model. It is responsible for the actual physical connection between the devices. This layer contains information in the form of bits and transmits individual bits from one node to the next. After receiving the data, this layer gets a signal received, converts it into 0s and 1s, and sends them to the Data Link layer.
  2. Data Link Layer: The data link layer is used to transmit messages form node to node. This layer ensures that the data transfer is error-free from one node to another over the physical layer. The Data Link Layer is divided into two sub-layers: Logical Link Control (LLC) and Media Access Control (MAC).
  3. Network Layer: The network layer is responsible for transmitting data from one host to the other located in different networks. It is also used for packet routing.
  4. Transport Layer: The transport layer is used to make communication between the application layer and network layer. The data in the transport layer is called Segments. It ensures the End to End Delivery of the complete message and provides an acknowledgment of the successful data transmission, and re-transmits the data if an error is found.
  5. Session Layer: The Session layer is used for connection establishment, maintenance of sessions, and authentication. It is also responsible for ensuring security.
  6. Presentation Layer: The Presentation layer is responsible for extracting the data from the application layer and manipulating it as per the required format to transmit over the network. The main functions of the presentation layer are translation, encryption/ decryption, and compression. It is also called the translation layer.
  7. Application Layer: The Application layer is the topmost layer of the OSI Reference Model. The network applications implement it. These applications produce the data, which has to be transferred over the network. This layer also acts as a window for the application services to access the web and display the received information to the user. That's why this layer is called the desktop layer.

26) What do you understand by TCP/IP? What does it do?

TCP/IP is an abbreviation for Transmission Control Protocol or Internet protocol. This collection of communication protocols connects various network devices on the internet. It serves as a way to transfer data over the network and allows for connections between hosts on the internet. Additionally, it can function as a communication protocol for private computer networks, intranets, and extranets.

27) How would you resolve this issue if audio is not working or there is no sound from your computer?

The following steps can be taken to fix the problem of audio malfunctioning or no sound emanating from your computer:

  • Verify the state of your cable connections.
  • Ensure that the speakers are receiving power.
  • Ensure that the device has a feature for controlling the volume.
  • Evaluate the presence and compatibility of software components that enable a computer to communicate with its hardware devices to ensure optimal system functionality.

28) What are the usages of RJ45 and RJ11 connectors?

RJ45 connectors are commonly used for connecting devices to a LAN or internet network, whereas RJ11 connectors are typically used for connecting devices to a telephone or modem using a traditional phone cable.

29) What do you understand by Cache memory? What is the advantage of a processor having more cache memory?

Cache memory serves as a buffer between the central processing unit (CPU) and the random access memory (RAM). It is a high-speed memory that temporarily stores requested data and instructions to be immediately accessed by the CPU. This type of memory aids in reducing the time required to access data from the main memory. As the capacity of the cache memory increases, so does the speed of the system. Thus, a processor with a higher cache memory capacity operates faster.

30) What is the full form of SATA? What is its usage?

SATA is an acronym for Serial Advanced Technology Attachment and is a computer bus interface that enables rapid transfer of data between storage devices such as hard disk drives and the computer's central circuit board. This interface superseded the PATA (Parallel Advanced Technology Attachment) interface, which had been in use for a long time.

31) What do you understand by Jumper, and why is it required?

A jumper is a small conductor used in electronics to bridge or disconnect certain parts of an electronic circuit. Its primary function is to complete the electrical circuit and alter the specifications of the board. Jumpers are commonly employed to configure and set up printed circuit boards in computer systems, particularly motherboards.

32) What is the difference between SDK and an API?

Here are the major distinctions between SDK and an API:

SDK API
SDK refers to a set of tools, utilities, and libraries that software developers use to create new software applications. An API is an acronym that stands for Application Programming Interface.
An SDK is a collection of resources that is generally provided by a software company to developers so that they can build applications using specific platforms. These resources typically include development tools, sample code, libraries, documentation, and processes. An API is a software interface that enables two different programs to communicate with each other.
An SDK provides developers with a comprehensive platform to build applications beyond the capabilities of API. An API is capable of interpreting and transmitting dissimilar sets of instructions to enable communication and mutual comprehension between two entities.
An SDK is an essential component in the development of most software applications. It serves as the foundation for creating and running programs that we rely on in our daily lives. The application programming interface (API) can vary in terms of its design and implementation. In certain instances, it can be as simple as copying and pasting code.
An SDK may include an API within it. The World Wide Web relies on API for a wide range of functions. API enables seamless communication between different systems, specifically designed for particular use cases.

33) What are the different types of DRAM, and what is its usage?

DRAM is a kind of RAM that stands for Dynamic Random-Access Memory. It is utilized by a computer processor to work with data or program code. DRAM is a prevalent kind of semiconductor memory used in servers, workstations, and personal computers. There are several types of DRAM, such as SGRAM, VRAM, DDR-SDRAM, and SRAM, among others.

36) What is a heat sink, and what is its usage in the system?

A heat sink is an essential part of a computer's cooling system that helps to dissipate the heat generated by a device, such as the microprocessor. Inadequate cooling can result in the computer shutting down automatically to prevent overheating, so a reliable heat sink is crucial.

38) What do you understand by chipset? How is it different from processor and Motherboard?

Chipset is an essential component in a computer that helps to manage the flow of information between the processor and other components. It's comprised of a series of microchips that work together to perform specific functions. Unlike the processor and motherboard, chipset is an integrated feature of the motherboard that enables efficient communication between the computer's various parts.

A processor is an essential component in a computer system that performs functions based on a set of programmed instructions. It is responsible for logical, arithmetic and input/output tasks within the system.

The Motherboard is the central hub where all computer components are connected, including the CPU, memory, and other external devices such as drives and connectors.

40) What are the advantages and disadvantages of using Imaging Software?

The following are the benefits and drawbacks of utilizing imaging software:

Benefits of utilizing imaging software.

  • To duplicate content from one hard disk to another, it is possible to utilize Imaging Software to produce an exact duplicate.
  • It has the capability of transmitting intricate drive images to multiple systems over the network at the same time.
  • The resizing of file system partitions can be performed using a utility that has knowledge of the individual partitions of the file system. This can be accomplished for a variety of file systems.

Imaging software has disadvantages that should be considered before deciding to use it.

  • Due to its lack of comprehensive understanding of file systems, the imaging tool performs a block-by-block duplication of the source hard disk. Consequently, the process of copying the entire hard disk into an image file is time-consuming.
  • The process of using disk defragmentation is time-consuming, especially for larger disks, as it operates at a slow pace.
  • This feature allows for the detection and recovery of errors during the creation and deployment of images, providing a level of resilience in the process.
  • High-quality imaging software can come with a hefty price tag as the best options are typically sold commercially and can be expensive for users to purchase.

41) What do you understand by Ghost Imaging? Why is it used?

Ghost imaging is a technique that involves copying the contents of a disk onto another server in the form of a compressed file or set of files, which is commonly referred to as an image. This process is facilitated through software and is often used as a backup measure. If necessary, a ghost image can also be restored back to its original form. Ghost imaging is particularly useful during system reinstallation, and is sometimes referred to as cloning.

Ghost imaging has various applications as it can be utilized for several reasons, such as:

  • A cloning or restoration tool is employed to facilitate the process of replicating a system onto multiple devices or restoring a system swiftly.
  • This approach is frequently employed to rapidly configure clusters of Tablets, Notebooks, or Servers. It's also utilized to facilitate the migration from one computer or disk to another.

42) What do you know about Disk Partition? How many partitions can a hard drive have?

Disk partitioning is a process of creating separate spaces on a hard drive to store data in an organized way. This is important to ensure that the data is efficiently managed and easily accessed. It is recommended that users store operating system data and applications on one partition and user data on another. This is useful in case of any issues with the operating system, as the partition with the OS can be easily reformatted and reinstalled without affecting the data partition.

There is a limit of up to four primary partitions that can be created on a hard disk. Among these, we can choose to allocate three primary partitions and one extended partition. The extended partition allows for the creation of multiple logical partitions within it, offering greater flexibility in terms of storage and organization.

43) What do you understand by BOOT.INI? What are the different sections in it?

The BOOT.INI is an important initialization file used by Microsoft Windows NT, 2000, and XP. It stores the boot options and is typically found in the root directory of the primary hard drive, usually the C drive.

BOOT.INI comprises of two parts or sections:

  • The configuration settings in the boot loader section are applicable to all boot entries of the system, such as default settings, timeout values, and more.
  • The operating systems section of a computer's boot menu contains entries for each installed OS or bootable program that allows the user to choose which one to launch on start-up.
