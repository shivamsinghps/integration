11) What types of vouchers can be created in Tally ERP 9? What are the shortcuts for creating these vouchers?

Here are the types of vouchers that can be generated on Tally ERP 9:

  • Contra Voucher: This voucher is used to point to the transfer of funds from a bank account to the cash account or cash account to a bank account or in a different bank account of another bank. The shortcut key for creating this voucher is F4.
  • Payment Voucher): This voucher explains the historical payments on each account like payment of salary, rental, etc. The shortcut key for creating this voucher is F5.
  • Receipt Voucher): This voucher describes the transactions that relate to payments received. The shortcut key for creating this voucher is F6.
  • Journal Voucher): This voucher is used to cover the expenses to maintain the organization's running costs. The shortcut key for creating this voucher is F7.
  • Sales/Invoice Voucher: This voucher describes the sales made. It also displays the sale invoice. The shortcut key for creating this voucher is F8.
  • Credit Note Voucher: Entry is made in this voucher when a customer returns the products which weren't correctly dispensed. The shortcut key for creating this voucher is Cntrl+F8.
  • Purchase Voucher): This voucher is for items purchased for running the business like raw materials, etc. The shortcut key for creating this voucher is F9.
  • Debit Note Voucher): This voucher is used for keeping records when goods are returned to suppliers due to any damages. The shortcut key for creating this voucher is Cntrl+F9.
  • Reversing Journals): This voucher is a special entry that gets automatically reversed after the date of the issue. The shortcut key for creating this voucher is F10.
  • Memo Voucher): This voucher is used to mark the unknown expense at a moment which can be converted to an actual sale voucher or deleted. The shortcut key for creating this voucher is Cntrl+F10.

12) What do you understand by deferred tax liability? Deferred taxation is a part of which equity?

A deferred tax liability is an accounting term used to describe a future tax obligation that a company owes but is not yet due for payment. The term "deferred" refers to the time difference between when the tax liability was recognized and the due date for payment. This record is included in the company's balance sheet as part of its equity ownership.

46) Where can we find the sales tax on the balance sheet in Tally ERP 9 software?

The tool designed for migrating data from Tally 7.2 to Tally 8.2 is called the Tally Data Migration Tool. It is compatible with the Tally 8.1 executable and enables the migration of company data like Masters, Vouchers, and Reports. Additionally, it also supports the migration of TCP files.

56) How can you activate the GST in Tally ERP 9 software?

To set up the GST feature in Tally ERP 9, navigate to the Statutory and Taxation section. Then, follow the steps provided to activate GST in the software.

  • To begin, access the Company Operation Alteration screen and activate GST. Then, select the option to modify the GST details.
  • To begin the process of registering for a GST, you need to go to the GST portal and fill out the necessary forms. This includes providing your business details such as name, address, and PAN number. Once this is done, you will receive a temporary reference number (TRN) which you will use to log in to the portal. From here, you will need to provide additional information such as the name of the state, registration type, GSTIN number for invoices, and more. It is important to include all relevant details about your business, including tax liability, GST rate, and e-way bill applicability.
  • Please make sure to save the modifications after accepting them.
  • It is now possible to use all the features of GST in Tally ERP 9, as it has been activated for general ledgers, stock items, and transactions.
