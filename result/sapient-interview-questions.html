About the Company: Sapient

Sapient is an international firm that offers consulting and marketing services to its clients in areas such as technology, business, and marketing. The company was founded on November 6, 1990, and its headquarters is located in Boston, Massachusetts, in the USA. Sapient is divided into three units: SapientNitro, Sapient Global Market, and Sapient Government Services. They specialize in digital marketing strategies, providing technology-based business solutions, and partnering with public organizations like healthcare and educational institutions to deliver creative and strategic services. Sapient has a global workforce of over 11,900 employees.

Key People:

  • Founders: Jerry Greenberg and Stuart Moore
  • CEO and Co-Chairman: Alan J. Herrick

Sapient Recruitment Process

The Sapient recruiting process involves three key stages.

Individuals are required to successfully pass each round of the selection process in order to proceed to the subsequent round.

The primary stages are:

  1. First Round: Online Written Exam
  2. Second Round: Technical Interview
  3. Third Round: HR Interview

Academic Criteria

  1. To be eligible for applying at Sapient, one must have scored a minimum of 6.5 CGPA or 60-65% marks in Class X, XII and Graduation combined.
  2. The difference in education levels should not exceed one year.
  3. The ideal candidate for this position would have a degree in either B.E. / B. Tech or MCA.

First Round: Written Round

In the initial stage, candidates are required to take a written test online that contains four sections.

  • Verbal Ability
  • Quantitative Aptitude
  • Technical aptitude
  • Logical reasoning

In the initial phase of the recruitment process, candidates undergo a screening round where they are evaluated on their logical and quantitative abilities as well as their understanding of fundamental technical concepts. While the difficulty level of this stage is moderate, applicants are expected to be quick on their feet.

13) Differentiate between overloading and overriding?





Method Overloading
Method Overriding




Method overloading refers to a situation in a class where various methods have the same name, but their parameters or return types differ.
Method overriding refers to when a subclass has a method that is identical in terms of name, parameters and return type to a method in the superclass. This allows the subclass to provide its own implementation of the method while still having access to the properties and methods of the superclass.




Method overloading is a technique that enables us to achieve compile-time polymorphism. This means that at the time of compilation, the appropriate method is selected based on the number or types of arguments passed to it.
One way to achieve polymorphism at runtime is through method overriding. This involves defining a method in a subclass that has the same name and parameters as a method in its superclass, thereby replacing the superclass method with the subclass method during runtime. This allows for different behavior of the same method depending on the class instance it is invoked upon.




Method overloading requires that the methods in question belong to the same class.
For method overriding to occur, there needs to be a hierarchical relationship between methods, with one serving as the parent and the other as the child.




When implementing method overloading in Java or any programming language, it's important to ensure that the parameters used in the different methods must be unique or different from each other. This helps to distinguish one method from another and allows the compiler to identify which method to execute based on the arguments passed to it.
In method overriding, it is essential for the method parameter to match in both the parent and child class method definition. This means that the child class cannot change the parameter list of the method defined in the parent class.




In method overloading, it is possible to have the same return type or a different return type for the methods.
When overriding a method, the return type should match the original method's return type.
