The SSB is a group that assesses and interviews potential officers for the Indian Armed Forces. It is established by the Defense Ministry of India and aims to determine whether the candidates possess the necessary qualities to become an officer. It is focused on evaluating whether the individuals have the right attributes to fulfill the role.

6) Is the AFCAT exam the same as the NDA exam and CDS exam?

AFCAT, which stands for Air Force Common Admission Test, is a separate exam from the NDA and CDS exams. It is solely for individuals who want to join the Indian Air Force and allows them to apply for positions in flying, technical, or ground duty branches. The exam is held twice a year and is conducted by the Indian Air Force.

The Indian Air Force conducts exams to recruit candidates for various positions. The Air Force Common Admission Test is one such exam, which is conducted to recruit officers in different branches of the Indian Air Force. The exam tests a candidate's knowledge in General Aptitude, Reasoning, Numerical Ability, Verbal Ability, and Military Aptitude. The test is followed by a physical fitness test, group discussion, and personal interview. The candidates who pass all these rounds and fulfill the eligibility criteria join the Indian Air Force. In contrast, the National Defense Academy (NDA) and Combined Defense Services (CDS) exams are conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) to recruit eligible candidates into the Army, Navy, or Air Force, depending on their choice and merit. The NDA and CDS exams are specifically for those who want to become pilots in the Indian Air Force. For other branches of the Indian Air Force, candidates need to apply for AFCAT.
