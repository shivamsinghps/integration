Digital Electronics Interview Questions

Here are some popular Digital Electronics Interview Questions and answers that are frequently asked.

1) What is the difference between Latch And Flip-flop?

Latches and flip-flops are two types of electronic circuit components used in digital systems. The main difference between them is in the way they respond to the input signal. Latches are level triggered which means they change the output as the input changes. On the other hand, flip-flops are edge triggered which means they only respond to a change in the control signal from high to low or low to high.

Latches are known for their fast performance, while flip-flops are generally slower in operation.

22) Define Fan-in and Fan-out?

Fan-in refers to the ability of a gate to receive multiple inputs without affecting the voltage level of the system. It indicates the maximum number of inputs that a gate can handle without degrading its performance.

The Fan-out refers to the maximum number of identical inputs that a gate from a particular IC family can drive while still keeping its output levels within the specified limits.

The term "period time" refers to the amount of electrical power consumed by the logic circuits. This value is typically measured in units of milliwatts or nanowatts.

The amount of power dissipated by a device can be calculated by multiplying the supply voltage by the average current drawn from the power source.

27) What is a Multiplexer?

A multiplexer is an electronic device that merges digital data from multiple sources into a single output stream.

  3. These devices play a crucial role in the data acquisition system.

29) What is a Demultiplexer?

A demultiplexer is an electronic circuit that has the ability to receive an input signal through a single line and transmit it onto multiple output lines. This is achieved by using n select lines to choose which output line is to be connected to the input. It is also commonly known as a data distributor.

32) Define Rise Time?

The rise time indicates the amount of time taken to switch the voltage level from 10% to 90%.

33) Define fall time?

The fall time refers to the duration needed to transition the voltage level from 90% to 10%.

34) Define Setup time?

The Setup time is the amount of time needed to maintain consistent voltage levels at the excitation inputs of a flip-flop device before the triggering edge of the clock pulse, to ensure reliable clocking of the levels. This time is denoted as tsetup.

35) Define Hold time?

The Hold time, represented by thold, refers to the minimum duration required for the voltage level to stabilize after the clock pulse is triggered. This duration is essential to ensure reliable operation of the flip flop.
