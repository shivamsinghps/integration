1) What is ITIL?

ITIL is an acronym for Information Technology Infrastructure Library. It comprises a range of practices and principles that enable software developers to offer high-quality IT services that meet the demands of the current market. These guidelines also help businesses establish an advantage over their rivals and accomplish their objectives efficiently. They include effective planning, management, and delivery processes.

4) What are the processes involved in ITIL Service life cycle model?

The ITIL Service life cycle model includes five primary processes that are involved in managing services. These processes are:

  • Service Strategy: This process helps implement, define and maintain service strategies, rules, and policies.
  • Service Design: This process helps to design the processes to ensure the proper implementation of strategies.
  • Service Transition: This process helps form communication between projects and operations to effectively improve service quality for better service transitions.
  • Service Operation: This process helps create end-to-end process overflow to provide stable services to the end-user.
  • Continual Service Improvement: This process is mainly used to improve service quality by adding new options and features to meet the current industry standards.

22) What is the primary function of Configuration Management in ITIL?

In ITIL, Configuration Management is responsible for receiving, storing, managing, updating and verifying information related to IT assets and configurations within an organization. It ensures that accurate and up-to-date information is available for effective decision-making.

A service request refers to a formal request made by a user to access IT services or modify its standards. Conversely, a change request is a proposal to modify a particular product or the entire system.

28) How will you differentiate an end-user from a customer?

An end-user, also referred to as an end-customer, is the individual who directly utilizes or receives a service or product.

A client may be empowered or not have the authority to make decisions on product and supplier selection.

29) How will you define an Incident and a Problem?

An incident is an event that deviates from the normal course of operations. It is an unexpected occurrence that disrupts the delivery of an IT service.

The term "problem" typically refers to the root cause of a dispute or series of incidents.

30) How will you differentiate proactive problem management from reactive problem management?

Proactive problem management aims to prevent incidents by identifying potential issues and detecting errors in IT infrastructure before they occur. In contrast, reactive problem management focuses on identifying the root causes of known incidents and resolving them.

31) What is the primary function of ITIL Change Management?

The main purpose of change management is to minimize or eliminate potential risks in business processes and systems by implementing consistent and standardized procedures. This crucial responsibility is typically handled by a Change Manager, who executes it in an agile and efficient manner. By doing so, change management guarantees that projects meet their objectives and necessary criteria within the designated timeframe and budget.

32) List down the Seven R's of Change Management.

Here are seven key concepts known as the "Seven R's of Change Management":

  • First, who RAISED the change?
  • Second, what is the REASON for the change?
  • Third, what RETURN will the change deliver?
  • Fourth, what RISKS are there is we do or do not carry out the change?
  • Fifth, What RESOURCES will be required to perform this change?
  • Sixth, who is RESPONSIBLE for this change being performed?
  • Seventh, what RELATIONSHIPS are there between this and other changes?

33) What is defined by the term PIR in the context of ITIL?

The acronym PIR stands for Post Implementation Review in the context of ITIL. Essentially, it involves analyzing the entire process and solution once a change request has been implemented into the system. The purpose of the review is to determine the effectiveness of the implementation after it has been put into action. It is a flexible report that is typically conducted after the project has gone live.

34) What is the primary goal of Capacity Management? Also, list its sub-processes.

Capacity management aims to ensure that IT services and resources are effectively sized to meet the service level objectives of the business, both presently and in the future. This involves cost-effective and timely planning to accommodate changing needs.

The process of Capacity Management consists of three sub-processes:

  • Service Capacity Management
  • Component Management
  • Business Capacity Management

35) What is meant by the term OLA?

An OLA refers to an acronym for Operational Level Agreement, which is a contractual agreement outlining the relationship and responsibilities between different IT teams within an organization. It specifies how the IT groups will design and deliver their services to support SLAs (Service Level Agreements).

36) What are some commonly used Recovery options?

The most frequently utilized recovery choices include:

The Fast Recovery option is a quick recovery method that occurs within a day and is sometimes referred to as hot standby.

An intermediate recovery mode typically lasts for a few days and is known as warm standby. This recovery mode involves load balancing with mirroring and may take up to three or four days to complete.

A gradual recovery is a recovery process that can take a long time, usually more than 24 hours. It is also known as a cold standby. This type of recovery may last for a few weeks.

37) What does the Freeze period mean in ITIL?

The freeze period refers to a particular stage in the development process wherein modifying the source code becomes significantly challenging.

38) What is defined by the term CAB?

A Change Advisory Board (CAB) is a group of experts who provide assistance with Change Management. They evaluate, approve, prioritize, and determine the scheduling of requested changes. The members of CAB represent different authorities and organizations to ensure that necessary changes are implemented smoothly.

39) Define the following terms: Service pipeline, Service portfolio, and Service catalog.

A service pipeline refers to a list of services that are still under development and not yet ready for use or implementation.

A service portfolio refers to a comprehensive collection of services provided by a particular service provider, which could be for the benefit of their customers or the wider market.

A Service Catalog is a comprehensive list of services that have been developed to cater to the needs of customers. It is a part of the larger Service portfolio.

40) Define Expedite/Urgent Changes and Emergency Changes.

Expedite change refers to a type of change that fulfills a specific legal or business requirement rather than addressing a restoration service.

An emergency change is a type of change in an organization that requires urgent attention and swift implementation. It is given high priority and is considered as a critical change that needs to be handled quickly.
