Most Asked Electrical Engineering Interview Questions

Below are some commonly asked questions for an Electrical Engineering Interview along with their potential answers.

Basic Interview Questions

1) What do you understand by the term electrical engineering? What are its different applications?

Electrical engineering is a field that involves the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. It mainly focuses on the use of high voltage, particularly AC above the typical range of 110 or 220V. Electrical machines, components, and devices utilize AC voltage and are often larger in size, requiring 230V (in the UK) and 110V (in the US) single-phase AC voltages. Industries and power stations may require voltages of up to 11kV, while transmission may require voltages exceeding 400kV.

Electrical engineering has a range of applications, encompassing various fields. Some of the diverse applications include:

  • Electrical Power Systems
  • Electrical Control Systems
  • Possible rephrased content: Diodes, rectifiers, and transformers are important components in electrical circuits. A diode is a semiconductor device that allows current to flow in one direction only, typically used to convert AC (alternating current) to DC (direct current) or to protect other components from reverse current. A rectifier is a circuit that uses one or more diodes to convert AC to DC, by removing the negative part of the waveform and letting only the positive part pass. A transformer, on the other hand, is a device composed of two or more coils of wire that are magnetically coupled, used to transfer electrical energy from one circuit to another, often with a change in voltage or current. Transformers can step up or step down the voltage depending on the ratio of turns in the coils, and are widely used in power supplies, electric motors, and other applications. Understanding the principles and characteristics of diodes, rectifiers, and transformers is essential for designing and troubleshooting circuits.
  • Digital Circuits
  • The following is an original rephrased version of content about AC and DC motors: AC and DC motors are devices that convert electrical energy into mechanical energy to generate rotational motion. AC motors are commonly used in household appliances, such as fans, while DC motors are used in machines that require precise speed control, such as robotics. Both AC and DC motors can operate on different power sources, including batteries and power grids. However, AC motors are typically safer and more efficient than DC motors, which can generate sparking and require more maintenance.
  • AC and DC generators are two types of machines that generate electricity. They both work by converting mechanical energy into electrical energy. The main difference between the two is the way in which they produce electrical current. AC generators produce alternating current, while DC generators produce direct current. AC generators are commonly used in power plants and electrical grids, while DC generators are used in applications such as battery charging and welding. Despite their differences, both types of generators play an important role in providing electricity for various systems and devices.
  • Instrumentation Systems
  • Communication Systems
  • Here are some examples of topics that fall under the area of electronics: Digital Electronics, Analog Electronics, etc.

2) What are the primary responsibilities of an electrical engineer?

An electrical engineer's main duties consist of designing, testing, and monitoring electrical components for various projects. The field is diverse, ranging from product manufacturing to power grid design, and as a result, the tasks may differ depending on the position.

The main duties and obligations of an electrical engineer consist of:

  • Repairing and addressing issues with electronic gadgets.
  • Performing the tests to verify if the functions are working correctly.
  • Assessing the present patterns and data.
  • Creating procedures for the production and construction of electric components and systems.

3) What are the critical skills required to become an electrical engineer?

An electrical engineer's primary responsibility is the maintenance and repair of electrical system components. They are responsible for preventing and resolving any issues or malfunctions that occur within the system. A capable electrical engineer must perform a range of tasks to ensure that they can efficiently troubleshoot and resolve any issues that may arise.

  • Examine information to identify significant patterns or developments.
  • To effectively obtain decisions from supervisors and clients, it is important to communicate in a clear and concise manner. This can help ensure that the decision-making process is efficient and productive.
  • Stay informed about the latest developments and trends within the sector to keep up with the ever-evolving landscape of the industry.
  • Follow the safety regulations and guidelines stringently.
  • Collaborate closely with team leaders and higher-ups.

4) Why did you decide to become an electrical engineer? / What made you want to study electrical engineering?

The reason why interviewers often ask the question "What inspired you to pursue a career in electrical engineering?" is to gauge your level of interest and passion for the field, as well as to gain a better understanding of your background and how it relates to your chosen profession. This is one of the most commonly asked questions during electrical engineering interviews. Your response should touch on your educational background, your interest in electrical engineering, and your personal experiences in the field. Be sure to highlight your enthusiasm for the discipline and avoid answers that might suggest that you are only pursuing electrical engineering for financial reasons or because someone else encouraged you to.

Technical Interview Questions

5) What are the fundamental components of electrical circuits?

Here are the primary elements that make up an electrical circuit:

  • Active Circuits: Active Circuits are the elements used to generate energy from within their system as a form of source.
  • Passive Circuits: Passive circuits are the elements that allow the electric current to pass through it and do not generate any energy from it.
  • Here's a possible rephrased version of the content: Electric circuits can run on either alternating current (AC) or direct current (DC). In an AC circuit, the direction of the electric current changes periodically, producing a sinusoidal waveform. AC is used by power companies to distribute electricity to homes and businesses. In a DC circuit, the electric current flows in one direction only and is typically produced by batteries or electronic devices. DC is often used in electronics, such as computers, cell phones, and portable devices, because it provides a steady and predictable voltage. AC stands for Alternating Current, and DC stands for Direct Current. AC always produces some fluctuating current, whereas DC always produces a stable current in a constant source.
  • Series Circuits: When the electrical components are connected in series within a circuit, it is known as a series circuit.
  • Parallel Circuits: When the electrical components are connected in parallel within a circuit, it is known as a parallel circuit.

6) What is the difference between the term Capacitance and Inductance?

Capacitance and inductance are two important properties that affect the behavior of electrical circuits. Capacitance refers to the ability of a component to store an electrical charge, while inductance refers to the ability of a component to create a magnetic field when current flows through it. These properties can have different effects on the behavior of a circuit, and understanding them is crucial for designing and analyzing circuits.

  • Capacitance: The term capacitance can be specified as an amount of charge stored inside a capacitor at a specifically given voltage.
  • Inductance: Inductance is the property of a coil to resist any changes in the electric current flowing through it. Mutual inductance happens when a secondary coil opposes the current change in the primary coil.

7) What would happen when two positively charged materials are placed together?

The concept of positive charges repelling and opposite charges attracting is well-known. If you bring two materials with a positive charge together, they will push each other away due to this repulsion.

Electric transmission systems use various types of cables to transfer electrical power. Three common cable types include...

  • A high tension wire is utilized for transmitting electrical energy up to 23000 volts.
  • Super Tension Wire is a type of wire that is designed to carry electricity up to a maximum of 132 kV.

9) What is the difference between a Capacitor, Resistor, and Inductor?

A capacitor is an electronic device that works passively by resisting current flow and storing electric charges when a voltage is applied. It serves as an electrical component that stores the energy temporarily and opposes the current flow.

  • Resistor: A resistor is an electrical component that opposes the flow of current. It is a two-terminal component and is mainly used to reduce the flow of current.
  • Inductor: An inductor is an electrical component used to build electrical circuits and store the energy in the form of a magnetic field. It is also called a choke or a coil.

10) What is the definition of an ideal transformer?

An ideal transformer is one that experiences no losses during the transformation process. This means that the input power to the transformer should be equal to the output power, resulting in 100% efficiency.

Transformer Input Power = Transformer Output Power PIN = POUT.

11) What do you understand by reverse polarity? What is the process of fixing it?

Reverse polarity is a term used to describe the situation where electrical wires are connected incorrectly. This occurs when the neutral and hot wires are switched, so that the white wire is connected to the hot side and the black wire is connected to the neutral side. To fix this problem, it's necessary to investigate the wiring and check the connection from the outlet to the receptacle. Once the mistake is identified, the polarity can be corrected by swapping the wires around.

12) What are the different types of networks used to build an electrical circuit?

There are two primary categories of networks that are utilized to create an electrical circuit: Passive Networks and Active Networks. A Passive Network is a type of network that includes passive components within it, like Capacitance, Resistance, or Inductance. In contrast, Active Networks are those that have active components like Voltage or Current Sources incorporated into them.

13) How a tube light circuit is connected, and how does it work?

In a tube light circuit, there is a component called a choke that is connected to one end of the tube light, and a starter is also included in the circuit. When the current is supplied, the starter interrupts the flow of AC in the circuit. This sudden interruption causes the choke to produce a high voltage of approximately 1000 volts. This voltage is sufficient to ionize the gas within the tube and allow the flow of electrons to start. Once the voltage passes through the tube, the starter circuit is no longer needed, and it is disconnected. This causes the choke voltage to return to normal and reduces the current flowing through the tube.

14) What are the functions of the different colored wires in the electric circuit?

The various colored wires in an electric circuit have specific functions assigned to them. Typically, there are seven distinct wire colors used in electrical work, each performing a different purpose.

  • Black: The black wire is the primary source of power supply in the electric circuit. This is not used for ground wires. It is either used in the hot state or live state.
  • Green: The green wire is used to connect to the ground terminal. An outlet box contains green wires.
  • Red: The red wire is the second primary wire. It is used to transmit the live state of the wire. It can be used in different types of interconnections and are present in a circuit of 220 volts. Moreover, it can either be connected to another red wire or a black wire.
  • White/Gray: The white or gray wire is the neutral wire. It is used to carry out the unbalanced load to the terminal. The terminal of this wire is located in the ground. This wire can connect itself to its types of wires.
  • Blue/Yellow: The blue or yellow wires carry power, so they are used as live wires. Generally, most devices do not have these wires.

18) What is Armature Reaction?

Armature reaction refers to how the magnetic field produced by the armature conductors interacts with the main field. It is caused by the current flowing through the armature conductors, creating a magnetic field that affects the overall magnetic field.

When selecting a wire, there are various factors that must be considered, such as wire capacity and gauge size. For heavier electrical products like furnaces or heaters, a smaller wire gauge should be used as it can handle a higher current capacity. Gauge 6 wire is an example of a wire that can handle higher currents. For household items like lamps or lights, which require a lower voltage, a wire with a higher gauge value should be used. Gauge 12 wire would be appropriate for these kinds of products.

21) What do you understand by ASCR cable, and where is it used?

ACSR cable stands for Aluminum Conductor Steel-Reinforced cable. It is a type of high-capacity, high-strength stranded conductor wire typically used in overhead power lines. It is also used in broadcast & distribution. It is best suited for broadcast & distribution because of its good conductivity, low weight, low cost, resistance to corrosion, and decent mechanical stress resistance.

22) What is the Control System, and what are its different types?

A Control System is a system that operates with a relationship between its input and output parameters, where the input parameters influence the output parameters. Typically, Control Systems are classified as either Open-loop Control systems or Closed-loop Control Systems.

  • Open-loop Control System: In Open-Loop Control Systems, there is no feedback from the output-input for correction.
  • Closed-Loop Control Systems: In Closed Loop Control Systems, there is feedback from the output to input for correction to control the desired output quantities.

23) What do you understand by the RLC current?

RLC circuit is a combination of Resistor (R), Inductor (L) and Current (C). It is named so because each of these components provides a significant contribution to the circuit's overall functionality. RLC circuits have the capacity to allow direct current flow throughout, and this capacity is characterized by a second-order differential equation. As a result of this, they are referred to as second-order circuits.

25) What do you understand by Alternator and Generator? What are the main differences between them?

Both Alternators and Generators are devices utilized for generating power, but they work differently. Alternators produce electricity on a smaller level, while Generators generate electricity by transforming one type of energy into another.

The following outlines the key distinctions between an Alternator and a Generator:

Alternator Generator
The Alternator is a device that can produce electricity in small applications. A device known as a Generator is utilized for producing electricity for vast quantities of energy needs or distribution.
An Alternator is a device that generates electrical energy through the rotation of a magnetic field within copper wire windings. A generator employs the use of a dynamo to transform the energy from mechanical to electrical. This transformation is facilitated through the concept of electromagnetic induction.
The use of alternators helps to conserve energy by providing only the necessary amount of energy needed. This ensures that energy is not wasted. Generators consume all the energy they generate, limiting their ability to conserve energy.
An alternator produces an alternating current by electromagnetic induction. A generator can produce two different types of electrical current: alternating current (AC) and direct current (DC).
The efficiency of alternators is widely acknowledged. Generators are typically deemed to be less effective in terms of energy usage.
Polarization is not necessary for alternators. Generators need to undergo polarization measures after installation.
The stator in an alternator contains a rotating magnetic field. The generator operates with a fixed magnetic field while the winding of the armature rotates.
The component in an alternator which generates an electrical current by electromagnetic induction is known as the armature. This part of the alternator remains fixed and does not move. The Generator's core component, known as the armature, is in motion and rotating.
The Alternator is powered by the electricity generated by the stator. The Generator obtains its power source from the rotor.
Alternators produce electricity only when there is a demand for it. Generators are capable of producing electrical voltage continuously.
Alternators require a high range of RPM to operate. Generators typically operate at high RPMs.
The size of alternators is typically smaller than other similar electrical components. In general, generators have a larger physical size and require a larger space to be accommodated in.
The charging capacity of an alternator is insufficient to charge a completely dead battery. It is possible to use a generator to replenish the charge of a discharged battery.
The durability of the brushes present in alternators is quite high. The durability of the brushes in generators is lower than that of alternators.
The primary application of alternators is in the automotive sector, where they function as a means of charging the battery. Generators are primarily utilized for the production of electrical power on a large scale.

31) How the NPN and PNP transistor works?

When incorporating NPN transistors into an electronic circuit:

No current flowing from A to D = No flow from X to Z Current flowing from A to D = Current allowed to flow from X to Z

When considering the use of a PNP transistor in an electronic circuit:

No current flowing from A to D = Current is allowed to flow from X to Z Current flowing from A to D = No current flow from X to Z

32) What do you understand by a laser diode?

A laser diode is a small electronic device with two or more leads. It is a type of semiconductor device that functions similarly to a light-emitting diode. When an electrical current is applied, the diode begins to emit laser light at its junction. Laser diodes have the ability to convert electrical power directly into light.

33) What do you understand by reverse polarity, and how can you fix it?

Reverse polarity is an issue that occurs when one or more receptacles are not connected properly. To fix this problem, you need to check the wiring at the outlet and examine the receptacle closely. Reverse polarity occurs when the white wire is attached to the hot side and the black wire is connected to the neutral side of a receptacle. A simple solution to this issue is to interchange the wires.

34) What is the full form of FPGA, and why is it used?

FPGA, or Field Programmable Gate Array, is a type of logic chip designed to function as any system or digital circuit. It is similar to PLDs, but differs in that FPGAs can support more gates, up to thousands. To program the FPGA architecture, designers typically use a language called HDL, which specifies the configuration of the chip.

35) What do you understand by a two-phase motor?

A two-phase motor is an electric motor that has two windings positioned 90 degrees apart from each other. It requires two live wires and one ground wire, as it operates in two phases. The motor produces two voltages out of phase with each other. This technology is now obsolete and no longer widely used.

36) What is a rectifier and what are its different types?

A rectifier is an electric component that transforms AC (alternating current) into DC (direct current) by enabling the current to flow in one direction.

There are two distinct categories of rectifiers.

  • Half-wave rectifier: The half-wave rectifier is a type of rectifier that has only a single PN junction.
  • Full-wave rectifier: The full-wave rectifier is a type of rectifier that has two PN junctions.

37) What are the main differences between an Analogue and a Digital circuit?

An analogue circuit and a digital circuit have distinct characteristics that set them apart from each other. While they both deal with processing electronic signals, they differ in the way they handle and transmit information.

Analogue Circuit Digital Circuit
The analogue circuits function on signals with continuous values, as opposed to digital circuits which operate on discrete signals with defined levels. Digital circuits use a signal that consists of two distinct levels: 0 and 1. These levels represent different states of the signal.
This particular circuit has the capability to perform logic operations and generate analog output without any need for input signal conversion prior to transmission. The process of transmitting signals in this circuit involves the conversion of the input signal into digital form before transmission.
This circuit provides more accurate results compared to others as it doesn't involve any data conversion, which eliminates the risk of losing any information during the process. The accuracy of the circuit is lower due to the possibility of losing some data during the signal conversion process.
The analogue circuit does not offer much in the way of adaptability or customizability. Digital circuits have a vast potential to provide users with maximum flexibility.

38) What do you understand by a transistor?

A transistor is an electronic component made of semiconducting materials that allows for the interpretation of electrical signals and powers. It needs to be connected to an external circuit with at least three terminals. It consists of a combination of both n-type and p-type semiconductors.

39) What are the main usages of a transistor?

Transistors serve the main purpose of amplifying the input current, leading to a corresponding increase in the output. Their fundamental function involves enhancing both electric and electrical power.

40) What do you understand by AVR or automatic voltage regulator?

The Automatic Voltage Regulator, commonly abbreviated as AVR, is a crucial component in Synchronous Generators that helps regulate the voltage. Its primary function is to maintain a consistent voltage by addressing any fluctuations in the electrical supply system. These voltage fluctuations can be caused by varying loads and have the potential to damage the power system's equipment. Installing voltage control devices near transformers, feeders, and generators is critical to controlling these fluctuations.

41) What is SF6 gas? Why is it used?

Sulphur Hexafluoride gas, commonly known as SF6 gas, is used to extinguish electrical arcs in circuit breakers. It is highly effective due to its superior physical and chemical properties, making it an excellent dielectric and arc quenching medium, surpassing other alternatives such as oil or air. The SF6 circuit breaker is available in three main types.

  • A non-puffer piston circuit breaker is a type of circuit breaker that doesn't use a gas-filled chamber to quench the arc. Instead, it uses a piston to compress the arc, extinguishing it and interrupting the flow of current.
  • A breaker circuit with a piston that has a singular puffer mechanism.

This technology utilizes a unique mechanism whereby two puffer actions (compressing and releasing gas) occur for each single operation of the breaker. This results in significantly increased performance and reliability compared to traditional circuit breakers.

42) Why is SF6 gas used in circuit breakers?

SF6 gas is utilized in circuit breakers for its low viscosity, which allows it to efficiently transfer heat via convection. This makes it an ideal choice for use in a broad range of medium and high voltage electrical power systems. As a result, SF6 has become an essential component in electrical engineering.
